# Code of Conduct

Coco follows a simple norm: be kind, focus on the work, give credit.

## In short

- Treat others with respect, regardless of role, background, or skill level
- Argue ideas, not people
- Assume good intent; ask before assuming bad
- Give credit where it's due
- Don't disrupt others trying to participate

## Out-of-scope behavior

Anyone whose actions consistently make the project less welcoming or less productive may have their participation paused or removed by a maintainer.

## Reporting

Concerns can be reported by [opening an issue](https://github.com/rkz91/coco/issues) (use the `private` label or email if sensitive). Reports are handled discreetly.

## External reference

For a more detailed framework, see the widely-adopted [Contributor Covenant 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/). Coco aligns with its principles even though we keep our wording short here.
