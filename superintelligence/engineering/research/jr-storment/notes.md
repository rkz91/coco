## 3. Canonical definition of FinOps (he authored / stewards it)

Official FinOps Foundation definition (verbatim, fetched 2026-05-30):

> "FinOps is an operational framework and cultural practice which maximizes the business
> value of technology, enables timely data-driven decision making, and creates financial
> accountability through collaboration between engineering, finance, and business teams."

- Source: https://www.finops.org/introduction/what-is-finops/
- The page stresses FinOps is a **cultural shift, not just a cost-cutting tool**;
  distributed teams take **shared ownership** of cost and usage, supported by a central
  best-practices group. The "engineering + finance + business" triumvirate is the
  distinguishing structure.

### 4a. FinOps X 2025 — Day 1 Keynote (2025-06-03)
- Storment opened FinOps X 2025 declaring the practice is evolving into the **"Cloud+ Era."**
- Introduced **FinOps Scopes** — "apply the FinOps Framework to various segments of
  technology spend and usage data" (SaaS, PaaS, data center, licensing — beyond public cloud).
- Introduced **FOCUS 1.2** (the open billing-data spec), folding SaaS + PaaS billing into
  the same schema as core cloud spend (formal announcement by Shawn Alpay, FOCUS WG chair).

  "designed for the age of AI," with full certification targeted by **March 2026**.
- Source: https://www.finops.org/insights/finops-x-2025-day-1-keynote/

### 4d. State of FinOps 2026 report (published 2026)
- 6th annual survey. **1,192 respondents** representing **$83B+ in annual cloud spend.**
- **AI cost management is the #1 forward-looking priority** and the single most-desired
  skillset for the next 12 months.
- **98% now manage AI spend** (vs ~31% two years prior / 63% in 2025 per different cuts).
- Scope expanded beyond cloud: **90% manage SaaS**, 64% licensing, 57% private cloud,
  48% data center.
- **78% of FinOps teams report into CTO/CIO** (up 18% vs 2023); exec-aligned practices
  show **2–4x more influence** over tech-selection decisions.
- **Mission change:** "Advancing the People who manage the Value of **Cloud**" →
  "Advancing the People who manage the Value of **Technology**."
- Notable quote from the report: "We have hit the 'big rocks' of waste and now face a
  high volume of smaller opportunities."
- Sources:
  - https://data.finops.org/
  - https://www.finops.org/insights/2026-finops-framework/
  - https://www.flexera.com/blog/finops/finops-enters-its-technology-value-era-insights-from-the-state-of-finops-2026/

## 7. Blind spots (inferred from the body of work, not cited as quotes)

- **Institutional/consensus bias.** As the steward of a standards body funded by vendors
  and large enterprises, his framing favors process, certification, and committee-driven
  specs (FOCUS) over fast unilateral cuts. Corey Quinn's critique that "optimization is a
  point solution" cuts both ways — Storment can over-index on the long cultural program
  when a blunt instance-rightsizing pass would save more, sooner.
- **Enterprise-scale assumption.** The State of FinOps cohort is large cloud spenders
  ($83B aggregate). His prescriptions assume an org big enough to fund a central FinOps
  team; they map poorly onto a solo founder or small startup.
- **Not a hands-on engineer voice.** He is a discipline-definer and operator, not a
  systems builder; deep technical cost-mechanism detail (spot markets, kernel-level
  efficiency, GPU scheduling) is Mike Fuller / Erik Peterson / vendor-engineer territory.
