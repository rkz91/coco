## Biography (verified)

- **1999:** Co-founded **Ximian** with Miguel de Icaza (GNOME apps; Mono / .NET). Acquired by Novell 2003. Friedman was CTO/Chief Strategy Officer for Open Source at Novell until Jan 2010.
- **May 2011:** Co-founded **Xamarin** (CEO) with de Icaza — commercial Mono / cross-platform mobile .NET. **Microsoft acquired Xamarin in 2016.**
- **June 2018:** Microsoft acquires GitHub for **$7.5 billion**; Friedman named CEO. Assumed role **Oct 29, 2018**.
  - Shipped under his tenure: **GitHub Actions, GitHub Copilot, GitHub Codespaces, mobile apps (iOS/Android), GitHub Advanced Security, GitHub Sponsors, Discussions, GitHub CLI, CodeQL, Dependabot.** Acquired six companies incl. **npm** and **Semmle**. Reached ~50M+ developers.
  - **Copilot launched June 29, 2021** as "your AI pair programmer," built with OpenAI (Codex). Friedman authored the launch blog.
- **November 2021:** Announced stepping down; **Thomas Dohmke** succeeded him.
- **2023:** Created **nat.dev** (LLM playground / multi-model access). Launched **NFDG** venture fund with **Daniel Gross** — raised ~$1.1B debut fund; ~4x return on ~$550M deployed in under two years. Portfolio incl. SSI (Safe Superintelligence), Perplexity, Character.AI, Magic, etc.
- **AI Grant** (with Daniel Gross): accelerator giving AI-native startups **$250K funding + $250K Azure credits**. Started making AI investments ~2021.
- **Vesuvius Challenge** (launched **March 15, 2023** with Daniel Gross + Brent Seales): prize to read carbonized Herculaneum scrolls via CT + ML.
  - **Feb 2024:** 2023 Grand Prize ($700K) won — first readable text from a sealed scroll (Philodemus, Epicurean philosophy on pleasure/music/food). Friedman tweet Feb 5, 2024.
  - 2024 Grand Prize (read 90% of 4 scrolls) **not won**; pivoted to progress prizes. $60K First Automated Segmentation (Dec 2024); **$60K First Title Prize (May 2025)** — Marcel Roth + Micha Nowak found the end-title of a sealed scroll.
  - **Feb 7, 2025:** Oxford / Bodleian announced first look inside Scroll PHerc. 172.
  - **$200K Kaggle Surface Detection competition (March 2026).** Total awarded to date ~$1.78M.

## Engineering philosophy / stances (from nat.org + Dwarkesh 2023 + Antoine Buteau notes)

- **"Going fast makes you focus on what's important; there's no time for bullshit."** — speed as a forcing function. (nat.org)
- **"It's important to do things fast. You learn more per unit time because you make contact with reality more frequently."** (Buteau / nat.org)
- **"Smaller teams are better. Faster decisions, fewer meetings, more fun."** Many tech companies "2–10x overstaffed." "No room for mediocre people." (nat.org)
- **"Work on raising the ceiling, not the floor."** (nat.org)
- **Rejects the efficient-market hypothesis** — "it's more accurate to model the world as 500 people than 8 billion." Obvious opportunities go unexploited. (Dwarkesh 2023)
- **AI coding trajectory:** Copilot went from ~20% → ~50% of code; "I don't see any reason why that won't be 95%." (Dwarkesh 2023)
- **AI models are "grown," not engineered** — we don't fully understand mechanics. (Buteau)
- **Scale + proprietary data + talent wins;** founders who scale data+compute+talent outpace those who don't. (Buteau)
- **Open source** thrives in infra/horizontal tech where coordination burden is low; struggles for user-facing apps needing editorial/aesthetic control — favors "benign dictators" over pure consensus. (Dwarkesh 2023)
- **"As human beings it is our right (maybe our moral duty) to reshape the universe to our preferences. Technology, which is really knowledge, enables this."** (nat.org)
- **DeepSeek tweet (Jan 2025):** "The deepseek team is obviously really good. China is full of talented engineers. Every other take is cope. Sorry." — respects raw engineering talent over geopolitical narrative.
