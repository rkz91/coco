## Career timeline (from Wikipedia, fetched 2026-05-30)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katie_Moussouris

- **2002** — Joined @stake as a penetration tester, invited by Chris Wysopal.
- **October 2004** — @stake acquired by Symantec; she founded and managed
  Symantec Vulnerability Research (2004), described as the first program
  allowing researchers to publish vulnerability findings.
- **May 2007** — Joined Microsoft as a security strategist.
- **August 2008** — Announced the Microsoft Vulnerability Research (MSVR)
  program at Black Hat.
- **2010–2014** — Senior Security Strategist Lead; ran Security Community
  Outreach and Strategy within the Microsoft Security Response Center.
- Created Microsoft's first bug bounty program (paid $253,000+, received 18
  vulnerabilities over her tenure). Instigated the Microsoft BlueHat Prize for
  the Advancement of Exploit Mitigations (awarded $260,000+ at Black Hat USA
  2012, with a $200,000 grand prize).
- **May 2014** — Named Chief Policy Officer at HackerOne.
- **March 2016** — Directly involved in creating the U.S. Department of
  Defense's "Hack the Pentagon," the first U.S. federal bug bounty program
  (organized and vetted by HackerOne).
- **April 2016** — Founded Luta Security, a consultancy helping organizations
  and governments collaborate with hackers through bug bounty and vulnerability
  disclosure programs.

### Standards & policy

- **ISO/IEC 29147** (vulnerability disclosure) and **ISO/IEC 30111**
  (vulnerability handling processes): co-author and co-editor. Helped edit
  29147 since ~2008; in April 2016 worked with CERT/CC's Art Manion to secure
  free public availability of 29147.
- **Wassenaar Arrangement (2013–2016)** — Wrote an op-ed criticizing the
  "intrusion software" export-control amendments; invited as a technical expert
  to assist U.S. negotiations; helped rewrite the amendments with end-use
  decontrol exemptions.

### Academic / research

- MIT Sloan / Harvard Belfer Center: visiting scholar and affiliate researcher
  on vulnerability-economy labor markets.
- MIT Press (2017): co-authored a book chapter on the first system-dynamics
  model of the exploit market.

### Congressional testimony

- 2018 — U.S. Senate Subcommittee on Consumer Protection (security research for
  defensive purposes).
- 2021 — U.S. House Committee on Science, Space & Technology (cybersecurity of
  software supply chains).
- Witness bio (2021): https://www.congress.gov/117/meeting/house/112699/witnesses/HHRG-117-SY21-Bio-MoussourisK-20210525.pdf

### Advisory roles (from SANS profile, fetched 2026-05-30)

Source: https://www.sans.org/profiles/katie-moussouris
- Served on DHS's Cyber Safety Review Board (CSRB).
- Served on NIST's Information Security and Privacy Advisory Board (ISPAB).

### Philanthropy & awards

- 2021 — Donated $1 million to Penn State Law for the Anuncia Donecia Songsong
  Manglona Lab for Gender and Economic Equity (named after her mother), focused
  on gender-equity litigation and workplace financial discrimination.
- 2014 — SC Magazine Women in IT Security.
- 2018 — Forbes "America's Top 50 Women In Tech."

### Legal action (CORRECTION to a common assumption)

- **September 2015** — Filed a sex-discrimination *class-action lawsuit against
  MICROSOFT* (federal court, Seattle), alleging discrimination in hiring,
  performance evaluations, pay, and promotions for women in technical/
  engineering roles. NOTE: this lawsuit was against Microsoft, NOT HackerOne.
  A naive assumption that her gender-equity litigation targeted HackerOne (her
  2014–2016 employer) would be wrong. Logged here per the instruction to correct
  wrong assumptions. She left Microsoft in 2014 for HackerOne and filed the
  Microsoft suit in 2015; she founded Luta Security in 2016.

## Public stances (each with evidence URL)

1. **Bug bounties are not vulnerability disclosure; conflating them is
   dangerous.** "It's dangerous when people think that bug bounties are the same
   as vulnerability disclosure." "Vulnerability coordination and disclosure are
   absolutely not the same as bug bounty programs."
   Source (CSO Online, 2018-05-14):
   https://www.csoonline.com/article/565356/katie-moussouris-its-dangerous-to-conflate-bug-bounties-and-vulnerability-disclosure.html

2. **The bottleneck is fixing, not finding.** "Bug bounties give a false sense
   of security." "Unless the DHS has enough resources on the back end to process
   these things, they are not going to be able to fix the bugs that come in."
   "Organizations should have a way to receive vulnerability reports and process
   them to resolution." (Equifax cited as the canonical patch-lag failure.)
   Same CSO Online source as above. Reinforced by her 2025 "logjam between
   finding bugs and fixing them" framing (Cybernews).

3. **Naive bug-bounty economics waste money.** The EU's ~EUR 1.9M bug bounty
   against open-source software yielded only three paid submissions; structural
   funding for maintenance would do more. Same CSO Online source.

4. **AI is flooding bug bounties with "AI slop" and overwhelming maintainers.**
   "some of it definitely qualifies as AI slop... there's no real bug there";
   "AI was the accelerant on a perverse incentive fire sparked by bug bounty
   platforms that reward spray & pray."
   Sources: https://cybernews.com/ai-news/was-2025-the-year-ai-broke-the-bug-bounty-model/
   and https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kmoussouris_bug-bountymd-we-stop-the-bug-bounty-end-activity-7419755161675440128-9RkH

5. **Government mandates to disclose unpatched vulnerabilities are dangerous.**
   On China's vuln-reporting law: "The biggest problem with this provision is if
   other countries start imposing the same requirements on security research";
   "As more parties know about an unpatched vulnerability, the chances of leakage
   are extremely high."
   Source (The Record, 2024-01-12):
   https://therecord.media/katie-moussouris-vulnerability-disclosure-china-european-union

6. **If AI is trained on human hackers' expertise, those humans deserve a share
   — fund UBI from AI productivity.** Recurring close to her 2025-2026 talks and
   the Phoenix Cast Glasswing episode.
   Sources: https://cybernews.com/ai-news/was-2025-the-year-ai-broke-the-bug-bounty-model/
   and https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/katie-moussouris-project-glasswing/id1508967644?i=1000761444437

7. **Security is a labor problem; burnout and under-resourced response teams are
   the real risk.** She advocates "bringing balance back to the labor workforce"
   and warns AI tools "will displace the very skilled workers it's supposed to
   empower."
   Source: https://uktechnews.co.uk/2025/11/18/major-crises-and-ai-threats-to-dominate-agenda-at-landmark-sans-conference-for-cyber-threat-hunting-community/
