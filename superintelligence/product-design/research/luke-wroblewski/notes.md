## Canonical works

### Books
- **Web Form Design: Filling in the Blanks** — Rosenfeld Media, May 1, 2008, 226 pp. Definitive guide to form UX; draws on original research, Yahoo!/eBay experience, international usability testing, eye-tracking, 10+ years designing web apps. Covers form types, input fields, labels, validation, feedback, calls to action, surrounding visual elements. Sources: https://rosenfeldmedia.com/books/web-form-design/ ; https://www.lukew.com/resources/web_form_design.asp ; widely circulated PDF distillation https://static.lukew.com/webforms_lukew.pdf.
- **Mobile First** — A Book Apart (#6), October 2011. Two parts: "Why Mobile First?" (Growth, Constraints, Capabilities) and "How to go Mobile" (Organization, Actions, Inputs, Layout). Thesis: design for the constrained mobile screen first; constraints force prioritization; scales up better than desktop scales down. Sources: https://www.lukew.com/resources/mobile_first.asp ; https://abookapart.com/products/mobile-first.
- **Site-Seeing: A Visual Approach to Web Usability** — his first book (visual treatment of web usability). Source: https://www.lukew.com/about/.

### Blog (lukew.com/ff/)
The single largest practitioner body of writing on AI product design. As of Nov 2025: 70 articles over 2.5 years. Source: https://x.com/LukeW/status/1985745937634115673.

## Recent signals (all dated after 2025-06-01 except the 2025-04 spine essay)

Pulled from lukew.com/ff index and individual entries, verified 2026-06-01:

1. **"Collaborative Steering"** — 2026-05-18 — https://www.lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2153=
   Quotes: "Today, AI tools are mostly solo sports. Developers write more code. Designers create more images." / "when anybody can build what they want, you feel it in the product because you ship fifteen different ideas instead of one unified point of view." / "The tools that figure out how to make collaborative steering natural and lightweight are going to change how teams, not just individuals, build."
   Argument: current AI tools boost individuals; fragmentation hurts product cohesion; advocate shared mechanism ("collaborative steering") for teams to co-create the context that directs AI agents.

2. **"Mockups Were Never the Hard Part"** — 2026-05-11 — https://lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2152=
   Quotes: "With AI, anyone on a team can generate mockups in minutes. That was never the hard part." / "The difficult work is, and always has been, maintaining coherency and intention across a product so it works for people." / "The layout should stem from the underlying purpose of an application and how we represent the system that enables it to people."
   Scene: a meeting where backend engineers, PMs, and front-end devs each arrived with their own AI-generated mockups/builds.

3. **"Finally, the Handoff is Dead"** — 2026-04-30 — https://lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2150=
   The designer/developer handoff persists despite AI raising output; demoed a different workflow with Amelia Wattenberger at Design Futures Assembly; changing how the two disciplines collaborate means unwinding tools, habits, and politics.

4. **"Should Designers 'Code'?"** — 2026-04-07 — https://www.lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2147=
   Stance: yes. AI coding agents reopen a door that ~a decade of front-end complexity had closed.
   Quote: "Just like a sculptor needs to know how marble chisels, breaks, and buffs, a Web designer should know how CSS, HTML, and Javascript construct interfaces within a Web browser." / "Designing software is better when you work in the medium, not a level or two abstracted away."

5. **"Durable Patterns in AI Product Design"** — 2026-03-12 — https://www.lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2145=
   Four years building AI-native companies → three durable patterns:
   - Suggested questions ("When you ask something, the system shows follow-up suggestions tied to your question and the broader corpus." — did enormous heavy lifting in testing, teaching capability).
   - Citation/trust ("Even just seeing where information comes from gives people a real trust boost.").
   - Walls-of-text problem ("Bringing back multimedia, like responding with images alongside text, or using diagrams and interactive elements, helped a lot.").
   Three persistent issues across AI evolution: capability awareness, context awareness, output presentation.

6. **"The Evolution of AI Products"** — 2025-04-28 — https://lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2096=
   (Spine essay; pre-window but foundational, included as canonical work + 6th recent signal for context.)
   Staged model: behind-the-scenes ML (Google Translate, YouTube recs ~2016) → chat (ChatGPT brings models to the foreground) → retrieval-augmented context (RAG; "AI models provided much better results if they were given more context") → agentic tools (models "plan which ones to use and how") → background agents ("only bother you when they are done or need help") → agent-to-agent collaboration (emerging frontier).

Additional 2025/2026 posts seen on index (not all individually fetched): "Wrapper vs. Embedded AI Apps" (2025-10-06), "We're (Still) Not Giving Data Enough Credit" (2025-10-02), "The New Designer/Developer Collaboration" (2026-04-15), "Design Futures Assembly" (2026-05-05), "Finding the Role of Humans in AI Products" (2026-03-10).
