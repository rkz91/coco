## Direct quotes (for voice + public_stances citations)

From **"Adapting Ourselves to Adaptive Content"**
(https://karenmcgrane.com/talks/adapting-ourselves-to-adaptive-content/):
- "We are not in the web page publishing business. We are in the content publishing business."
- "We are in a war of Blobs versus Chunks. You all are on Team Chunk. We cannot let the blobs win."
- Blobs = "giant, unstructured blobs of content"; Chunks = "clean, well-structured fields of
  content that have metadata attached."
- "Metadata is the new art direction."
- "It is not a luxury to think about having a great application for content creators. In fact,
  it is a requirement."
- NPR's COPE (Create Once, Publish Everywhere) and TV Guide's three-length descriptions as the
  precedents for structured, reusable content.

From **"The Mobile Content Mandate"**
(https://karenmcgrane.com/talks/the-mobile-content-mandate/):
- "You don't get to decide which device people use to access the internet: they do."
- "There is no reason why someone is going to need to do that on mobile." (the wrong assumption
  she mocks)
- "It's not a strategy if you can't maintain it."
- "Responsive design won't fix your content problem."
- Digital-divide data she cites: ~large shares of low-income and minority Americans, and teens,
  rely primarily on mobile for internet access; therefore "the real website" excuse fails.
- Uses Clayton Christensen's disruptive-innovation theory (DEC/PCs, transistor radios, Kodak)
  to frame mobile as inevitable disruption. NOTE: this is a direct intellectual link to
  `clayton-christensen` in the same team roster.
