### The emotional-design "hierarchy of user needs"

Walter's central framework, modeled on Maslow's hierarchy. Bottom → top:

1. **Functional** — the design works / serves its purpose.
2. **Reliable** — it is consistent and trustworthy; it won't fail you.
3. **Usable** — it is easy and intuitive to use.
4. **Pleasurable** — it delights, surprises, and forms an emotional connection. (Top of the pyramid.)

Superior needs (pleasure/delight) can only be reached after foundational ones (functional, reliable, usable) are met. Sources: <https://blog.tubikstudio.com/design-for-emotion-expert-tips-by-aarron-walter/>, <https://www.researchgate.net/figure/Hierarchy-of-needs-of-users-by-Aarron-Walter_fig2_283960360>, <https://www.uxmatters.com/mt/archives/2018/05/designing-for-emotion.php>

- He extends user personas into a **design/brand persona** for the product itself — giving the product a personality the way you'd profile a user. Did this exercise for Mailchimp. Source: tubikstudio.com summary above.
- Companion book: **"Principles of Product Design."** Source: aarronwalter.com homepage; <https://designbetterpodcast.com/p/principles-of-product-design>

## Design maturity work (InVision era)

- At InVision he led a research team studying how design teams at Netflix, Spotify, Google, IBM, Verizon, Cisco operate. Output: a **Design Maturity Model**, ~11 books, 3 documentary films, and an award-winning podcast.
- Study scale: ~**2,200 companies** participated. Used by Fortune 100 firms (Google, IBM, Verizon, Cisco). Co-authored maturity research with **Leah Buley**.
- Sources: <https://www.aarronwalter.com/portfolio/invision>, <https://www.thisishcd.com/episode/leah-buley-aarron-walter-invisions-design-maturity-model>, <https://www.intercom.com/blog/podcasts/invisions-aarron-walter-on-design-culture/>
