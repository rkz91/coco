- **Career history (in his own bio):**

## 3. Public stances (each with evidence URL)

1. **Empowered product teams vs feature teams.** Empowered teams are given "a small number of significant problems to solve (the objectives)" rather than features to build; they exist "to serve the customers, in ways that meet the needs of the business," not merely "to serve the business."
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/empowered-product-teams/ (October 31, 2018)

2. **Missionaries, not mercenaries.** "We only want people in our organization that are excited about and dedicated to this vision – missionaries." References John Doerr.
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/empowered-product-teams/

3. **The four big product risks: value, usability, feasibility, business viability.** Every product idea must be tested against whether "customers will buy it or users will choose to use it" (value), "whether users can figure out how to use it" (usability), "whether our engineers can build what we need with the time, skills and technology we have" (feasibility), and "whether this solution also works for the various aspects of our business" (viability).
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/four-big-risks/ (December 4, 2017)

4. **Product discovery before product delivery — reduce risk before you build.** Discovery exists to "discover whether there are real users out there that want this product" and "discover a product solution to this problem that is usable, useful, and feasible," instead of using the engineering team as "a very, very expensive prototype" with live customers as "unwitting test subjects."
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/product-discovery/ (September 24, 2007)

5. **An MVP should never be a product.** One of "the most abused and misunderstood concepts in product management"; the MVP should be an experiment/prototype, not a QA'd shippable product — building a real product to learn wastes time and money. (This is the documented basis for the productive conflict with Eric Ries / Lean Startup.)
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/product-success/ (and the broader MVP critique threaded through INSPIRED, 2nd ed.) — secondary corroboration: https://wind4change.com/product-management-discovery-delivery-inspired-empowered-marty-cagan/
   - NOTE: SVPG's canonical MVP-critique article is titled "Product Success" / "MVP" in his older catalogue. Used `product-success` as evidence_url; flagged as slightly lower-confidence URL match (article content verified via search excerpt, not a direct fetch).

6. **The product operating model is the destination, not OKRs or Agile bolted onto a feature factory.** "If you take a company with feature teams and overlay OKRs, you get a mess — the real issue is moving to the empowered team model." Transformation is a move from the project model (output) to the product model (outcomes).
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/the-product-operating-model/

7. **In the AI era, foundation models can coach product creators at least as well as most managers.** "we now believe that the foundation models, when configured with appropriate project instructions and your company's strategic context, can provide product coaching at least as good as most managers." Explicitly framed as a reversal of two decades of SVPG advocacy for human coaching.
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/product-coaching-and-ai/ (February 4, 2026)

8. **In the AI era, the bottleneck has moved from building to discovery.** "The real bottleneck is in discovering a solution that's worth building." PMs build "to learn" (prototypes), engineers build "to earn" (delivery). "I believe that we are entering a golden era for skilled product people."
   - Evidence: https://www.svpg.com/build-to-learn-vs-build-to-earn/ (April 16, 2026)
