## 2. Biographical timeline (dated)

- **Born 1945** (age 80–81 as of 2026). Built his first electro-mechanical computer in 1957; worked with NCR's ERMA at Bank of America starting 1959.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini
- **June 1978 — joins Apple Computer.** Hired by Steve Jobs (and Jef Raskin) as Apple's first applications software engineer. **Employee #66.**
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini ; https://asktog.com/atc/the-third-user/
- **September 1978 — publishes the first edition of *The Apple Human Interface Guidelines*.** Seven subsequent editions followed; the guidelines reached the Macintosh group around 1982 and shaped Apple's product line from the early days into the 1990s.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini ; https://apple.fandom.com/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini
- **At Apple (14 years, 1978–1992):** founded the Apple Human Interface Group; designed Macintosh hierarchical menus; invented time-out dialog boxes; invented the "package" illusion for Mac applications; published *Super Hi-Res Chess* for the Apple II; co-authored the *Apple Presents…Apple* tutorial.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini ; https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/
- **1992 — leaves Apple; joins Sun Microsystems** as Distinguished Engineer for Strategic Technology (SunSoft). Tenure ~1992–1996.
  - Source: https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/
- **1992–1994 — produces the *Starfire* video prototype.** A usability-centered vision of the 21st-century "Office of the future," drawing on 100+ engineers, designers, futurists, and filmmakers. Inspired in part by Apple's 1987 *Knowledge Navigator*; the team constrained every depicted object/action to something buildable within ~10 years. The video anticipated the rise of the World Wide Web. Released/filmed 1994.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Starfire_video_prototype ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Starfire_(film) ; case-history paper: https://rauterberg.employee.id.tue.nl/presentations/SUN%20Starfire/tognazzini-1994.pdf
- **WebMD / Healtheon (~4 years, late 1990s–2000):** lead designer / led design. (WebMD founded 1996 by Jim Clark.) Note: he held employee #66 at WebMD too — a coincidence he has noted.
  - Source: https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini
- **2000 — co-founds / joins the Nielsen Norman Group** as the third principal alongside Jakob Nielsen and Don Norman, specializing in human-computer interaction. Founded the webzine *AskTog*.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nielsen_Norman_Group ; https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/
- **Now (2026): Retired Principal at NN/g.** His NN/g profile title is explicitly "Retired Principal." He continues to publish the free webzine *AskTog* (asktog.com), though new output is sparse.
  - Source: https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/

**Patents:** 56–58 US patents (NN/g says 56 issued; Wikipedia/other sources cite 57 issued of 58 filed). Domains: aviation safety, radar, GPS (incl. US 6278660, a time-zone-tracking GPS wristwatch), eye-tracking, flat-panel displays, portable calendaring, digital-camera electronic viewfinders, and (with Jakob Nielsen) an eye-track-driven browser.
  - Source: https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini

**Books (both Addison-Wesley):**
- *Tog on Interface* (1992) — the back cover is the source of the "Apple Employee #66" designation.
- *Tog on Software Design* (1995).
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Tognazzini ; https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/

## 3. Canonical works & key writings

- **First Principles of Interaction Design** (askTog; Revised & Expanded). The defining heuristic set. NN/g version published/revised **March 5, 2014**; askTog version last revised **March 5, 2014**.
  - askTog: https://asktog.com/atc/principles-of-interaction-design/
  - NN/g: https://www.nngroup.com/articles/first-principles-interaction-design/
- **"A Quiz Designed to Give You Fitts"** (askTog column, **February 1999**). The essay that popularized Fitts's Law for working UI designers — the "magic" screen corners/edges as effectively infinite targets, the Mac top-of-screen menu bar vs. Windows window-mounted menu bars, pie/circular menus, and the "<"-shaped buffer for hierarchical menus.
  - https://www.asktog.com/columns/022DesignedToGiveFitts.html
- **"Maximizing Human Performance"** (askTog basics / column 023). The famous mouse-vs-keyboard study: users were on average ~50% FASTER with the mouse, yet every user *reported* feeling faster with the keyboard. Tog's lesson: objective measurement beats user self-report; perceived effort ≠ actual performance.
  - https://www.asktog.com/basics/03Performance.html ; https://asktog.com/columns/023MaxHumanPerf.html
- **"The Third User: …Exactly Why Apple Keeps Doing Foolish Things"** (askTog / NN/g, **March 6, 2013**). The first user = power user; second = the user-we-design-for; the often-ignored **third user = the buyer / potential user**.
  - https://asktog.com/atc/the-third-user/
- **"Skeuomorphic Design: Don't Apply It Blindly"** (NN/g video, **May 4, 2018**) — his most recent dated NN/g content.
  - https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/
- **"Providing Predictable Targets"** (askTog / NN/g, **April 11, 2013**); **"The Apple iWatch"** (Feb 6, 2013, predicting a wrist device); **"Browse vs. Search: Which Deserves to Go?"** (Dec 1, 2011).
  - https://asktog.com/atc/providing-predictable-targets/ ; https://www.nngroup.com/people/bruce-tognazzini/
- **"Magic and Software Design"** (askTog paper). Stage magic as the deep analogue for GUI illusion; the line of descent from Xerox PARC. "Programmers tend to be uncomfortable with illusion."
  - https://www.asktog.com/papers/magic.html

## 5. Direct / near-direct quotes (for voice + public_stances)

- **Fitts's Law:** "The time to acquire a target is a function of the distance to and size of the target."
  - https://asktog.com/atc/principles-of-interaction-design/
- **Magic corners (Fitts essay):** the four screen corners and edges are effectively infinite targets — "you can throw the mouse toward the top of the screen with the assurance that it will never penetrate" (Mac menu bar advantage).
  - https://www.asktog.com/columns/022DesignedToGiveFitts.html
- **Latency:** "Acknowledge all button clicks by visual or aural feedback within 50 milliseconds."
  - https://asktog.com/atc/principles-of-interaction-design/
- **Defaults:** replace the word "Default" with a more meaningful, responsive term (e.g., "Restore Initial Settings"); meaningless defaults are a usability failure.
  - https://asktog.com/atc/principles-of-interaction-design/
- **Autonomy:** "The computer, interface, and task environment all 'belong' to the user," but user autonomy does not mean abandoning rules; keep status information current and visible.
  - https://asktog.com/atc/principles-of-interaction-design/
- **Protect Users' Work:** "Ensure that users never lose their work" — an "all but absolute" principle.
  - https://asktog.com/atc/principles-of-interaction-design/
- **Complexity / cognition:** "Any attempt to hide complexity will serve to increase it." Designers must deeply understand both the task domain and the user.
  - https://asktog.com/atc/principles-of-interaction-design/
- **Maximizing Human Performance:** users self-reported the keyboard felt faster while objectively being ~50% slower than the mouse — therefore *measure*, don't ask.
  - https://www.asktog.com/basics/03Performance.html
