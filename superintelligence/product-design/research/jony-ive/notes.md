## 1. Identity & biography (confirmed)

- Full name: **Sir Jonathan Paul "Jony" Ive**. Born 27 February 1967, Chingford, London, UK.
  Father Michael Ive was a silversmith and craft-teacher who lectured at Middlesex
  Polytechnic; grandfather was an engineer. Diagnosed with dyslexia in school.
  Source: Wikipedia, Britannica.
- Studied **industrial design at Newcastle Polytechnic** (now Northumbria University);
  influenced by Bauhaus and by Dieter Rams's Braun work. Source: Wikipedia, Britannica.
- Joined **Apple September 1992**; promoted to **Senior VP of Industrial Design 1997**
  after Steve Jobs's return; **Chief Design Officer May 2015**; **departed Apple
  27 June 2019** (~27-year tenure). Source: Wikipedia.
- Honors: **RDI** (Royal Designer for Industry), **HonFREng**, **CBE 2006**,
  **KBE / knighthood 2012** ("Sir Jony Ive"). Chancellor of the **Royal College of Art
  from 1 July 2017**. Appointed a **British Museum Trustee, June 2025**. Source: Wikipedia, ABC News.

### Canonical Apple products he led (design)
iMac G3 (1998, translucent case) · iPod (2001) · iPhone (2007) · iPad (2010) ·
MacBook line · Apple Watch (2015) · AirPods · the **"Deference, Clarity, Depth"**
direction of **iOS 7** (2013). Source: Wikipedia, MacRumors.

### Post-Apple
- **LoveFrom** — creative collective co-founded **2019** with longtime collaborator
  **Marc Newson**. Spans software, hardware, architecture, branding. Early/known clients:
  Airbnb, Ferrari, OpenAI; designed the **King Charles III coronation emblem (2023)**;
  redesigned the **Christie's auction rostrum (March 2026)**. Source: Wikipedia, Monocle, MacRumors.
- **io Products** — hardware startup **founded 2024** by Ive with **Scott Cannon,
  Evans Hankey, Tang Tan** (all ex-Apple). ~55 engineers/designers/physicists.
  Source: Wikipedia, TechCrunch.

## 2. The OpenAI "io" partnership (the headline 2025–2026 arc)

- **Announced 21 May 2025:** OpenAI acquires **io** in an **all-equity deal valued at
  ~$6.5 billion** (OpenAI already held a 23% stake from a prior agreement, so ~$5B
  incremental). OpenAI's **largest acquisition to date**.
  Source: TechCrunch (2025-05-21), NPR, Fortune, CNBC.
- **Deal closed 9 July 2025.** The io *team* merges into OpenAI; **LoveFrom remains
  independent** and "OpenAI becomes a customer of LoveFrom," with **Ive and LoveFrom
  assuming creative and design responsibilities across all of OpenAI's operations**
  (not just hardware — includes input on future ChatGPT). Source: MacRumors (2025-07-09), Wikipedia.
- **"A letter from Sam & Jony"** published on openai.com (2025-05-21). Note: the openai.com
  page returns HTTP 403 to automated fetch; quotes below are corroborated via TechCrunch,
  Fortune, NPR.
  - Altman: *"thrilled to be partnering with jony, imo the greatest designer in the world.
    excited to try to create a new generation of AI-powered computers."*
  - Ive (paraphrased in coverage): everything he's learned over 30 years "has led me to
    this place and this moment." Mission: a **"family of devices"** that move people
    **"beyond screens."**

### Device roadmap (as of early 2026)
- **First device delayed to 2027** (was targeted late 2026). Court filings via *Wired*
  (Feb 2026) and OpenAI VP/GM **Peter Welinder** confirm the product **will NOT be called
  "io"** — OpenAI **lost a trademark dispute to iyO**, a hearing-aid startup, and "decided
  not to use the name 'io' (or 'IYO,' or any capitalization of either)."
  Source: 9to5Mac (2026-02-10).
- **First product = a smart speaker WITH a camera**, ~**$200–$300**, **2027** earliest.
  Features reported: facial recognition (Face-ID-like, for purchase auth), contextual
  awareness, proactive suggestions. **Smart lamp** and **smart glasses** in early
  exploration for **2028+** (lamp may be cancelled). 200+ people on the devices team.
  Industrial design led by Evans Hankey under Ive's final sign-off.
  Source: MacRumors (2026-02-20), Engadget, Tom's Guide.
- Earlier framing (May 2025): first device "won't be a wearable / in-ear," will **sit in a
  pocket or on a desk alongside a Mac and an iPhone** — a *third* core device. Source: NPR, MacRumors.

### Design vision quotes for the device (dated)
- **Emerson Collective Demo Day '25**, onstage with **Laurene Powell Jobs**
  (event 2025-11-20, made public 2025-11-24):
  - Ive: *"I love solutions that teeter on appearing almost naive in their simplicity."*
  - Ive: *"We are going to make people smile. We are going to make people feel joy."*
  - Ive indicated unveiling **"even less than"** two years away; Altman: *"we have the
    first prototypes."* Altman recounted Ive's polish standard: users should want
    *"to lick it or take a bite out of it."*
  Source: Axios (2025-11-24), CNBC (2025-11-24).
- Ive on the broader motive (2025 coverage): a current **"uncomfortable relationship with
  technology,"** hoping AI devices can make people **"happy, fulfilled, peaceful, less
  anxious, and less disconnected."** Altman said Ive **pushed back** on a device that
  "knows everything users have thought about, read, or said" (privacy). Source: The Rundown AI, Axios.

## 3. Ferrari Luce — flagship LoveFrom industrial-design work (recent signal)

- **Unveiled 25 May 2026 in Rome.** Ferrari's **first all-electric car**, designed by
  **LoveFrom (Jony Ive + Marc Newson)** with Ferrari's in-house teams over a **five-year**
  collaboration. Source: Dezeen (2026-05-25), designboom, MacRumors (2026-05-25).
- Specs: four-door, **first five-seat Ferrari**; four motors, up to **1,035 hp**;
  **122 kWh, 800V** battery; range ~530 km; **drag coefficient 0.254** (lowest in Ferrari
  history); **60+ new patents**. Price **from ~€550,000 / ~$640,000**; production late
  2026, US Q2 2027.
- **Design thesis (load-bearing for the persona):** they **rejected the large central
  touchscreen** that dominates EV interiors in favor of **physical buttons, dials and
  switches** in **glass and anodised aluminium**; a hybrid instrument cluster with two
  stacked OLED panels and **physical needles** sandwiched between; a clock with motorized
  hands that becomes a stopwatch/compass. Body = a single teardrop "glasshouse" cabin
  wrapped in an aluminium shell.
  - Marc Newson: *"Aero is everything when it comes to designing an EV."*
  - Newson: *"Coherence was just so, so important for us"* (interior + exterior + interface
    as one design language). Source: Dezeen (2026-05-25).
- Critical reception: **mixed** per coverage (Wikipedia notes "mixed critical reception").

## 4. Design philosophy — canonical quotes (for voice + stances)

From the *Objectified* documentary (2009), AppleInsider 2013, London Life, Wikiquote:
- **On simplicity:** *"Simplicity is not the absence of clutter; that's a consequence of
  simplicity. Simplicity is somehow essentially describing the purpose and place of an
  object and product."* / *"True simplicity is derived from so much more than just the
  absence of clutter and ornamentation. It's about bringing order to complexity."*
- **On care:** *"The most important thing is that you actually care... when you spend an
  enormous amount of time and energy to try to get something right... your intent is felt."*
  / *"It's like finishing the back of a drawer. Nobody's going to see it, but you do it
  anyway. Products are a form of communication — they demonstrate your value system."*
- **On deference:** on the iPhone "everything defers to the display... a lot of what we're
  doing is getting design out of the way"; drove iOS 7's **Deference, Clarity, Depth**.
- **On inevitability:** *"When forms develop with that reason, and they're not just
  arbitrary shapes, it feels inevitable, it feels un-designed."*

### Stripe Sessions 2025 (interview with Patrick Collison, 2025-05-08/09)
- *"What we make stands testament to who we are."*
- The **team-breakfast ritual** (one designer cooks for the whole team each Friday) — empathy/care.
- *"Make things for each other."*
- **Warning for the tech industry:** *"Even if you're innocent in your intention, if
  you're involved in something that has poor consequences, you need to own it."*
- *"Joy in humans has been missing. Products are complicated, and sometimes joy gets
  confused with being trivial."*
  Source: MacRumors (2025-05-09), 9to5Mac (2025-05-08), AppleInsider (2025-05-09).
