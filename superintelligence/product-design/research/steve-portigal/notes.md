## Interviewing craft — core technique (from *Interviewing Users* + interviews)

- "Interviewing is easy, right? Anyone can do it… but few do it well enough to unlock the benefits and insights that interviewing users and customers can yield." (Rosenfeld 2nd-ed framing.)
- **Interviewing addresses a business need** — not generic information-gathering. Research studies and logistics flow from concrete business goals.
- **The gap between what people SAY and what they DO** is the central reason to do contextual research rather than just ask questions.
- **Embrace silence.** Silence after a question gives the participant room to think and produces richer answers; it signals respect and encouragement, and surfaces the difficulty of a question. Source: https://www.userinterviews.com/blog/interviewing-users-steve-portigal-applied-to-remote-research
- **Build rapport by making space, not by forcing.** "Creating a conversation where it builds and grows and you're not forcing someone to tell you something, you are creating space for them that they are filling." Sharing about yourself takes focus off the participant — embrace *their* worldview, spend the time on them.
- **Interviewing is like improv** — know when to stick to the plan and when to deviate from the script. (One Knight in Product, 2023-12-02.)
- **Contextual methods are "more than just asking questions"** — chapter title; observation + artifacts + environment.
- Recording sessions captures "exactly what people say," enabling transcription, analysis, and high-fidelity reuse.

## Reusable research / impact / "doomed to repeat" theme

From "Steve Portigal on Reusable Research, Interview War Stories, and Letting Go of Implicit Beliefs" (portigal.com, dated 2019-09-16):
- Siloed and **repetitive research wastes organizational resources**; making "the work of researchers (from data through results)… accessible and reusable" maximizes research impact.
- Meaningful research "means letting go of previously held beliefs. It's especially difficult with those that are implicit beliefs." Works to transform data into team ownership of new knowledge so change is less painful.
- War stories matter "because these experiences teach us and change us" — fieldwork's uncontrollable human element is a teaching device.
- URL: https://portigal.com/steve-portigal-on-reusable-research-interview-war-stories-and-letting-go-of-implicit-beliefs/

From One Knight in Product (episode dated 2023-12-02, https://www.oneknightinproduct.com/steve-portigal/):
- Impactful research must influence decisions, not exist "for research's sake."
- **Good research sometimes says "don't ship."**
- On bias: "bias just represents how our brains are wired" — don't fear it, acknowledge it and counteract via better practice.
- Continuous discovery (tight coupling with product) vs. point-in-time research (room for "truly disruptive insights") — both have merit; no universal formula.
- Build "actionable, accessible repositories" so research doesn't languish unused.

Research-practice maturity (from podcast appearances, 2025): how the practice has matured — ResearchOps, research leadership, insourcing/in-house teams; "no one right way." ResearchOps as a discipline developed over the last ~10 years to build tools and processes.
