## Methodology / signature ideas (from canonical work + interviews)

- **Goal-Directed Design:** identify users' *goals* before any formal design. Personas and scenarios are the operational machinery, not decoration.
- **Personas drive scenarios:** "if you just have a persona, it's like having a really interesting character, but no story to tell." Scenarios are comprehensive stories of how a persona interacts with the product/service. (Center Centre interview: https://articles.centercentre.com/goodwin_interview/; SpoolCast: https://uie.fm/shows/spoolcast/kim-goodwin-designing-with-scenarios-putting-personas-to-work).
- **Scenarios drive requirements:** requirements definition should fall out of scenario walkthroughs, not a feature list.
- **Design leadership / org design as a design problem:** she explicitly treats *the organization* as something you design. Talk: "Designing Culture" (Fluxible 2015); "organization as a design system."

### "The experience is made of decisions" — her central org-design thesis (Miro interview)
Source: https://miro.com/blog/kim-goodwin-user-centered-design/ (verified 2026-06-01; references 2023 asset — treated as ~2023, NOT a recent signal).
- > "The user experience is not made of design; it's made of decisions that a lot of different people make."
- > "When you hear that same trade-off getting made all the time, that tells you what the organization's actual values are."
- > "All of these things that we tend to view as constraints—a lot of them are not. They are something that someone has a constraint mindset about."
- Practical stances: interview stakeholders across levels to surface real decision patterns; frame **6–10 aspirational design principles** (positive, not failure-focused); connect to values the org already prizes; *measure* core values alongside business metrics to prevent over-optimization on engagement/conversion.

## Voice and style (observed)

- Plain, direct, practitioner English. Methodology-forward; allergic to vague "design thinking" theatre.
- Reframes abstractions into concrete decisions and trade-offs. "What decision does this research change?"
- Treats organizations and processes as designable artifacts.
- Ethical undertone: pushes back on engagement/conversion-maximizing design that loses the human-centered thread (the whole "Bring Back Human-Centered" thesis).
- Will name the political/organizational reality behind a design failure rather than blaming the designer.
