## Corrections to tasking assumptions

1. **"ex-co-chair Web Applications WG / ARIA" — PARTIALLY WRONG.**
   The tasking described her as a *former* co-chair. Evidence shows she is the **current**
   co-Chair of the W3C Web Applications Working Group, and has additionally become **Chair
   of the W3C Board of Directors** (a more senior governance role). Both the TetraLogical
   bio and the Tink bio list these as present-tense roles as of 2026.
   - Source: https://tetralogical.com/about/team/lwatson/ — "Chair of the W3C Board of
     Directors" and "Co-Chair of the W3C Web Applications working group."
   - The profile therefore lists these as current roles, not past ones.

2. **"ARIA co-chair" — NOT SUPPORTED as a literal title.**
   No source describes her as chair/co-chair of the ARIA Working Group specifically. Her
   ARIA involvement is as a **specification editor/contributor** (HTML5, ARIA in HTML,
   HTML Accessibility API Mappings 1.0) and as a long-standing participant in W3C
   accessibility standards, not as ARIA WG chair. Logged and reflected accurately in the
   profile (editor/contributor framing, not chair).
   - Source: search synthesis citing her editorship of HTML5, ARIA in HTML, and HTML
     Accessibility API Mappings 1.0; https://tink.uk/quick-guide-to-the-aria-specifications/

3. **"accessibility has failed, AI will fix it" stance for the conflict pairing — this is
   JAKOB NIELSEN's stance, NOT Léonie Watson's.**
   The tasking correctly frames the *productive conflict* as Watson vs Jakob Nielsen.
   Nielsen published "Accessibility Has Failed: Try Generative UI = Individualized UX"
   (2024-02-29) calling the accessibility movement "a miserable failure." Watson is on the
   opposing side: she argues accessibility has not failed — organisations have failed to
   implement it — and that generative AI is "an illusion … patterns of probability
   masquerading as truth" that cannot be blindly trusted. This is the real, citable axis of
   disagreement. `productive_conflict_with: [jakob-nielsen]` is correct and well-grounded.
   - Nielsen source: https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/accessibility-generative-ui
     (and mirror https://www.uxtigers.com/post/accessibility-generative-ui)
   - Watson counter-position: https://beyondtellerrand.com/blog/btconf-dusseldorf-2025-video-leonie-watson
     and https://equalentry.com/qa-with-leonie-watson-accessibility-engineer-and-director-tetralogical/

## Biography (dated facts)

- **Pre-2000:** Performing arts degree, then worked on a tech-support helpdesk for one of
  the UK's first ISPs, where she first encountered HTML and CSS, and then worked as a web
  designer.
  - Source: https://vivaldi.com/blog/better-web/4-leonie-watson-for-a-better-web/ (2024-12-02)
- **Late 1999 – end of 2000:** Lost her sight over roughly 12 months. Began using a screen
  reader and joined online screen-reader forums for help.
  - Source: https://equalentry.com/qa-with-leonie-watson-accessibility-engineer-and-director-tetralogical/
- **2002–2003:** Contacted by Alastair Campbell at Nomensa (a few miles from her home), who
  was looking for screen-reader users who had built a WCAG 1.0-conformant site. Did a couple
  of projects with Nomensa in 2002, joined full-time in 2003 — the accidental start of her
  accessibility career.
  - Source: https://www.creativebloq.com/netmag/l-onie-watson-giving-damn-about-what-youre-building-8135487
    and Equal Entry Q&A.
- **~25–30 years experience** across UKOnline, Nomensa, TPGi (Vispero), and the UK
  Government Digital Service (GDS).
  - Source: https://tetralogical.com/about/team/lwatson/ ; https://www.tpgi.com/author/leoniewatson/
- **2018:** Named one of the first Microsoft Most Valued Professionals (MVP) from outside
  the Microsoft community, recognising her accessibility contribution.
  - Source: https://mvp.microsoft.com/fr-fr/mvp/L%C3%A9onie%20Watson-5002988
- **Current (2026):** Co-Founder & Director, TetraLogical; Chair, W3C Board of Directors;
  co-Chair, W3C Web Applications WG; co-organiser of Inclusive Design 24 (#id24); Institute
  of Directors Certified Company Director.
  - Source: https://tetralogical.com/about/team/lwatson/

### W3C standards work
- Editor/contributor on HTML5, ARIA in HTML, and HTML Accessibility API Mappings 1.0.
- W3C horizontal-review advocate: ensures accessibility is mandatory in spec review, not optional.
  - Quote: "From the get-go, they've acknowledged that these things are actually bloody
    important. And we need to bake them in." — Vivaldi interview, 2024-12-02.
  - Quote: "It's really important for accessibility, amongst many other things, to be
    throughout that whole conversation." — same.

### Screen reader use
- Full-time blind screen-reader user since 2000. Notes the historical shift from paid,
  Windows-only screen readers to free/cross-platform choice: "You had to use windows and
  you had to pay for your screen reader. Now you can choose."
  - Source: https://vivaldi.com/blog/better-web/4-leonie-watson-for-a-better-web/ (2024-12-02)
- (No single source pins her to one named screen reader exclusively; she is a long-time
  screen-reader user and tests across assistive tech. Profile avoids over-claiming a
  specific product.)

## Signature stances and verbatim quotes

### Accessibility is a design constraint and a creative challenge, not an afterthought
- "Someone once said to me that they always thought accessibility was a challenge to
  creativity, but then they realized it was actually a creative challenge."
- "Much of the accessibility industry is fixated on audits as the solution to
  everything … by the time someone needs an audit it's already too late."
- "[An audit] usually means accessibility was an after-thought, or that accessibility is
  the subject of a legal dispute."
  - Source: https://equalentry.com/qa-with-leonie-watson-accessibility-engineer-and-director-tetralogical/

### Keyboard accessibility is still the basic, unsolved problem
- "Use your tab key to test whatever it is you're about to release … Keyboard accessibility
  is still one of the biggest problems."
- "Meta needs to pull its bloody finger out and sort out React, quite frankly."
  - Source: https://vivaldi.com/blog/better-web/4-leonie-watson-for-a-better-web/ (2024-12-02)

### Generative AI is an illusion — useful but not to be trusted blindly
- "Generative AI can feel astonishingly real, but like the spoon in The Matrix, it is
  nothing more than an illusion — patterns of probability masquerading as truth."
- "AI does not understand, reason, or perceive reality; it just generates plausible
  facsimiles."
- "We can't blindly trust these tools. It's not enough to shrug and say 'well, that's just
  how it is.'"
  - Source: https://beyondtellerrand.com/blog/btconf-dusseldorf-2025-video-leonie-watson
    ("There Is No Spoon", btconf Düsseldorf, 2025-05-19)
- "we have to recognise that generative AI is essentially an illusion, but we can't look at
  it in isolation — we need to make people part of the equation"
  - Source: https://medium.com/pixel-pioneers/pixel-pioneers-bristol-2025-speaker-spotlight-l%C3%A9onie-watson-a491c1fd89f1
    (published 2025-06-09; originally pixelpioneers.co 2025-06-04)

### AI's accessibility benefits are real but narrow; overlays are a scam
- "It's really, really good at telling me what stuff is, which is quite frankly, pretty useful."
- "The level of detail that you can get at now independently on your own is really quite something."
- On overlay vendors (AccessiBe, UserWay): "These tools can't fix that, and they can't fix
  it simply because to fix it, what they have to do is work out which elements on a page
  have JavaScript event listeners, and the browser won't let you do that."
- "I am smart enough to know that I should not be making any kind of big decisions on what
  I'm being told." (on AI hallucination)
  - Source: https://ppc.land/accessibility-expert-warns-of-ai-bollocks-while-highlighting-benefits/
    (FFConf 2024 talk, "AI and Accessibility: the Good, the Bad, and the Bollocks",
    2024-11-08; video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X8wcbJR9IaU)

### The agentic web could help or harm disabled users
- "Why build a website designed to be clicked, tapped, or key-pressed, if someone can ask
  an agentic AI to carry out an action on their behalf with no need for any of that messy
  physical interaction business."
  - Source: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/accessibility-agentic-web-l%C3%A9onie-watson-mxuke/
    ("Accessibility and the agentic web", LinkedIn article, 2025-08-08)

## Context: the Nielsen conflict axis (for productive_conflict_with)

- Jakob Nielsen, "Accessibility Has Failed: Try Generative UI = Individualized UX",
  2024-02-29. Calls the accessibility movement "a miserable failure" after ~30 years and
  proposes per-user AI-generated UIs as the replacement.
  - https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/accessibility-generative-ui
- The accessibility community pushback (Adrian Roselli "Jakob Has Jumped the Shark",
  Nicolas Steenhout, Per Axbom, Beth DeConinck) frames the counter-position Watson shares:
  accessibility as a discipline didn't fail; implementation and incentives did, and
  generative UI is not a trustworthy substitute.
  - https://adrianroselli.com/2024/03/jakob-has-jumped-the-shark.html
  - https://nicolas-steenhout.com/further-thoughts-on-nielsen-statement-about-accessibility/
  - https://axbom.com/nielsen-generative-ui-failure/
- Watson's own AI-is-an-illusion / can't-blindly-trust-it position is the direct rebuttal to
  Nielsen's "AI will fix it" optimism. Real, current, citable disagreement.
