## Signature frameworks (confirmed with quotes)

### The Sean Ellis Test / Product-Market Fit Survey
- Core question: **"How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?"** Options include "Very disappointed," "Somewhat disappointed," "Not disappointed."
- **40% threshold:** "If 40% or more respond with 'Very disappointed,' you have a strong indication of PMF." (Lenny's Newsletter, 2024-09-05.)
- Built into the free **pmfsurvey.com** tool with GoPractice.
  - https://pmfsurvey.com/about/
  - https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-original-growth-hacker-sean-ellis

### North Star Metric (NSM)
- From "Finding Your North Star Metric" (Medium / GrowthHackers):
  - "the North Star Metric is probably the most powerful concept for driving sustainable growth"
  - "Your North Star Metric is how you quantify expansion of this value"
  - NSM "should be actionable, not a ratio, and capable of scaling up over time. It should correlate with revenue growth but should not be revenue itself." (Lenny's recap.)
  - Four requirements: (1) rooted in genuine customer value; (2) capable of sustainable "up and to the right" growth; (3) resistant to perverse incentives; (4) simple enough for all team members to remember.
  - Examples: Facebook = DAUs; Uber = weekly trips.
  - https://medium.com/growthhackers/finding-your-north-star-metric-fc1c1f71cbcb

### ICE prioritization framework
- "Impact, Confidence, Ease" — each idea rated 1–10 across the three categories for systematic comparison of growth experiments. Used to run high-tempo experiment backlogs in *Hacking Growth*.
  - https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-original-growth-hacker-sean-ellis

### High-Expectation Customer (HXC) / must-have value
- The PMF survey isolates the segment that would be "very disappointed"; growth is built by understanding what that must-have segment values, then expanding it. NSM = quantification of that must-have value.
