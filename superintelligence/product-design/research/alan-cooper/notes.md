## Biography (verified)

Sources: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cooper_(software_designer)> (fetched 2026-06-01); Dubberly article; Wipro/Designit profile.

- **Born:** June 3, 1952. Grew up in Marin County, CA. Studied **architecture** at College of Marin; self-taught programmer.
- **1975:** Founded **Structured Systems Group (SSG)**, Oakland CA — among the first serious business software for microcomputers (General Ledger accounting). Sold his interest in 1980.
- **Early-mid 1980s:** Worked with Gordon Eubanks on CBASIC; founded the R&D department at **Digital Research, Inc.**; authored **SuperProject**, sold to Computer Associates (1984).
- **1988:** Created **"Ruby,"** a visual programming language / shell-construction tool for Windows. **Microsoft purchased it and turned it into Visual Basic.** Also invented the VBX dynamically-installable-control interface. Hence **"father of Visual Basic."**
- **1992:** Co-founded **Cooper** (with wife Sue Cooper) in Menlo Park CA, as Cooper Software; renamed **Cooper Interaction Design (1997)** — the first consultancy dedicated solely to interaction design. Pioneered goal-directed design and personas there.
- **~2002:** Began public training (interaction design, service design, design leadership) → later **Cooper Professional Education**.
- **2017:** Cooper became part of **Designit** (Wipro Digital's strategic design arm). Cooper says he **retired** this year.
- **2018:** Began **Ancestry Thinking** writing/speaking; co-created the **Ancestry Thinking Lab** with **Renato Verdugo** (YouTube UX researcher); taught "Thinking Like a Good Ancestor" at UC Berkeley's Jacobs Institute for Design Innovation.
- **May 29, 2020:** Cooper Professional Education **closed**.
- **2025-2026:** Semi-retired; regenerative rancher (Monkeyranch, Petaluma); occasional writer/speaker (IAC26).

### Awards / honors
- **1994:** First **Windows Pioneer Award**, presented by Bill Gates.
- **1998:** **SVForum Visionary Award.**
- **2004:** **ACM SIGDOC Rigo Award.**
- **April 28, 2017:** **Computer History Museum Hall of Fellows** — "for his invention of the visual development environment in Visual BASIC, and for his pioneering work in establishing the field of interaction design and its fundamental tools." Source: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cooper_(software_designer)>

## Goal-Directed Design — the method (Dubberly)

Source: <https://www.dubberly.com/articles/alan-cooper-and-the-goal-directed-design-process.html> (fetched 2026-06-01)

Five methodological changes Cooper advocates:
1. **Design first; program second** — finish interaction design before programming begins.
2. **Separate design from programming** — different roles; programmers optimize for code simplicity, designers for human goals; the conflict of interest is structural.
3. **Designer accountability** — designers responsible for product quality + user satisfaction, with authority to enforce the design.
4. **Persona-driven development** — design for specific researched personas, not abstract "end users."
5. **Two-person teams** — a designer paired with a "design communicator" (art-director / copywriter model).

Key verbatim framing: **"Personas are the single most powerful design tool"** and **"the bright light under which we do surgery."** Critical distinction: **"goals are not the same thing as tasks. A goal is an end condition, whereas a task is an intermediate process."** Goals are stable; tasks change. Task-based design asks "what tasks should users perform?"; goal-directed design asks "what do users actually want to achieve?"

Primary-persona principle (from the CMU-hosted Cooper reading <https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~jhm/Readings/cooper_personas.pdf>): design to satisfy a single **primary persona** without alienating secondaries; "when you design for everyone you design for no one"; beware the **elastic user**.

## Personas — invention + the misuse debate

Sources: <https://mralancooper.medium.com/defending-personas-2657fe26dd0f> (Mar 9, 2021); <https://mralancooper.medium.com/in-1983-i-created-secret-weapons-for-interactive-design-d154eb8cfd58> (Feb 2020).

- Personas were introduced to the public in **The Inmates Are Running the Asylum (1998)**. Origin traces to a **1983** project-management product where Cooper role-played a user named "Kathy."
- **"Defending Personas" (2021)** — key points and verbatim quotes:
  - "At Cooper, we did our field research and then synthesized personas as a tool for understanding and communicating the goals, motivations, and desired end-states of our real-world users."
  - "If practitioners were intrigued by the idea, well then, I thought, 'Here, world, take personas and use them.'"
  - "My defense of them is seen simply as one man's opinion. Defending their 'proper' usage is a quixotic battle."
  - Names Microsoft's practice of inventing hundreds of personas to **justify pre-built features** as a **"180-degree inversion"** of the method's purpose.
- **2025-2026 relevance:** active debate over **LLM-generated synthetic personas** (arXiv audits, CHI papers, IxDF articles). The debate is effectively a referendum on Cooper's insistence that personas are DISCOVERED through research, not invented. Cooper's historical stance ("personas are something we discover, not make") implies he would be skeptical of purely AI-generated personas lacking empirical grounding. (No direct 2025-2026 Cooper quote on LLM personas was found; framed as inference in the persona, not as a cited stance.)

## "The Inmates Are Running the Asylum" — core claims

Source: <https://www.google.com/books/edition/The_Inmates_Are_Running_the_Asylum/9Y5_QgAACAAJ>; InfoQ About Face 4 interview <https://www.infoq.com/news/2014/10/cooper-about-face-4/>

- Programmers, left to design the interaction, design for themselves → hostile software.
- **"Dancing bearware"** — software we tolerate/forgive because it is amazing it works at all, not because it is good. ("It works" ≠ "it is good.")
- Interaction design is a **distinct discipline** that must precede and be separate from programming.
- The cost of complexity is paid by every user forever (cognitive rent), while the vendor banks the build cost.

## Ancestry Thinking — the design-ethics second act

Sources: <https://mralancooper.medium.com/ancestry-thinking-52fd3ff8da17> (Aug 17, 2018); <https://www.wipro.com/digital/alan-cooper-wants-to-create-a-taxonomy-for-bad-technological-and-design-behavior/>; <https://jacobsinstitute.berkeley.edu/thinking-like-good-ancestor-finding-meaning-technology-build/>; User Defenders podcast 053 (2019).

- **Definition (verbatim):** "Ancestry thinking is the study and practice of being a good citizen for the long term."
- **Verbatim:** "The individuals who create some of the most oppressive digital systems are mostly good people."
- **Verbatim:** "We need a methodology that works. We need a set of tools."
- Core argument: technology is **neither morally neutral nor self-regulating**; market forces and laws fail to prevent harm; a "good ancestor" doesn't borrow from the future ("How can I maximize benefit to everyone, in perpetuity?" vs. "How can I maximize my benefit now?").
- Wants to create a **taxonomy of bad technological/design behavior**, mirroring how he created tools for good interaction design. Detect harmful behavior "when products are tiny little babies."
- **Ancestry Thinking Lab** — co-created with Renato Verdugo; in a 2018 interview Cooper called it "kind of an aspirational creation at this point," hoping a large org would fund it. (ancestrythinking.com was unreachable — ECONNREFUSED — on 2026-06-01; treated as dormant/aspirational.)
