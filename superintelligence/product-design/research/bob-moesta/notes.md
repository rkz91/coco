## 2. Biography (sourced)

- **Quality-engineering roots.** Moesta began his career as an intern under **Dr. W. Edwards Deming** (the quality-revolution pioneer) and studied under **Dr. Genichi Taguchi** in Japan, learning lean product development and Toyota's methods. He also cites **Dr. Willie Hobbs Moore** as a teacher. This Deming/Taguchi quality lineage is the distinctive thing that separates his JTBD practice from the others — he frames demand as something to be reduced to causal structure, not surveyed.
  - Source: https://therewiredgroup.com/about/bob-moesta/ · https://www.christenseninstitute.org/people/bob-moesta/
- **Education:** degrees from Michigan State University, Harvard Business School, and Stanford University; additional study at Boston University's School of Management and MIT School of Engineering.
  - Source: https://therewiredgroup.com/about/bob-moesta/
- **JTBD co-creation.** In the mid-1990s Moesta (with Rick Pedi) brought a product-development problem to Clayton Christensen, who suggested the now-famous "go observe how people actually buy" approach. This led to the **McDonald's milkshake study** — a researcher logged 18 hours of milkshake purchases and found ~40% sold 6:30–8:30 a.m. to solo commuters "hiring" the shake to make a boring drive more interesting. The milkshake story became the canonical JTBD illustration and was later immortalized in Christensen's 2016 book *Competing Against Luck*.
  - Source: https://therewiredgroup.com/case-studies/milkshakes/ · https://jobstobedone.org/news/brand-labs-bob-moesta/
- **Scale of practice:** has personally worked on / helped launch **more than 3,500 new products, services, and businesses** across defense, automotive, software, financial services, and education over ~30 years.
  - Source: https://www.christenseninstitute.org/people/bob-moesta/

## 4. The frameworks (the load-bearing IP)

### Four Forces of Progress
Two forces **push toward** a switch, two **hold back**:
- **Push (F1):** the struggling moment — dissatisfaction with the status quo.
- **Pull (F2):** the attraction of the new solution / desired outcome.
- **Habit (F3):** the comfort/inertia of the present.
- **Anxiety (F4):** fear/uncertainty about the new.
A switch happens when **Push + Pull > Habit + Anxiety**.
- Source: https://humanising.co/archive/bob-moesta-demand-side-sales-101-book-review/ · https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/demand-side-sales-the-forces-behind-every-yes/id1206998957?i=1000720181107

### The "Switch" interview / timeline
Moesta reconstructs the **timeline** of a real purchase — from "first thought" through passive looking, active looking, deciding, and consuming — and uses **forensic / criminal-interrogation-style questioning** to recover the actual causal sequence ("the causal layer," real events, not opinions). Distinctive claims:
- "**10 interviews helps me understand 80% of the 90% of the market**" — patterns saturate around interview 6–7; broad surveys miss them.
- 10 strategic interviews reveal **"three to five distinct buying patterns that cover 90% of any market."**
- Interview is 30–90 minutes; he insists on equal **debrief** time with a cross-functional team.
- Source: https://saasclub.io/podcast/jobs-to-be-done-bob-moesta-423/ · https://therewiredgroup.com/learn/complete-guide-jobs-to-be-done/

### Demand-Side Sales
Reframes selling as **helping people buy / make progress** rather than pushing supply. "Nobody buys any product randomly, ever." Buying starts with a **struggling moment**, not a need or a persona.
- Source: https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/demand-side-sales-the-forces-behind-every-yes/id1206998957?i=1000720181107 · https://therewiredgroup.com/learn/demand-side-sales-101/

## 5. The JTBD "three-camp" split (for productive_conflict_with)

JTBD is famously fractured into camps. The roster (`/SI-PD`) explicitly wires the **Torres ↔ Ulwick ↔ Moesta three-camp JTBD tension** (ROSTER.md, `/ultra-think` patch note, 2026-06-01):

- **Tony Ulwick (`tony-ulwick`) — Outcome-Driven Innovation (ODI), "Jobs-as-Activities."** Treats a job as a structured process (define, locate, prepare, confirm, execute, monitor, modify, conclude) with ~50–150 measurable desired-outcome statements you **quantify via large-N survey** to find under/over-served outcomes. Rigorous, statistical, supply-shaping. **This is the cleanest, most-documented productive conflict with Moesta** — Ulwick has publicly drawn lines between his "real JTBD" and the Klement/Moesta "demand-side / Jobs-as-Progress / switch" school.
  - Source: https://gopractice.io/product/jobs-to-be-done-the-theory-and-the-frameworks/ · https://jobs-to-be-done.com/alan-klements-war-on-jobs-to-be-done-dad8eaed567c · https://www.productfocus.com/the-jtbd-challenge/
- **Bob Moesta — "Jobs-as-Progress" / Switch / demand-side.** Qualitative, small-N (10 interviews), timeline + forces, emotional & social jobs weighted equally with functional. Demand-generation lens (help people *want* / *buy*), not product-spec lens.
- **Teresa Torres (`teresa-torres`) — Continuous Discovery / Opportunity Solution Trees.** Not strictly a JTBD camp, but adjacent and in tension: Torres uses **weekly product-trio interviews** + opportunity-solution trees as a *continuous habit* embedded in the delivery team. Moesta runs **deep, episodic, forensic switch interviews by a trained interviewer** and is skeptical of asking customers what they want. The tension is methodological: continuous-trio-discovery vs. forensic-switch-interview; "interview everyone weekly" vs. "10 good interviews beat 100 surveys." Productive, not hostile.
  - Source: https://www.producttalk.org/opportunity-solution-trees/ · https://www.mindtheproduct.com/reversing-teresa-torres-opportunity-solution-tree-to-find-the-why-behind-solutions/

**Decision:** `productive_conflict_with: [tony-ulwick, teresa-torres]`. Both are real ROSTER.md slugs in the same cell (product-discovery-research). `pairs_well_with: [clayton-christensen]` (per brief) plus `eric-ries` and `des-traynor` (both roster slugs; Ries shares the build-measure-learn/struggle lens, Traynor at Intercom is a heavy public JTBD adopter who has interviewed Moesta repeatedly).

## 7. Corrections / assumptions logged

- **Brief said "Demand-Side Sales" as the headline work.** Correct, but Moesta's **most recent book is *Job Moves* (Nov 2024)**, and *Learning to Build* (2022) is also load-bearing. The profile lists all three so the persona isn't frozen at the 2020 *Demand-Side Sales* era.
- **The Circuit Breaker podcast (his own show with Greg Engle) appears dormant** — listing/aggregator data shows the last published episode around **April 2023**. I therefore did **not** use it as a recent signal and instead used his **guest** appearances (Brand Lab, Renegade Marketers Unite, SaaS Podcast). Logged so a future re-sync doesn't waste time hunting for new Circuit Breaker episodes.
- **"Co-architect with Christensen" framing is accurate** but should be stated carefully: Christensen is the academic origin/disruption-theory anchor (the JTBD *theory* + *Competing Against Luck* authorship); Moesta is the **practitioner-architect of the interview method** (switch interview, timeline, four forces, demand-side framing). Both true; the persona keeps the distinction.
- **Affiliation title nuance:** Stern lists him as "President and Co-Founder, The ReWired Group"; The Re-Wired Group's own About page says "CEO and Founder." Both are current and self-described. Profile uses "Founder & CEO/President, The Re-Wired Group" to cover both. The Christensen Institute title is "Research Fellow for Jobs to Be Done Theory" (sometimes rendered "Fellow" / "Adjunct Fellow").
