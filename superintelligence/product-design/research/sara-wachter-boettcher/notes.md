   URL: https://newsletter.activevoicehq.com/p/it-s-not-just-burnout
   Argues tech exhaustion is not only burnout but *moral injury* — "the pain borne of having a conscience, but not acting on it." Direct quotes:
   - "Designers are expected to build features that manipulate users, that steal and sell their data, that get them hooked on quick dopamine hits."
   - "They're asked to shove AI into literally everything—even if it results in confusion or misinformation."

## Key ideas / canon quotes (for narrative + public_stances)

From *Technically Wrong* (W.W. Norton, 2017):
- "There's nothing wrong with you. There's something wrong with tech." (the book's thesis, inverting self-blame)
- "There's no reason to allow tech companies to obfuscate their work, to call it special and exempt it from our pesky ethics."
- "Every digital product bears the fingerprints of its creators." (bias is authored, not neutral)
- Homogeneous teams build personas/products that "alienate audiences."
Source: https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/7051757.Sara_Wachter_Boettcher

From *Design for Real Life* (with Eric Meyer, A Book Apart, 2016):
- Reframe "edge cases" as **"stress cases"**: "Instead of thinking of changes for a minority of people as being 'edge cases', referring to them as 'stress cases' really makes the point."
- "Learn to identify stress cases and design with compassion, so you can create interfaces that support more of your users, more of the time."
- Critiques the "we design for the 90%, not the 10%" mentality as "a shorter way of saying, *That's a difficult use case that I don't want to think about.*"
- The NPR News app stress-case workshop (Libby Bawcombe) is the worked example.
Source: https://exclusive-design.vasilis.nl/stress-cases/ , https://www.amazon.com/Design-Real-Life-Sara-Wachter-Boettcher/dp/1937557405

From *Content Everywhere* (Rosenfeld Media, 2012):
- Structured, flexible, reusable content; systems thinking applied to content (the COPE / content-as-data lineage that connects her to Karen McGrane's adaptive-content work).
Source: https://www.sarawb.com/books

Current leadership/coaching framing:
- "Hey designers, they're gaslighting you" — Nice Work / Medium. Argues orgs gaslight designers into doubting their own read of dysfunction.
  URL: https://medium.com/nice-work-from-active-voice/hey-designers-theyre-gaslighting-you-e02e5a4d9cff
- On Andy Polaine's *Power of Ten*: "breaking the gaslighting cycle of people-pleasing & overwork."
  URL: https://www.polaine.com/power-of-ten/sara-wachter-boettcher-breaking-the-gaslighting-cycle-of-people-pleasing-overwork/
