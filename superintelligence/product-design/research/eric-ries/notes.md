## 3. Core methodology (foundational, for canonical_works / public_stances)

- **Minimum Viable Product (MVP):** "the version of a new product which allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort." Ries stresses the MVP need not be a sellable product — the **Dropbox explainer video** is his canonical MVP example. Sources: The Lean Startup (2011); http://theleanstartup.com/principles
- **Build-Measure-Learn:** the core feedback loop. The MVP is whatever lets a team complete one full turn of the loop with minimum effort. Source: http://theleanstartup.com/principles
- **Validated learning:** progress is measured by learning validated through experiments with real customers, not by features shipped or vanity metrics. Source: http://theleanstartup.com/principles
- **Innovation accounting:** a rigorous way to measure progress, set milestones, and prioritize work under extreme uncertainty — establishing baseline metrics, tuning the engine, and deciding to pivot or persevere. Source: http://theleanstartup.com/principles ; The Lean Startup.
- **Pivot:** "a structured course correction designed to test a new fundamental hypothesis about the product, strategy, and engine of growth." Source: http://theleanstartup.com/principles
- **Vanity metrics vs. actionable metrics; cohort analysis; the "engine of growth"** (sticky / viral / paid). Source: The Lean Startup.
