## Education and early career (verified)

- BA and MA in English, Brigham Young University (BYU). Co-founded *Student Review*, an independent BYU newspaper, in 1986. Taught English and design at BYU 1992–1993.
- Spent a summer in France after grad school studying French and Aristotle's *Rhetoric* — credits this with opening his eyes to persuasive communication. (Stanford Lifestyle Medicine profile)
- Came to Stanford in 1993 for a PhD in Communication. MA + PhD in Communication, Stanford, 1997. Doctoral thesis: "Charismatic Computers."
- **PhD advisers:** Clifford Nass, Byron Reeves, Terry Winograd (Computer Science), Phillip Zimbardo (Psychology). Was a teaching assistant to Zimbardo.

## Captology and Persuasive Technology (verified)

- Fogg was the first scientist to articulate **"captology"** (Computers As Persuasive Technologies), beginning the research in 1993.
- Published the first peer-reviewed paper on persuasive computers (1998); guest-edited an ACM persuasive-technology issue (1999).
- **Book:** *Persuasive Technology: Using Computers to Change What We Think and Do.* Morgan Kaufmann, 2003 (some catalogs list the trade run as 2002/2003). ISBN 1-55860-643-2. 283 pp. Foreword by Phillip Zimbardo. Based on ~9 years of captology research. Chapters include the Functional Triad (tool / media / social actor), credibility, mobility, and **the ethics of persuasive technology**.
- Founded the **Stanford Persuasive Technology Lab in 1998** (captology.stanford.edu).

Source: https://www.amazon.com/Persuasive-Technology-Computers-Interactive-Technologies/dp/1558606432 ; https://lib.ugent.be/en/catalog/rug01:001235489 ; https://medium.com/@bjfogg/the-facts-bj-fogg-persuasive-technology-37d00a738bd1

## Fogg Behavior Model — B=MAP (verified, canonical)

- Published the **Fogg Behavior Model (FBM)** in 2009. Canonical site: behaviormodel.org (footer © 2026 — actively maintained).
- **B = MAP:** A behavior happens only when **M**otivation, **A**bility, and a **P**rompt converge **at the same moment**. If any one is missing, the behavior does not occur.
- **Motivation × Ability trade-off:** the two sit on an "action line." High motivation can compensate for low ability and vice versa. Fogg's design bias: it is usually easier (and more durable) to **increase ability — i.e. make the behavior smaller/easier — than to pump motivation**, because motivation is unreliable and fluctuates.
- **Three types of prompts** (chosen by where the user sits on motivation/ability):
  - **Facilitator** — for high motivation, low ability (make it easier).
  - **Spark** — for high ability, low motivation (boost motivation).
  - **Signal** — for high motivation and high ability (just a reminder; no need to change motivation or ability).
- Over 1,900 academic publications reference the Fogg Behavior Model.

Source: https://www.behaviormodel.org/ ; https://www.behaviormodel.org/prompts ; https://thedecisionlab.com/reference-guide/psychology/fogg-behavior-model

## Tiny Habits — the method (verified, canonical)

- **Book:** *Tiny Habits: The Small Changes That Change Everything.* Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, December 31, 2019. ISBN 9780358003328. NYT bestseller; Amazon editors' #1 leadership/business book of 2020.
- Method developed circa 2011. He has personally coached tens of thousands of people through the free 5-day Tiny Habits program (figures cited range from 40,000 to "millions" across his properties).
- **ABC recipe:** *After I [ANCHOR], I will [BEHAVIOR], and [CELEBRATION].*
  - **Anchor** = an existing routine or reliable event that cues the new behavior.
  - **Behavior** = a deliberately tiny version of the target habit (floss one tooth; two push-ups; read one sentence).
  - **Celebration** = an immediate positive feeling ("Shine!", a fist-pump, "I did a good job") to encode the habit via the brain's reward circuitry.
- Signature claims: **"Emotions create habits"** (not repetition); **"You change best by feeling good"**; **"behavior is a design problem, not a character flaw."** Self-compassion over self-criticism.

Source: https://www.amazon.com/Tiny-Habits-Changes-Change-Everything/dp/0358003326 ; https://tinyhabits.com/book/ ; https://www.tosummarise.com/book-summary-tiny-habits-by-bj-fogg/ ; https://psychkit.org/insight-articles/tiny-habits-method-bj-fogg/

## The Stanford Behavior Design Lab — current status (verified)

- Founded 1998 as the **Persuasive Technology Lab**; **renamed Behavior Design Lab in 2018** when the focus shifted fully to behavior design.
- **2021:** relocated to Stanford's School of Medicine, under Vaden Health Services (Division of Health and Human Performance) and the **Stanford Flourishing Project**.
- **Current projects (as of the Wikipedia record):** Climate Action, Upregulating Positive Emotions, the Rosetta Project, and the P.E.P. (Positive Experience Project).
- Lab mission: "Teach good people how human behavior works so they can create solutions that effectively increase health, boost happiness, and promote human flourishing."
- Fogg + student William Shan launched free Behavior Design for Climate Action training (behaviordesign.info/climateaction; climatebehaviordesign.com).

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_Behavior_Design_Lab ; https://behaviordesign.stanford.edu/about-us (note: the /people/bj-fogg page returned HTTP 403 to the fetch tool)

## Lineage to Nir Eyal and Tristan Harris (verified — the engagement-vs-ethics axis)

- **Nir Eyal** was **Fogg's original Behavior Design student**. Eyal's "Hook Model" (trigger → action → variable reward → investment) is explicitly built on Fogg's trigger/motivation/ability foundation. This is the documented academic-root → applied-popularizer lineage the roster wires up via `pairs_well_with: nir-eyal`.
- **Tristan Harris** studied in Fogg's Stanford Persuasive Technology Lab (master's, HCI focus), then left Google to start "Time Well Spent" and co-found the Center for Humane Technology — the anti-engagement counter-pole. **Mike Krieger** (Instagram co-founder) also came through the lab.
- Fogg has publicly stated he has "long been a champion" of Harris and humane technology — but in his own Medium piece "The Facts: BJ Fogg & Persuasive Technology" he pushes back on the framing (notably in *The Social Dilemma* and a Harris TED talk) that his lab "taught engineers to hijack minds." He notes Harris later apologized for an inaccurate implication in a TED talk and that the TED transcript was corrected.
- Fogg's consistent position: **persuasion ≠ manipulation; he taught ethics of persuasive technology from 1997 onward** (first ethics panel 1999; first book chapter on the ethics of persuasive tech 2002; a 2006 video warning policymakers about "political use of persuasion profiles"). This is the citable basis for his `productive_conflict_with` entry against `tristan-harris` (the dispute is about *attribution and framing of behavior-design ethics*, not about whether ethics matter).

## CORRECTION — wrong assumption caught and logged
