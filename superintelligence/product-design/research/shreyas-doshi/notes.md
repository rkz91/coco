## 2. Signature frameworks (verified from primary + canonical sources)

### LNO Framework (Leverage / Neutral / Overhead)
A task-prioritization lens. **Leverage** tasks return 10x–100x the effort (do these excellently); **Neutral** tasks return roughly 1x (do them "well enough", do not over-invest); **Overhead** tasks return less than the effort (minimize/satisfice). The mistake most people make is treating all tasks as Leverage tasks and over-investing in Overhead.
- Source: Lenny's Podcast Ep. 3 (2022-06-07): https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/episode-3-shreyas-doshi

### Three Levels of Product Work — Impact / Execution / Optics
Product work happens at three levels: **Impact** (the long-term effect on customers/business), **Execution** (the day-to-day building and shipping), and **Optics** (how the work is perceived). Most team dysfunction is a *level mismatch*: CEOs talk at the Impact level, PMs operate at the Execution level, and they talk past each other. Optics is legitimate (it builds energy and support) but must not become the goal.
- Source: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/episode-3-shreyas-doshi
- Secondary: https://www.lennyspodcast.com/blog/shreyas-doshi-on-pre-mortems-lno-framework-3-levels-of-product-work-and-prioritization/

### Pre-mortems
Run a meeting *before* launch in which the team imagines the initiative has already failed, then works backward to surface and mitigate the risks. The proactive inverse of a post-mortem.
- Source: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/episode-3-shreyas-doshi

### "Most execution problems are actually strategy problems"
A recurring thesis: teams misdiagnose strategic ambiguity as an execution failure and try to fix it with more process/hustle. Quote captured: *"Most execution problems are not really execution problems."*
- Source: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/episode-3-shreyas-doshi

### ROI vs. Opportunity-cost thinking
Move from "Is this a good use of my time?" (ROI) to "Is this the *best* use of my time?" (opportunity cost). Subtle reframe, large consequences for prioritization.
- Source: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/episode-3-shreyas-doshi

### High Agency
*"High Agency is about finding a way to get what you want, without waiting for conditions to be perfect or otherwise blaming the circumstances."* He maps talent × agency: "Highly Talented, High Agency people are Game Changers"; "Highly Talented, Low Agency people … end up capitulating to 'the system'." Cultivated via ownership mindset, Circle of Influence vs. Circle of Concern.
- Source (LinkedIn long-form): https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/high-agency-its-importance-how-cultivate-shreyas-doshi
- Source (the 1-tweet PM summary listing High Agency as a core trait): https://x.com/shreyas/status/1254064006412656640

### Product Sense (his current flagship idea)
Product Sense decomposes into ~five integrated skills: deep/cognitive **empathy** (unstated needs), **simulation** (anticipating outcomes), **strategic thinking** (differentiation), **taste** (consistently making the right call), and **creative execution** (conceiving unique solutions). Now central to his "AI age" argument (see §3).
- Source (X): https://x.com/shreyas/status/1270799017417437184 ("Key elements of Product Sense")
- Source (Maven course): https://maven.com/shreyas-doshi/product-sense
- Source (essay): https://shreyasdoshi.substack.com/p/why-product-sense-is-the-only-product

### "Good Product Managers, Great Product Managers"
A Andreessen-style "Good X / Great X" contrast essay on his site. Example: good PMs write detailed PRDs their teams depend on; great PMs write PRDs *iteratively* so eng/design are rarely blocked on them.
- Source: https://shreyasdoshi.com/good-product-managers-great-product-managers/ (page rendered as encoded payload via WebFetch; contrast captured from search-result snippet — flagged as lower-fidelity)

## 3. AI-age stance (his dominant 2025–2026 theme)

Thesis: **as AI commoditizes execution, human judgment ("Product Sense") becomes the only durable moat.**

Key essay — "Why Product Sense is the only product skill that will matter in the AI age" (Substack, **2026-03-05**):
- *"Tools have never been a significant source of alpha in product success and that is not changing"* (with AI tools).
- *"The only real long-term career moat for product people is how you can improve on the already-brilliant"* outputs AI provides.
- *"A small number of human beings will still be accountable for making the right calls for high quality inputs and outputs"* created by AI.
- The strongest reframe: from "what AI tools should I use" → "what can you do that improves upon what AI gives you."
- URL: https://shreyasdoshi.substack.com/p/why-product-sense-is-the-only-product

Supporting X posts (dates decoded from snowflake IDs, see §5):
- **2025-07-11** — *"Long-term, AI will be all powerful. Over the medium-term, as AI takes over most of the seemingly complex but mundane work we do, product people will need better-than-AI insights to thrive."* — https://x.com/shreyas/status/1943689228313067545
- **2025-08-19** — On PMs and AI: some are *avoiding* AI, others *over-relying* on it to write proposals/strategies; "right now it isn't good enough for the depth & judgment I'd expect from senior PMs." — https://x.com/shreyas/status/1957841513142268286
- **2025-12-13** — Taste "in practice" (vs. theoretical) is one of the most actionable things covered in the Product Sense course. — https://x.com/shreyas/status/1999864240916877513
- **~2026-05** — Recommends giving AI deep, persistent product context (strategy docs, customer insights, decision logs) and using it live to flag inconsistencies and keep teams honest ("AI as team copilot"). — https://genaipm.com/wiki/people/shreyas-doshi
