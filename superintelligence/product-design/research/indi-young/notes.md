## Key concepts (verified, with quotes)

### Problem space vs. solution space
> "Re-centering on people's goals or intents is a way to actually serve business goals, increase adoption, reduce friction & churn, and innovate."
— https://indiyoung.com/method/

Problem-space research = study the person's purpose and inner cognition. Solution-space = your product. Research the problem space first.

### Listening sessions vs. interviews
A listening session gives the person space to narrate their own inner thinking; the researcher gets out of the way. "Assumptions aside." This is distinct from a typical interview, which imposes the researcher's frame.
— https://indiyoung.com/method/ ; https://indiyoung.com/listening-deeply/

### Mental model skylines / opportunity maps
> Mental Model Skylines function as "opportunity maps, laid out a bit like a city skyline." Windows in towers represent summaries of inner thinking, emotional reactions, and personal rules people developed while addressing their purpose.
— https://indiyoung.com/method/

### Thinking styles (not demographics)
> "There is no such thing as a human who is an edge case. I don't care if you are in a wheelchair, you are not an edge case."
> "[With market/demographic data] you are really only looking at probably... less than a 10th of the people that you think are in your market actually fit exactly that average."
— https://www.userinterviews.com/blog/thinking-styles-research-indi-young

She calls her alternative to personas "behavioral audience segments" or "thinking styles."

### Cognitive vs. emotional empathy
Cognitive empathy = purposeful intent to understand the other person. Emotional empathy = rapport/feeling-with, used to establish trust during a session. The two are "kind of similar but also kind of opposite." She develops cognitive empathy by going through data to find concepts and patterns.
— https://rosenfeldmedia.com/books/practical-empathy/ ; https://fluidhive.com/problem-spaces-understanding-how-people-think-and-practical-empathy/
