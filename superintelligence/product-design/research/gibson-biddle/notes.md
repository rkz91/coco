## 3. The six (sometimes called seven) frameworks

Source of the consolidated list: "Ask Gib": A Summary of my Product Strategy Frameworks, **2025-03-05** — https://askgib.substack.com/p/ask-gib-a-summary-of-my-product-strategy

1. **DHM Model** — "Delight customers in Hard-to-copy, Margin-enhancing ways." The signature framework.
2. **GLEe Model** — Get big, Lead, Expand (+ Expand). A long-term (10–20 year) phased product vision. Netflix example: Get big on DVD-by-mail → Lead streaming → Expand worldwide → Expand into originals (out-HBO HBO).
3. **GEM Model** — Growth, Engagement, Monetization. Force-rank the big three and assign a proxy metric to each. Netflix proxies: Growth = YoY member-growth rate; Engagement = monthly retention (proxy for product quality); Monetization = LTV + gross margin.
4. **SMT Lockup** — Strategy / Metric / Tactic. Each strategy gets a proxy metric and a set of tactics (projects). Netflix personalization-pod proxy metric: "% of new members who give at least 50 ratings during their first six weeks."
5. **Positioning & Branding Model** — brand promise = what the product is + customer benefit + personality. ("Branding for Builders" essay, 10K claps.)
6. **Product Roadmap** — projects mapped against strategies across quarters and swimlanes.
7. **Quarterly Product Strategy Meeting** — the operating ritual that ties it all together; he runs a live simulation of a "modern-day Netflix Quarterly Product Strategy Meeting" in his workshop.

(He sometimes counts "Proxy Metrics" and the "Quarterly Product Strategy Meeting" separately, hence "six to seven tools" in different sources.)

## 4. DHM model — direct material

Source: "How to Delight Customers in Hard-to-Copy, Margin-Enhancing Ways," Medium, **2020-12-07** — https://gibsonbiddle.medium.com/how-to-delight-customers-in-hard-to-copy-margin-enhancing-ways-ee53e77b214d

- Core line: "Delight customers in Hard-to-copy, Margin-enhancing ways."
- **Hard-to-copy** Netflix advantages: "unique technology (personalization, streaming encoding), network effects (a large device ecosystem), economies of scale (a $20B annual content spend), and a trusted brand."
- **Netflix cancel-rate claim:** monthly cancel rate improved "from 10% to 2%" over two decades by balancing delight against margin. (Used verbatim, attributed to Biddle.)
- **2005 "Perfect New Release" experiment:** 10,000-customer A/B test; retention improved only 0.05% despite a $5M investment — used to illustrate that intuition must be validated by experiment.
- **2016 Free Trial Reminder:** Netflix accepted a ~$50M revenue hit (free-trial conversion dropped 90% → 85%) to build brand trust / long-term delight — illustrating the margin-vs-delight trade.

## 5. Consumer science / customer obsession — direct quotes

Source: "How Netflix's Customer Obsession Created a Customer Obsession," Medium, **2018-04-17** — https://gibsonbiddle.medium.com/customer-obsession-8f1689df60ad

- **The Reed Hastings exchange (key quote):** "In 2005, as I joined Netflix as VP of Product, I asked Reed Hastings, the CEO, what he hoped his legacy would be. His answer: 'Consumer science.' He explained, 'Leaders like Steve Jobs have a sense of style and what customers seek, but I don't. We need consumer science to get there.'"
- **Customer focus vs obsession:** "Customer focus is great, obsession is even better." And: "customer obsession means a healthy preoccupation with customers' unanticipated, future needs through a mix of research techniques that put the customer in the center of everything you do."
- **Four sources of data + experiment:** existing quantitative data, qualitative research (focus groups / usability), surveys — then "AB tested the hypotheses formed via the above inputs to see what worked."
- Talk title he uses: "Customer Obsession: Satisfying the Unsatisfiable via Consumer Science." (The "inherently unsatisfiable customers" framing appears in his SlideShare talk title; could not pull an exact prose quote, so I phrased the stance around the talk title rather than a fabricated sentence.)

## 8. Blind spots (reasoned, inverse of stances)

- Consumer-internet / subscription bias: DHM, GEM proxies, and the cancel-rate lens are tuned for B2C subscription businesses (Netflix, Chegg). Weaker fit for deep-enterprise / B2B platform, dev-tools, or regulated products where "delight" and monthly-retention proxies don't map cleanly.
- A/B-testing presumes scale: the consumer-science loop needs large user volume for statistical power; early-stage / low-traffic products can't run his 10,000-user experiments.
- Top-down strategy cascade can crowd out bottom-up discovery (the Cagan/Torres critique).
- Frameworks are vision/strategy heavy and relatively light on engineering, delivery, design-craft, and org/people execution detail.
