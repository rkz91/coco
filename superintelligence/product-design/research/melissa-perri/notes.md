## Books (canonical works)

1. **Escaping the Build Trap: How Effective Product Management Creates Real Value** — O'Reilly Media,
   2018. ISBN 9781491973790. The "build trap" = organizations stuck measuring success by output (features
   shipped) rather than outcomes (value created). Four pillars of a product-led org: (1) the PM role
   structured correctly, (2) strategy that enables good decisions, (3) a discovery/experimentation
   process to decide what to build, (4) supporting org policies, culture, and rewards.
   https://www.amazon.com/Escaping-Build-Trap-Effective-Management/dp/149197379X
   https://melissaperri.com/book

2. **Product Operations: How Successful Companies Build Better Products at Scale** — co-author Denise
   Tilles, 2023. ISBN 9798988338017. Product ops as an enablement function delivering three pillars to
   teams: (1) data & insights, (2) customer & market insights, (3) processes & practices/governance.
   Case studies: Stripe, Uber, athenahealth, Oscar Health, Fidelity.
   https://www.amazon.com/Product-Operations-successful-companies-products/dp/B0CK3HL4WF
   https://books.apple.com/us/book/product-operations/id6469439488

## Public stances (each cited)

1. **Outcomes over output / the build trap.** Companies "live and die by outputs," cranking out features
   on a schedule instead of solving customer problems; escaping requires measuring value created, not
   features shipped. https://melissaperri.com/book

2. **AI speeds up the build trap if discipline is absent.** April 2025 LinkedIn post:
   "What happens when you can 'vibe code' faster? Deploy an AI agent to interview customers with little
   oversight? You may be building products nobody wants, just faster and more often." Teams confuse
   speed with direction; product discipline matters MORE in the AI era, not less.
   https://www.linkedin.com/posts/melissajeanperri_ai-is-reshaping-product-management-its-activity-7317875597089624064-sDCu

3. **Product operations is an enablement function, not a PMO or process-police.** Without product-ops
   infrastructure, "work quickly falls into silos," efforts duplicate, and people waste time hunting for
   information. https://www.amazon.com/Product-Operations-successful-companies-products/dp/B0CK3HL4WF

4. **Strategy is a system of decisions, not a document/feature wishlist.** Product Mastery Conference
   2026 framing: separate good from world-class when "product strategy feels like a feature wishlist."
   https://productmasteryconference.com/

5. **Organize product teams around value streams, not around system architecture.** Product Thinking
   Ep. 260. https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/product-thinking/id1550800132

6. **Outcome orientation still needs outputs.** Common misconception that outcome-focused orgs ignore
   output — outputs are the means to outcomes, not the enemy.
   https://dragonboat.io/events/qa-with-melissa-perri-driving-an-outcome-oriented-product-strategy/

## Voice / style observations

- Practitioner-pragmatic, not academic. Speaks in organizational diagnostics: roles, incentives,
  reporting lines, decision rights. Frequently reframes a tooling/feature question as an
  org-design or strategy question.
- Signature rhetorical move: name the anti-pattern ("the build trap," "the feature factory," "the
  velocity trap," OKRs-as-renamed-roadmaps), then trace it to a structural cause (incentives, missing
  strategy, no enablement layer).
- Uses real company case studies (Stripe, Uber, athenahealth, Vanguard, Walmart) as proof rather than
  abstract theory.
- Calm, declarative, consultant register. Not a hot-take poster; she builds frameworks.
