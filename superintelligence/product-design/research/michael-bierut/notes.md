## Corrected assumptions from the brief

The original task brief listed several "canonical works" attributions that needed verification. Findings:

1. **"How to" / "Now You See It"** — CONFIRMED as two distinct books, not one.
   - *How to Use Graphic Design to Sell Things, Explain Things, Make Things Look Better, Make People Laugh, Make People Cry, and (Every Once in a While) Change the World* (Harper Design, 2015; revised & expanded 2021). Source: https://www.amazon.com/Graphic-Design-Things-Explain-Better/dp/0062413902
   - *Now You See It and Other Essays on Design* (Princeton Architectural Press, 2017). Source: https://www.amazon.com/Now-You-Other-Essays-Design/dp/1616896248
   - His first essay collection is *Seventy-Nine Short Essays on Design* (Princeton Architectural Press, 2007). The brief did not mention this; it is arguably his most-cited writing work and is included.

2. **Hillary 2016 logo** — CONFIRMED. He designed the "H" logo for Hillary Clinton's 2016 presidential campaign as a *volunteer*. Source: Pentagram about page; Wikipedia.

3. **MIT Media Lab** — CONFIRMED but with a nuance worth logging. The famous algorithmic 7×7-grid identity that generated tens of thousands of permutations was created for the Media Lab's **25th anniversary in 2011 by designer Richard The** (then a Media Lab student). Bierut (with Aron Fay at Pentagram) **extended and systematized** that grid logic into the **2014 permanent identity** covering the Lab plus its 23 research groups. So Bierut's authorship is of the 2014 system, building on Richard The's 2011 concept — not sole invention of the generative idea. Sources: https://www.dezeen.com/2014/10/29/pentagram-mit-media-lab-rebrand-visual-identity/ ; https://www.fastcompany.com/3037339/pentagrams-michael-bierut-rebrands-the-mit-media-lab

4. **Saks (Fifth Avenue)** — CONFIRMED. Brand strategy, the 1907 spiral / black-and-white packaging system. Listed among his signature corporate work. Source: Pentagram about page; Wikipedia.

5. **Design Observer co-founder** — CONFIRMED. Co-founded 2003 (some sources say 2002 for the precursor) with Jessica Helfand, William Drenttel, and Rick Poynor. Source: Wikipedia; AIGA Design Observer 10th-anniversary talk page.

6. **Pentagram role in 2025** — CORRECTED/CLARIFIED. The brief calls him "partner at Pentagram." As of **early 2025** (Pentagram's own bio says the advisory transition began **October 2024**), he stepped down from full partnership to a **part-time consulting partner / advisor** role after ~35 years (joined as partner 1990). He calls this "semi-retirement." So affiliation should read consulting/advisory partner, not active managing partner. Sources: https://www.pentagram.com/about/michael-bierut ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Bierut ; 99% Invisible ep. 655.

## Career timeline (verified)

- **1980:** B.S. graphic design, University of Cincinnati College of Design, Architecture, Art and Planning (DAAP), summa cum laude / highest honors.
- **1980–1990:** Vignelli Associates, New York (worked for Massimo & Lella Vignelli); rose to Vice President for Graphic Design. (~10 years.)
- **1990–2024/25:** Partner, Pentagram, New York office.
- **Oct 2024 → early 2025:** Transitioned to advisory / part-time consulting partner ("semi-retirement").
- **Teaching:** Senior critic in graphic design, Yale School of Art (from 1990s); lecturer, Yale School of Management (from 2016). Originator of the Yale "100 Days" project.

## Design philosophy / voice (for narrative + voice_style)

- Design is fundamentally **social and rhetorical**: the designer mediates between client, stakeholders, and audience; the work is a persuasion/communication act, not self-expression. (Theme across *How to* and *Now You See It*.)
- **"There are no boring projects, only boring approaches."** / curiosity as the core professional trait. (Wallpaper; recurring in his talks and the *How to* introduction.)
- He resists the cult of the signature style — proudly has **no single house style**; each solution starts from the client's content. (Contrast with Vignelli's strict modernist grammar, which he trained under and then deliberately diverged from.)
- Storytelling: design ideas should be **explainable as a story** — the rocket/coffee-table invitation, the H-as-arrow-toward-progress. He is a famously persuasive presenter; selling the idea is part of the craft.
- Warm, witty, anecdotal, self-deprecating writerly voice — he is as known as a *writer/critic* (Design Observer, three books) as for the logos.
- Analog-first: keeps numbered sketchbooks, designs with notebook and pencil before screen.
