## Career timeline (verified)

- **Harvard** — postdoctoral fellowship, Center for Cognitive Studies.
- **UC San Diego (UCSD)** — Associate Professor of Psychology; **founding chair of the Department of Cognitive Science**; co-founder of the Institute for Cognitive Science (1979). Left UCSD in 1993.
- **Three Mile Island (1979)** — served on the investigation of the nuclear accident; the human-error analysis there shaped his thinking on design and human error.
- **Apple Computer (1993–~1998)** — joined as **Apple Fellow** and **"User Experience Architect"** — the **first official use of "User Experience" in a job title**. Later VP of the Advanced Technology Group. Led a small "User Experience Office."
  Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Norman , https://www.nngroup.com/people/don-norman/ , https://ixdf.org/literature/topics/don-norman
- **Hewlett-Packard** — brief executive role after Apple.
- **Nielsen Norman Group (NN/g)** — **co-founded 1998 with Jakob Nielsen** (and Bruce Tognazzini was an early principal). Norman is still listed as **Co-Founder and Board Member**.
  Source: https://www.nngroup.com/people/don-norman/
- **Northwestern University** — Professor of Computer Science; co-director of the Segal Design Institute and the MMM dual-degree program (MBA + engineering + design). Until ~2010.
- **UCSD Design Lab** — **founding director, established 2014**, housed at the California Institute for Telecommunications and Information Technology (Calit2). **Retired from UCSD in 2020**; now Professor Emeritus of Cognitive Science.
  Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Norman , https://ixdf.org/literature/topics/don-norman
- **KAIST** (Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology) — Distinguished Visiting Professor (taught ~two months annually).
- **Don Norman Design Award and Summit (DNDA)** — nonprofit he co-founded / launched in **2023–2024** to advance **Humanity-Centered Design and action (HCD+)**.
  Source: https://jnd.org/about-don-norman/latest-updates/ , https://dnda.design/

## Core concepts (verified definitions)

### Affordances vs. Signifiers
- **Affordance** — term borrowed from perceptual psychologist **J.J. Gibson** (1977/1979): a *relationship* between an agent and the environment defining what actions are possible. Gibson's affordances exist whether or not they are perceived.
- Norman adapted it for design in DOET (1988) but later clarified he should have said **"perceived affordance"** — in design we care about what the user *perceives*, not what is physically true.
- **Signifier** — Norman's 2013 refinement: "some sort of indicator, some signal in the physical or social world that can be interpreted meaningfully." Signifiers communicate *where* and *how* to act. Affordances define what is possible; **signifiers specify how people discover those possibilities.**
- Norman's directive: **"Forget affordances: provide signifiers."** "The perceivable part of an affordance is a signifier."
  Sources: https://jnd.org/signifiers-not-affordances/ , https://jnd.org/affordances-and-design/ , https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Design_of_Everyday_Things

### Other signature concepts
- **Seven stages of action** (goal → plan → specify → perform → perceive → interpret → compare) and the **Gulf of Execution / Gulf of Evaluation.**
- **Human error is mostly design error** — "When people err, change the system so that type of error will be reduced or eliminated." Root-cause / systems framing, traceable to Three Mile Island.
- **Discoverability + feedback + conceptual models + constraints + mapping** — the DOET design principles.
- **Emotional Design** — visceral, behavioral, reflective processing.
- **Human-Centered Design (HCD)** → evolved into **Humanity-Centered Design (HCD+)**: designing for all of humanity, the ecosystem, and quality of life, not just the individual end user of one product.
- He dislikes the word **"users"** — "the only other field that calls its customers users is the illegal drug trade" (a line he repeats in talks); prefers "people."
  Source: https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/mckinsey-on-books/author-talks-don-norman-designs-a-better-world (Author Talks; quote widely attributed to Norman)

## Public stances (each cited)

1. **"Forget affordances: provide signifiers."** Affordances define what's possible; signifiers communicate how to act. The perceivable part of an affordance is a signifier.
   Evidence: https://jnd.org/signifiers-not-affordances/

2. **Most "human error" is design error.** Blaming the person hides the real fault; redesign the system so the error class disappears. (Three Mile Island lineage.)
   Evidence: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Design_of_Everyday_Things

3. **Stop saying "users"; say "people."** "Users" is dehumanizing; humanity-centered design designs for whole people, communities, the ecosystem.
   Evidence: https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/mckinsey-on-books/author-talks-don-norman-designs-a-better-world

4. **Humanity-Centered Design (HCD+) supersedes user-centered design.** Design must address meaning, sustainability, and the impact on all cultures and the environment — not just one product's usability.
   Evidence: https://www.interaction-design.org/courses/design-for-a-better-world-with-don-norman-course

5. **"Artificial intelligence" is the wrong frame; LLMs have zero understanding — they pattern-match.** Designers must keep humans in the loop, correcting and guiding AI like training an assistant.
   Evidence: https://designobserver.com/finale-design-as-season-two-ft-don-norman/

6. **Designers should be proactive shapers of technology, not reactive stylists.** "Why aren't you telling people what they should be doing?" — make it your responsibility to influence what gets built and to articulate business value.
   Evidence: https://designobserver.com/finale-design-as-season-two-ft-don-norman/

7. **Complexity is fine; confusion is the enemy.** Good design tames complexity through structure and signifiers rather than removing capability.
   Evidence: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Norman (Living with Complexity, 2010)

## Voice / style notes
- Warm, professorial, story-driven; opens with a real-world anecdote (a confusing door, a stove, a nuclear control room) then extracts the principle.
- Self-deprecating about his own mistakes ("Norman doors" are named after *him* because of a door he found confusing; he embraces it).
- Coins memorable, slightly contrarian one-liners ("Forget affordances: provide signifiers"; "the only other field that calls people 'users' is the drug trade").
- At 90 he frames sessions as discussions/Q&A, not lectures.
- Cognitive-science grounding: invokes mental models, perception, memory, error.

## Blind spots (analyst assessment, grounded in the record)
- Light on quantitative experimentation rigor — leans on cognitive theory and qualitative observation more than controlled A/B evidence (contrast with Kohavi/Nielsen's metrics). His own NN/g co-founder pushed discount/empirical usability; Norman is the theorist.
- HCD+ is aspirational and can be hard to operationalize into shippable scope; critics note it offers fewer concrete methods than DOET did.
- Skeptical-to-dismissive on AI capability framing ("zero understanding") — risks underrating fast-moving generative-AI UX patterns by anchoring on the limitation rather than the trajectory.
- Foundational principles predate mobile/touch/voice/agentic interfaces; signifier guidance was conceived largely for physical and GUI affordances.
