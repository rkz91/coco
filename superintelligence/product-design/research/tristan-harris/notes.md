## Biography (sourced)

- **Born** 1983/1984, San Francisco Bay Area. Childhood **magician** — he repeatedly frames persuasion/attention through the lens of stage magic (knowing how minds can be exploited). Source: Wikipedia; Medium "How Technology Hijacks People's Minds."
- **Stanford:** studied computer science; pursued a master's in human-computer interaction; took classes at **B.J. Fogg's Persuasive Technology Lab** before leaving. Classmate of Instagram co-founder Kevin Systrom; collaborated with Mike Krieger on a demo. Source: Wikipedia. NOTE: This makes **BJ Fogg his academic root** — Fogg is in the same `sprints-behavior-bridge` cell. The persuasion-design lineage (Fogg → both Eyal and Harris) is the intellectual spine of this cell's debate.
- **Apture (2007):** founded a startup, acquired by **Google in 2011**. Then worked on Google Inbox. Holds patents from Apple, Wikia, Apture, Google. Source: Wikipedia.
- **Google (2011–2015):** In **February 2013** authored a 141-slide internal deck, **"A Call to Minimize Distraction & Respect Users' Attention,"** which went viral internally (tens of thousands of employees). Became Google's self-styled "Design Ethicist." Source: Wikipedia; Wikibooks.
- **Left Google December 2015** to co-found **Time Well Spent** (501(c)(3)), later renamed the **Center for Humane Technology**. Co-founder: **Aza Raskin**. Source: Wikipedia; humanetech.com.
- **The Atlantic (Nov 2016):** called Harris "the closest thing Silicon Valley has to a conscience." Source: Wikipedia, tristanharris.com.

## Signature concepts & phrases (sourced)

- **"Race to the bottom of the brain stem"** — the Time Well Spent framing of the industry's competition to capture attention. Source: Wikipedia (Time Well Spent section). This is the canonical phrase the brief flagged; it migrates directly into his AI framing as "the race to roll out / race to deploy."
- **"Human downgrading" (2019):** "an interconnected system of mutually reinforcing harms — addiction, distraction, isolation, polarization, fake news — that weakens human capacity, in order to capture human attention." Source: Wikipedia.
- ***The Social Dilemma* (2020):** Netflix documentary, primary subject. ~100M viewers across 190 countries / 30 languages; 2× Emmy-winning. Quote in film: "Never before in history have fifty designers made decisions that would have an impact on two billion people." Source: Wikipedia; humanetech.com/team-board/tristan-harris; tristanharris.com.
- **"If you're not paying for the product, you are the product"** — popularized through *The Social Dilemma* (attributed within the film's ensemble; Harris is the lead voice). Used carefully in profile — NOT attributed as his original coinage.
- ***The AI Dilemma* (March 2023):** talk with Aza Raskin warning GPT-4-era capability step-change deployed faster than safety. "It's a race to roll out. A race to take shortcuts to get AI into the world as fast as possible." Source: humanetech.com/podcast/the-ai-dilemma; transcript PDF; YouTube cB0_-qKbal4. Date: ~March 24–31, 2023.
- **"This Moment in AI: How We Got Here" (Aug 13, 2024)** CHT Substack interview: "It's a race to roll out. - **"The Narrow Path"** — his 2025–2026 framing: a route between two failure modes, **decentralized chaos** (powerful tools amplify misinformation, empower bad actors) and **centralized control** (governments/corporations use AI for pervasive surveillance, eroding autonomy). ## The marquee debate: Eyal ↔ Harris (engagement vs ethics) — sourced

This is the cell's headline tension and the brief's MARQUEE conflict.

- **Nir Eyal** wrote *Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products* (2014) — the engagement/habit-design playbook. He has **pushed back on broad regulation of habit-forming tech, arguing individual users bear responsibility** for their own use ("indistractable" thesis); he allows that *when users are genuinely addicted* companies should intervene, and that reaping rewards while users abuse a product is "exploitation." Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nir_Eyal; nirandfar.com/tooaddictive/.
- **Harris** emphasizes **corporate/structural accountability**: platforms wield asymmetric power over human psychology, and the design choices — not user willpower — are the lever. The contrast is explicitly framed as "Eyal's focus on user responsibility vs. Harris's emphasis on corporate accountability for addictive design." Source: buzzworthy.com/smartphones-make-you-addicted/.
- Both descend from **BJ Fogg's Stanford Persuasive Technology Lab** — Fogg taught both. So the cell holds a clean three-way lineage: Fogg (academic root) → Eyal (engagement pole) ↔ Harris (ethics counter-pole). `productive_conflict_with` therefore: **nir-eyal** (marquee). Fogg is the shared root, not a conflict — list as a mental-model anchor in narrative, not in conflict array.
