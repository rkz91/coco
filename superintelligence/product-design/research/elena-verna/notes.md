## Signature frameworks (canonical)

- **Growth loops vs funnels.** A growth loop = "a closed system designed to ensure every input goes
  through a series of predefined steps to generate an output that can be reinvested into input" — a
  compounding flywheel. Funnels are linear and serve as *fuel* to ignite loops, not the primary
  growth method. (userpilot.com; elenaverna.com)
- **Racecar Growth Framework.** Separates: **engine** = growth loops · **lubricants** =
  optimizations · **turbo boosts** = one-off tactics · **fuel** = funnels. Used to keep balance and
  avoid over-indexing on any one. (elenaverna.com/p/my-9-favorite-growth-frameworks, 2024-10-25)
- **Motions x Levers growth model.** Three motions — product-led, marketing-led, sales-led — that
  should evolve over time; growth model should be revisited ~every 18 months with 20-25% of effort
  spent exploring new loops. (search synthesis; lennysnewsletter.com "10 growth tactics")
- **PLG vs sales-led / dual motion.** "Why you need to be both product-led and sales-led." Freemium
  over trial. PLG often gets crushed moving upmarket. (lennysnewsletter.com 2022-06-23)
- **When NOT to do PLG** (4 scenarios): unpredictable existing motions; no self-serve activation;
  value requires explanation (e.g. cybersecurity — "nothing happens" when it works); enterprise
  buyers control the decision. (amplitude.com/blog/not-all-companies-should-do-plg, 2023-01-23)
- **Team-level vs user-level optimization (B2B PLG).** In B2B you must acquire individual users AND
  activate/engage a *team* to unlock team network effects before selling the business.
  (amplitude.com/blog/team-vs-user-level-optimization)
- **"Growth is a product team that happens to obsess over loops instead of features."** Her one-line
  encapsulation of "growth is a system, not a team." (userpilot.com synthesis)
