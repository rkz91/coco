## Frameworks & signature methods (verified)

### Design Sprint (5 days, co-created with Knapp at GV)
The five-day process to "solve big problems and test new ideas." Documented in *Sprint* (2016).

### Foundation Sprint (2 days, the successor method)
- Compresses "three or four months of validation into two days." (Lenny's Newsletter, 2025-07-13)
- Premise: **"most startups fail not because they can't build, but because they build the wrong thing."**
- The very beginning of a startup is the **"highest-leverage moment."**
- Three phases: (1) **The Basics**, (2) **Differentiation**, (3) **Approach**.
- **"Differentiation is the #1 predictor of startup success"** — evaluated with a **2x2 framework**.
- Output is the **"founding hypothesis"** — a consolidated articulation of the team's unified vision/strategy.
- Techniques: **"Note and Vote"** (eliminates groupthink), **"Magic Lenses"** (seven frameworks for choosing between ideas).
- "The paradox of speed: why 'building nothing first' can get you to product-market fit faster."
- Source: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-foundation-sprint-jake-knapp-and-john-zeratsky (2025-07-13); https://thefoundationsprint.com/ ; https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Click/Jake-Knapp/9781668072110

### OATS Scorecard (Character's founder-evaluation rubric)
- **O**pportunity, **A**pproach, **T**eam, **S**uccess-so-far. 21 criteria across the 4 categories.
- Built on GV research studying characteristics of successful founding teams.
- Distinguishes "visionary" vs "hypothesis-driven" founders.
- Source: https://www.understandingvc.com/uvc-john-zeratsky-from-character-vc/ (note: the *interview* is dated 2023-07-18, so the OATS scorecard is **not** a recent signal; cite it as a framework/canonical reference, not as recent activity).

### Make Time (4-step daily system)
- **Highlight → Laser → Energize → Reflect**, repeated every day.
  - **Highlight:** pick the single most important thing for the day; block 60–90 min for it like a meeting.
  - **Laser:** block "Distraction Kryptonite"; quit one "Infinity Pool."
  - **Energize:** treat the body like an animal — walk, move, sleep, eat real food.
  - **Reflect:** adjust the system daily.
- Two named antagonists of attention:
  - **"Infinity Pools"** — apps/sites with endlessly replenishing, pull-to-refresh content, engineered to capture attention.
  - **"The Busy Bandwagon"** — the culture of constant busyness (overflowing inboxes, stuffed calendars, endless to-do lists).
- Core reframe: the problem is **not lack of time, it's hijacked attention**. Make Time is "not about getting more done" but about "making time for things that matter."
- Quote: *"When you schedule something, you're making a commitment to yourself, sending yourself a tiny message that says: 'I'm going to do this.'"*
- Source: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/564727/make-time-by-jake-knapp-and-john-zeratsky/ ; summaries at durmonski.com, readingraphics.com
