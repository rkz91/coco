## 2. Career history (HIGH confidence)

From Wikipedia and corroborating sources:

- **MIT Media Lab** (~12 years): research professor, computational design / "creative commerce"; ran the Aesthetics + Computation Group; mentored the lineage that produced **Processing** (Casey Reas + Ben Fry were his students). His own **Design By Numbers** (1999) was the precursor environment.
- **Rhode Island School of Design (RISD)** — 16th **president**, June 2008 – December 2013. Coined the STEM → **STEAM** (adding Art) advocacy push during this period.
- **Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers (KPCB)** — Design Partner; produced the original **Design in Tech Report** series (launched 2015) connecting venture investing to design.
- **Automattic** (WordPress.com) — Global Head of Computational Design and Inclusion.
- **Publicis Sapient** (2019–2020) — Executive Vice President, Chief Experience Officer.
- **Everbridge** — **Chief Technology Officer**, October 2020 – October 2022.
- **Microsoft** — current (VP/CVP Design & AI, CoreAI / GitHub).

## 3. Canonical books (HIGH confidence)

- **Design By Numbers** (MIT Press, 1999) — programming environment + book democratizing code for designers; precursor to Processing.
- **Creative Code: Aesthetics + Computation** (2004).
- **The Laws of Simplicity** (MIT Press, 2006) — his most cited work. Ten laws: **Reduce, Organize, Time, Learn, Differences, Context, Emotion, Trust, Failure, The One.** Law 1 (Reduce) uses the **SHE** method — **Shrink, Hide, Embody.** Law 10 (The One): "Simplicity is about subtracting the obvious, and adding the meaningful."
  - https://lawsofsimplicity.com/
  - Summary: https://readingraphics.com/book-summary-the-laws-of-simplicity/
- **How To Speak Machine: Computational Thinking For The Rest Of Us** (Portfolio/Penguin, 2019) — the laws of how computers "think": loops (a computer "can sit in a loop and run forever, never losing energy or enthusiasm"), recursion (Russian matryoshka doll), and **exponential / infinity thinking** native to computation. Argues non-technologists must learn to "speak machine."
  - https://howtospeakmachine.com/
  - https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/539046/how-to-speak-machine-by-john-maeda/

## 4. Design in Tech Report — history and current edition (HIGH confidence)

Annual report Maeda has produced since **2015** (started at KPCB), now in its **12th edition (2026)**. Presented annually at **SXSW** in Austin. Editions of note:

- **2025: "Autodesigners on Autopilot"** — presented SXSW March 2025 (11th edition). Theme: AI as a self-sustaining design loop; "AI is not replacing designers but transforming how design is done"; the "Agent Era" shifts AI from models to task-completing agents; UX evolving into **AX**. Built "with an assist from OpenAI's GPT-4."
  - Medium: https://johnmaeda.medium.com/autodesigners-on-autopilot-88c5b07609b9
  - X takeaways thread: https://x.com/johnmaeda/status/1901538212897796268
- **2026: "From UX to AX"** — presented **SXSW, March 18, 2026** (Austin Marriott Downtown, Waller Ballroom C). Core thesis: design moves from **U**ser **eX**perience to **A**gentic **eX**perience; the design problem shifts from the **gulf of execution** ("How do I help someone do this?") to the **gulf of evaluation** ("How do I help someone know whether it was done well?"). Introduces the **Tree-shaped Designer** (evolution of the T-shaped designer).
  - Medium: https://johnmaeda.medium.com/design-in-tech-report-2026-from-ux-to-ax-f9d83164f4d2
  - SXSW schedule: https://schedule.sxsw.com/2026/events/PP1148536
  - LinkedIn takeaways: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/johnmaeda_design-in-tech-report-sxsw-2026-ux-to-ax-activity-7440156725170835456-In5G
  - Report home: https://designintech.report/

The report site history page lists downloadable editions 2015–2018; the recent editions live on Medium/YouTube/LinkedIn.

### 2026 report — eight takeaways (verbatim-ish, from LinkedIn takeaways post)
1. **Taste remains human:** "Taste is yours. AI cannot fake its way into having it."
2. **Momentum matters:** "Start slow. Learn fast. Slope beats intercept every time."
3. Craft evolves — specialized skill migrates to emerging tech rather than disappearing.
4. Design emphasis shifts from execution to **evaluation and decision-making.**
5. **Runtime interfaces:** "Interfaces now render at runtime. Design the system. Not the screen."
6. Hands-on tool usage is genuine upskilling, not theoretical study.
7. Task-level thinking over static job descriptions.
8. **Human accountability:** "Execution moved to machines. Responsibility stayed with you. You are in the orchestration loop now."
