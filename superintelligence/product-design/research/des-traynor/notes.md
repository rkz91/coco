## Identity confirmation

Des Traynor is a confirmed, unambiguous public figure: co-founder of Intercom (now corporately renamed **Fin**), a customer-communications and AI-customer-agent company founded in 2011 by Eoghan McCabe, Des Traynor, Ciaran Lee, and David Barrett. ## CORRECTION — "The Dribbblisation of Design" authorship

A common misattribution: "The Dribbblisation of Design" (https://www.intercom.com/blog/the-dribbblisation-of-design/) is part of the Intercom design canon but was written by **Paul Adams** (then VP of Product), NOT Des Traynor. It is NOT listed as one of Traynor's canonical works in this profile. The Intercom JTBD/job-story practice it describes is, however, shared house philosophy that Traynor co-authored and evangelizes.

### Job story format (Intercom house framing, Traynor evangelizes)
"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]." Focusing on the job-to-be-done makes you aware of the *context* the solution exists in. Source: https://www.intercom.com/blog/jobs-to-be-done-book/ and JTBD Radio interview https://jobstobedone.org/radio/des-traynor-on-jtbd-radio/

## Key quotes (verbatim, with sources)

### Product strategy / saying no — https://www.intercom.com/blog/product-strategy-means-saying-no/ (2013-07-03)
- "If you're building a product, you have to be great at saying no."
- "Building a great product isn't about creating tons of tactically useful features."
- "Making features optional ... weakens your product definition."
- "There are no small changes."
- On scope creep: "you're not willing to admit your product stops somewhere. You're afraid to draw the line." (cinema-owner-who-also-runs-a-restaurant-and-cab-company analogy)

### Product scope — the most-cited Traynor idea
"The most important thing a product manager does is decide where their product stops and someone else's product takes over." (Paraphrase of his scope thesis; consistently advanced across his "saying no" essay and BoS talks. See https://www.mindtheproduct.com/des-traynor-product-strategy-growing-company/ and https://businessofsoftware.org/talk/product-strategy-is-about-saying-no/)

### Not all good products make good businesses — https://www.intercom.com/blog/good-products-bad-businesses/ (2016-08-15)
- "Small, rare problems might be desirable and feasible, but just not that viable."
- "Survival is the best product strategy."
- "Raw experience of the problem is an essential ingredient in the solution."

### AI / Fin era — Pigment "Perspectives" podcast, "Rebuilding Intercom for the AI Era" — https://www.pigment.com/perspectives-podcast/intercom-des-traynor-how-we-rebuilt-the-company-for-the-ai-era (2026-03-10)
- "Fin is a very cannibalistic, hostile product to our core business. That's just the reality."
- "If Fin succeeds, there will be fewer CS people in the future. Seats will go down."
- "Everyone can get to a 20% or 30% resolution rate. That's not hard. It's also not worth deploying."
- "AI massively decreases the cost of exploration."
- "We thought the magnetic pull of Fin would be big enough to pull everyone away from their entrenched help desk... Instead, we got flirty eyes and longing looks."
- "Either you correct your business with AI, or the market will correct you."
- "The majority of your talent should focus on advancing your strategy, not replacing internal tools to shave a few grand off."

### EU-Startups Podcast Ep.134 (themes, summarized not verbatim) — https://www.eu-startups.com/2025/09/eu-startups-podcast-episode-134-des-traynor-co-founder-of-intercom-and-creator-of-fin-ai/ (2025-09-04)
- "AI isn't a feature. It's a foundational shift."
- "Customers measure AI by trust, not accuracy."
