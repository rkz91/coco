## 3. Core thesis & methodology (verified)

- **Central claim:** "People buy products and services to get a job done." (strategyn.com/jobs-to-be-done; Wikipedia)
- **Customers want outcomes, not solutions/products:** ODI focuses on "what customers' ultimate output goal is: what they want the product or service to do for them, not how it should do it." (Wikipedia)
- **Needs are measurable:** "Customer needs are measurable. Needs are not vague preferences. They are the metrics customers use to measure success when getting a job done." (strategyn.com/jobs-to-be-done)
- **The Opportunity Algorithm:** `Importance + (Importance − Satisfaction) = Opportunity` — importance weighted twice as heavily as satisfaction. Used to find over-served and under-served outcomes. (Wikipedia)
- **Method shape:** map the functional job → capture up to ~150 *desired outcome statements* (each phrased as direction + metric + object of control + context) → quantitative survey to score importance and satisfaction → segment by unmet outcomes → target under-served outcomes for innovation. (Wikipedia; strategyn.com)
- **Job Map vs Process Map:** the job map describes what the customer is *trying to do* (problem space), independent of any current solution; a process map describes what they *currently do* (solution space). Redesign the job, then automate. (strategyn.com/stop-automating-bad-processes...)
- **Documented success rate:** ODI cited at an **86% success rate vs. ~17% industry average** — "five times the industry average." (strategyn.com/tony-ulwick; airfocus.com)

### Endorsement quotes (about Ulwick)
- **Philip Kotler:** "I call him the Deming of Innovation because, more than anyone else, Tony has turned innovation into a science." (anthonyulwick.com/about)
- **Clayton Christensen:** "Ulwick's outcome-driven programs bring discipline and predictability to the often random process of innovation." (anthonyulwick.com/about)

## 4. The JTBD camps (for productive_conflict_with)

JTBD splits into (at least) two well-documented camps; this is the central tension to wire:

1. **Ulwick — Outcome-Driven Innovation / "jobs-as-activities" / quantitative.** Map the job, survey to quantify over-/under-served outcomes, prioritize with data. Patented, systematic, favored by large product teams wanting rigor. (gopractice.io; jtbd.info; innodyn.net)
2. **Bob Moesta (with Chris Spiek) — "Switch" / demand-side / "jobs-as-progress."** Every purchase is a switch — the customer "fires" an old solution and "hires" a new one. Qualitative switch interviews; emphasis on the forces of progress, anxiety, and habit. Worked under Christensen at HBS; later "Demand-Side Sales." (gopractice.io; jtbd.info)
3. **Clayton Christensen — disruption + JTBD origin/popularizer (archetype, d. 2020).** The "milkshake" framing; "Competing Against Luck." Bridges to Moesta. Ulwick credits Christensen with popularizing, and Christensen credits Ulwick with the rigor. Genuine lineage AND genuine competition between them.
4. **Alan Klement** — wrote a widely-cited essay labeling ODI the "jobs-as-activities" version; Ulwick responded combatively ("Alan Klement's War On Jobs-To-Be-Done," "triple-strawman argument"). Klement is NOT on the ROSTER, so not used as a conflict slug — but documents Ulwick's combative streak (a blind spot).

**On the ROSTER (`product-discovery-research` cell):** `teresa-torres` (continuous discovery, opportunity-solution trees — qualitative, continuous, designer-friendly), `bob-moesta` (switch interviews), `clayton-christensen` (archetype, JTBD origin), `indi-young`, `steve-portigal`, `erika-hall`, `eric-ries`.

- **pairs_well_with → `clayton-christensen`** — direct JTBD lineage; Ulwick introduced ODI to Christensen, who popularized "jobs." Both believe the unit of analysis is the job, not the customer demographic.
- **productive_conflict_with → `teresa-torres` + `bob-moesta`** — the three-camp JTBD tension flagged in ROSTER. Torres favors continuous, lightweight, qualitative discovery and assumption-testing; Ulwick favors heavy upfront quantification of outcomes before building. Moesta favors qualitative switch/demand-side interviews; Ulwick considers the switch lens insufficiently rigorous for prioritization. ROSTER `/ultra-think` patch note explicitly says: "Wires the … Torres↔Ulwick↔Moesta three-camp JTBD tension."
