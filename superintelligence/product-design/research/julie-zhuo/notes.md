## 2. Recent signals (all dated AFTER 2025-06-01)

### 2.1 "When AI Has Better Taste Than You" — 2025-06-24
- URL: https://lg.substack.com/p/when-ai-has-better-taste-than-you
- Mirror: https://joulee.medium.com/when-ai-has-better-taste-than-you-0dc8e870db9a
- Built on a conversation with **Ivan Zhao** (CEO/co-founder of Notion). Introduces a
  three-part framework for durable human value:
  1. **Capabilities** — skills and knowledge we possess (AI is rapidly conquering these).
  2. **Taste** — our values and preferences (AI will likely encroach here too).
  3. **Agency** — our will and motivation to act (the remaining uniquely human reserve).
- Key quotes:
  - "If excellent taste operates through pattern recognition across vast cultural knowledge,
    then it isn't a stretch to imagine that AI systems can replicate this process."
  - "In a world where capabilities and even taste becomes commoditized, the will of the hand
    to act according to our values may well be our most rare and precious resource."
  - "We determine whether technology serves our flourishing or something else entirely."
- Notable: she explicitly refuses to treat taste as a "safe fortress" for designers — it is
  more objective and learnable than designers like to admit. This is a sharper, more honest
  position than the comfortable "AI can't have taste" line.

### 2.2 Sundial raises $23M — 2025-07-08
- Announcement (Business Wire): https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250708725493/en/Sundial-Raises-$23M-to-Redefine-Data-informed-Decisions-with-AI
- Coverage: https://venturebeat.com/business/sundial-raises-23m-to-redefine-data-informed-decisions-with-ai/
- Her launch thread: https://x.com/joulee/status/1942600311790789092 (X returned HTTP 402 on
  direct fetch; content captured via search snippet)
- $23M total; $16M Series A led by **GPV / DJ Patil** (first U.S. Chief Data Scientist).
- Her framing in the thread (paraphrased from the search snippet, not verbatim-verified):
  "Sundial has raised $23M to build the analytics platform for the AI era … many have asked:
  Why? Aren't you into intuition and taste and experience which is ultimately unmeasurable?"
  — i.e. she anticipates and answers the apparent contradiction between her design-intuition
  reputation and building a data company. Her answer: she has *deep respect for data as a
  tool to refine intuition*, not replace it.
- Product positioning: "diagnose with data, treat with design"; democratize insight beyond
  dashboards; make everyone in an org able to make smarter, faster, data-informed decisions.

### 2.3 "You're definitely going to be a manager now" + revised book — September 2025
- Essay: https://lg.substack.com/p/managing-ai-is-like-managing-humans
- Mirror: https://joulee.medium.com/managing-ai-is-like-managing-humans-with-one-big-exception-87f4b1ea8aa5
- Announcement thread: https://x.com/joulee/status/1965518210087973320 ("Lots of words
  dropping today: 1. Revised and updated version of The Making of a Manager … 2 new chapters
  on remote + recession management. 2. New Looking Glass essay about how you're DEFINITELY
  going to be a manager")
- Argument: AI makes *everyone* a manager — not of people but of AI agents. The same
  foundational management skills (clear instructions, context, feedback, judgment about
  quality) transfer to directing AI. Predictions:
  - Fewer "pure middle managers"; more people in the "messy middle" — sometimes executing,
    sometimes designing process, sometimes coordinating.
  - Role boundaries (engineer / PM / designer) dissolve; she favors calling everyone
    **"builders."**
  - Leaner teams; fewer roles whose primary function is coordinating information.
  - "Managing AI is like managing a very literal intern — give clear instructions and they'll
    execute earnestly; leave things vague and you might get something accurate but irrelevant."

### 2.4 Lenny's Newsletter / Podcast: "From Managing People to Managing AI" — 2025-09-21
- URL: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/from-managing-people-to-managing-ai-julie-zhuo
- Takeaways post: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lennyrachitsky_my-biggest-takeaways-from-my-chat-with-julie-activity-7376710825320960000-JvK-
- Key lines:
  - "AI will make everyone a manager — and the same skills you use to manage people today
    will be essential for managing AI agents tomorrow."
  - "the dissolution of traditional role boundaries — engineer, PM, designer — and just
    calling everyone builders in the age of AI."
  - Reiterates the **"diagnose with data, treat with design"** heuristic as her core method.
