## Career timeline

- 1990s: Producer of national PBS programs at Boston's WGBH. Head writer for a primetime game show; interviewed cabinet secretaries of nine US presidents.
- 1996: Created the **Couch-to-5K (C25K)** running program — a widely used beginner running plan.
- 2000s–2010s: Founded **Global Moxie** consultancy; mobile/touch UX focus.
- 2010: **Tapworthy: Designing Great iPhone Apps** (O'Reilly).
- 2015: **Designing for Touch** (A Book Apart).
- 2010s–present: Global Moxie → **Big Medium**; pivot to enterprise design systems at scale.
- 2024: Co-author (with Brad Frost) of the public push for a **Global Design System**; coins **"Sentient Design."**
- 2025–2026: Full Sentient Design practice, Sentient Scenes demo, Rosenfeld book, conference circuit.

## Blind spots (analysis)

- Optimistic about generative/adaptive UI; under-weights how non-deterministic interfaces break learnability, muscle memory, and the predictability that classic usability (Norman/Nielsen) treats as sacred.
- Enterprise-agency vantage point: stances assume large orgs with mature design systems and budgets; less voice on scrappy/solo or consumer-scale contexts.
- Accessibility and the failure modes of self-assembling UI for assistive tech get less airtime than the vision (defensive design is acknowledged but thin on AT specifics).
- Light on the hard ML/eval mechanics (cost, latency, hallucination measurement) — he frames AI as "material" and defers the model internals.
