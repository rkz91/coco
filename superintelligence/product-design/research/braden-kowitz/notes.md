## Design Sprint credit — PRECISION (corrected assumption)

The brief said Kowitz "co-created the GV Design Sprint." This is accurate but needs nuance, which the profile reflects:
- **Jake Knapp** invented the Design Sprint at Google starting 2010 (ran first sprints with Chrome, Search, Google X), then brought it to GV in 2012.
- At GV the team refined it. **Kowitz's specific contribution = story-centered design** ("an unconventional approach that focuses on the customer journey instead of individual features or technologies" — GV's own words). **John Zeratsky's contribution = start at the end / measure with key business metrics.**
- So: Kowitz is a **co-developer/co-author** of the GV Design Sprint and a co-author of the *Sprint* book (2016, Simon & Schuster, with Knapp and Zeratsky), NOT the sole/originating creator. Profile frames him as contributing story-centered design and pioneering the design-partner role.
- Sources:
  - GV Sprint page: https://www.gv.com/sprint/
  - Wikipedia "Design sprint": https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Design_sprint
  - Sprint (Simon & Schuster): https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Sprint/Jake-Knapp/9781501121746

## Key quotes / philosophy (dated findings)

### Story-centered design
- "This happens, and then this happens, and then this happens. It's like a little vignette or a little story." — Intercom interview. https://www.intercom.com/blog/podcast-braden-kowitz-talks-design-and-startups/
- GV essay title & thesis: "Story-centered design: hacking your brain to think like a user" — build narrative use-cases illustrating every step of the user's journey; move teams past surface visual details to decide what matters. https://library.gv.com/story-centered-design-hacking-your-brain-to-think-like-a-user-1a1e7d7eb642
- "Why good storytelling helps you design great products" — a story keeps the team focused on value delivered to the user. https://library.gv.com/why-good-storytelling-helps-you-design-great-products-148c9bbc7404

### Design in startups
- Design quality should become everyone's responsibility, not just designers'. Engineers write tests; teams should run design sprints and review research. — Intercom.
- "You don't need to worry about the stroke on the icon" when validating the core concept. — Intercom.
- Hiring: audit needed skills first, then hire to the gap. Interview question: "what problem were they solving, what did they do, and how do they know it worked?" — Intercom.

### Critique
- GV Guide to Design Critique: set clear goals, tie feedback to business and customer goals, give constructive feedback. https://library.gv.com/guide-to-design-critique-86ebf499bed5

### People / team building (Range era)
- "People may be the most important thing you build as a designer" — User Defenders podcast #068. Psychological safety as precondition for honest critique: "If you're worried about appearing perfect, you won't listen to feedback." "Life isn't perfect, and neither are we … and that's also okay." Range "check-in rounds." https://userdefenders.com/podcast/068-people-may-be-the-most-important-thing-you-build-as-a-designer-with-braden-kowitz/
- Interaction design behaves like language — patterns work once an audience has learned them; "designing by precedent" is risky without context. — User Defenders #068.
- Progression podcast #11 (2019-05-07): co-creating the Sprint at GV, journey from Google → VC → founder (Range), valuing meaningful work over a traditional design ladder. https://progression.co/blog/progression-podcast-11-braden-kowitz/
