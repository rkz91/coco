## Canonical positions & quotes (for stances + narrative)

### "Design is decision-making"
- "A design project is a series of decisions... [it] will be successful or not successful based on the ability of everyone involved to make those decisions in a clear and straightforward manner." (paraphrase widely attributed; aligned with *Just Enough Research*).
- Research = "a shared framework for making decisions so that you can make better decisions faster." (9 Rules of Design Research)
- "Research is really doing evidence-based design." (9 Rules)

### The 9 Rules of Design Research (Mule Design blog / Medium)
1. Get comfortable being uncomfortable
2. Ask first, prototype later
3. Know your goal
4. Agree on the big questions
5. There is always enough time and money
6. Don't expect data to change minds
7. Embrace messy imperfection
8. Commit to collaboration
9. Find your bias buddies
- "Testing a prototype can help you refine an idea that is already good, not tell you whether you're solving the right problem."
- "Asking questions is inherently threatening to authority."
- Quotes Upton Sinclair: "It is difficult to get a man to understand something, when his salary depends on his not understanding it."
- https://www.muledesign.com/blog/the-9-rules-of-design-research
- https://medium.com/mule-design/the-9-rules-of-design-research-1a273fdd1d3b

### dscout / People Nerds interview ("Knows How to Fix Your Design Process…")
- "You can't put an ethical interface on top of an unethical business model."
- "80% of your job should be that. It should be talking to people."
- "Organizations have this fear of research... But they don't come out and say: 'I'm afraid.' They say: 'We just don't have the time.'"
- "Experience design is such a misnomer, because what you're actually designing is one tiny part of somebody's whole experience."
- "Creativity is finding novel connections among things that exist in the world."
- https://dscout.com/people-nerds/erika-hall-better-research-and-design

### Conversational Design (book + 5-year reflection, A Book Apart, 2023-04-03)
- Core principles: "listening before you speak... maintaining state in conversations that happen across modes and devices... being cooperative, context aware, truthful, and ethical."
- "We're going to be separating novelty and fantasy from utility for a while yet."
- Warns against AI for user testing — systems "hallucinate," are "not sentient, self-reflective systems."
- "There is so much shallow thinking on the topic, and generally about technology-driven hype."
- Alexa / voice-shopping: billions invested on assumptions, adoption didn't materialize → value, not novelty, is the point.
- https://abookapart.com/products/conversational-design
- https://abookapart.com/blogs/press/erika-hall-reflects-on-5-years-of-conversational-design.html
- https://alistapart.com/article/conversational-design/

### Boundaryless Conversations Podcast #110 — "Decisions, Not Data: Why Research needs Organizational Readiness"
- Research needs *organizational readiness* / functional collaborative decision-making to land; otherwise it has zero impact.
- "Skilled practitioners in well-funded research departments who generate magnificent reports that have zero impact on decision-making."
- "Demonstrate a commitment to research while allowing our product leaders to do what they want." (the unspoken org goal)
- Research as "anti-authoritarian practice that fosters curiosity and challenges assumptions."
- https://www.boundaryless.io/podcast/hall-erika/
