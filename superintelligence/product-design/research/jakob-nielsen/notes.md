### Corrected assumptions logged
- The brief implied IBM might be a past employer; **no IBM affiliation found**. His industry research roles were **Sun Microsystems** and **Bellcore**. Removed IBM.
- The "5 users" finding is sometimes cited as catching "85%" of problems (Nielsen–Landauer model) and sometimes "80%." The original article states 5 users find ~**85%** of usability problems; the "80%" figure is a common rounding/secondary paraphrase. Used 85% (the primary figure) in the profile.
- "5 users" applies to **qualitative** testing with a relatively homogeneous user group. NN/g's own later guidance recommends ~**40 users** for quantitative studies (Nielsen co-authored the "qual vs quant" clarification). This nuance is what fuels the productive conflict with Ronny Kohavi (quant/experimentation camp).

## AI / generative-UI era — the active second act

Nielsen's post-2023 thesis: AI is the **third UI paradigm in ~60 years** and **generative UI** (interfaces drawn fresh per user, per intent) will displace hand-crafted, one-size-fits-all design.

### Dated articles + exact quotes

**"AI: First New UI Paradigm in 60 Years"** — published **2023-06-22**.
- https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/ai-is-first-new-ui-paradigm-in-60
- Thesis: AI introduces the **third** UI paradigm (after batch and command-based). Shift from command-based to **intent-based** interaction: "the user no longer tells the computer what to **do**. Rather, he or she tells the computer what outcome they **want**." This "**completely reverses the locus of control**."

**"Accessibility Has Failed: Try Generative UI = Individualized UX"** — published **2024-02-29**.
- https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/accessibility-generative-ui
- Exact quotes:
  - "Accessibility has failed as a way to make computers usable for disabled users."
  - "the accessibility movement has been a miserable failure. Computers are still difficult, slow, and unpleasant for disabled users."
  - On the fix: "the user interface is generated afresh every time the user accesses the app… different users will get drastically different designs." "This is how we genuinely help disabled users."
- **This is the controversial stance.** Wires the conflict with Léonie Watson (accessibility cell-mate) and the broader a11y community.

**"Vibe Coding and Vibe Design"** — published **2025-03-07**.
- https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/vibe-coding-vibe-design
- Quote: "Vibe design **raises the bar for design quality** and frees designers to tackle more complex experiential challenges."

**"The Usability Scaling Law: Death of User Testing?"** — published **2025-05-15** (UX Tigers).
- https://www.uxtigers.com/post/usability-scaling-law
- Thesis: "Usability observation still beats usability prediction" **today**, but AI trained on vast usability data will make **prediction beat observation** for common design tasks by ~2035; user research reserved for genuinely novel ground.

**"Generative UI from Gemini 3 Pro"** — published **2025-11-19**.
- https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/generative-ui-google
- Key stat: in a Google study, users **preferred the Generative UI over the top traditional website 90% of the time.**
- Quotes: "Generative UI moves past the dot-com-bubble idea approach of personalization toward individualization of the user experience." / "AI gets dramatically better each year, whereas human UX professionals don't improve as a group."

**"18 Predictions for 2026"** — published **2026-01-13**.
- https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/2026-predictions
- Quotes:
  - "User Experience will replace Model Intelligence as the primary sustainable differentiator." (UX as the moat)
  - "Software interfaces are no longer hard-coded; they are drawn in real-time based on the user's intent, context, and history."
  - On agents: "AI stops feeling like a software update and starts feeling like a structural reorganization of the workforce." Main 2026 agent use will be **enterprise** multi-agent systems / "digital employees."
- Companion: "2026 Predictions as a Comic Book and Poster Series" (https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/2026-prediction-comic).

## The accessibility controversy (for productive_conflict)

Nielsen's 2024-02-29 "Accessibility Has Failed" post triggered a strong rebuttal from the accessibility community:

- **Léonie Watson** (cell-mate, design-foundations-usability) — "Nielsen needs to think again." She is the screen-reader-first / W3C-standards counter-position. This is the cleanest in-roster conflict.
- **Eric Bailey** — "On Jakob Nielsen, AI hype, and accessibility" (2024-03-05, https://ericwbailey.design/published/on-jakob-nielsen-ai-hype-and-accessibility/): calls the argument intellectually dishonest.
- **Adrian Roselli** — "Jakob Has Jumped the Shark."
- **Nicolas Steenhout**, **Beth DeConinck**, **Per Axbom**, **Hidde de Vries** — all published rebuttals.
- Core counter-argument: **"Accessibility hasn't failed. People and organizations have failed to implement accessibility."** No evidence generative UI is yet desirable (by disabled users) or possible (with current tech); accessibility is a human/social issue, not merely a technical one.

Secondary conflict (qualitative-vs-quantitative): **Ronny Kohavi** (growth-metrics cell, "Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments"). Nielsen's "5 users is enough" + heuristic evaluation is the **qualitative/discount** pole; Kohavi's large-N controlled experiments are the **quantitative** pole. Nielsen himself drew the qual/quant line in the "5 participants okay in qual, not quant" article — productive tension, not pure disagreement.
