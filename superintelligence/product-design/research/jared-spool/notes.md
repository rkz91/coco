## Canonical concepts & quotes (with sources)

### The $300 Million Button
- Originally written for **Luke Wroblewski's** book *Web Form Design: Filling in the Blanks*. A major e-commerce site placed a login/registration form (Email, Password, Login, Register) immediately after the user clicked **Checkout**. First-time buyers resented being forced to register; 75% of shoppers who reached this step failed to complete the purchase.
- **The fix:** replaced the register requirement with a single **"Continue"** button (buy now, register later if you want). Result: **+45% more customers in the first month = +$15M that month; +$300M in the first year.**
- Spool's recalled line (responding to Wroblewski asking for an example of design's business impact): *"You mean like $300 million of new revenue?"*
- Core lesson: empirical research into how people actually behave beats designer/stakeholder intuition; form problems are often about **placement and context**, not layout.
- Sources:
  - https://medium.com/uie-brain-sparks/the-300-million-button-76b566ae5f73 (Spool's own article)
  - https://archive.uie.com/brainsparks/2011/10/17/the-back-story-for-the-300-million-button/
  - https://www.fastcompany.com/90183823/the-300-million-continue-button

### Experience Rot
- Definition (Spool, exact quotes from centercentre articles): *"Chances are all those features you've been adding to your design are hurting your user experience."* / *"Every feature that's squeezed in, in the name of giving your design a competitive edge, has been making your design less competitive."* / *"As you add features, you're adding complexity to the design, and decreasing the quality of the experience."*
- Mechanism: feature accumulation → complexity → degraded experience → invites disruption by a simpler competitor. Remedy: disciplined "no" to most proposed features. TV-remote example (started On/Off + volume + channel; now dozens of buttons).
- Coined ~2014/2015. Original tweet: https://twitter.com/jmspool/status/553395904062185472 ("Experience Rot: All those features you've been adding to your design are hurting your user experience.")
- Sources:
  - https://articles.centercentre.com/experience_rot/ (fetched 2026-06-01)
  - https://jmspool.medium.com/experience-rot-2061259b2a30

### "Design is the rendering of intent."
- Spool's most-quoted definition. Full: *"Design is the rendering of intent. What we intentionally design can delight someone. It can also frustrate them. We can change our rendering, with the intention to create more delight & less frustration. When we do that, we're designing the user's experience."*
- Corollary: *"Design is the rendering of intent. Anyone who renders intent is a designer."* — radically inclusive definition (everyone is a designer; "everyone's a UX designer").
- Sources:
  - https://x.com/jmspool/status/992412455807614976
  - https://x.com/jmspool/status/967730832482938880

### UX Design Maturity Model (5 stages)
- **Dark Ages** → **Spot UX Design** → **UX Design as a Service** → **Embedded UX Design** → **Infused UX Design** (non-UX team members hold UX skills). Leaders must transform across stages: *ground breaker → process maker → operations scaler.*
- Signature leadership claim: *"The number one responsibility of UX leaders is to make their organization the world's foremost experts on their users and what their users need."*
- Spool argues UX maturity is "what's holding back" the discipline; teams with **more researchers than designers** solve the real user needs that drive business success.
- Sources:

### Experience Strategy: A New Branch of UX
- Spool's 2026 framing: *"Experience strategy is an executive-level approach to UX"* where strategists make *"a direct connection between increased user value and top business objectives."* Central question: *"How would improving the lives of our customers, users, and employees advance the achievement of my organization's top priorities?"* Acknowledges *"you won't find anyone with the title of 'experience strategist'"* — a label he coined for work people already do. They *"don't wait to be asked."*
- Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jmspool_heres-some-exciting-news-a-new-branch-of-activity-7422058353918554113-0npl

## Productive conflict: Jared Spool vs. Jakob Nielsen (the "5 users" debate)
- Nielsen & Landauer (1993) → Nielsen's famous 2000 article "Why You Only Need to Test with 5 Users." Spool & Schroeder (2001), **"Testing Web Sites: Five Users Is Nowhere Near Enough"** (CHI 2001) directly rebutted it: in their study, serious problems were still being found after dozens of users; the "magic number 5" generalizes from too few studies (Nielsen/Landauer analyzed only five studies) and doesn't hold for large, complex, multi-million-user sites.
- This is a real, well-documented, decades-long disagreement → justifies `productive_conflict_with: [jakob-nielsen]` (jakob-nielsen is a real ROSTER.md slug in design-foundations-usability).
- Sources:
  - https://www.researchgate.net/publication/200553186_Testing_web_sites_five_users_is_nowhere_near_enough
  - https://www.nngroup.com/articles/5-test-users-qual-quant/ (NN/g's counter-position)
  - https://measuringu.com/five-history/
