## Dated findings & quotes

### Canonical: "Four Fits For $100M+ Growth"
URL: https://brianbalfour.com/four-fits-growth-framework
- The four fits: **Market-Product**, **Product-Channel**, **Channel-Model**, **Model-Market**.
- Quote: *"products are built to fit with channels. Channels do not mold to products."*
- Quote: *"you need all four Fits to grow to $100M+. You can't think about the four Fits in isolation because together they form an ecosystem for growth."*
- Model-Market formula: **ARPU × Total Customers in Market × % Market Capture ≥ $100M**.
- Channel-Model expressed via the **ARPU ↔ CAC** spectrum.
- Concept: **Smooth Sailers** (growth comes easily despite imperfect execution) vs **Tugboats** (growth is hard despite best practice). The framework explains the difference.
- Note: original essay is ~10 years old; he calls it the most popular piece he's written and the one he returns to in almost every strategy conversation.

### Canonical: "Growth Loops are the New Funnels"
URL: https://www.reforge.com/blog/growth-loops (also mirrored at brianbalfour.com)
- Quote (X, 2018): *"The fastest growing products are better represented as a system of loops, not funnels. Loops are closed systems where the inputs through some process generates more of an output that can be reinvested in the input."* — https://x.com/bbalfour/status/1024417738351996928
- Funnels *"have no concept of how to reinvest what comes out at the bottom to get more at the top ... no compounding effect."*
- Paraphrase widely attributed: *"Growth loops compound momentum, whereas funnels run out of fuel."*
- Systems view: *"Growth is a system between acquisition, retention, and monetization."*

### RECENT SIGNAL 1 — "AI Native Product Teams: How They Will Think, Work, and Build Differently"
**Date: 2025-06-15** · URL: https://blog.brianbalfour.com/p/ai-native-product-teams-how-they
- Thesis: AI is a fundamental shift comparable to the cloud revolution; product teams will be *reinvented*, not incrementally improved. *"The ecosystem is underestimating rather than overestimating AI's impact."*
- Claims: parallel exploration (test many design/copy/technical paths at once); prototype-first over documentation; problem-space expansion (computational → learning problems); **role blurring** across PM/eng/marketing that can recreate startup agility inside large orgs; distribution-channel disruption as AI agents become decision-makers.
- Cited voices in essay: Scott Belsky (Adobe), Ravi Mehta, Dharmesh Shah (HubSpot, "0.95^12 ≈ 54%" error-compounding math).

### RECENT SIGNAL 2 — "The Next Great Distribution Shift"
**Date: 2025-06-16** · URL: https://blog.brianbalfour.com/p/the-next-great-distribution-shift
- X announcement: https://x.com/bbalfour/status/1935367415644438747 (June 2025) — *"As @onecaseman said a year ago, AI has been a technology shift without a distribution shift. But I think that's about to change. Every platform follows the same brutal playbook: Open → ..."* (X fetch returned HTTP 402; tweet text corroborated via WebSearch result snippet + LinkedIn activity post 7341129079196143617, dated June 2025.)
- LinkedIn corroboration: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/bbalfour_new-essay-the-next-great-distribution-activity-7341129079196143617-los_
- Thesis: major platform shifts require BOTH a new way of building AND a new way of reaching people (tech + distribution). AI so far = tech shift only. *"we're still distributing products like it's 2015."*
- SEO collapsing (answer engines retain users), social platforms becoming walled gardens; old channels dying before new ones emerge. The next $100B cycle hides in the emerging channel.
- Survivors of past shifts used the platform's channel while building owned assets (Facebook viral + email list; Google traffic + brand; App Store + web).

### RECENT SIGNAL 3 — "Why ChatGPT will be the next big growth channel" (Lenny's Podcast)
**Date: 2025-08-17** · URL: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/why-chatgpt-will-be-the-next-big-growth-channel-brian-balfour
- Also: Apple Podcasts id1627920305?i=1000722283502; Spotify 0aSnaj8DY0X9LIagg4njHj; YouTube https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cX4cL6B-_aU
- The 4-step platform cycle (Open → Extend → Restrict → Close); ChatGPT just entered **step 2**.
- Signals that ChatGPT will launch a third-party platform within ~6 months; companies that don't integrate with ChatGPT lose to those that do.
- He's studied every major platform shift (Facebook, Apple, Google) and sees the pattern repeating.

### RECENT SIGNAL 4 — "The Four Fits: A Growth Framework for the AI Era"
**Date: 2025-09-25** · URL: https://blog.brianbalfour.com/p/the-four-fits-a-growth-framework
- Decade-later update; structure holds, AI compresses the clock. Quotes:
  - *"The time between these steps in AI is much faster."*
  - *"companies can find and lose product market fit almost instantly"*
  - Chegg: *"A 90% decline in nine months ... losing a half a million subscribers"*
  - *"we're still distributing products like it's 2015"*
  - *"the magic of some AI tools is wearing off"* (willingness-to-pay)
- AI-economics claims: freemium breaks at material LLM cost — **Lovable ~35% gross margin vs traditional 70%+ SaaS**; larger TAMs don't guarantee monetization — **Replit ~0.44% conversion**; new buyer persona (operations leaders prioritizing headcount savings over IT's feature focus); **ChatGPT becoming primary discovery** for high-consideration purchases (travel, cars).
- Tied to Reforge Fall 2025 AI Growth course (first cohort Oct 15, 2025; created with Bruno Estrella (Clay) and Lauryn Isford (Notion), instructed by Ely Lerner).
