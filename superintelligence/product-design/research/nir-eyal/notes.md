## 2. Canonical works

### Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products (2014, Portfolio/Penguin; originally self-published 2013)
- The Hook Model: a four-phase loop — **Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment** — designed to build user habits.
  - **Trigger:** external (email, notification, ad) or internal (an emotion such as boredom, loneliness, uncertainty).
  - **Action:** the simplest behavior done in anticipation of a reward; leans on BJ Fogg's behavior model (motivation + ability + trigger).
  - **Variable Reward:** rewards of the tribe, the hunt, and the self; variability sustains engagement because novelty activates the dopamine system.
  - **Investment:** the user puts something in (data, content, social capital, effort), which loads the next trigger and increases the odds of return. "When we put work into something, we're more likely to return to it."
- Sources:
  - https://www.nirandfar.com/hooked/
  - https://www.amazon.com/Hooked-How-Build-Habit-Forming-Products/dp/1591847788
  - https://growthmethod.com/hooked-model/
  - https://amplitude.com/blog/the-hook-model
  - https://medium.com/googleplaydev/optimize-app-retention-with-the-hooked-model-a0781f8e5d29 (Eyal byline on Google Play Dev)

### The Manipulation Matrix (ethics chapter / framework inside Hooked)
- A 2×2 ethics tool. Two questions: (1) "Does it materially improve the user's life?" (2) "Would the maker use it themselves?"
- Four quadrants:
  - **Facilitator** — improves users' lives AND maker uses it. The ethical ideal.
  - **Peddler** — maker would use it but it doesn't materially improve lives. Not immoral, but low odds of success.
  - **Entertainer** — improves nothing but maker would use it; art for its own sake.
  - **Dealer** — neither improves lives nor would the maker use it. Eyal calls this **exploitation**.
- Sometimes paired with his "drug-dealer test": if you wouldn't use it yourself and it doesn't help users, you are dealing.
- Sources:
  - https://www.nirandfar.com/the-art-of-manipulation/
  - https://medium.com/@vaishali.samanta/navigating-ethical-design-understanding-nir-eyals-manipulation-matrix-7d14c0607eee
  - https://designli.co/blog/using-the-manipulation-matrix-for-ethical-behavioral-design/

### Indistractable: How to Control Your Attention and Choose Your Life (2019, BenBella; co-authored research with Julie Li)
- Thesis inversion of Hooked: the *same* behavioral science, turned around to help individuals resist distraction.
- Four-part model:
  1. **Master internal triggers** — distraction is an escape from discomfort, not a property of technology.
  2. **Make time for traction** — you can't call something a distraction unless you know what it pulled you from; timebox via calendar.
  3. **Hack back external triggers** — pings, dings, meetings, interruptions.
  4. **Prevent distraction with pacts** — precommitment devices (effort, price, identity pacts) as the last firewall.
- Core line: "The opposite of distraction is not focus; it's traction."
- Sources:
  - https://www.nirandfar.com/indistractable/
  - https://www.nirandfar.com/distractions/
  - https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/automatic-you/202207/the-4-steps-to-becoming-indistractable

### Beyond Belief: The Science-Backed Way to Stop Limiting Yourself and Achieve Breakthrough Results (March 10, 2026; Portfolio/Penguin Random House; with Julie Li)
- ISBN 9780593852033. Instant New York Times Bestseller.
- Introduces the **Three Powers of Belief**: **Attention** (you see what you expect to see), **Anticipation** (expectations shape experience before anything happens), **Agency** (your sense of control changes what you are physiologically capable of).
- Endorsed by Derek Thompson, Vishen Lakhiani, Shane Parrish.
- Sources:
  - https://www.nirandfar.com/beyond-belief/
  - https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/761993/beyond-belief-by-nir-eyal-with-julie-li/
  - https://www.amazon.com/Beyond-Belief-Science-Backed-Limiting-Breakthrough/dp/0593852036

## 4. Public stances (each cited)

1. **The Hook Model — habits are engineered via Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment.**
   - https://www.nirandfar.com/hooked/

2. **Ethical habit design via the Manipulation Matrix — build Facilitators, not Dealers.** The two-question test: does it improve the user's life, and would you use it yourself?
   - https://www.nirandfar.com/the-art-of-manipulation/

3. **REBUTTAL STANCE (marquee): Technology is overused, not "addictive" for most people; the "hijacking" framing is overstated and disempowering.** "If I call technology something that people get addicted to, there needs to be a pusher, a dealer doing it to you. But if I say technology is something that people overuse, then it's, 'Oh, crap, now I need to do something about it myself.'" Direct review of *The Social Dilemma*: "No, social media is not hijacking your brain." "We have the power to control our tech habits, but only if we believe we can."
   - https://www.nirandfar.com/social-dilemma-review/
   - https://news.slashdot.org/story/19/10/06/2139203/consumer-expert-argues-tech-addiction-is-the-users-responsibility

4. **The root of distraction is internal, not the device.** "The opposite of distraction is not focus; it's traction." Distraction is an attempt to escape an uncomfortable internal state.
   - https://www.nirandfar.com/distractions/

5. **Shared responsibility, not zero corporate responsibility.** This corrects a common over-simplification of his view — see Section 6. Companies that can detect overuse "have both an economic imperative and a social responsibility to identify addicts and intervene"; the hands-off status quo is "exploitation." He has called for a "use and abuse policy."
   - https://www.nirandfar.com/tooaddictive/
   - https://www.cnbc.com/2018/01/30/nir-eyal-addictive-design-expert-says-tech-industry-needs-new-ethics.html

6. **AI makes the Investment phase dramatically more powerful — which raises, not lowers, the ethics bar.** "Ask whether you'd want someone you love to use your product the way your heaviest users use it."
   - https://www.productstate.com/p/nir-eyal-ai-personalization-beyond-belief

7. **Beliefs are tools, not facts; Attention/Anticipation/Agency are levers on behavior.**
   - https://www.nirandfar.com/beyond-belief/

## 6. Corrections to common assumptions (logged per instructions)

- **WRONG ASSUMPTION CORRECTED #1:** "Eyal says all responsibility is personal / corporations bear none." This is the popular caricature (and the framing in the task brief's shorthand "responsibility is personal"). His actual position is **shared responsibility**: he rejects the *addiction* framing and over-broad regulation, AND he explicitly argues companies have a "social responsibility to identify addicts and intervene" and should adopt a "use and abuse policy." He also wrote that "the tech industry needs a new ethical bar." The persona reflects the nuanced stance, not the caricature. Source: https://www.nirandfar.com/tooaddictive/ and https://www.cnbc.com/2018/01/30/nir-eyal-addictive-design-expert-says-tech-industry-needs-new-ethics.html
