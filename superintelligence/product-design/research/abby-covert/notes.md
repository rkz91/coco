## PUBLIC STANCES (each must carry an evidence_url)

1. **"Information architecture is for everybody"** — IA is a teachable, universal sensemaking skill, not a specialist tech job title. The entire premise of *How to Make Sense of Any Mess: Information Architecture for Everybody*.
   - Evidence: https://abbycovert.com/make-sense/ ; https://www.howtomakesenseofanymess.com/

2. **"Language is the material of information architecture; controlled vocabularies are agreements, not dictionaries."** — Shared, agreed language is the substrate of clarity: "When we say X, we all agree it means Y — not Z."
   - Evidence: https://abbycovert.com/writing/guide-to-controlled-vocabularies/

3. **"When you're stuck, diagram."** — Diagramming is a thinking tool, not a deliverable; making the invisible structure visible is how you get unstuck. Whole thesis of *Stuck? Diagrams Help.*
   - Evidence: https://abbycovert.com/stuck/

4. **"Sensemaking is a habit, not a job title or certification."** — Clarity is built incrementally through repeated practice with real people in real contexts; it is interpersonal work, not a credential.
   - Evidence: https://abbycovert.com/sensemaking/

5. **"A sensemaker's level of self-awareness directly impacts how the sensemaking work is done; your own bias is information."** — From "Challenges of Contemporary Sensemakers" — intentions and bias shape the work and must be surfaced.
   - Evidence: https://abbycovert.com/writing/challenges-of-contemporary-sensemakers/

6. **"Clarity is a practice with rules of thumb, not a framework you can follow."** — "The rules don't always fit, and frameworks only get you so far." She favors heuristics over rigid process.
   - Evidence: https://abbycovert.com/writing/ia-in-practice/

7. **"To change an information environment you must argue for it — with logical, emotional, and political resonance — and demonstrate ROI."** — Structural improvements need stakeholder, incentive, and ROI arguments, not just better diagrams. (IA in Practice Sept–Oct 2025 + IAC26 workshop.)
   - Evidence: https://www.theiaconference.com/sessions/timeless-sensemaking-skills-for-the-modern-sensemaker/

## CORRECTIONS TO BRIEF ASSUMPTIONS

- The brief described her as "ex-president of the IA Institute, IA Conference." Confirmed: she was **President of the IA Institute 2014–2016** and **co-chair of the IA Summit (2014)**, plus a 2020 IAC keynote — but she was **not** a "president of the IA Conference." The IA Conference (IAC) is the renamed successor to the IA Summit; she has been a co-chair / keynote / workshop facilitator there, not its president. Phrasing in the persona is precise about this.
- The brief listed two books ("How to Make Sense of Any Mess" and "Stuck? Diagrams Help."). There is now a **third book, *Timeless Sensemaking for Modern Sensemakers*** (announced late 2025, retail "Available April 16th" 2026), plus a 2023 Teacher's Guide and a 2024 10th-anniversary hardcover of *Make Sense*. The persona includes all three.
- Her current primary affiliation is **The Sensemakers Club ("Chief Sensemaker")**, not the IA Institute (which she left in 2016) — the IA Institute itself wound down (archived site). She is independent / self-publishing as of 2026.
