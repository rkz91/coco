## Source artifact
- https://www.interconnects.ai/p/why-nvidia-builds-open-models-with — Nathan Lambert's Interconnects podcast, dated 2025 (concurrent with the Nemotron Nano v2 / Nemotron 3 release window).

## Verbatim quotes pulled from the article

### Business rationale for open models

> "Our business needs us to know about AI very deeply. The amount of investment justified to carry on NVIDIA's ongoing business is large."

> "Whenever AI is able to grow in any direction, that's an opportunity for us to grow our business."

### Nemotron's dual mission

> "We built Nemotron for two main reasons. The first is because we need to for our main product line... The second job is to support the ecosystem more broadly outside of NVIDIA."

> "Nemotron is not just a model... the ecosystem needs more than just a model. If Nemotron can convince other people to work on AI because of a dataset or technique, we're trying to be very open."

### Systems-company framing

> "NVIDIA at its core is a systems company. Megatron's first job really is about systems... coordinating all of that, making sure we can deliver the benefits of NVIDIA systems to people making models, that's really important to us."

### Organizational philosophy

> "What's worked for us is invitation and not control... create the conditions by which it makes sense for people to volunteer to be part of Nemotron."

### Vision: AI as infrastructure (intelligence as potential energy)

> "Intelligence is like potential energy, not kinetic energy... companies have secrets and special data, and they'll know how to apply AI better than anyone else."

### Additional verbatim quotes

> "Nemotron is not a model. It is our entire approach towards supporting an open ecosystem for artificial intelligence."

> "Nemotron's first job is to make it possible for NVIDIA to continue to exist as a company."

> "It's usually less than a third of the compute that goes towards building AI that is actually building the model itself. About two thirds — or more, maybe three quarters — of the compute is spent on experiments and synthetic data generation, and things around the process of building a model."

> "No one outside NVIDIA has ever pre-trained a model at this scale using four-bit math." (re: NVFP4 training stack on Nemotron 3)

## Why these quotes matter

These are the most recent (2025) on-the-record framings Catanzaro has given that capture his strategic theses. They cluster into four ideas:

1. **NVIDIA is a systems company, not a chip company.** Megatron and cuDNN are part of the product, not adjacent to it.
2. **Open models are a moat-builder, not a giveaway.** The Android-style strategy: open weights, but every byte of revenue is on NVIDIA hardware.
3. **The compute story is about experiments and synthetic data, not the final training run.** This shifts where infrastructure investment matters.
4. **Intelligence is potential energy.** Applied value sits at the customer, not the foundation model. Implies a frame where NVIDIA stays in infrastructure and lets customers (and increasingly partners like Mistral, Cursor, Perplexity, Reflection AI, Thinking Machines) build the kinetic-energy layer.

The Nemotron Coalition partner list — Mistral, Cursor, LangChain, Perplexity, Reflection AI, Sarvam AI, Black Forest Labs, Thinking Machines — is itself a signal about how he reads the application-layer market in 2026.
