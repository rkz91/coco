## Verified biography

- **Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences** from UC Berkeley, completed 2011 under **Prof. Kurt Keutzer**. Doctoral work centered on **Copperhead**, a language and compiler that let Python programmers use nested data-parallel abstractions and obtain high efficiency on contemporary parallel platforms. Catanzaro and Keutzer also co-authored "The Landscape of Parallel Computing Research: A View from Berkeley" (EECS-2006-183, 3,100+ citations) and "Fast Support Vector Machine Training and Classification on Graphics Processors" (EECS-2008-11).
- **First NVIDIA stint (2011–2014):** Joined as a research scientist after an internship during his PhD. Worked on programming models for parallel processors and deep-learning libraries. Wrote the research prototype that became **cuDNN**, the low-level NVIDIA library used by virtually every modern deep-learning framework.
- **Baidu Silicon Valley AI Lab (2014–2016):** Senior researcher under Andrew Ng. Part of the **Deep Speech** and **Deep Speech 2** teams (co-authors include Awni Hannun, Carl Case, Jared Casper, Greg Diamos, Erich Elsen, Adam Coates, Andrew Y. Ng). Built next-generation systems for training and deploying end-to-end deep-learning speech recognition. Deep Speech 2 surpassed published results on the Switchboard Hub5'00 benchmark.
- **NVIDIA second stint (October 2016 → present):** Returned as **Vice President of Applied Deep Learning Research** (ADLR). At the time he was the only member of the new lab. The team now spans video games (DLSS), chip design, autonomous-driving simulation, audio/speech, and large-scale LLM training (Megatron-LM, Nemotron).

## Pre-NVIDIA personal background

- Dual humanities + CS background as an undergraduate (mentioned in long-form profiles).
- Served as a missionary in Serbia before graduate school.
- Started a family early; supported three children during graduate school through interleaved internships at Intel, Qualcomm, and other firms before settling at NVIDIA.

## Why he matters to the AI super-intelligence roster

- **Systems-kernels-serving lead-driver:** He is the canonical figure for the position that frontier-scale model training is a systems problem, not just an ML problem. The Megatron-LM line of work is the defining proof.
- **Hardware-software co-design:** Sits at the seam between NVIDIA hardware roadmap (Hopper, Blackwell, NVFP4) and the ML workloads that run on it. His team's prototypes inform GPU design.
- **Open-source posture inside a hyperscaler hardware vendor:** Rare combination. He runs Megatron and Nemotron in the open while NVIDIA's commercial moat depends on those workloads running on NVIDIA silicon.
