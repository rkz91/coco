## Paris AI Action Summit (February 10, 2025) — opening keynote

Delivered the opening keynote. Her tweet from the event: "Live from Paris, at the #ParisAISummit. I gave the opening keynote about AI's past, present, future and opportunities. '75 years ago, Alan Turing dared humanity to imagine thinking machines. But perhaps that vision is too narrow and inward-looking...'"

### On sci-fi sensationalism
- "It's essential that we govern on the basis of science, not science fiction."
- "So much of today's AI conversations are covered by sensationalism and result in misleading policies."

### On governance approach
- Advocated for "a pragmatic attitude instead of an ideological one when it comes to AI governance."
- Wants assessment of AI's actual capabilities and limitations scientifically, not through speculation.

### On research freedom
- Warned against "hindering the upstream exploration of research."
- Called for focus on "practical applications" to ensure beneficial outcomes while guarding against harms.

### On ecosystem health
- "Investing in healthier and more vibrant AI ecosystems where academia and the community can produce."
- Cautioned that concentration among few companies would undermine "curiosity-driven research, top educational talents, open source efforts, and multidisciplinary explorations."

### On AI's significance
- Characterized this period as "the first true era of AI" with "civilizational consequences."
- AI can "augment and empower us" with proper decision-making by policymakers, companies, and researchers.

## SB-1047 (California, summer 2024) — public opposition

She published a Fortune op-ed and went on record opposing California's SB-1047, arguing it was "well-meaning" but had unintended consequences.

### Key argument
The bill would "harm our budding AI ecosystem, especially the parts of it that are already at a disadvantage to today's tech giants: the public sector, academia, and 'little tech.'"

### Specific objections
- **Kill-switch mandate** for downloaded/derivative models: developers would hesitate to write code and collaborate if their work could be deleted. (Bill author Scott Wiener publicly disputed her characterization of this provision.)
- **Academia impact** — regulations would dampen academic and public-sector research.

### Aftermath
Newsom vetoed SB-1047 in September 2024. Subsequently appointed Li to the working group developing California's alternative AI guardrail framework.

## Posture summary
Fei-Fei Li is a **pragmatist on regulation, not an x-risk hawk and not a doomer-skeptic.** She is consistently:
- Pro academic and public-sector access to compute and research.
- Anti regulations she sees as concentrating power with frontier labs.
- Pro practical, application-level governance over pre-emptive model-tier rules.
- Pro human-centered design — but the "human" in human-centered means civic/educational/healthcare access, not alignment/safety in the Bostrom sense.

This places her in productive friction with both Yann LeCun (who is more openly dismissive of x-risk framing) and with Dario Amodei / Dan Hendrycks (who anchor catastrophic-risk regulatory frames).
