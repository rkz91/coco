## ImageNet (CVPR 2009)
With Jia Deng, Wei Dong, Richard Socher, Li-Jia Li, Kai Li, and others. The dataset that broke computer vision out of its stagnation:
- ~14M images across 22,000 categories.
- Hierarchical taxonomy based on WordNet.
- Mechanical Turk for labeling — first deployment of crowd labeling at this scale in CV.
- ILSVRC competition (2010-2017) provided the benchmark that AlexNet (2012) crushed, catalyzing the deep learning revolution.

## TED talk — "How we're teaching computers to understand pictures" (March 2015)
Most-viewed AI TED talk of the 2010s. Established her as the public face of computer vision. Frames the field as teaching machines to see in the way a 3-year-old child does — perception fused with cognition.

## TED talk — "With spatial intelligence, AI will understand the real world" (April 2024)
The sequel framing, delivered before World Labs came out of stealth. Establishes spatial intelligence as the natural next chapter beyond ImageNet.

## "The Worlds I See" (Flatiron Books, November 7, 2023)
Memoir. Major themes per reviews (NPR, Kirkus, Goodreads, Digitopoly, Medium):
- **Immigration and identity** — child immigrant from China; working at the family dry-cleaning business; being a low-income woman of color at Princeton; code-switching between home and school.
- **Vision and cognition** — vision is more than perception; deeply tied to how the brain interprets sensory input to form meaning.
- **Human-centered AI** — passionate case for technology built with care, inclusion, compassion; warning against AI driven solely by profit or power.
- **Ethics and responsibility** — what private firms neglect; why neural network opacity makes transparency challenging.
- **The long road to "overnight" AI** — ImageNet was a decade of unglamorous data work that made the 2012 deep-learning breakthrough possible.

San Francisco Public Library named it "One City One Book" selection.
