# Fei-Fei Li — Biographical Spine (Wikipedia + Stanford profiles)

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fei-Fei_Li
**Pulled:** 2026-05-27

## Identity and origin
- Born July 3, 1976 in Beijing, China; raised in Chengdu, Sichuan.
- Father immigrated to Parsippany, New Jersey when she was 12; she joined four years later.
- Worked weekends at family dry-cleaning business while attending Parsippany High School (inducted into the school's hall of fame in 2017).

## Education
- **Princeton University** — BA in Physics, 1999. Senior thesis on auditory computation.
- **California Institute of Technology** — MS in Electrical Engineering (2001), PhD in Electrical Engineering (2005). Dissertation: "Visual Recognition: Computational Models and Human Psychophysics" under Pietro Perona and Christof Koch.

## Academic career
- University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign — Assistant Professor (2005-2006)
- Princeton University — Assistant Professor (2007-2009)
- Stanford University — Assistant Professor (2009), Associate Professor with tenure (2012), Full Professor (2018), Sequoia Professor of Computer Science.
- Director, Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (2013-2018)
- Co-director, Stanford Vision and Learning Lab
- Co-director, Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI), since founding in 2019

## Industry
- Google Cloud — Chief Scientist of AI/ML and Vice President, sabbatical from Stanford 2017-2018.
- During Google tenure she was internally enthusiastic about Project Maven but cautioned against publicizing its AI component; when emails leaked she said: "I believe in human-centered AI to benefit people in positive and benevolent ways. It is deeply against my principles to work on any project that I think is to weaponize AI."

## World Labs (current)
- Co-founded April 2024 with Justin Johnson, Christoph Lassner, and Ben Mildenhall.
- Came out of stealth September 2024 with $230M seed at $1B valuation.
- February 2026: raised $1B at reported $5B valuation. Investors: Nvidia, AMD, Autodesk ($200M and advisory role), Fidelity Management, Emerson Collective, Sea.
- On partial academic leave (January 2024-end of 2025), now returning under new Stanford role.

## Key relationships
- Married to Stanford professor Silvio Savarese; son and daughter.
- **Andrej Karpathy was her PhD student at Stanford Vision Lab.** They co-designed and co-taught CS231n (Stanford's first deep learning course) which grew from 150 students in 2015 to 750 in 2017. Karpathy is "jokingly the reference human for ImageNet" because he competed against early ConvNets on the 1,000-class categorization task.

## Honors
- 2018 — ACM Fellow
- 2020 — National Academy of Engineering, National Academy of Medicine
- 2021 — American Academy of Arts and Sciences
- 2023 — UN Scientific Advisory Board member; Time 100 AI; published *The Worlds I See*
- 2024 — VinFuture Prize; Gold House A100 Most Influential Asians
- 2025 — Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (shared with Hinton, Bengio, LeCun, Hopfield, Bill Dally, Jensen Huang); Time "Architects of AI"
- Other: Twitter Board (2020-2022, removed at Musk acquisition); Council on Foreign Relations (2020)

## Co-founded AI4ALL (2017)
With Melinda French Gates and Jensen Huang. Educates next-generation AI technologists with diversity and human-centered principles. Grew from SAILORS at Stanford (2015 with Olga Russakovsky) to AI4ALL@Stanford in 2017, then to Princeton, CMU, BU, UC Berkeley, Simon Fraser.

## ImageNet (2007-2017)
Developed at Princeton starting 2007. Labeled 14M+ images across 22,000 categories via Amazon Mechanical Turk. Annual ImageNet Large-Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC) ran 2010-2017. AlexNet (2012) is the canonical inflection point that catalyzed the deep learning revolution.
