# "From Words to Worlds: Spatial Intelligence is AI's Next Frontier"

**Source:** https://drfeifei.substack.com/p/from-words-to-worlds-spatial-intelligence
**Author:** Fei-Fei Li
**Published:** November 10, 2025
**Pulled:** 2026-05-27

This is the canonical Fei-Fei Li manifesto for the World Labs era — the analog of Karpathy's "Software 3.0" talk for spatial-vision instead of LLMs.

## Core thesis
AI has reached a critical juncture. Large language models excel at text but remain **"eloquent but inexperienced, knowledgeable but ungrounded."** The next frontier is spatial intelligence — the capability to understand, reason about, and interact with physical and virtual worlds.

## On language vs. vision
- Language alone cannot capture how humans understand reality.
- Current AI models "rarely perform better than chance on estimating distance, orientation, and size" or mentally rotating objects.
- Invokes Wittgenstein: "the limits of my language mean the limits of my world" — but argues AI requires more than words to truly understand physical reality.

## Definition of a world model — three essential capabilities
1. **Generative** — models must create geometrically and physically consistent worlds.
2. **Multimodal** — they should process diverse inputs (images, text, gestures, actions).
3. **Interactive** — they must output next world-states based on input actions.

## Why it is harder than language
"The scope of this challenge exceeds anything AI has faced before" because worlds operate under complex physical laws unlike language's sequential nature.

## Vision for applications
Transforming creativity, robotics, scientific discovery, and healthcare. Spatial intelligence is positioned as crucial for building machines that "augment human capability, not replace it" — the same human-centered framing she's used since the founding of Stanford HAI in 2019.
