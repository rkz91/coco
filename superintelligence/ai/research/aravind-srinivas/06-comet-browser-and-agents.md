# Comet Browser and the Agentic Pivot (2025–2026)

Compiled 2026-05-27.

## Comet — the browser as the next OS for AI

### Launch timeline

- **July 2025:** Initial launch to Perplexity Max ($200/mo) subscribers. Multi-million-person waitlist. Built on Chromium.
- **October 2025:** Free download for all users.
- **October 2025:** Srinivas teases iOS version: *"Wait till Comet iOS is there! The first real competition to Safari begins then."*
- **February 6, 2026:** Comet browser agent defaults to Claude Opus 4.6.
- **March 2026:** Comet iOS launched. Custom connectors. Deep Research with assets. Model Council.
- **2026 declared "the year of the browser"** internally at Perplexity.

### Architecture

- AI-native browser, not a chatbot bolted onto a Chromium fork.
- **Comet Assistant** — in-page research, summarization, autonomous multi-step tasks (book flights, manage email, fill forms).
- Max subscribers choose the underlying model: Opus 4.6 (default), Sonnet 4.5, others.

### Strategic framing (Srinivas quotes from CNBC, Verge Decoder, India TV interviews)

- *"Comet is meant to be a true personal assistant."* (CNBC, Oct 3, 2025)
- *"Companies won't have to hire more people"* — productivity argument for enterprise adoption.
- *Comet could "kill Android"* — combative positioning of agent browsers as platform-displacing.
- *Browsers are the future home of true AI agents — not chat windows.*

## Perplexity Computer — the super-agent (Feb 25, 2026)

Aravind's tweet (text reconstructed from search excerpt):

> "What has Perplexity been up to last two months? We've silently been working on the next big thing: Perplexity Computer. Computer unifies every current capability of AI into a single system. Files, tools, memory, and models, orchestrated together, working for you."

Coverage from Fortune (Feb 26, 2026):

- Runs **remotely in the cloud**, in a locked-down environment.
- Carries out tasks in the background — *"more like assigning work to a coworker on Slack than watching an AI take over your screen."*
- Positioned against OpenAI's Operator and Anthropic's Computer Use.
- Safety pitch: cloud sandbox is "safer and more dependable" than local computer-control.

## Bigger strategic picture

1. **Browser as the next OS for AI.** Apps inside the browser become agent-addressable surfaces. The browser, not the OS, becomes the integration point.
2. **Speed as a UX moat.** Sub-second answers are repeatedly cited as the metric Aravind protects most aggressively.
3. **Antitrust positioning.** The $34.5B Chrome bid (Aug 2025) was a public move to capitalize on DOJ antitrust pressure on Google. It was almost certainly never going to close, but it positioned Perplexity as the obvious heir-apparent to a forced Chrome divestiture.
4. **From chat → browser → super-agent.** Three explicit generations of product, each subsuming the last.
