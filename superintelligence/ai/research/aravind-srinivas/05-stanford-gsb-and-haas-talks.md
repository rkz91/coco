# Aravind Srinivas — Stanford GSB and UC Berkeley Haas Talks

Compiled from public coverage 2026-05-27.

## Stanford GSB — View From The Top (late 2024 / early 2025)

URL: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/insights/perplexitys-aravind-srinivas-infinite-value-knowledge
Event page: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/events/view-top-aravind-srinivas
Interviewer: Aislin Roth, MBA Class of 2025.

### Direct quotes

- **On wealth vs. knowledge:** "You can probably focus on wealth and your net worth... but at some point it taps out. On the other hand, there is no end to knowledge."
- **Perplexity tagline:** "Where knowledge begins."
- **On citations as a product axiom:** "Every sentence in an academic paper needs a corresponding citation… What if we do that for AI?"
- **On plagiarism allegations:** Attribution of sources is "a core part of the definition" of plagiarism; when "you're always attributing sources, it's very hard to say you're plagiarizing content."
- **On building teams:** Team members "should be a lot better" at what they excel at than the leader is.
- **On not building foundation models:** "Most successful businesses are wrappers of some form." Defends not building a $10B+ foundation model and instead differentiating via post-training, UI, and verticals.
- **On monetization:** Suggests follow-up *questions* (not answers) as ad placements — keeps the answer surface honest while monetizing intent.

### Strategic framing

- Inspired by Spotify's revenue-share model rather than Google's referral-traffic model for working with publishers.
- The product mission is **equitable access to knowledge across languages and cultures**, not "be a better Google."

## UC Berkeley Haas — Dean's Speaker Series (2025)

URL: https://newsroom.haas.berkeley.edu/deans-speaker-series-perplexity-ai-ceo-aravind-srinivas-phd-21-on-why-he-ditched-pitch-decks/

### Direct quotes

- **On being unemployable:** "Some people are unemployable. They just don't listen to what the boss tells them to do. I'm one of them."
- **On entrepreneurship:** "Driving change the way you think should be done can only be truly done as an entrepreneur."
- **On fundraising style:** "I've never done a pitch deck for any of the other Perplexity funding rounds. I just write a memo, and I tell them, 'You can do a Q&A.'"
- **On using Perplexity as his own tool:** "It already knows everything... I'm not even exaggerating."
- **On shipping imperfect products:** "You have to build products that 80% work... 80% is enough for users to get excited about it."
- **On unchanging principles:** "No one's going to ask for a slower product. No one's going to ask for a less accurate product. No one's going to ask for a crappy user experience."
- **On risk-taking:** "There's really nothing to lose from taking risks."
- **On persistence:** "[The greatest have] one quality... they don't ever give up."
- **On investor selection at seed:** "You're really betting on the founder, the team, the mentality of the founder."

### Strategic framing

- Memo-not-deck fundraising is a signal of his bias: he prefers prose and direct Q&A to slideware. (Mirrors Karpathy's "build the thing, don't talk about the thing.")
- "80% works" is his explicit ship-cadence rule — Perplexity ships imperfect features and lets users compound the feedback loop.

## Why these talks matter

These two academic-affiliated talks are the cleanest source of **his own voice on product philosophy** outside of podcast transcripts. They consistently emphasize:

1. Speed and accuracy are non-negotiable; everything else is optional.
2. Citations are the trust contract.
3. Memo over slideware. Ship over pitch.
4. "Wrappers" are not a slur — they're the structure of most successful businesses.
5. Knowledge is the unbounded good, not money.
