# Aravind Srinivas — Distilled Public Stances

Compiled 2026-05-27 from primary sources.

## The seven stances he advances most consistently

### 1. Search should be answer-first, not link-first.

- **Origin:** Lex Fridman #434 (Jun 2024); Stanford GSB View From The Top (2024).
- **Mechanics:** Ten-blue-links was a UX accident of 1998 hardware constraints, not an inviolable contract with the user. The user wanted the answer.
- **Quote:** *"Search engines optimize for ten blue links. Users want one good answer."*

### 2. Citations are the trust contract.

- **Origin:** Stanford GSB. Anchored in his academic-paper analogy.
- **Quote:** *"Every sentence in an academic paper needs a corresponding citation. What if we do that for AI?"*
- **Operational consequence:** If an answer can't be cited, it doesn't ship. Citation is post-training discipline, not a UI ornament.

### 3. The browser is the next OS for AI.

- **Origin:** Verge Decoder podcast (2025); India TV interview (Oct 2025); CNBC (Oct 2025).
- **Mechanics:** Apps inside the browser become agent-addressable. The browser, not the OS, is the integration point for agentic workflows.
- **Aggressive framing:** *"Comet could kill Android."*

### 4. Sub-second speed is a UX moat.

- **Origin:** Haas, Stanford GSB.
- **Quote:** *"No one's going to ask for a slower product. No one's going to ask for a less accurate product. No one's going to ask for a crappy user experience."*
- **Operational consequence:** Latency is a first-class product metric, not an SRE concern.

### 5. Most successful businesses are wrappers.

- **Origin:** Stanford GSB.
- **Defense of not building a foundation model:** Differentiation lives in post-training, UI, vertical specialization, and distribution — not in the raw weights. Foundation-model competition burns $10B+ for table stakes.

### 6. Ship at 80%. Memo, not deck.

- **Origin:** Haas Dean's Speaker Series.
- **Quote:** *"You have to build products that 80% work. 80% is enough for users to get excited about it."*
- **Fundraising corollary:** *"I've never done a pitch deck for any of the other Perplexity funding rounds. I just write a memo, and I tell them, 'You can do a Q&A.'"*

### 7. Knowledge has no ceiling; wealth does.

- **Origin:** Stanford GSB ("Infinite Value of Knowledge").
- **Quote:** *"You can probably focus on wealth and your net worth, but at some point it taps out. On the other hand, there is no end to knowledge."*
- **Strategic frame:** The TAM for *people trying to learn things* is structurally bigger and longer-lived than for *people trying to navigate to a known page*.

## Secondary stances worth tracking

- **R1 1776:** Post-training can excise government-imposed censorship from a frontier reasoning model without harming its core reasoning ability. *"The post-training to remove censorship was done without hurting the core reasoning ability of the model."*
- **AI layoffs are a "glorious future":** All-In Podcast (Mar 2026). Layoffs free people from jobs they disliked and create new entrepreneurship surface area. Combative on labor-displacement discourse.
- **Apple should ship answers in Siri:** Implicit position throughout 2025 — Perplexity made itself the obvious M&A target by demonstrating the product Apple needed.
