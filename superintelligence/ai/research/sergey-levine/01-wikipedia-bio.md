## Education

- **BS** Computer Science — Stanford University (2009)

- **PhD** Computer Science — Stanford University (2014). Doctoral advisor: Vladlen Koltun. Thesis work centered on optimal control, guided policy search, and data-driven control policies.
- **Postdoc** — UC Berkeley Robot Learning Lab under Pieter Abbeel (2014–2016).

The user's brief mentions Pieter Abbeel as his PhD advisor, but the public record is consistent: Vladlen Koltun supervised the PhD at Stanford; Abbeel was the postdoctoral host at Berkeley and is the long-standing collaborator who shaped the Berkeley robot-learning lineage Levine joined as faculty.

## Lab and teaching

- Leads the **Robotic AI and Learning Lab (RAIL)** at Berkeley — `rail.eecs.berkeley.edu`.
- Primary instructor of **CS 285: Deep Reinforcement Learning**, widely considered the canonical graduate RL course in the field. He also teaches **CS 182: Deep Learning**.
- Member of the **Berkeley AI Research (BAIR)** lab.

## Research domains

- Deep reinforcement learning, especially actor-critic and maximum-entropy methods (SAC).
- End-to-end visuomotor policy learning (vision → joint torques).
- Offline / batch reinforcement learning (CQL, AWAC, IQL, D4RL).
- Imitation learning and large-scale robot data collection.
- Cross-embodiment robot learning and generalist policies.
- Vision-Language-Action (VLA) foundation models for robotics.

- Highly cited researcher in robotics and reinforcement learning across multiple years.

## Notable framings he has coined or popularized

- **"Generalist robotic policies"** — the argument that data scale across many robots and tasks beats specialist policies on any one task.
- **"Self-improving robot flywheel"** — robots deployed in the world generate the data that improves the next generation of models.
- **"Sporks of AGI"** — surrogate data (simulation, human video) is a spork: it does both jobs badly compared to the real thing.
- **"LLMs in Plato's cave"** — language models work because they reverse-engineer human cognition from text, not because they understand the world. Robotics cannot rely on that shortcut.
