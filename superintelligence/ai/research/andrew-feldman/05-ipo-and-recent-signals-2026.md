## IPO mechanics — May 14, 2026 on Nasdaq

- Ticker: **CBRS** on Nasdaq (not NYSE — the original task hint was incorrect on the exchange).
- IPO price: **$185/share**.
- Opening trade: **$350/share**.
- First-day close: 68% above IPO price.
- Shares sold: 30 million.
- Capital raised: **$5.55 billion** — largest US tech IPO since Uber in 2019.
- First-day market cap: roughly **$95 billion**; the No Priors recap referenced a $63B IPO valuation and a market cap close to $100B at close.
- Feldman's personal stake: $3.2 billion (Bloomberg, IPO day). Forbes later estimated $3.4B.
- Sean Lie's personal stake: $1.7 billion.
- The IPO turned two of the five co-founders into billionaires on day one.

## Direct quotes from Andrew Feldman, ordered by date

### March 2026 — Benzinga, pre-IPO interview

On switching from NVIDIA to Cerebras for inference:
> "To move from using NVIDIA GPUs, for inference, to Cerebras in our cloud will take about 10 keystrokes and should take you less than a minute."

On training migration:
> "Training is a harder problem, but there too, within hours, you should be able to move a training workflow from a GPU cluster to Cerebras."

On the inference inflection:
> "Right now, inference time computes — improving the quality of the answer based on additional use of tokens through reasoning is an extremely powerful tool."

On TAM:
> "This is a huge market, there's lots of opportunity… I think there'll be many winners."

On power:
> "I think our chips use a fraction of the power of GPUs."

On the S-1 refiling:
> "We withdrew our S1 because it was out of date and because we now had a new cap table with new investors. We will update it as quickly as we can and go forward towards an IPO."

On the timing of the broader moment:
> "Right now, you want to spend every minute you can in AI because so much exciting is happening… It's really an extraordinary time."

### May 14, 2026 — CNBC IPO-day video

> "We have tremendous opportunities for growth, and this was the right way to fund our growth."

### May 14, 2026 — Benzinga, on US chip manufacturing onshoring

Feldman warned that expanding advanced US semiconductor manufacturing is a 10–15-year project requiring "deeply integrated supply chains, specialized infrastructure, and massive capital investment that cannot be replicated quickly." He also noted Cerebras has relied on TSMC for advanced chip production since its founding, and that space-based AI computing "could eventually become viable" but is "another decade-long project."

### May 16, 2026 — TechCrunch, retrospective on the near-death period

> "We were spending about $8 million a month. At this point, we had incinerated nearly $200 million trying to solve one technical problem."

On the engineering culture during the crisis: chips were "58 times larger" and "using 40 times as much power as anybody had ever used." When the team finally solved the packaging problem in July 2019, engineers "just stood in the lab and stared at it… stunned that we'd solved this." Feldman called it "one of the greatest moments of my life."

On current go-to-market discipline:
> "We're going to work with part of the buffet only… before we attack the rest."

## Why this matters for the persona

The May 2026 IPO is the inflection point that converts Feldman from "scrappy challenger CEO" to "public-company CEO defending a $95B market cap against NVIDIA's $4T+ market cap." His public voice will narrow over the next 12 months — public companies do not get to make ambient strategic claims the way pre-IPO companies do. The persona should treat his pre-IPO archive (No Priors, ARK podcast, Stratechery references, ODD Lots, the Cremades interview) as the high-fidelity source for his unfiltered framings, and the post-IPO archive (CNBC, Bloomberg, Benzinga IPO-week, Cerebras blog) as the source for his polished, board-cleared positioning.
