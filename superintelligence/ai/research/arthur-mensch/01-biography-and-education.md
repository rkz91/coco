# Arthur Mensch — Biography and Education

Source: Wikipedia, "Arthur Mensch" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Mensch (fetched 2026-05-28).

## Personal

- Born July 17, 1992, Sèvres, France. Raised in Ville-d'Avray, Hauts-de-Seine.
- Father: businessman. Mother: physics teacher.
- Became a father in 2024 (per Wikipedia note).
- Reported individual net worth ~$1.1 billion by 2025 (Challenges magazine, post Series C).
- Honors: TIME100 AI (2024) — described as the only Frenchman on the inaugural list. Knight of the National Order of Merit (May 15, 2025).

## Education

- **École Polytechnique** — entered 2011. Met co-founders Guillaume Lample and Timothée Lacroix during studies here.
- **Télécom Paris** — engineering school.
- **Université Paris-Saclay** — specialized master "Mathematics, Vision, Learning" (MVA).
- **PhD (2015–2018)** at Inria / NeuroSpin (CEA, Saclay) under Bertrand Thirion, Gaël Varoquaux, and Julien Mairal.
  - Focus: predictive models and stochastic optimization for functional MRI analysis.
- **Postdoctoral research (2018–2020)** at École Normale Supérieure (Paris). Focus: optimal transport and stochastic optimization.
- Visiting research at NYU Courant Institute with Joan Bruna on multi-agent reinforcement learning.

## Career arc

- **2020–May 2023:** Research scientist at Google DeepMind (Paris office). Worked on large language models, multimodal systems, and retrieval-augmented architectures. Co-authored the Chinchilla scaling-laws paper during this period (see file 03).
- **April 28, 2023:** Co-founded Mistral AI in Paris with Guillaume Lample (Chief Scientist) and Timothée Lacroix (CTO). Both co-founders came from Meta AI Research (FAIR), Paris — they had worked on LLaMA before joining Mistral.
- **CEO of Mistral AI** since founding. Company name references the Mediterranean mistral wind, "symbolizing speed and scale."

## Policy / public roles in France

- September 2023: Joined Prime Minister Élisabeth Borne's generative AI expert committee.
- May 2024: Testified before the French Senate. Called for increased public research funding, expanded research tax credits, and labor-law flexibility for startups.
- May 12, 2026: Testified before French National Assembly inquiry commission on "structural dependencies and systemic vulnerabilities in the digital sector" — central event of his 2026 public positioning (see file 02).
