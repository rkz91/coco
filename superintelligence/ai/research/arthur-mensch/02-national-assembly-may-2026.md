# Arthur Mensch — French National Assembly testimony, May 12, 2026

The defining public moment of Mensch's 2026 sovereignty campaign. He testified for ~90 minutes before the National Assembly's inquiry commission on "structural dependencies and systemic vulnerabilities in the digital sector."

## Headline framings

- **AI is infrastructure technology.** Europe has "a few months" — operationally, ~24 months — to decide whether it produces AI or merely consumes it. After that window, the choice closes.
- **"Vassal state" warning.** If Europe continues importing digital services from the United States without sovereign capacity, the continent becomes a vassal state. Mensch: "Once supply is monopolized by American players, suddenly we no longer have supply, and we can no longer transform electrons into tokens."
- **Cloud and AI are now the same thing.** "Today, the cloud is artificial intelligence. The growth of the cloud is artificial intelligence." Strategic implication: losing AI sovereignty is losing cloud sovereignty.
- **Strategic recommendation: capture margin first.** "If you have no volume and no margin, you have no chance of reaching scale. You have to start from high-value services and work down."
- **Trillion-euro extrapolation.** AI consumption at Mistral already accounts for ~10% of payroll. Extrapolated across the European economy over three to four years, this reaches ~€1 trillion in annual AI spend. If Europe captures none of it, the continent's GDP composition shifts toward technology imports it does not control.

## Mistral's credentials in the hearing

Mensch positioned Mistral as the European existence proof:

- 1,000 employees
- €12B valuation (post September 2025 ASML round)
- €1B revenue target end-2026
- €1B R&D spend
- 75% European sales
- Less than 30% foreign capital ownership
- Major customers: CMA CGM, Stellantis, TotalEnergies, BNP Paribas, French armed forces

## Specific policy / regulatory points

- Criticized regulatory stacking — GDPR, AI Act, copyright law — as paradoxically slowing European players and favoring American giants who can absorb compliance costs.
- Pushed for European preference clauses in public procurement.
- On military use: "We can't let Mythos scan the source code of the French armed forces. That creates an irreparable dependency, we need to find a solution." (Mythos = unnamed US AI provider in the hearing.)
- "The army is sovereign, it has democratic legitimacy that we don't have." Framing: even sovereign defense customers cannot rely on foreign-controlled foundation models for code review.

## Strategic stance summarized by analysts

- Digital sovereignty ≠ autarky. Sovereignty = negotiating leverage. Without sovereign production capacity, Europe has nothing to put on the table opposite the US.
- The "two-year deadline" is not a doom prediction — it is a window during which European compute, training capacity, and downstream commercial deployment can still be built without subsidy beyond reach.
