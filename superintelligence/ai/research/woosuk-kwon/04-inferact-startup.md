## Product plan

Two-track strategy:
1. **Open-source vLLM continues as the engine.** Inferact provides financial and developer resources to expand vLLM's support for new model architectures, additional hardware platforms, and multi-node deployments.
2. **Commercial product = "universal inference layer."** A serverless / managed vLLM offering with observability, troubleshooting, disaster recovery, and Kubernetes integration. Designed to complement existing inference providers (Together, Anyscale, etc.) rather than compete head-to-head — the framing is that they "collaborate with existing providers."

## Existing users / install base context

a16z's announcement: vLLM runs on **400,000+ concurrent GPUs globally**. 2,000+ contributors. 50+ core devs. Used in production by Meta, Google, Amazon (AWS Bedrock), Character.ai, Roblox, LinkedIn, Spotify, Mistral AI, Cohere, IBM.

## Competitive landscape

- **SGLang** (also out of UC Berkeley, Ion Stoica's lab; commercialized as **RadixArk** at a ~$400M valuation in early 2026) — closest peer.
- **TensorRT-LLM** (NVIDIA-owned, NVIDIA-only).
- **TGI** (Hugging Face) — officially in maintenance mode; HF recommends vLLM or SGLang.
- **llama.cpp / Ollama** — edge-case inference, not the same enterprise lane.
