# Lilian Weng — Biography and Trajectory

## Identifying details

- **Real name:** Lilian Weng (Chinese: 翁丽莲)
- **Web identity:** Author of `lilianweng.github.io` (the "Lil'Log" blog), `@lilianweng` on X / Twitter, `lilianweng` on LinkedIn and GitHub
- **Domain identity:** Long-form technical surveyor of LLM, RL, and AI-safety topics; her blog posts function in the field as canonical reference texts that researchers cite the way they cite arXiv papers

## Education

- **PhD, Computer Science, Indiana University Bloomington (2014).** Dissertation: "Information diffusion on online social networks." Advisor: **Filippo Menczer** at the Center for Complex Networks and Systems Research. Source: The Mathematics Genealogy Project (id 288809), ResearchGate, and her own LinkedIn. The user-supplied hint of "NYU PhD" is incorrect; the verifiable record is Indiana University. Persona file reflects the corrected fact.
- Undergraduate work in computer science (multiple sources point to Hong Kong University of Science and Technology for undergrad, though this varies — kept implicit in the persona file because not all sources confirm).

## Professional trajectory

1. **Affirm (early career, ~2014–2017).** Applied ML and data science work on lending and fraud signals. Cited on her LinkedIn as one of the early stops.
2. **Dropbox (~2017–2018).** Applied ML and personalization. Brief stint before OpenAI.
3. **OpenAI (February 2018 – November 15, 2024). Seven years.** Her trajectory inside OpenAI moved up the safety ladder:
   - **Robotics team (2018).** Worked on robotic-hand manipulation problems (Rubik's Cube solving robotic hand, etc.). Pre-LLM era contributions to OpenAI's robotics line.
   - **Applied AI Research (2021).** Moved into the post-GPT-3 product-research surface.
   - **Head of Safety Systems (2023).** Built and led the Safety Systems team responsible for moderation, deployment safety, jailbreak resistance, evaluations, and the practical safety stack for shipped products like ChatGPT and the API.
   - **Vice President of Research and Safety (August 2024 – November 2024).** Elevated three months before her departure during the post-Mira-Murati internal restructuring.
4. **Departure announcement (November 8, 2024).** Posted resignation note to X (`@lilianweng/status/1855031273690984623`). Last day November 15, 2024. The departure landed in a cluster — Mira Murati had left in September 2024, Bob McGrew and Barret Zoph the same month, Andrej Karpathy and John Schulman in August 2024, Ilya Sutskever and Jan Leike in May 2024.
5. **Fellows Fund Distinguished Fellow (announced ~Q1 2025).** A short bridge engagement before the public Thinking Machines Lab unveil.
6. **Thinking Machines Lab co-founder (announced publicly February 18, 2025).** Founded by Mira Murati as CEO; founding leadership team included John Schulman (Chief Scientist), Barret Zoph (CTO at launch), Andrew Tulloch, Luke Metz, and Lilian Weng. Of the 29 employees announced at launch, at least seven were ex-OpenAI. Headquartered in San Francisco, structured as a Public Benefit Corporation.
7. **TML $2B seed round (July 2025).** Led by Andreessen Horowitz at a ~$12B valuation. Investors included NVIDIA, AMD, Cisco, Jane Street Capital, and the government of Albania. One of the largest seed rounds in tech history.
8. **TML Tinker launch (October 1, 2025).** First public product — an API for fine-tuning open-weight language models.
9. **Co-founder shuffle (January 14, 2026).** Barret Zoph and Luke Metz returned to OpenAI; Weng and Schulman remained as the senior research-leadership core under Murati. This made Weng the de facto safety leader of TML's research stack.
10. **NVIDIA partnership (March 2026).** TML announced a 1-gigawatt compute partnership with NVIDIA — a material capacity step for the lab's research roadmap.

Public sources confirm Weng is a **co-founder of Thinking Machines Lab**. The user-supplied hint described her as "Chief Safety Officer" — this is consistent with her OpenAI background and TML's research focus, but it is not the title used in current public TML announcements, which describe her as "co-founder" and former OpenAI VP. The persona records this with conservative phrasing: "Co-founder, Thinking Machines Lab (safety research lead)." Confidence on the exact title is 0.85; confidence on the co-founder fact is 1.00.

## Why the trajectory matters for the persona

- Weng is one of a small number of senior safety leaders who left OpenAI in 2024 explicitly during the broader cultural fracture about how the company was weighing safety against shipping. Her departure is paired in the public narrative with Mira Murati's, Jan Leike's, and Ilya Sutskever's.
- Her seven-year arc inside OpenAI gives her ground-truth experience with the productization of safety — moderation systems, jailbreak resistance, the Preparedness Framework, GPT-4o and o1 system cards — that pure-research safety leaders (Christiano, Hubinger) do not have.
- The TML founding pattern (sister-shop to the post-OpenAI safety alumni: Anthropic for some, TML for others) places her in a productive dialogue position with Anthropic Alignment Science (Leike, Christiano) without being a direct competitor for the same alignment-research slot.
