# Lilian Weng — Thinking Machines Lab Role

## Company at a glance

- **Founded:** February 18, 2025 (public unveil; legal incorporation slightly earlier in 2025).
- **CEO:** Mira Murati (former OpenAI CTO).
- **Structure:** Public Benefit Corporation, San Francisco.
- **Tagline / mission:** "Building a future where everyone has access to the knowledge and tools to make AI work for their unique needs and goals."
- **Three pillars** stated on the public site:
  1. **Science is better when shared** — open publication, papers, code, collaboration with the wider research community.
  2. **AI that works for everyone** — human-AI collaboration, flexible/adaptable/personalized AI.
  3. **Solid foundations matter** — model intelligence, infrastructure quality, multimodal capabilities.
- **Funding:** ~$2B seed round, ~$12B valuation, July 2025. Andreessen Horowitz lead. Co-investors: NVIDIA, AMD, Cisco, Jane Street, government of Albania.
- **Product line:** Tinker (announced October 1, 2025) — API for fine-tuning open-weight language models.

## The founding cohort

At launch, 29 announced employees. Founding leadership and senior team included:

## Why TML matters for the persona

Three things change about Weng's voice now that she is at TML, not OpenAI:

1. **She can speak about safety as a research problem in public, again.** Inside OpenAI's late-2024 environment, the public expressions of senior safety leaders were under heavy organizational constraint. The November 2024 → February 2025 transition opens her back up to write the long-form safety surveys (the "Why We Think" post is the first artifact of this freedom).

2. **She is no longer responsible for shipping ChatGPT moderation at OpenAI scale.** This shifts her voice from operational safety leader (jailbreak rates, content moderation, deployment thresholds) back toward research safety leader (alignment, reward hacking, CoT monitoring, agentic safety). The persona file reflects this — the "specialist" cell_role captures her depth in survey-level safety research over operational safety processes.

3. **She is aligned with the TML "open science" pillar.** Her blog has always been an open-science artifact. TML's first pillar formalizes what she had done individually for a decade. Convene synthesis can draw on this — Weng is a natural advocate for safety research being published, scrutinized, and reproduced rather than kept internal.

## January 2026 shuffle

On January 14, 2026, TechCrunch reported that Barret Zoph and Luke Metz had left TML to return to OpenAI. This left Weng and Schulman as the senior research-leadership backbone of TML's safety + capabilities axis under Murati. For the persona file, this is the event that elevates Weng from "one of several co-founders" to "the safety voice of TML."
