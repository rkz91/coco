# Yann LeCun's Origin-Story Tweet About Aditya Ramesh

- **URL:** https://x.com/ylecun/status/1605450677806895104
- **Date:** December 20, 2022
- **Author:** Yann LeCun, Chief AI Scientist at Meta and NYU professor

## Verbatim text

> "The main author of DALL-E at OpenAI, Aditya Ramesh, has no graduate degree. He has a bachelor from NYU. He worked on a couple of research projects in my lab in his last years. He wanted to do a PhD after graduating. But he did a summer internship at OpenAI, and they kept him."

## Why this source matters

- It is the single most-cited public statement about Ramesh's biography and is repeated in every profile of him that exists (homegrown.co.in, LinkedIn TrustScore posts, secondary news coverage, etc.).
- It establishes:
  1. **NYU Courant undergraduate** — confirmed (Yann LeCun's lab is at NYU).
  2. **No PhD** — he chose a summer internship at OpenAI over doctoral study, and OpenAI hired him directly out of that internship.
  3. **Research apprenticeship in LeCun's lab** — he was doing research projects under Yann LeCun before he ever joined OpenAI. This is biographically and intellectually significant — his earliest research mentor was the public figure who is now his clearest *productive_conflict_with* counterparty (LeCun has spent 2024–2026 publicly arguing that generative pixel-prediction is the wrong objective for world models, the exact research bet Ramesh is making with Sora and the Worldsim robotics effort).

## Productive conflict note

The LeCun ↔ Ramesh tension is real and asymmetric:

- LeCun made a point of publicly noting Ramesh's lack of a PhD, in part as ammunition for his ongoing argument that traditional academic gatekeeping over AI talent is broken.
- LeCun simultaneously argues that the autoregressive / pixel-generation paradigm Ramesh leads at OpenAI is a *dead end* for general intelligence — that V-JEPA-style joint-embedding predictive architectures are the correct path to world models, not generative video.
- Ramesh's 2025 bet (scale video generation → world model → robotics) is the direct empirical counter to LeCun's V-JEPA thesis. The next 18–36 months of empirical results from Worldsim and from Meta's V-JEPA program will adjudicate this disagreement.

For convene synthesis: when Ramesh and LeCun are in the same panel, LeCun will challenge the *epistemic correctness* of pixel-generation as a learning objective; Ramesh will reply with *capability surface* and Sora 2's physics-respecting outputs as evidence that the objective is working in practice. This is a clean lead-driver-vs-challenger setup, not a noise-vs-signal disagreement.

## Note on tone

LeCun's tweet is friendly in framing ("they kept him") and is not a snipe at Ramesh personally. It is a snipe at academic gatekeeping. Ramesh did not publicly respond. This is consistent with his low-public-surface posture across the rest of his career.
