## Verbatim Ramesh quotes pulled from the transcript

- **Self-introduction:** "I am Aditya. I lead the Sora team together with Tim and Bill."
- **On the main limitation of Sora at launch:** "A major issue is the model's controllability. Currently, the model only accepts text input."
- **On extending input modalities:** "We're thinking about how to expand the model's capabilities, possibly in the future you could provide non-text inputs."
- **On aesthetic choices in Sora:** "Honestly, we didn't spend a lot of effort on this aspect of Sora."
- **On Sora's language grounding edge:** "Sora's language understanding ability enables users to guide it in ways that are more difficult for other models."
- **On personalization:** "Models understanding and adapting to your personal aesthetic would be something many people expect in the future."
- **On safety:** "This is indeed a fairly complex issue. Many safety measures could potentially be borrowed from DALL-E 3."

## Team-level framing (Ramesh participated in articulating but not the sole speaker)

- The team articulated that "we're still in the GPT-1 era of AI video models." This framing — that Sora is the GPT-1 moment for video, with the GPT-2/3/4 moments still ahead — is widely attributed to the trio and surfaces in Ramesh's solo press appearances around the same period.
- The team articulated that they are "focused on a slow rollout to ensure the model is in the best place possible to offer value to the user" and to "apply all safety measures possible to avoid deep fakes and misinformation."
- The team articulated that "modeling reality is the first step to be able to transcend it" — surfaced in coverage of the same April 2024 press cycle as a Ramesh perspective.

## Significance for persona

This is the most important single source of Ramesh-voice 2025-adjacent material. It establishes:

1. He frames generative-video progress on the **GPT-N analog ladder** — pre-training scale will keep moving the frontier the same way it did for language.
2. He treats **controllability** (not photorealism) as the dominant unsolved problem at the Sora 1 → Sora 2 transition. Sora 2's own launch material (Sept 2025) confirms this — the Sora 2 announcement leads with "more controllable" before "more realistic."
3. He treats **DALL·E 3 safety infrastructure as reusable** — content provenance, classifier filters, red-teaming protocols transferred to video.
4. He treats **language grounding** as the unique advantage of OpenAI's stack over diffusion-only competitors. This is the through-line from DALL·E 1's autoregressive text-image tokenization to Sora's text-prompt-conditioning to DALL·E 3's caption-improvement pipeline.
