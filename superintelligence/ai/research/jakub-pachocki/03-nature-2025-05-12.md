# Nature News Q&A — Jakub Pachocki, OpenAI Chief Scientist

Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-01485-2
Published: 2025-05-12
Interviewer: Elizabeth Gibney
Title: "'AI models are capable of novel research': OpenAI's chief scientist on what to expect"

## Direct quotes

### On reasoning

- "I would say it is a form of reasoning, but that doesn't mean it's the same as how humans reason."
- "Reasoning models don't learn how to think in a vacuum, they are rooted in a model that has learned through pre-training."

### On novel insights

- "I definitely believe we have significant evidence that the models are capable of discovering novel insights."

### On AGI definition and timelines

- "This for me is closest to what I previously emotionally thought of as AGI."
- Expects "substantial progress" in autonomous AI research by end of decade.
- First practical applications (such as autonomous software creation) may arrive in 2025.

## Editorial framings

- Pachocki sees the next major step toward AGI as economic: AI models that can deliver commercial results and conduct autonomous research.
- The article is part of Nature's News Q&A series for 2025.
