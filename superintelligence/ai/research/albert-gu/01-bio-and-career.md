## Education

## Career timeline

- **~2018–2023:** PhD student at Stanford under Christopher Ré, working in the Hazy Research lab alongside Karan Goel, Tri Dao, Dan Fu, and others. Published the HiPPO / S4 / S5 / H3 / Mamba sequence of papers during this period.
- **August 2023 → present:** Assistant Professor of Machine Learning at Carnegie Mellon University (joined Fall 2023). Director of the Goomba Lab (https://goombalab.github.io/). Five PhD students and at least one MS student as of 2026 (Kevin Li, Aakash Lahoti, Sukjun Hwang, Aviv Bick, Isaac Liao; Ricardo Buitrago as affiliated MS).
- **September 2023 → present:** Co-Founder and Chief Scientist of **Cartesia AI** (cartesia.ai). Cartesia was founded the same month Gu joined CMU. Co-founders are Karan Goel (CEO), Arjun Desai, Brandon Yang, and faculty advisor Christopher Ré.
- **Ongoing concurrent role:** CMU + Cartesia is a parallel arrangement, not a sequential transition. The CMU group does long-arc research (architecture, theory, tokenization-free models); the Cartesia work productizes SSM for voice and real-time audio.

## Why his career arc matters for the persona

Gu is the rare researcher who keeps one foot squarely in foundational architecture work at CMU and the other in a production-scale company that bets the runway on a non-Transformer architecture. The Stanford → CMU → Cartesia arc is not a pivot story; it is a sustained, deliberate argument that **state space models are a viable, complementary substrate to attention** — and that voice and audio are the modalities where the argument lands soonest because latency is the hard constraint. He is also a credible advocate for tokenization-free architectures via the 2025 H-Net work, which means his frame is not "SSM instead of Transformer" but "rethink the whole compression stack from raw bytes up."
