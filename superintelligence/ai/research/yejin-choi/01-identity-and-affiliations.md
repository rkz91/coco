## Current affiliations (as of 2026-05-28)

Verified across the Stanford HAI announcement (BusinessWire, January 2025), her personal website at `yejinc.github.io`, and the NVIDIA research profile:

1. **Stanford University** — Dieter Schwarz Foundation Professor and Senior Fellow at the Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI), Professor of Computer Science. Joined in 2024–2025. Office: Gates 362.
2. **NVIDIA** — Distinguished Research Scientist, Language and Cognition Research. Concurrent appointment alongside Stanford, working on large language models, large reasoning models, and alternative architectures.

Note on the "Wei-Tao Chair" hint in the user prompt: the verified Stanford HAI announcement and her personal page list her chair as the **Dieter Schwarz Foundation Professor at HAI**, not the Wei-Tao Chair. I am using the verified title.

## Past affiliations

- **University of Washington, Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering** — Wissner-Slivka Professor of Computer Science (the modern name for her endowed chair, sometimes referred to in older coverage as the Brett Helsel Professorship). Faculty member from 2014 until her move to Stanford. Chair of CS in 2023.
- **Allen Institute for AI (AI2)** — Senior research manager and Mosaic team lead (joined 2018 as senior research manager while concurrently at UW).
- **Stony Brook University** — Assistant Professor (her first faculty role), where she developed techniques for identifying deceptive online reviews.
- **Cornell University** — PhD in Computer Science, 2010, advised by Claire Cardie. Dissertation: "Fine-grained opinion analysis: structure-aware approaches."
- **Seoul National University** — BS, Computer Science.

## Honors

- **MacArthur Fellowship ("genius grant")** — 2022, $800,000 stipend. Citation: natural language processing systems that can perform commonsense reasoning.
- **AI2050 Senior Fellow** — Schmidt Sciences, 2024. Project: pluralistic alignment.
- **ACL Fellow** — 2022, for contributions to natural language generation, language and vision, and commonsense reasoning.
- **TIME 100 Most Influential People in AI** — 2023 and 2025.
- **Distinguished Research Fellow** — Institute for Ethics in AI at Oxford, 2023.
- **Test-of-Time awards** — at least two (ACL and CVPR confirmed via her Stanford profile).
- **Best / Outstanding Paper Awards** — 8 across ACL, EMNLP, NAACL, ICML, NeurIPS, AAAI. Including ACL 2025 Outstanding Paper (HALoGEN), NeurIPS 2025 Best Paper, EMNLP 2025 Best Paper.
