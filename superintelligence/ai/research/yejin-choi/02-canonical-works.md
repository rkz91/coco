# Yejin Choi — Canonical Works

The works that anchor her intellectual identity, in roughly chronological order of impact.

## Foundational commonsense knowledge graphs

### ATOMIC: An Atlas of Machine Commonsense for If-Then Reasoning (AAAI 2019)

The if-then commonsense knowledge graph. Built on crowdsourced labels covering nine relation types ("X intends", "X needs", "X is seen as", etc.). The substrate for everything she built after on commonsense.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1811.00146
- Project: https://homes.cs.washington.edu/~msap/atomic/

### COMET: Commonsense Transformers (ACL 2019)

Trained a transformer to generate ATOMIC-style commonsense tuples. Bridges symbolic commonsense knowledge bases and neural language models. The result that turned "knowledge graph" from a closed list of facts into a generative process.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.05317

### COMET-ATOMIC 2020 (AAAI 2021)

The scaled, refined version. Combined a much larger ATOMIC-2020 KB with a more capable transformer. Demonstrated that neural models can be reliable producers of commonsense knowledge — a key result for the "small models with right inductive biases" thesis.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.05953

## Generation and decoding

### The Curious Case of Neural Text Degeneration (ICLR 2020)

The nucleus sampling paper, with Ari Holtzman, Jan Buys, Maxwell Forbes. Introduced top-p sampling — the decoding strategy now standard in every production LLM, from GPT to Claude to Gemini. Probably her most operationally influential paper.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.09751

### Defending Against Neural Fake News (NeurIPS 2019)

The Grover system, with Rowan Zellers et al. Built a generator of fake news to study how to detect generated fake news. A canonical pre-ChatGPT result on the dual-use problem.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.12616

## Benchmarks

### RAINBOW: A Universal Commonsense Reasoning Benchmark (AAAI 2021)

A unified suite of commonsense reasoning benchmarks. The exemplar of her "benchmarks should test reasoning, not pattern matching" stance.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.13009

### HellaSwag (ACL 2019)

Adversarial commonsense inference benchmark. Used as one of the standard pretraining-evaluation benchmarks for every subsequent LLM. With Rowan Zellers.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.07830

## Multimodal

### MERLOT and MERLOT Reserve (NeurIPS 2021, CVPR 2022)

Multimodal reasoning over video + text. With Rowan Zellers. Won NeurIPS 2021 Outstanding Paper. The line of work that made her one of the early voices arguing that vision-language grounding was necessary for commonsense.

- MERLOT arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.02636
- MERLOT Reserve arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.02639

## Position papers

### A Roadmap to Pluralistic Alignment (2024)

With Taylor Sorensen and others. The agenda-setting paper for her current alignment program. Argues that "alignment with human values" is incoherent because humans don't agree on values; we need pluralistic alignment instead.

- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.05070

## Talks

### TED 2023 — "Why AI is incredibly smart and shockingly stupid"

Her highest-reach public talk. Three problems with LLMs (extreme scale concentrating power, opacity blocking safety, environmental cost) plus the "shockingly stupid" examples (clothes drying, water jug, bridge bicycle). Concludes with the moon-from-tallest-building line.

- TED page: https://www.ted.com/talks/yejin_choi_why_ai_is_incredibly_smart_and_shockingly_stupid
- Transcript mirror: https://singjupost.com/why-ai-is-incredibly-smart-and-shockingly-stupid-yejin-choi-transcript/

### KDD-style ACL keynote — "2082: An ACL Odyssey: The Dark Matter of Language and Intelligence"

Her ACL keynote framing common sense as the dark matter of intelligence — present everywhere, invisible to most measurement instruments.

### NeurIPS 2025 — "The Art of (Artificial) Reasoning"

December 4, 2025. Argues that current scaling has limits in sustainability and robustness, that arxiv growth has outrun scientific understanding, and that small reasoning models can compete with large ones in specific domains.

- NeurIPS page: https://neurips.cc/virtual/2025/invited-talk/109603

### Schwartz Reisman 2025 — "The enigma of LLMs: On creativity, pluralism, and paradoxes"

February 26, 2025. Introduces the **Generative AI Paradox**: LLMs may excel at generation while struggling with comprehension — the inverse of humans, for whom generation is harder than understanding.

- SRI page: https://srinstitute.utoronto.ca/events-archive/seminar-2025-yejin-choi

## Books

### "Open Book" — popular-press book (in development as of 2025)

Mentioned in interviews; framed as her public-facing argument about what LLMs are and what they aren't. Treat as forthcoming, not yet citable.
