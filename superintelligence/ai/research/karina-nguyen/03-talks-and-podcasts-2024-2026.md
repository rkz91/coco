# Karina Nguyen — talks and podcasts timeline

Pulled from karinanguyen.com/talks/ and supplementary search on 2026-05-27. Source for `recent_signal_12mo` entries — note that the constraint requires signals dated AFTER 2025-05-27, so anything before that date is included only for context.

## 2025 (mid-year and later)

- **IEEE International Conference on LLM-Aided Design (ICLAD), Stanford** — invited talk: "Building Useful Agents via Reinforcement Learning." **June 26–27, 2025.** This is the first qualifying recent_signal after the 2025-05-27 cutoff. Frames RL as the construction technique for agent capabilities (tool use, long-horizon work) rather than as an alignment afterthought. URL: https://iclad.ai/.
- **SHV Speaker Series** — "The Art of RL." May 22, 2025 (slightly pre-cutoff, but the talk title became one of her most-quoted framings throughout the rest of 2025). She argues RL pipeline design is closer to a craft than an algorithm — co-host LukeW summary: "RL is shaping up to be the most consequential phase of post-training and the part that benefits the most from taste." URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lukew_our-next-ai-speaker-series-event-is-with-activity-7331767429536137216-0DI5/.

## 2026

- **"Things I learned at OpenAI"** essay on sémaphore — **March 28, 2026.** The 2026 capstone reflection. Core claims: post-training data mixtures are closer to art than engineering; the product–data–model feedback loop is the unit of progress; greater capability correlates with better alignment because more sophisticated models can reason about deception's long-term consequences; the most durable researcher skill is "the ability to repeatedly identify what matters now." URL: https://semaphore.substack.com/p/things-i-learned-at-openai.

## Recurring framings across these appearances

- "RL is shaping up to be the most consequential phase of post-training and the part that benefits the most from taste." (SHV; reused on Lenny's; reused at CS25.)
- "Co-design loop" — RL research and product design co-evolve; you do not pick one and then the other.
- "Designing post-training data mixtures is closer to art than engineering."
- "The product shapes the data, the data shapes the model, the model shapes what the product can become."
- "You will end up with 70 models and every model will have its own brain damage." (Latent Space; reused in Lenny's.) She uses "brain damage" deliberately, as a software-engineering bug metaphor.
- "Honesty versus harmlessness is more art than science." (Latent Space, on Claude 3.)
- "We are bottlenecked by human creativity." (Latent Space.)
- "Things like Canvas and Tasks should and could have happened like two years ago." (Latent Space.)
