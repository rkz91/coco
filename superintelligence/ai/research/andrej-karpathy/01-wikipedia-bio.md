# Karpathy — Wikipedia Biography (extracted 2026-05-27)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrej_Karpathy

## Identity

- **Full name:** Andrej Karpathy
- **Born:** October 23, 1986, Bratislava, Czechoslovakia (now Slovakia)
- **Nationality:** Slovak-Canadian; moved to Toronto at age 15

## Education

- **BS** Computer Science & Physics — University of Toronto (2009)
- **MS** — University of British Columbia (2011)
- **PhD** — Stanford University (2015), advised by Fei-Fei Li

## Career timeline

| Years | Role | Organization |
|---|---|---|
| 2015–2017 | Founding member, research scientist (deep learning, computer vision) | OpenAI |
| 2017–2022 | Director of AI and Tesla Autopilot Vision (reporting to Elon Musk) | Tesla |
| 2023–2024 | Returned to research (midtraining, synthetic data generation) | OpenAI |
| 2024–present | Founder, AI education startup | Eureka Labs |
| May 2026–present | Pretraining research team lead | Anthropic |

**Note 2026-05-27:** As of May 19, 2026, Karpathy joined Anthropic to lead a pretraining research team. Eureka Labs remains his independent education venture (LLM101n course, "Zero to Hero" series).

## Stanford CS 231n

Primary instructor of Stanford's CS 231n (Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition). The course grew from 150 to 750 students under his tenure and became one of Stanford's most popular CS classes.

## Recognition

- MIT Technology Review's Innovators Under 35 (2020)
- TIME100 Most Influential People in AI (2024)

## Notable coinages

- **"Software 2.0"** (2017 Medium essay) — neural network weights as a new kind of software.
- **"Vibe coding"** (February 2025) — using AI tools to build applications via natural language prompts without traditional coding expertise.
- **"Software 3.0"** (YC AI Startup School 2025) — LLM-orchestrated systems where "prompts are now programs."
- **"Summoning ghosts not building animals"** (Dwarkesh Podcast, Oct 17, 2025) — contrast between AI development via internet imitation vs biological evolution.
- **"Jagged Intelligence"** (2025) — LLMs' uneven capability distribution: genius in some domains, catastrophic failure in others, and it's not obvious which is which.
