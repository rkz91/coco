# Karpathy — Dwarkesh Podcast (October 17, 2025)

Source: https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/andrej-karpathy

Episode title: "Andrej Karpathy — AGI is still a decade away"
Duration: 2h 25m

## Verbatim quotes

### On reinforcement learning

1. **"Reinforcement learning is terrible. It just so happens that everything else is much worse."**

2. **"You're sucking supervision through a straw."**
   — On how RL compresses an entire trajectory into a single final reward signal, losing intermediate information.

3. **"Every single token gets upweighted like 'Do more of this.'"**
   — On the noisy nature of outcome-based reward, where incorrect intermediate steps are reinforced if the trajectory reaches the right answer.

### On the AGI timeline

4. **"It will take about a decade to work through all of those issues."**
   — Continual learning, multimodality, computer use, and other cognitive deficits.

5. **"The problems are tractable, but they're still difficult."**
   — On why a decade rather than 30 years or 6 months. Based on 15 years of field experience watching prediction accuracy.

### On the nature of LLMs

6. **"We're not building animals. We're building ghosts or spirits."**
   — Contrasting AI development via internet-data imitation vs evolutionary biology.

7. **"Pre-training is this crappy evolution."**
   — Pre-training as a practical-but-poor substitute for evolutionary bootstrapping.

### On model collapse

8. **"The models are silently collapsed. They have a collapsed data distribution."**

9. **"If you ask it 10 times, you'll notice that all of them are the same."**
   — Demonstrating model collapse: ask an LLM to reflect on a book chapter ten times, get ten near-identical responses.

### On current AI capabilities

10. **"The models have so many cognitive deficits."**
    — In the context of discussing his nanochat work and how SOTA agents struggled with the repository.

11. **"Coding models were of very little help...they're slop."**
    — His direct experience using SOTA LLMs for novel, non-boilerplate code in nanochat.

### On AGI and economic integration

12. **"AGI will blend into the previous ~2.5 centuries of 2% GDP growth."**
    — Arguing AI is continuous automation, not a discrete event detectable in GDP data.

## Adjacent themes

- Why model collapse prevents LLMs from learning the way humans do
- Why self-driving took so long to crack
- The future of education (his focus at Eureka Labs)
- Continual learning as the missing piece for true agency
