## Education

- **High school:** The Abelard School, Toronto. Graduated 2010 as a National AP Scholar.
- **University:** None completed. Left university at age 18 without a degree — a fact he is publicly known for and that has become part of his self-narrative in the ML community.

## Notable research artifacts

- **Distill journal** — co-founded with Shan Carter; pioneered interactive, visually rigorous publication standard for ML research.
- **DeepDream (2015)** — second author on the original Google Brain release that visualized convolutional network features by gradient ascent in the input space.
- **Activation Atlases (OpenAI)** — high-level interpretability tooling for inspecting feature representations across networks.
- **"Zoom In: An Introduction to Circuits"** (Distill, 2020) — the foundational essay arguing that neural networks can be broken into understandable circuits.
- **"A Mathematical Framework for Transformer Circuits"** (Anthropic, 2021) — first principled mechanistic decomposition of attention.
- **"Toy Models of Superposition"** (Elhage, Hume, Olsson, Olah et al., 2022) — established superposition as the central obstacle to interpretability.
- **"Towards Monosemanticity"** (Bricken, Templeton, Batson, et al., 2023) — sparse autoencoders disentangle superposed features.
- **"Scaling Monosemanticity"** (Templeton et al., 2024) — 34M features extracted from Claude 3 Sonnet via SAEs.
- **"On the Biology of a Large Language Model"** (Lindsey, Gurnee, Ameisen et al. with Olah as senior co-author, March 2025) — attribution graphs and cross-layer transcoders on Claude 3.5 Haiku.

- **July 2025:** Authored "A Mathematical Framework for Transformer Circuits" follow-up note on the July 2025 Circuits Update, reframing earlier eigenvalue-based formalism in terms of features.
- **October 2025:** 80,000 Hours podcast appearance on what's going on inside neural networks (note: 80k Hours episode framed as a deep interview around feature/circuit progress).

Spoke as Anthropic co-founder about the mysteriousness of model internals, the limits of training-side control, and the necessity of external (religious, governmental, civil-society) oversight of frontier AI.
