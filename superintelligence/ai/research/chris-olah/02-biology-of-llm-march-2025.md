## Core technique

- **Cross-Layer Transcoders (CLT):** Replace MLP blocks with sparse, interpretable substitute components that act as a "wiring diagram" for the network's computation.
- **Attribution graphs:** Show feature-to-feature interactions as nodes and edges; map computational paths step by step across layers.
- **Model:** Applied to Claude 3.5 Haiku in production.

## Behaviors investigated

- Two-hop reasoning ("the capital of the state Dallas is in")
- Planning in poem composition (pre-selecting rhymes before completing the line)
- Multilingual transfer and "language-of-thought" hypothesis
- Arithmetic addition across contexts
- Medical diagnostic reasoning
- Entity recognition vs hallucination triggers
- Refusal mechanisms for harmful requests
- Jailbreak vulnerability
- Chain-of-thought faithfulness (whether the rationale actually reflects internal computation)
- Models with hidden goals (hidden-reward backdoor analysis)

## Framing (paper's own language)

"Language models are generated by simple, human-designed training algorithms, [yet] the mechanisms born of these algorithms appear to be quite complex." The title's biology metaphor is deliberate — the team treats the trained model as a complex grown system to be reverse-engineered, not a designed program to be read.

## Why this matters for Olah's profile

- This is the **2025 capstone** of his thesis that "interpretability is now primarily an engineering problem, not a fundamental one" (carrying forward his Oct 2023 tweet at https://x.com/ch402/status/1709998674087227859).
- The senior-author position (last) on a 25+ author paper signals lead-driver status on the research direction without being the day-to-day implementer.
- It is the direct successor to "Scaling Monosemanticity" (2024) — that paper showed you could find features; this paper shows you can read circuits built from them.
- It is also the basis of the Vatican framing one year later — when Olah said models contain "internal states that functionally mirror joy, satisfaction, fear, grief, and unease," he was referencing the kind of features the Biology paper made it possible to identify.
