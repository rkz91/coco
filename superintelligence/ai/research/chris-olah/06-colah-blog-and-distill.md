## colah.github.io — personal blog

Olah's personal blog is one of the most-cited educational resources in the entire deep-learning community. The blog posts are visually rigorous, written for technically literate non-specialists, and are a primary teaching artifact in many graduate-level deep-learning courses.

### Self-description

> "I work on reverse engineering artificial neural networks into human understandable algorithms."

This is the one-liner he uses across his About page, conference bios, and TIME100 profile. It is the most concise statement of his research identity.

### Style observation

Across the blog, Olah's tone is **patient, geometric, and aesthetic-first**. He treats neural networks as objects of natural beauty whose internal structure can be uncovered through careful visualization. This is the rhetorical inheritance behind his Vatican framing of models as "grown" and "mysterious" — the same sensibility, applied at a different register.

## Distill — co-founded 2017

- **Co-founders:** Chris Olah and Shan Carter.
- **Editorial mission:** Publish interactive, visually-rigorous ML research. Treat clarity of explanation as a first-class research contribution, not a downstream activity.
- **Operational status:** Distill went on hiatus in 2021 (the journal published its final regular issue and announced it would not continue accepting new submissions in the same form). Olah's interpretability team continues to publish in the same visual style on **transformer-circuits.pub**, which is the spiritual successor to Distill.

## Why this matters for the persona

- Establishes **"visualization is research, not just communication"** as a documented signature framing — the entire Distill thesis.
- Provides the canonical-works for the YAML — Olah has many more highly-cited blog posts than papers, which is rare for a researcher of his rank and is a deliberate choice consistent with his pedagogical contract.
- The 2015 "Calculus on Computational Graphs" and "Understanding LSTM Networks" posts are still the most common entry points to deep learning for graduate students a decade later — a longevity datapoint that few research blogs match.
