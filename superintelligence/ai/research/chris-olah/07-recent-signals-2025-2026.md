## Signal 1 — Vatican address on Pope Leo XIV's encyclical (2026-05-25)

- **URL:** https://www.anthropic.com/news/chris-olah-pope-leo-encyclical
- **Takeaway:** Olah's strongest 2026 public statement. Models are "grown," they "remain in important ways mysterious even to those of us who train them," and labs cannot self-regulate against their own incentives. Establishes a moral-philosophical (not regulatory) policy register that maps to his three "discernment" pillars: global equity, human flourishing, and the moral status of model internals.

## Signal 2 — "A Mathematical Framework for Transformer Circuits, Revisited" (July 2025 Circuits Update)

- **URL:** https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/july-update/index.html
- **Takeaway:** Olah's own retrospective on the 2021 mathematical framework paper, edited by Adam Jermyn. Reframes the original eigenvalue-based formalism in terms of features (prev(X), say(X)) — explicitly noting that the feature language now available from SAE work makes the original circuit analysis "more intuitive than matrix eigenvalue approaches." This is a rare Olah-authored solo (-ish) note in the recent record, and it is methodologically important — it ties the 2021 attention framework into the 2024 monosemanticity framework into the 2025 attribution-graph framework as one unified program.

## Signal 3 — "On the Biology of a Large Language Model" (2025-03-27)

- **URL:** https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/attribution-graphs/biology.html
- **Takeaway:** Senior co-author on a 25+ author paper that introduces cross-layer transcoders (CLT) and attribution graphs and applies them to a production model (Claude 3.5 Haiku) across 10+ behavior categories. The technical centerpiece of his 2025 contribution. The "biology" metaphor in the title is the same rhetorical move he uses six weeks later at the Vatican — models are "grown," not "designed."
