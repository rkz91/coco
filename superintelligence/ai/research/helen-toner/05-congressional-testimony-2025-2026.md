## April 22, 2026 — Senate Judiciary Committee

**Hearing Title:** "Stealth Stealing: China's Ongoing Theft of U.S. Innovation"
**Toner's Role:** Witness, in her capacity as CSET Interim Executive Director

### Focus

Distillation attacks — the technique of training a Chinese model on outputs from an American frontier model — as an under-recognized IP and national security concern in the US-China AI competition.

### Key Quotes from Written Testimony

> "There is strong evidence that some Chinese AI companies are employing distillation techniques in order to use American AI models to advance their own research and development."

> "Efforts to counter distillation should focus on broader measures that are also helpful in countering other threats, such as sharing threat intelligence and assisting companies in monitoring for misuse of their models."

> "The winner of any AI race between the U.S. and China is the AI. We need to be working to make sure that is not the case."

### Policy Recommendations

- Threat intelligence sharing between government and frontier labs
- Model misuse monitoring infrastructure
- Counterintelligence measures that serve multiple security purposes
- Resist framings of US-China AI competition that treat winning as the only goal — the goal is safe and beneficial AI under either flag

### Significance

This testimony is the cleanest 2026 articulation of Toner's China-AI strategic posture: she takes the threat seriously, she is not naive about industrial espionage, but she refuses to accept "we must win at any cost" as the policy frame. The AI itself is the agent that wins or loses, not the country deploying it.

## May 2025 — House Judiciary Subcommittee

**Subcommittee:** Courts, Intellectual Property, Artificial Intelligence, and the Internet
**CSET URL:** https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/testimony-before-the-u-s-house-committee-on-the-judiciary-subcommittee-on-courts-intellectual-property-artificial-intelligence-and-the-internet/

### Focus

AI policy generally, with emphasis on the structural disclosure gaps that prevent policymakers from understanding what frontier models can do.

## Pattern Across Testimonies

Toner uses congressional testimony as a vector for the same policy program articulated in her writing:

1. Measurement and disclosure norms
2. Independent auditing
3. Rejection of US-China zero-sum framings
4. Targeted counter-misuse infrastructure rather than blanket nonproliferation

She is treated by both parties as a credible technical witness on AI governance — a credibility partly earned by her direct experience at OpenAI as a board member.
