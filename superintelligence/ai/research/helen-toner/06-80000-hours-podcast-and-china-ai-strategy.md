## Themes and Direct Stances

### Export Controls — Coherent vs. Confused Policy

Toner draws a sharp distinction within the export controls portfolio:

- **Semiconductor manufacturing equipment (SME) controls** — sound. Targets genuine chokepoints (EUV lithography from ASML). These are durable and strategically coherent.
- **Chip controls themselves** — confused. The policy goal has never been articulated clearly.

> "There's been very little clarity... on what the goal of these export controls is."

This is a notable position because it cuts across the dominant Washington narrative that frames all chip controls as equivalent. Toner is willing to defend SME controls and criticize chip controls in the same sentence.

### China's AGI Posture

Toner explicitly rejects the framing that China is racing toward AGI in the way US frontier labs are. She acknowledges DeepSeek and similar Chinese players are pursuing scaling, but argues that Chinese leadership does not share Silicon Valley's conviction about imminent AGI. The current US lead in frontier models is roughly nine months.

### US-China Diplomatic Stalemate

> The US and China "are barely talking at all" on AI governance.

China weaponizes dialogue access as a bargaining chip. Without shared understanding of AGI feasibility and timeline, substantive negotiation is nearly impossible.

### Against Non-Proliferation Framing (consistent with her April 2025 Substack essay)

Toner advocates **adaptation buffers** over nonproliferation. Build societal resilience before dangerous capabilities spread, rather than attempt impossible prevention.

### Power Concentration Dilemma

She rejects both extremes:

- Concentrating AI development in a few labs ignores the historical dangers of unchecked power
- Maximum diffusion enables misuse at scale

She acknowledges this honestly: "I don't really know" how to resolve the tension.

This intellectual honesty — willingness to articulate a dilemma she cannot solve — is a recurring voice characteristic.

### "Steerability" Over "Alignment"

Linguistic claim: "alignment" misleadingly implies a singular objective. "Steerability" better captures the technical challenge — reliably directing AI systems toward whatever target a legitimate user picks.

### Gulf Data Centre Concerns

Strong criticism of agreements that provide autocratic governments with "world-class supercomputers." Cites UAE's 18/100 Freedom House democracy score, banned political parties, mass dissident trials, and persecution of critics' families. Rejects the "if we don't sell it, China will" argument as factually incorrect given China's production constraints.
