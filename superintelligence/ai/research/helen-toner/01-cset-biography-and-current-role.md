## Current Role (as of 2025-2026)

**Title:** Interim Executive Director, Center for Security and Emerging Technology (CSET) at Georgetown University's Walsh School of Foreign Service.

She stepped into the executive director role in September 2025, leading the roughly 50-person research organization. Prior to that, she was Director of Strategy and Foundational Research Grants at CSET (promoted March 2022), and Director of Strategy from January 2019.

## Career Path

- **Born:** 1992 in Melbourne, Australia, to two physicians.
- **Education:**
  - B.Sc. in Chemical Engineering, University of Melbourne (graduated 2014)
  - Diploma in Languages, University of Melbourne (concurrent)
  - M.A. in Security Studies, Georgetown University's Walsh School of Foreign Service (2019-2021)
- **Open Philanthropy** (2015-2019): Senior Research Analyst. Advised policymakers on AI policy, military applications of AI, and the geopolitics of AI development. Recommended the $1.5M grant to UCLA Law for AI governance research (May 2017) and the $55M founding grant to Georgetown that established CSET.
- **Beijing residency** (2018): Nine months as a Research Affiliate with Oxford University's Center for the Governance of AI, studying the Chinese AI ecosystem in person. This is the original source of her China-AI strategic expertise.
- **CSET** (January 2019 - present): Director of Strategy → Director of Strategy and Foundational Research Grants (March 2022) → Interim Executive Director (September 2025).
- **OpenAI Board of Directors** (late 2021 - November 21, 2023): Invited to join the board by Holden Karnofsky (Open Philanthropy). Voted to remove Sam Altman on November 17, 2023; departed the board four days later as part of the resolution.

## Areas of Focus

Per the CSET staff page:

- Artificial intelligence policy
- Export controls (especially semiconductor manufacturing equipment controls)
- Machine learning
- Military-civil fusion
- China's AI strategy

She has written for **The New York Times, TIME, The Wall Street Journal, Foreign Affairs, The Economist, and Lawfare**.

## Personal Life

Married to a German scientist. Has one child.
