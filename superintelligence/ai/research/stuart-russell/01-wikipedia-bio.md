# Stuart J. Russell — Wikipedia biographical synthesis

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stuart_J._Russell
Retrieved: 2026-05-27

## Identity and birth

- Full name: Stuart Jonathan Russell, OBE FRS
- Born: 1962, Portsmouth, England
- Nationality: British (also operates as a US-resident academic at UC Berkeley since 1986)

## Education

- BA in Physics, Wadham College, Oxford, 1982 — first-class honours
- PhD in Computer Science, Stanford University, 1986
- Doctoral advisor: Michael Genesereth (Stanford Logic Group)
- Doctoral research focus: inductive and analogical reasoning

## Current affiliations (as of 2026-05-27)

- Professor of Computer Science, UC Berkeley (EECS)
- Smith-Zadeh Professor in Engineering, UC Berkeley
- Founder and Director, Center for Human-Compatible Artificial Intelligence (CHAI), UC Berkeley, est. 2016
- Founding Director, Kavli Center for Ethics, Science, and the Public, UC Berkeley (appointed inaugural director 2022)
- Founder and President, International Association for Safe and Ethical AI (IASEAI), inaugurated 2025
- Honorary Fellow, Wadham College, Oxford

## Past affiliations

- Adjunct Professor of Neurological Surgery, UCSF (2008–2011)
- Visiting positions: Paris (Blaise Pascal Chair, 2012), Cambridge, MIT, others

## Major awards and recognition

- IJCAI Computers and Thought Award (1995)
- AAAI Fellow (1997)
- ACM Fellow (2003)
- AAAS Fellow (2011)
- Blaise Pascal Chair (2012)
- BBC Reith Lecturer (2021) — series titled "Living with Artificial Intelligence"
- Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE), 2021 Birthday Honours, "for services to AI research"
- IJCAI Award for Research Excellence (2022)
- AAAI Award for Artificial Intelligence for the Benefit of Humanity (2025)
- Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS), 2025
- National Academy of Engineering (NAE) member (2025)

## Key publications

- *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (with Peter Norvig). First edition 1995. Fourth edition 2020. Standard textbook in the field; adopted by 1,500+ universities in 135 countries. Often referred to as "AIMA."
- *Human Compatible: Artificial Intelligence and the Problem of Control*. Viking, 2019. The defining popular book on AI safety from the "provably beneficial" school.
- *Do the Right Thing: Studies in Limited Rationality* (with Eric Wefald). MIT Press, 1991.
- Research papers on inverse reinforcement learning, cooperative inverse reinforcement learning (CIRL), inverse reward design, off-switch games.

## Notable recent activity

- March 2023: signed the Future of Life Institute open letter calling for a six-month pause on training AI systems more powerful than GPT-4.
- July 25, 2023: testified at the U.S. Senate Judiciary subcommittee hearing "Oversight of A.I.: Principles for Regulation."
- 2023: BBC Reith Lectures aired (a four-part series; the 2021 lectures were recorded earlier and published over multiple years).
- 2024-2025: extensive commentary track on Bletchley, Seoul, and Paris AI safety summits.
- January 31, 2025: Newsweek op-ed "DeepSeek, OpenAI, and the Race to Human Extinction."
- February 6-7, 2025: founded and chaired the inaugural IASEAI conference in Paris.
- April 2025: signed open letter with Geoffrey Hinton and others urging California and Delaware Attorneys General to block OpenAI's for-profit conversion.
- December 4, 2025: appeared on *The Diary of a CEO* with Steven Bartlett — flagship podcast appearance with extensive reach.
- February 18, 2026: cited in Fortune piece on the "Russian roulette" framing of the AI race.
- April 16, 2026: CNBC *Squawk Box* appearance on AI regulation.

## Personal

- Holds dual British/US functional residency. Has been at Berkeley continuously since completing PhD.
- Long-running public-intellectual register; speaks frequently at UK House of Lords, UN CCW meetings in Geneva, and policy fora in Washington DC.
