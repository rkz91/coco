## Event

- Name: IASEAI '25 — International Association for Safe and Ethical AI inaugural conference
- Date: February 6-7, 2025
- Venue: OECD La Muette Headquarters, Paris, France
- Timing: deliberately scheduled to precede the Paris AI Action Summit (February 10-11, 2025) at the Grand Palais
- Attendance: ~700 in-person, ~1,400 online

## Russell's role

- Founder and President of IASEAI.
- Did NOT deliver an opening keynote — that slot went to Nobel laureates and other scientific figures.
- Delivered the **closing statement** synthesizing the conference and outlining the path forward for safe and ethical AI.

## Russell's signature line from closing remarks (widely quoted)

> "The development of highly capable AI is likely to be the biggest event in human history. The world must act decisively to ensure it is not the last event in human history."

## Conference framing (Russell's contribution)

The conference's stated goal is to ensure AI systems are **provably beneficial for humanity** — a direct port of Russell's *Human Compatible* technical agenda into an international scientific-association format. The deliberate model is the **International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA)** for nuclear, or the **IPCC** for climate science: a standing international scientific body that produces consensus reports and informs governance.

## Notable attendees and co-signers

- Geoffrey Hinton (Nobel laureate in Physics 2024, "godfather of deep learning")
- Joseph Stiglitz (Nobel laureate, economist)
- Maria Ressa (Nobel Peace Prize 2021, journalist)
- Yoshua Bengio (Turing Award, AI safety convergence)
- Multiple representatives from national AI Safety Institutes (UK AISI, US AISI)

## Why Paris in February 2025

Russell positioned IASEAI '25 as a counterweight to the Paris AI Action Summit (Feb 10-11), which he and other safety researchers viewed as having drifted away from safety priorities in the Bletchley Park → Seoul → Paris track. The drift, in Russell's reading, was driven by industry lobbying and economic-nationalism framings (especially France's positioning of AI as a strategic competitiveness lever) at the expense of the original Bletchley focus on catastrophic and existential risk.

## Criticism

The Effective Altruism Forum review (FBB #8) argued that IASEAI '25 underdelivered on its potential — too many panels, too little binding policy output, too much overlap with the broader summit circuit. Russell's response in subsequent talks has been that IASEAI is intended as a standing scientific body that compounds over years, not a one-shot policy lever.

## Significance for the persona

IASEAI is Russell's institutional answer to the question "what would an effective international AI-safety regime look like?" His policy stance — that AI governance requires an IAEA-style international scientific consensus body — is no longer just a written argument; it is a thing he has founded and presided over.
