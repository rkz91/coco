   Takeaway: Madry's first major public artifact post-Preparedness, jointly with the OpenAI reasoning team. 2. **"Chain of Thought Monitorability: A New and Fragile Opportunity for AI Safety"** — Korbak et al., 40+ authors including **Madry**, Bengio, Hendrycks, Hubinger, Krakovna, Pachocki, Nanda, Shlegeris. arXiv community position paper, mid-to-late 2025.

   Takeaway: cross-lab co-signed statement that CoT monitorability is a real but fragile safety property and that labs should preserve it deliberately rather than train it away. Madry's signature placing him with the major safety voices outside OpenAI as well as inside.

3. **"AI Supply Chains: An Emerging Ecosystem of AI Actors, Products, and Services"** — Hopkins, Cen, Ilyas, Struckman, Videgaray, **Madry**. arXiv preprint, 2025.

   Takeaway: economics-of-AI framing co-authored with MIT Sloan colleagues. Argues AI is no longer a one-lab-one-product market — it is a layered supply chain with attendant deployment, attribution, and safety implications. Bridges his MIT-side deployability work with policy.
