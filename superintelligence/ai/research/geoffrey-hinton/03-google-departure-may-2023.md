# Geoffrey Hinton — Departure from Google, May 2023

**Primary sources:**
- New York Times, May 1, 2023 — interview by Cade Metz (paywalled; widely quoted)
- Fortune coverage: https://fortune.com/2023/05/01/godfather-ai-geoffrey-hinton-quit-google-regrets-lifes-work-bad-actors/
- MIT Technology Review: https://www.technologyreview.com/2023/05/01/1072478/deep-learning-pioneer-geoffrey-hinton-quits-google/

## The event

On May 1, 2023, Geoffrey Hinton publicly announced his resignation from Google after a decade as VP and Engineering Fellow at Google Brain. He made the announcement via interview with the New York Times rather than through Google's communications channels. He was 75.

## His stated reasons

The framing he gave the NYT was deliberate and consequential:

> "I want to be able to freely speak out about the risks of AI."

> "It is hard to see how you can prevent the bad actors from using it for bad things."

> "A part of me now regrets my life's work."

He also clarified that Google itself "has acted very responsibly" and that he did not leave to criticize Google specifically; he left so he could speak about the field in general without his words being read as Google's position.

## Why this is the pivotal moment in his persona

Before May 2023, Hinton was a deep-learning researcher who occasionally made cautious public-policy remarks. After May 2023, he is the most cited "godfather of AI" voice in mainstream media on existential risk. The May 2023 NYT interview is the single most-quoted Hinton text in any 2023–2026 article about AI safety.

Every persona attribute that follows — the 10–20 percent extinction estimate, the "digital beings" framing, the Romanes Lecture, the Nobel banquet speech, the 60 Minutes appearance, the Diary of a CEO interview — is downstream of this 2023 pivot.

## The "I regret my life's work" line

This single sentence has been frequently misquoted. The accurate New York Times rendering is closer to "a part of me now regrets my life's work" — a qualified statement of regret about how the technology is being deployed, not a repudiation of the science. Hinton has clarified this on multiple occasions, including the Diary of a CEO interview in June 2025, where he said his regret is conditional: if AI ends well, he does not regret the work; if it ends badly, he does.

## What changed in his timeline estimate

In the same May 2023 interviews, Hinton revised his AGI timeline downward:

- Pre-2023 stated view: AGI is 30 to 50 years away
- May 2023 revised view: AGI is fewer than 20 years away

The trigger for the revision was his experience with GPT-4 in 2022–2023, particularly its ability to do common-sense reasoning tasks that he had publicly bet against for years.
