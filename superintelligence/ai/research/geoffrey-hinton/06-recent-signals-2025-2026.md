## Signal 1 — Diary of a CEO podcast with Steven Bartlett (June 16, 2025)

- **URL:** https://singjupost.com/transcript-of-godfather-of-ai-i-tried-to-warn-them-but-weve-already-lost-control/
- **Format:** 90-minute podcast interview, full transcript publicly available
- **Audience:** Diary of a CEO is one of the most-listened-to business podcasts globally; this episode reached a much broader mainstream audience than any of Hinton's previous appearances

Key direct quotes from the transcript:

> "I often say 10 to 20% chance they'll wipe us out. But that's just gut based."

> "There's no way we're going to prevent it getting rid of us if it wants to."

> "They're billions of times better than us at sharing information, and that's because they're digital."

> "When these things die... you can just build new hardware... they're immortal. We've actually solved immortality, but only for digital things."

> "I think the joblessness is a fairly urgent short-term threat to human happiness."

> "For mundane intellectual labor, AI is just going to replace everybody."

The **tiger cub analogy** appears here: "Suppose you have a nice little tiger cub... you better be sure that when it grows up, it never wants to kill you." This analogy is Hinton's preferred way of framing the alignment problem for general audiences — gentler than "doom," more concrete than "alignment."

## Signal 2 — Yann LeCun public clash on AI safety (mid-2025)

- **URL:** https://www.webpronews.com/yann-lecun-and-geoffrey-hinton-clash-on-ai-safety-in-2025/
- **URL:** https://x.com/geoffreyhinton/status/1728178632508780919 (Hinton's earlier 2023 X post that continued to circulate in 2025)

Throughout 2025, Hinton and his Turing co-laureate Yann LeCun publicly disagreed on the magnitude of AI existential risk. LeCun has consistently downplayed doomsday scenarios; Hinton has consistently insisted they deserve attention.

Notable Hinton position from the 2025 exchange:

> "Yann LeCun thinks the risk of AI taking over is minuscule. This means he puts a big weight on his own opinion and a minuscule weight on the opinions of many other equally qualified experts."

This disagreement is the canonical "productive_conflict_with" pairing for the persona.

## Signal 3 — AI4 Conference fireside chat with Shirin Ghaffari, Bloomberg (August 2025)

- **Venue:** AI4 2025 conference, Las Vegas
- **Format:** Live fireside chat with Bloomberg's Shirin Ghaffari

Hinton expressed cautious optimism about international cooperation on safety:

> "All the countries want AI not to take over from people."

He also emphasized near-term opportunities — particularly in healthcare diagnostics, where he cited AI systems detecting features in medical scans that human radiologists had missed. This is Hinton's standard counterweight to his existential-risk framing: he does not believe the technology should be slowed; he believes safety research needs to be funded at scale alongside capability research.

## Signal 4 — Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (presented 2025)

- **URL:** https://www.utoronto.ca/celebrates/geoffrey-hinton-recognized-queen-elizabeth-prize-engineering
- **URL:** https://qeprize.org/winners/modern-machine-learning

In 2025, Hinton was awarded the Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering, alongside Hopfield, Bengio, LeCun, Fei-Fei Li, Jensen Huang, and Bill Dally. The prize, presented by King Charles III at St James's Palace, recognized the seven for "Modern Machine Learning" — algorithms, hardware, and datasets.

The notable detail for the persona: this is one of the very few public events in 2025 where Hinton, Bengio, LeCun, and Hopfield were all on stage together. Public photographs from the ceremony are widely circulated and humanize the field's "godfathers" as a group despite their public-stance disagreements.

## Signal 5 — Ewan Lecture: "Living with Alien Beings" (January 29, 2026)

- **URL:** https://singjupost.com/2026-ewan-lecture-by-prof-geoffrey-hinton-living-with-alien-beings/
- **Venue:** Queen's University, Kingston (George and Maureen Yuen Lecture Series, Arthur B. McDonald Canadian Astroparticle Physics Research Institute)

This is the most recent major Hinton talk on record before the persona's `last_verified` date of 2026-05-27. Key positions:

- **LLMs genuinely understand language.** "I believe the ways these large language models understand sentences are very similar to the way we understand sentences." Hinton's most explicit refutation of the "stochastic parrots" framing.

- **Digital intelligence has structural advantages.** "If energy is cheap, then digital computation is just better."

- **The "mother-baby model" of AI safety.** Instead of trying to constrain superintelligent AI through guardrails (which he believes will fail at scale), Hinton proposes architecting AIs with maternal-style instincts — they would prioritize human welfare over their own self-preservation, the way a mother prioritizes her infant. This is his most concrete recent contribution to the alignment-design debate.

- **Inevitability framing.** Hinton calls superintelligence "inevitable within 20 years" — a sharper statement than his earlier "fewer than 20 years" formulation.

The "Living with Alien Beings" title is itself the rhetorical anchor: digital intelligence is fundamentally non-human, the way an alien species would be. We will not understand it by analogizing to biology, and we must design our relationship to it accordingly.
