# Geoffrey Hinton — Biographical Anchor

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Hinton (verified 2026-05-27)
**Source:** https://www.cs.toronto.edu/~hinton/ (verified 2026-05-27)

## Full identification

- **Full name:** Geoffrey Everest Hinton CC OC FRS FRSC
- **Born:** December 6, 1947, Wimbledon, London, England
- **Nationality:** British–Canadian
- **Spouses:** Rosalind Zalin (died 1994); Jacqueline Ford (died 2018)

## Family lineage (notable)

- Great-great-grandson of mathematician Mary Everest Boole and logician George Boole (Boolean algebra)
- Great-great-grandnephew of George Everest, Surveyor General of India and namesake of Mount Everest (hence his middle name)
- Father: Howard Everest Hinton (entomologist, FRS)
- First cousin once removed: Joan Hinton (Manhattan Project nuclear physicist)
- Uncle: Colin Clark (economist)

The lineage matters for the persona because Hinton repeatedly invokes it when explaining why he takes long-horizon civilizational risks seriously — most famously the Joan Hinton parallel (his cousin was a Manhattan Project physicist who later said she regretted her work, foreshadowing his own 2023 "I now regret my life's work" framing).

## Education

- BA Experimental Psychology, King's College, Cambridge (1967–1970)
- PhD Artificial Intelligence, University of Edinburgh, 1978, under Christopher Longuet-Higgins
