# Recent signals — what Thomas Wolf has said or shipped since May 28, 2025

Constraint check: persona must have ≥3 signals post 2025-05-28. The following are the catalogued signals from the trailing twelve months, in reverse chronological order.

## 2026

### May 25, 2026 — LeRobot Hub crosses 58,000 datasets
Source: https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317129/20260525/open-source-robotics-ai-reaches-inflection-point-lerobot-hub-surpasses-58000-datasets-one-year.htm
Takeaway: One-year-after-launch milestone for LeRobot Hub. 50× growth in five months. Robotics is now the largest dataset category on Hugging Face Hub. Wolf framed the milestone publicly as the inflection-point validation of the open-robotics thesis.

### Recent (May 2026) — Reachy Mini agentic appstore push
Source: https://huggingface.co/thomwolf (recent post / upvoted article on "Introducing the agentic robotics appstore for 10,000 Reachy Minis"); thomwolf's own posts on Hugging Face Hub
Takeaway: Wolf is actively pushing the "appstore for robots" vision he laid out in November 2025, with 10,000 Reachy Minis going to developers as the first wave.

## 2025

### November 2025 — Sequoia "Training Data" podcast (Episode 62)
Source: https://sequoiacap.com/podcast/training-data-thomas-wolf/
Takeaway: Wolf's most-detailed public 2025 statement of the robotics thesis. Coined "the app store for robots." Established the diversity-over-volume training-data position. Quoted the WiFi-disconnect-and-run-into-your-kids local-deployment argument.

### October 24, 2025 — LeRobot v0.4.0 release
Source: https://huggingface.co/blog/lerobot-release-v040
Takeaway: Major LeRobot release. Datasets v3.0, PI0/PI0.5, GR00T N1.5, plugin system, multi-GPU training. Wolf listed as contributor/author. Public proof point that the LeRobot stack can host frontier robotics models, not just toys.

### September 18, 2025 — TechCrunch Disrupt 2025 announcement
Source: https://techcrunch.com/2025/09/18/building-the-future-of-open-ai-with-thomas-wolf-at-techcrunch-disrupt-2025/
Takeaway: Wolf's Disrupt 2025 session was framed around "the future of AI won't just be defined by closed labs and Big Tech budgets — it will be written in open source repos, global research collaborations, and moonshot experiments that anyone can build on." Restates the canonical Wolf thesis at scale.

### September 16, 2025 — MIT Sloan Management Review podcast
Source: https://sloanreview.mit.edu/audio/challenging-the-average-with-open-source-ai-hugging-faces-thomas-wolf/
Takeaway: The clearest single articulation of the "challenging the average" frame. LLMs are "very good at average thinking" but "quite bad at really challenging the average." Cost-per-token as the metric driving the chip industry. Vibe-coding hackathon for 9–12 year olds.

### July 8, 2025 — SmolLM3 release
Source: https://huggingface.co/blog/smollm3
Takeaway: 3B model competitive with 4B closed-frontier models. 11.2T tokens. 128k context via YARN. Full release of data, recipes, configs, training logs. Architecture choices (GQA, NoPE, intra-document masking) ablation-validated. The artifact form of the open-recipe doctrine.

### June 20, 2025 — Fortune feature "AI more likely to create yes-men on servers"
Source: https://fortune.com/2025/06/20/hugging-face-thomas-wolf-ai-yes-men-on-servers-no-scientific-breakthroughs/
Takeaway: The interview that made "yes-men on servers" a widely-quoted phrase across the AI press. Wolf's clearest direct quotes on asking-vs-answering, scientific genius, and current-AI limitations. Most-quoted Wolf piece of 2025.
