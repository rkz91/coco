# Chelsea Finn — Stanford profile and IRIS group

Source: https://ai.stanford.edu/~cbfinn/ (canonical Stanford homepage; redirected from cs.stanford.edu/~cbfinn/), fetched 2026-05-27.

## Position

Chelsea Finn is an Assistant Professor in Computer Science and Electrical Engineering at Stanford University. She is also affiliated with SAIL (Stanford AI Lab) and the Stanford Machine Learning Group, and she co-directs the IRIS group ("Intelligence through Robotic Interaction at Scale"). She is a co-founder of Physical Intelligence and continues to serve in a research-lead capacity there alongside her Stanford appointment.

## Education

- B.S. Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, MIT.
- Ph.D. Computer Science, UC Berkeley, 2018, advised by Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine.
- Post-PhD: Google Brain (2018-2021) before moving to Stanford as faculty.

## Research framing

Finn frames her research goal as "the capability of robots and other agents to develop broadly intelligent behavior through learning and interaction." The IRIS group operationalizes that mission through robot learning at scale rather than through hand-designed controllers or task-specific RL. Methodologically her work spans:

- Meta-learning and few-shot learning.
- Deep reinforcement learning, including model-based RL.
- Robot learning from human demonstrations (imitation learning, behavioral cloning).
- Multi-task and transfer learning, including cross-embodiment policies.
- Foundation models for robotics (Vision-Language-Action models).

## Why this matters for the AI Super Intelligence Team

Finn is the canonical academic-plus-startup voice for the bet that robotics needs its own foundation-model substrate, trained primarily on real-world teleoperation data rather than internet video. She is one of a very small number of people who can credibly speak from inside both Stanford academic robotics and the most heavily-funded robot foundation model lab (Physical Intelligence). For the multimodal-embodied cell she is the natural specialist anchor.
