# "My views on doom" — Christiano's p(doom) breakdown

Primary source: https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/xWMqsvHapP3nwdSW8/my-views-on-doom (LessWrong, 2023-04-27)
Cross-reference: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Christiano

## The 46% figure

> "Probability that humanity has somehow irreversibly messed up our future within 10 years of building powerful AI: **46%**."

Decomposed by Christiano into three lanes:

| Failure mode | Probability |
|---|---|
| AI takeover | 22% |
| Extinction from non-takeover causes | 9% |
| Other ways of "messing it up" during accelerated technological change | 15% |
| **Total** | **46%** |

## The 20% figure

> "Probability that most humans die within 10 years of building powerful AI (powerful enough to make human labor obsolete): **20%**."

Decomposed:

| Cause of death | Probability |
|---|---|
| Deaths from AI takeover | 11% |
| Deaths from non-takeover reasons (war, terrorism, etc.) | 9% |
| **Total** | **20%** |

## The 50/50 figure (separate, from Dwarkesh Podcast)

In the Dwarkesh interview (Oct 31, 2023) Christiano gave a separate framing: roughly **50/50 chance of doom shortly after you have AI systems that are human level**. This is a *conditional* probability (given human-level AI is built), not the unconditional 46% above.

## Key temporal anchor

The 46% and 20% figures are *not* "by 2050." They are framed as "within 10 years of building powerful AI." This is a load-bearing distinction in any panel synthesis: Christiano's risk framework is conditional on capability arrival, not calendar time. Get this wrong and the persona will sound like Geoffrey Hinton or Yoshua Bengio rather than himself.

## Tone of the post

The post is striking precisely because Christiano is the *moderate* of the doom camp — he is on record disagreeing publicly with Eliezer Yudkowsky on the probability of fast takeoff and on the inevitability of catastrophe ("Where I agree and disagree with Eliezer"). The 46% is "low" by Yudkowsky's standards but "extraordinarily high" by mainstream-ML standards. This is the framing that gives Christiano credibility on both sides of the safety vs accelerationism debate.

## Why this matters for the persona

The numbers are not bluster — they are the analytical output of a decomposition. The persona's `public_stances` for "existential risk is real" must cite these specific numbers, not a generic "AI is dangerous" claim, and must preserve the conditional structure (post-powerful-AI, not by-calendar-year).
