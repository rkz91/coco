## Topics covered (per Dwarkesh's notes and aggregator summaries)

1. **Responsible Scaling Policies.** Christiano is the intellectual architect behind Anthropic's RSP. He frames RSPs as the operational bridge between alignment research and frontier-lab governance.
2. **AI coup / bioweapon scenarios.** Concrete catastrophic risks rather than abstract X-risk hand-waving.
3. **Timeline estimates.** 15% by 2030, 40% by 2040 (note: these are *2023* numbers; subject to update).
4. **Post-AGI world.** He expects, in the long run, to "drive down the rate of war to very low levels" — strongly continuous-takeoff worldview.
5. **A new proof system.** His then-current research direction: formal explanations for model behavior as a route to alignment. This is the lineage that flows into "heuristic explanations" / "no-coincidence principle" work at ARC.

## Why this is the canonical Christiano podcast

The 80,000 Hours episode (#44, 2018) is older and pre-LLM-era. The AXRP episode (#12, 2021) is shorter and more technical. The Dwarkesh episode is the canonical long-form Christiano interview where he sets out the full worldview — alignment is hard, plan A is "don't build until you understand," RSPs and third-party evals are the operational fallback, and the failure modes are deception + competitive lock-in.
