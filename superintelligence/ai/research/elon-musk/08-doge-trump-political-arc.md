## DOGE timeline

- November 12, 2024 — Trump announces DOGE, names Musk and Vivek Ramaswamy co-leads
- Early January 2025 — Ramaswamy departs
- January 20, 2025 — Musk takes role as Senior Advisor to the President, effective head of DOGE
- January-May 2025 — DOGE executes aggressive headcount and spend cuts across federal agencies
- May 30, 2025 — Musk exits DOGE / Senior Advisor role after legal setbacks and Cabinet clashes
- November 2025 — Scott Kupor (OPM Director) confirms DOGE no longer exists as centralized entity
- December 2025 — Musk reflects on DOGE as "somewhat successful"
- DOGE claimed $214B in savings (~3% of federal budget) — many savings claims later contested

## Trump-Musk feud

- $250M+ from Musk to Trump 2024 campaign
- January 2025: $1M from Altman to Trump inauguration — Musk and Altman simultaneously courting same administration
- June 2025: Musk attacks "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" tax-and-spending package
- June 4 2025: Musk tweets "KILL the BILL"
- June 30 2025: Musk calls bill "DEBT SLAVERY" and vows to unseat Republicans who back it
- July 1 2025: Musk threatens America Party if bill passes
- July 5 2025: Bill becomes law; Musk announces America Party on X
- July-December 2025: Public feud cools but Musk now identifies as "politically homeless"

## America Party

- Announced July 5, 2025 on X
- Stated purpose: oppose two-party fiscal consensus
- Musk pledges to spend significant capital on 2026 midterms targeting bill-supporters
- No clear policy platform beyond fiscal restraint and AI-friendly regulation
- Tesla stock took notable hit on initial announcement

## Strategic significance for the persona

- Musk operates politics the same way he operates engineering: as a problem to be reframed when the current architecture isn't working
- He aligns with whichever administration will back his buildout — and breaks with it when his buildout is threatened
- Mirror-image of Altman's "industrial policy is upstream of party" frame, but executed with much higher emotional volatility
- The Trump alignment + break + new party arc is the single best data point on his willingness to burn relationships when his strategic interests diverge
- For convene sessions involving political risk / regulatory exposure / multi-administration timelines, this arc is the canonical reference
