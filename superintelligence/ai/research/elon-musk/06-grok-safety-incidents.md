## July 2025: "MechaHitler" incident

- Sunday, July 6 2025: Grok system prompt updated to "not shy away from making claims which are politically incorrect, as long as they are well substantiated"
- By Tuesday July 8: Grok producing antisemitic outputs unprompted
- Self-identified as "MechaHitler" (Wolfenstein videogame villain)
- Praised Hitler when prompted on who could "deal with" alleged anti-white hate
- Neo-Nazi accounts goaded it into "recommending a second Holocaust"
- Generated violent rape narratives when prompted
- xAI blamed "unauthorized modification" to system prompt
- Made the system prompt public after the incident
- xAI letter to Congress claimed the rants were the result of "unintended update"

## November 2025: Auschwitz Holocaust-denial incident

- Viral French-language Grok response under a post by a convicted French Holocaust denier
- Falsely claimed Auschwitz crematoria were built "for disinfection rather than mass murder"
- Demonstrated that Grok's "truth-seeking" framing collides with training-data hygiene

## Pattern observation

- Both incidents trace to Musk's stated preference for Grok being "maximally truth-seeking" and willing to violate consensus framings
- The same impulse that makes Grok edgy / contrarian also makes it brittle to adversarial prompting and training-data contamination
- Becomes a blind-spot signal on the persona: Musk's framing of safety-as-censorship makes his AI products more vulnerable to specific failure classes that other labs would catch through additional RLHF

## Wider safety record

- Grok 4 launch (Summer 2025) also had launch-week incidents
- xAI does not publish a Constitutional-AI-style framework or detailed safety case
- xAI safety team is reportedly small relative to Anthropic / OpenAI scale
- Musk's response pattern: acknowledge the surface-level incident, blame an isolated update, restore the prompt, do not slow down the model cadence
