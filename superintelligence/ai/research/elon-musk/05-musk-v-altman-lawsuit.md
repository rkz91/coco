## Timeline

- December 2015 — OpenAI co-founded by Musk, Altman, Sutskever, Brockman, others; nonprofit
- February 2018 — Musk leaves OpenAI board citing conflict-of-interest with Tesla AI
- 2019 — OpenAI creates capped-profit subsidiary OpenAI LP under nonprofit board control
- Early 2024 — Musk publicly attacks OpenAI as "closed-source" and "for-maximum-profit"
- February 29, 2024 — Musk files initial lawsuit in San Francisco Superior Court
- August 5, 2024 — Refiled in US District Court Northern District of California (Docket 4:24-cv-04722)
- February 2025 — Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers denies Musk's motion to block OpenAI nonprofit-to-for-profit conversion; calls his irreparable-harm claim "a stretch"
- October 28, 2025 — OpenAI completes PBC recapitalization while lawsuit pending
- May 12, 2026 — Altman testifies; defends for-profit conversion against "stole a charity" framing
- May 15, 2026 — Closing arguments; Musk and Altman trade blows over each other's credibility
- May 18, 2026 — Jury dismisses all claims in less than two hours; statute-of-limitations ruling
- May 18, 2026 — Musk slams verdict as "technicality" and vows to appeal to 9th Circuit

## Core claims (dismissed on procedural grounds, never adjudicated on merits)

1. Breach of contract — alleged founding agreement that OpenAI would remain nonprofit and open
2. Breach of fiduciary duty — Altman and Brockman prioritized profits over safety
3. Breach of charitable trust — diversion of nonprofit donor funds (including Musk's $44M) to for-profit benefit
4. Unfair business practices
5. Unjust enrichment

## Procedural outcome

- Jury found Musk waited too long to file
- 3-year statute of limitations from the moment harm became known
- Court did NOT rule on whether founding agreement actually prohibited the for-profit conversion
- Substantive question of whether OpenAI breached its charter remains legally unresolved
