## Cybercab and Robovan — "We, Robot" event Oct 10 2024

- Venue: Warner Bros Studios, Los Angeles
- Cybercab: two-seat autonomous taxi, no steering wheel, no pedals
- Target price: under US$30,000
- Volume production target: end of 2026
- Long-run capacity target: 2 million Cybercabs per year at full design capacity across multiple factories
- Robovan: up to 20 passengers, autonomous, no timeline disclosed
- Production starting line: Gigafactory Texas; later Fremont conversion after Model S/X end-of-life

## FSD v14

- Rolling out from October 2025 through 2026
- v14.3 confirmed launching April 2026 ("last piece of the puzzle" — Musk)
- 10x larger neural network than v13
- Trained on Cortex (Tesla's training cluster) + Dojo silicon
- Unified end-to-end neural net (camera → driving controls in single model)
- v14.3.2 described by Musk as final-stage iteration toward unsupervised driving

## Hardware compatibility issue

- Hardware 3 vehicles (older Teslas) lack memory bandwidth required for unsupervised FSD — Musk confirmed Q1 2026 earnings call
- Tesla preparing FSD v14-lite for HW3 vehicles Summer 2026
- Creates legal exposure: Tesla owners promised full self-driving capability years ago

## NHTSA investigation

- March 2026: NHTSA upgraded from preliminary evaluation to Engineering Analysis
- Scope: 3.2 million Tesla vehicles
- Triggered by FSD inability to handle reduced visibility (sun glare, fog, dust)
- An adverse finding could trigger the largest software-driven recall in automotive history

## Optimus humanoid robot

- Gen 2 demonstrated at "We, Robot" October 2024
- Several thousand units targeted for 2025 production
- 20,000-unit target for 2026 (~4x of 2025)
- Optimus 3: Summer 2026 production start, high volume by Summer 2027
- 10M units/year capacity target by 2027 at dedicated Gigafactory Texas facility
- November 2025: dedicated humanoid production facility announced at Giga Texas
- Q2 2026: Fremont Model S/X lines converting to Optimus

## Strategic frame

Tesla AI is the integrated bet: same vision-language model substrate (under Ashok Elluswamy + Milan Kovac historically) drives both cars and humanoids. Data is the moat — billions of miles of fleet driving data. Compute is the substrate — Dojo + Cortex + Colossus. Hardware is the leverage — bring AI into the physical world and you collapse the cost of physical labor the same way LLMs collapse the cost of intellectual labor.
