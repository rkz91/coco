## Colossus 1

- Memphis, Tennessee — original site
- Came online late 2024
- Built in approximately 122 days (vs industry-standard 3-4 years for comparable scale)
- ~200,000 NVIDIA H100 GPUs at peak Colossus 1 buildout
- On-site natural gas turbines for power (controversial — Shelby County air-quality lawsuits)

## Colossus 2

- January 2026: Musk announced purchase of third Memphis building
- Expanded to 2 gigawatts total capacity
- 555,000 NVIDIA GPUs (H100 + H200 + Blackwell mix)
- ~$18B total GPU spend
- World's largest single-site AI training installation as of January 2026
- First training cluster to surpass 1 GW (January 2026)
- Upgrading to 1.5 GW by April 2026

## Target: 1 million GPUs

- Public stated goal: 1 million GPUs across multiple Memphis-area sites
- Third site planned for Southaven, Mississippi
- $659M construction permit filed for new 312,000 sq ft four-story building on 79-acre parcel adjacent to existing facility (early 2026)

## Construction velocity

- xAI demonstrates 19-day buildout cycles for new pods
- Compresses what hyperscalers typically take 3-4 years into weeks
- Operational philosophy: use natural gas turbines and pre-fab modular pods to bypass conventional utility-grid timelines
- This is the literal embodiment of Musk's "make requirements less dumb → delete process → accelerate cycle time" playbook applied to data-center construction

## Strategic positioning

Colossus is the pure expression of Musk's core thesis: AI competitive advantage is bottlenecked on compute and power, and the team that builds gigawatts fastest wins. Direct counterpart to Altman's Stargate ($500B / 10 GW) but with a single-CEO-driven operating model rather than a multi-party JV.
