# Ilya Sutskever — Wikipedia biographical extract

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_Sutskever
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Birth and early life

- Born 1986 in Gorky (Nizhny Novgorod), Soviet Union
- Immigrated to Israel at age 5; grew up in Jerusalem
- Attended Open University of Israel starting in 8th grade
- Moved to Canada at age 16

## Education

- University of Toronto: BSc Mathematics (2005), MSc Computer Science (2007), PhD Computer Science (2013)
- Doctoral advisor: Geoffrey Hinton
- PhD thesis: "Training Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Career positions

- 2012: Postdoc with Andrew Ng at Stanford University (~2 months)
- 2012-2013: DNNResearch (Hinton's spinoff, acquired by Google)
- 2013-2015: Google Brain (research scientist)
- End of 2015 to May 2024: OpenAI (cofounder, chief scientist)
- June 2024 to present: Safe Superintelligence Inc. (cofounder; became CEO in July 2025)

## Canonical publications and major contributions

- **AlexNet** (2012): Co-created with Alex Krizhevsky and Geoffrey Hinton. Convolutional neural network for ImageNet classification.
- **Sequence-to-Sequence Learning with Neural Networks** (2014): With Oriol Vinyals and Quoc Viet Le at Google Brain. NeurIPS 2024 Test of Time award.
- Contributions to TensorFlow
- Co-author on the AlphaGo Nature paper (2016)
- Oversaw research breakthroughs leading to GPT models and ChatGPT
- Contributed to CLIP and DALL-E
- Led research on o1 and subsequent reasoning systems at OpenAI

## Notable quotes

- "It may be that today's large neural networks are slightly conscious." (Tweet, 2022)
- "Our first product will be the safe superintelligence, and it will not do anything else up until then." (June 2024, on SSI launch)

## OpenAI board crisis (November 2023)

- November 17, 2023: Board voted to fire CEO Sam Altman. Sutskever was instrumental in the decision.
- Authored a 52-page memo citing Altman's lack of candor, manipulation, and internal division.
- Stated the firing was "the board doing its duty" in a company meeting.
- A week later expressed regret at Altman's ouster.
- Altman was reinstated; Sutskever stepped down from the OpenAI board.

## Departure from OpenAI

- May 2024: Announced departure to pursue a "very personally meaningful" project.
- June 17, 2024 (sic — actually May): Jan Leike (Superalignment co-lead) also departed shortly after, citing eroded safety and trust.

## Safe Superintelligence Inc. (SSI) timeline

- **June 19, 2024**: Founded SSI with Daniel Gross and Daniel Levy. Offices in Palo Alto and Tel Aviv.
- **September 2024**: Raised $1 billion at ~$5 billion valuation (Andreessen Horowitz, Sequoia Capital, DST Global, SV Angel).
- **March 2025**: Raised additional $2 billion; valuation reached ~$32 billion (Greenoaks-led).
- **April 2025**: Google Cloud announced TPU provision for SSI research.
- **June 2025**: Meta attempted to acquire SSI; offer rejected.
- **June 29, 2025**: Daniel Gross officially departs SSI to join Meta Superintelligence Labs.
- **July 3, 2025**: Sutskever became CEO; Daniel Levy became President.

## Awards and honors

- 2015: MIT Technology Review's 35 Innovators Under 35
- 2018: Keynote speaker at Nvidia Ntech 2018 and AI Frontiers Conference 2018
- 2022: Elected Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS)
- 2023 & 2024: Time Magazine's 100 Most Influential People in AI
- 2022, 2023, 2024: NeurIPS Test of Time Award (three consecutive years)
- 2025: Honorary Doctorate from University of Toronto
- 2026: National Academy of Sciences Award for Industrial Application of Science (first time awarded in AI)
