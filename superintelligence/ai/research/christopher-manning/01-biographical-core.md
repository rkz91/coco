# Christopher D. Manning — Biographical Core

Compiled 2026-05-27 from Wikipedia, Stanford NLP group page, and Stanford profile.

## Identity

- **Full name:** Christopher David Manning
- **Born:** September 18, 1965 (age 60 as of compilation)
- **Nationality:** Australian-American
- **Twitter / X handle:** @chrmanning (joined September 2014, ~160K followers)

## Education

- **BA (Hons)** in Mathematics, Computer Science, and Linguistics — Australian National University (1989)
- **PhD** in Linguistics — Stanford University (1994), advised by Joan Bresnan
- **Dissertation:** *Ergativity: Argument Structure and Grammatical Relations* — note that Manning's academic origin is in formal syntax, not in machine learning. He spent his first decade as a working linguist before pivoting to statistical and then neural NLP.

## Major honours

- 2010: AAAI Fellow
- 2011: Inaugural ACL Fellow
- 2013: ACM Fellow
- 2015: President, Association for Computational Linguistics
- 2023: Honorary Doctorate, University of Amsterdam
- 2024: IEEE John von Neumann Medal — "for advances in computational representation and analysis of natural language"
- 2025: Elected Fellow, National Academy of Engineering
- 2025: Elected Fellow, American Academy of Arts and Sciences
- 2025: ACL Test of Time Award (10-year), for the 2015 Luong-Pham-Manning attention paper — his third consecutive Test of Time award
- 2026: EACL Best Paper Award (with Jasper Jian) — *Humans and transformer LMs: Abstraction drives language learning*
