# Christopher D. Manning — Recent Signals (2025–2026)

Compiled 2026-05-27. Focus: anything dated after 2025-05-27 to satisfy the persona's recent-signal requirement.

## 1. Move to AIX Ventures as full-time General Partner (2025-07-02)

- **Source:** https://aijourn.com/world-leading-ai-researcher-chris-manning-joins-aix-ventures-as-general-partner-to-back-deep-ai-startups/
- **Announcement date:** July 2, 2025
- After serving as an investing partner since AIX's founding in 2021, Manning took a full-time role as General Partner and went on leave from Stanford.
- Manning's stated quote: "Joining AIX Ventures as a General Partner allows me to support the next generation of founders building the tools and infrastructure that will define that future."
- His framing: deep AI systems will fundamentally reshape how we interact with technology and knowledge, and researchers should not merely publish findings but actively fund and construct the future of artificial intelligence.
- Investment focus: machine learning and natural language reasoning frontier.
- Prior portfolio: Perplexity, Wispr, Workera, Workhelix. AIX has also backed Hugging Face, You.com, Weights & Biases.

**Why this matters for the persona:** Manning's centre of gravity shifted from pure academic NLP to venture-backed deep-AI startup formation. His 2026 stances on which systems get built and shipped will be filtered through both an academic linguist's frame and a working seed-stage GP's frame.

## 2. SAIL directorship handover to Carlos Guestrin (2025-02-17)

- **Source:** https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2025/02/carlos-guestrin-to-lead-stanford-ai-lab-as-it-joins-forces-with-stanford-hai
- Carlos Guestrin (Fortinet Founders Professor of CS) was named new Director of SAIL, effective 2025.
- Manning led SAIL from 2018 to 2025, after Fei-Fei Li.
- SAIL was structurally integrated with Stanford HAI as a joint laboratory between the Computer Science Department and HAI. Manning continues as HAI Associate Director and Senior Fellow.

## 3. KDD 2025 Keynote — "The Surprising Victory of NLP: From Philosophy to Agentic Language Models"

- **Slides:** https://nlp.stanford.edu/~manning/xyzzy/KDD2025-Keynote-Language-Models.pdf
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d6XBrx_7rIE
- Solo-authored KDD 2025 keynote. Tracks the arc from philosophy-of-language origins through statistical NLP through deep learning to agentic LLMs. Framed by Manning as a "surprising victory" — the field he entered as a syntactician in the early 90s has won at a scale he did not predict, but with caveats about what was actually solved versus what was bypassed.

## 4. EMNLP 2025 — Stronger Baselines for RAG with Long-Context Models

- **Co-authors:** Alex Laitenberger, Nelson F. Liu
- **URL:** https://aclanthology.org/2025.emnlp-main.1656/
- A methodological cold-shower paper for the RAG-heavy 2025 ecosystem: simple long-context baselines often outperform fancier retrieval-augmented pipelines when given a fair comparison. Consistent with Manning's recurring "be honest about your baselines" stance.

## 5. EMNLP 2025 — Mechanisms vs. Outcomes: Probing for Syntax Fails to Explain Performance

- **Co-authors:** Ananth Agarwal, Jasper Jian, Shikhar Murty
- **URL:** https://aclanthology.org/2025.emnlp-main.1712/
- Argues that syntactic probes do not actually predict targeted syntactic evaluation performance — i.e. the standard "BERT knows syntax" story is more complicated than the field assumes. Continues his interpretability + linguistic-structure thread.

## 6. EACL 2026 Best Paper — Humans and transformer LMs: Abstraction drives language learning

- **Co-author:** Jasper Jian
- **URL:** https://aclanthology.org/2026.eacl-long.32/
- Won EACL 2026 Best Paper Award. Direct comparison of how humans and transformers acquire language abstractions. Continues Manning's long-running interest in LLMs as cognitive-science instruments, not just engineering artefacts.

## 7. National Academy of Engineering and American Academy of Arts and Sciences (2025)

- Elected Fellow of both bodies in 2025. NAE citation specifically for "development and dissemination of natural language processing methods."

## 8. ACL 2025 Test of Time Award for Luong-Pham-Manning 2015

- **Tweet:** https://x.com/aclmeeting/status/1950537132059382192
- ACL 2025 awarded the 10-year Test of Time award to "Effective Approaches to Attention-based Neural Machine Translation" (Luong, Pham, Manning, EMNLP 2015). Manning's third consecutive Test of Time award.

## 9. CS224N Winter 2026 — taught by Diyi Yang and Yejin Choi
