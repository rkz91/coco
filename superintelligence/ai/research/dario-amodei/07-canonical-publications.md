# Canonical publications co-authored or directed by Dario Amodei

**Retrieved:** 2026-05-27

## Concrete Problems in AI Safety (2016)

- Authors: Dario Amodei, Chris Olah, Jacob Steinhardt, Paul Christiano, John Schulman, Dan Mané.
- ArXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.06565
- The foundational taxonomy of practical safety problems: reward hacking, scalable oversight, safe exploration, distributional shift, side effects, etc.
- Written while Amodei was at Google Brain / transitioning to OpenAI. Sets the agenda that becomes Anthropic's research program five years later.

## Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models (2020)

- Authors: Kaplan, McCandlish, Henighan, Brown, Chess, Child, Gray, Radford, Wu, Amodei.
- ArXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361
- Empirical demonstration that LM loss follows power-law in compute, data, model size. Becomes the quantitative backbone for the GPT-3 / GPT-4 / Claude scale-up era.
- Amodei is last author; the paper anchors his "scaling hypothesis" public stance.

## Constitutional AI (2022)

- Authors: Bai, Kadavath, Kundu, et al. (Anthropic).
- ArXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073
- The technique behind Claude's character: RLAIF using a written "constitution" of principles. Replaces / augments the RLHF feedback loop with self-critique driven by stated values.
- Directed by Amodei as CEO; the technique is the product expression of his "values at the character level" framing.

## Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy (2023)

- https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropics-responsible-scaling-policy
- The ASL framework (1, 2, 3, 4+) tied to capability evaluations and deployment commitments.
- Amodei's structural answer to "how does a frontier lab race responsibly?" Pre-commits the company to halt deployment if a safety case cannot be made.

## Machines of Loving Grace (2024)

- https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/machines-of-loving-grace
- October 2024. ~14,000 words. The positive case.

## On DeepSeek and Export Controls (2025)

- https://www.darioamodei.com/post/on-deepseek-and-export-controls
- January 2025. The geopolitical case for chip export controls.

## The Adolescence of Technology (2026)

- https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/the-adolescence-of-technology
- January 2026. ~15,000–20,000 words. Updates the risk case to account for 2025 developments.

## Pattern across the corpus

- One technical paper or framework, then one public essay translating it for policy and public consumption.
- Long-form essays, not Twitter threads. Reasoned arguments, not slogans.
- Recurring vocabulary: "powerful AI" (not AGI), "country of geniuses in a datacenter," "ASL" tiers, "constitutional," "compress decades into years."
- Recurring frames: scaling hypothesis is real; risk is real; export controls are essential; surgical regulation beats broad bans; the next tier of risk is the AI labs themselves.
