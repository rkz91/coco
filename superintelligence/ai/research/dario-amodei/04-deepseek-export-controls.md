# On DeepSeek and Export Controls — Dario Amodei (January 2025)

**Source:** https://www.darioamodei.com/post/on-deepseek-and-export-controls
**Retrieved:** 2026-05-27

## Core arguments

### On DeepSeek itself

DeepSeek-V3 represents expected progress, not a breakthrough. The company achieved "a model close to the performance of US models 7-10 months older, for a good deal less cost (but not anywhere near the ratios people have suggested)." Cost reductions of approximately 4x annually are normal scaling-trend behavior. "Claude 3.5 Sonnet remains much better on some other key tasks, such as real-world coding."

DeepSeek's chip composition — a mixture of restricted and older models — actually validates that export restrictions are working as intended, not failing.

### On export controls

"Well-enforced export controls are the only thing that can prevent China from getting millions of chips, and are therefore the most important determinant of whether we end up in a unipolar or bipolar world."

"Export controls serve a vital purpose: keeping democratic nations at the forefront of AI development. To be clear, they're not a way to duck the competition between the US and China."

"The idea that the technology getting *more powerful*, having *more* bang for the buck, is a reason to lift our export controls makes no sense at all."

### Unipolar vs bipolar AI future

Two scenarios for 2026–2027:

- **Bipolar world** (China obtains millions of chips): Both superpowers develop frontier AI. Even with parity, "China could direct more talent, capital, and focus to military applications of the technology."
- **Unipolar world** (export controls succeed): Only US and allies access frontier systems. "A temporary lead could be parlayed into a durable advantage" because AI systems can recursively improve themselves.

### Geopolitical framing

"What's different this time is that the company that was first to demonstrate the expected cost reductions was Chinese. This has never happened before and is geopolitically significant."

"Combined with its large industrial base and military-strategic advantages, this could help China take a commanding lead on the global stage, not just for AI but for everything."
