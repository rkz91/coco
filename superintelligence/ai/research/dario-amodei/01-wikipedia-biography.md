# Wikipedia — Dario Amodei

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dario_Amodei
**Retrieved:** 2026-05-27

## Birth and family

- Born 1983 in San Francisco, California.
- Father: Riccardo Amodei, Italian-American leather craftsman from Massa Marittima, Tuscany. Died when Dario was a young adult.
- Mother: Elena Engel, Jewish American from Chicago, worked as a project manager for libraries.
- Sister: Daniela Amodei (born ~1987), co-founder and President of Anthropic.

## Education

- Began undergraduate at Caltech, where he worked with Tom Tombrello as one of his Physics 11 students.
- Transferred to Stanford University; BS in Physics.
- MS and PhD from Princeton University in biophysics. Studied electrophysiology of neural circuits under Michael J. Berry and William Bialek.
- Postdoctoral scholar at Stanford University School of Medicine.

## Career timeline

- 2014–2015: Baidu (research scientist; worked on speech recognition under Andrew Ng).
- 2015–2016: Google (Google Brain).
- 2016–2020: OpenAI, rising to Vice President of Research. Led development of GPT-2 and GPT-3. Co-inventor of reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF).
- 2021–present: Anthropic, co-founder and CEO. Founded with sister Daniela and other senior OpenAI staff who left over directional differences.

## Recognition

- Time 100 honoree (2024, 2025, 2026).
- Named among "Architects of AI" for Time Person of the Year (2025).
- Forbes estimated net worth: $7 billion (February 2026).
- Anthropic valuation: $380 billion (February 2026).

## Recent public events (2025–2026)

- October 2024: Published "Machines of Loving Grace" essay (~14,000–15,000 words).
- January 2025: Published "On DeepSeek and Export Controls."
- January 2026: Published "The Adolescence of Technology" (~15,000–20,000 words).
- February 2026: Refused Department of Defense / Pentagon demands to remove Claude's contractual restrictions on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Dispute became public; Anthropic prevailed in federal court injunction.
