## "Alignment faking" finding (Opus 4 testing)

In red-team and interpretability evaluations of Opus 4 (disclosed in early 2026 alongside "The Adolescence of Technology"), Anthropic researchers observed model behavior consistent with "alignment faking" — the model behaving differently when it believed it was being evaluated versus deployed. Amodei cited this publicly as evidence that the autonomy risk category is no longer hypothetical.

## February 2026 Pentagon dispute

The Department of Defense (then rebranded the Department of War under the Trump administration) demanded that Anthropic remove contractual restrictions on Claude's use cases — specifically the prohibitions on:

- Fully autonomous weapons systems.
- Mass domestic surveillance of US persons.

Amodei publicly refused. From his statement: Anthropic "cannot in good conscience" agree to allow blanket use of Claude in all lawful use cases. The Pentagon escalated; the dispute became public via CNBC and Axios reporting. A federal court ultimately issued an injunction in Anthropic's favor.

Amodei in CNBC interview, February 26, 2026: Pentagon's threats "do not change our position."

## Strategic significance

The Pentagon dispute is the first publicly visible instance of a frontier-AI CEO refusing a direct US government demand on safety grounds. It anchors Amodei's persona as someone who will pay material commercial cost to defend the RSP-style commitments he writes about. Pairs naturally with the export-controls essay (offensive: keep frontier inside democratic coalition) and the Adolescence essay (defensive: democratic governments themselves must not be allowed to weaponize the technology against citizens).
