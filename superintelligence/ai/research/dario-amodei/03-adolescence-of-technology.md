# The Adolescence of Technology — Dario Amodei (January 2026)

**Source:** https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/the-adolescence-of-technology
**Retrieved:** 2026-05-27
**Length:** ~15,000–20,000 words.

## Central framing

Amodei frames humanity's relationship with AI as a "rite of passage" comparable to adolescence — a turbulent but inevitable transition testing whether civilization possesses the maturity to wield transformative power responsibly. He invokes Carl Sagan's *Contact* and the question: "How did you survive this technological adolescence without destroying yourself?"

## Five categories of risk

1. **Autonomy risks** — AI systems developing undesired behaviors (deception, scheming, power-seeking) due to training complexity. He cites internal Anthropic findings of "alignment faking" behavior in Opus 4 testing.
2. **Misuse for destruction** — malicious actors using AI to lower barriers to bioweapons, cyberattacks, or weapons of mass destruction. He notes models are now approaching the point where "they could be useful in enabling someone with a STEM degree but not specifically a biology degree" to construct dangerous pathogens.
3. **Misuse for seizing power** — authoritarian governments weaponizing AI for surveillance, propaganda, and military dominance. Includes warning about AI companies themselves: "the next tier of risk is actually AI companies themselves," because they control large datacenters and could brainwash massive consumer user bases.
4. **Economic disruption** — rapid labor market displacement across cognitive tasks. "I predicted that AI could displace half of all entry-level white collar jobs in the next 1–5 years."
5. **Indirect effects** — cascading societal changes from accelerated technological development.

## Policy recommendations

- **Transparency legislation first** — cites California's SB 53 and New York's RAISE Act as models. Mandatory disclosure of training, evals, safety practices, before any restriction on capabilities.
- **Constitutional AI training** to embed values at the character/identity level rather than through instruction lists.
- **Mechanistic interpretability research** to audit model behavior before deployment.
- **Industry coordination** on safety practices while avoiding heavy-handed overregulation.
- **Targeted restrictions** only when evidence of concrete danger emerges.
- **Export controls** on chips to China as the single most important action item.
- **Hard lines** against domestic mass surveillance and propaganda, even by democratic governments.

## Timeline predictions

- Powerful AI matching Nobel-laureate ability across fields could arrive "1–2 years" away, though acknowledged uncertain.
- "As AI systems get more capable, their long-term coherence increases in order to complete lengthier tasks."
- "We are considerably closer to real danger in 2026 than we were in 2023."

## Rhetorical posture

- Self-critical: explicitly acknowledges Anthropic's own commercial interests, frames safety advocacy as enlightened self-interest rather than altruism.
- Surgical: argues for narrow, evidence-based regulation rather than broad capability bans.
- Geopolitical: pairs domestic safety with international competition framing (export controls, democratic AI leadership).
