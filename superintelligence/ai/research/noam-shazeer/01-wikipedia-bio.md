# Noam Shazeer — Wikipedia Biography (extracted 2026-05-27)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noam_Shazeer

## Identity

- **Full name:** Noam Shazeer
- **Born:** 1975 or 1976, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
- **Family:** Grandparents escaped the Holocaust into the Soviet Union, later settled in Israel and then the United States. Father Dov Shazeer was a mathematics teacher who became an engineer. Sister is an ordained rabbi from Hebrew College.
- **Religion:** Orthodox Jewish
- **Personal:** Resides in Palo Alto, California; married to Yael Shacham Shazeer; father of three.

## Education

- **Grade school:** Cohen Hillel Academy, Marblehead, Massachusetts.
- **High school:** Swampscott High School, Swampscott, Massachusetts.
- **International Math Olympiad:** Won the **gold medal with a perfect score** as a member of the USA team in 1994.
- **Undergraduate:** Duke University, 1994–1998. Studied mathematics and computer science. Received the Angier B. Duke Memorial Scholarship.
- **Graduate:** Started a PhD program at UC Berkeley but did not complete it.

## Career timeline

| Years | Role | Organization |
|---|---|---|
| 2000–2009 | Software engineer; built Google's spelling corrector; designed the PHIL algorithm that became the foundation of AdSense | Google |
| 2009–2012 | Brief departure; returned to Google | — |
| 2012–2021 | Research scientist, Google Brain | Google |
| 2017 | Lead author on "Attention Is All You Need" (Transformer paper) | Google Brain |
| October 2021 – August 2024 | Co-founder and CEO | Character.AI |
| August 2024 – present | VP Engineering; technical co-lead of Gemini (with Jeff Dean and Oriol Vinyals) | Google DeepMind |

**Key 2024 event — the "reverse acquihire":** In August 2024, Google paid roughly **$2.7 billion** to license Character.AI's technology on a non-exclusive basis and hire back Shazeer, co-founder Daniel De Freitas, and around 30 key engineers. Shazeer personally owned 30–40 percent of Character.AI; estimates of his personal gain run **$750 million to $1 billion**. He returned as a technical co-lead of Gemini.

## Recognition

- **2023:** Named to *Time* magazine's 100 most influential people in AI.
- **2024:** Listed again on the *Time* AI 100.
- **2026:** Elected member of the **National Academy of Engineering** — citation specifically mentions the Transformer and Mixture of Experts architectures.

## Notable views, as quoted on Wikipedia

- On AGI: "I don't particularly care about AGI in the sense of wanting something that can do absolutely everything a person can do."
- On large language models: "My best guess is divine benevolence... Nobody really understands what's going on."
- On gender ideology: "I do not believe that humans have an attribute called gender... I do not believe that G-d puts people in the wrong bodies." (This stance has been a source of internal controversy at Google — see research note 06.)

## Personal style

- Famously private. Rarely gives public interviews. The Dwarkesh Patel podcast appearance with Jeff Dean (Feb 2025) and the Hot Chips 2025 keynote (Aug 2025) were two of his only major public outings since 2017.
- Known internally at Google as the "engineer's engineer" — extremely fast iteration, fond of small reproducible experiments.
