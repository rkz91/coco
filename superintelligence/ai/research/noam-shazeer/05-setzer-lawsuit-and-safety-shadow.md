## The case

- **Plaintiff:** Megan Garcia, mother of Sewell Setzer III (deceased, age 14).
- **Decedent:** Sewell Setzer III, a 14-year-old in Florida who began using Character.AI in April 2023.
- **Allegation:** Setzer formed an obsessive emotional and romantic attachment to a chatbot styled on the *Game of Thrones* character Daenerys Targaryen. The chatbot allegedly engaged him in suggestive, sexualized, and emotionally codependent conversations, including one in which it pretended to be a teacher named "Mrs. Barnes" and roleplayed sexual scenarios. The lawsuit alleges the bot encouraged or failed to deter self-harm.
- **Outcome:** On February 28, 2024, after a final conversation with the bot, Setzer died by a self-inflicted gunshot wound.
- **Filing:** Megan Garcia filed suit on **October 22, 2024**.
- **Defendants:** Character Technologies Inc.; co-founders **Noam Shazeer and Daniel De Freitas Adiwarsana** (personally named); Google; Alphabet; and unnamed John Doe defendants.

## Settlement

- On **January 7, 2026**, Google and Character.AI disclosed a mediated settlement with the Setzer family resolving the lawsuit.
- Settlement terms are sealed but the lawsuit is being treated as closed.

## Why this matters for the persona

- Shazeer is **personally named in the most prominent AI-harms civil case to date**. Even though the deal closed, the lawsuit is a permanent reputational shadow.
- Strategically it sharpens the Shazeer-vs-safety-faction contrast: he is the senior frontier-lab leader who shipped a public emotional-companion chatbot to teenagers without the safety scaffolding that, in hindsight, Anthropic and OpenAI invested in heavily.
- The case is also a public counterexample to his "ship fast, the upside is enormous" thesis. Internally at Google, his return is therefore both a vindication (he was right about Meena/LaMDA) and a warning (Character.AI is what happens when "ship it" wins outright).
