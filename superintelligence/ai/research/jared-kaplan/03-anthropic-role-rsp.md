# Anthropic role and the Responsible Scaling Policy

## Co-founder slot

Anthropic was incorporated in early 2021 by seven core co-founders who departed OpenAI together:

- Dario Amodei (CEO)
- Daniela Amodei (President)
- Tom Brown
- Jack Clark
- Jared Kaplan
- Sam McCandlish
- Chris Olah

Ben Mann is named in some lists as an eighth co-founder. Across reporting, Kaplan is universally listed as a co-founder and the Chief Science Officer.

## Chief Science Officer

As CSO, Kaplan is reported across Anthropic's public materials and external profiles to lead the company's scientific research direction, including:

- Scaling-laws-derived strategy for model design and training.
- Constitutional AI research line — the harmlessness-from-AI-feedback methodology.
- Reinforcement learning research, including RLHF and post-training.

## Constitutional AI authorship

The 2022 paper "Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback" (arXiv 2212.08073) was drafted by Yuntao Bai and Jared Kaplan, with Kaplan credited as developing the main ideas in discussion with Yuntao Bai, Amanda Askell, and others. Kaplan publicly describes Constitutional AI as "an AI system that supervises itself" using a written set of principles, contrasted against pure human-feedback RLHF.

URL: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.08073

## Responsible Scaling Officer

- Appointed in October 2024, succeeding Sam McCandlish.
- Authority: set the safety assessments and precautions adopted before any model release.
- Anthropic frames the role as enforcing the company's Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP) — the first public framework among major AI labs to formally bind safety measures to model capabilities at each AI Safety Level (ASL).

## ASL-3 decision for Claude 4 / Opus 4 (May 2025)

TIME's 100 Most Influential People in AI 2025 reporting and surrounding coverage record the following:

- Internal Anthropic testing in early 2025 found that Claude Opus 4 might be capable enough at biology to materially assist amateur scientists in developing a biological weapon.
- Kaplan, in his Responsible Scaling Officer role, decided to release Claude Opus 4 / the Claude 4 family under AI Safety Level 3 protections — including classifier-based filters for chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear (CBRN) content, jailbreak resistance, and additional protections against model weight exfiltration.
- Anthropic characterised the ASL-3 designation as "a precautionary and provisional action" pending more definitive risk evidence.
- Kaplan's reported quote on biological capability: "You could try to synthesise something like COVID or a more dangerous version of the flu — and basically, our modeling suggests that this might be possible."

## February 2026 RSP update

In February 2026, Anthropic updated the Responsible Scaling Policy. Coverage described the update as dropping the "hard limit" prohibiting more capable training without proven safety measures, while Kaplan publicly stated that the company is "not making any kind of U-turn" on safety. The update was framed by Anthropic as a refinement of the RSP, not an abandonment.
