## Quotes attributed to Kaplan

On biological capability of Claude Opus 4:

> "You could try to synthesize something like COVID or a more dangerous version of the flu — and basically, our modeling suggests that this might be possible."

On the rationale for ASL-3:

- Kaplan said Claude Opus 4 performed more effectively than prior models at advising novices on how to produce biological weapons in internal testing.
- Kaplan decided Anthropic would release the model under ASL-3, requiring stronger safeguards against jailbreaking and additional internal measures to prevent leakage.

On the framing across Time 100 AI 2025 coverage:

- Kaplan is credited with "decisive actions in enforcing safety protocols" and a "race to the top" in AI safety practices.
- The TIME 100 entry frames his physicist's instinct as the through-line — "intelligence scales in a predictable, almost physical way."

## What ASL-3 protections include (per Anthropic and reporting)

- Classifier-based filters for CBRN content on inputs and outputs.
- Jailbreak prevention measures and red-team-resistant guardrails.
- Internal security upgrades to reduce the risk of model-weight exfiltration.
- Release-side governance: Kaplan as Responsible Scaling Officer signs off before a model crosses an ASL threshold.
