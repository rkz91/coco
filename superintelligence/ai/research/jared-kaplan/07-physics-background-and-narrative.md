# Physics background and intellectual narrative

## Why the physics background matters for the persona

Kaplan is the rare frontier-AI lead whose intellectual formation is in theoretical high-energy physics rather than in CS or applied ML. He brings the physicist's bias for empirical regularities — patterns visible in the data that hold across orders of magnitude — to a field where most researchers reason through architectures and algorithms. The scaling-laws paper is the canonical artifact of this stance: ten authors, seven orders of magnitude, one power law. The physicist's question — "is there a simple equation here?" — is the move.

## Specific physics research lines

From the Johns Hopkins faculty directory and NSF / Sloan grant records:

- **Effective field theory** — the framework for reasoning about how physics at one scale generates effective laws at another. Directly analogous to "how does training-scale behavior generate effective capability at deployment scale?"
- **Particle physics and cosmology** — the long-distance limits of fundamental theories.
- **Scattering amplitudes** — modern reformulations of perturbative quantum field theory.
- **Conformal field theory and the bootstrap programme** — using the consistency conditions of a theory (conformal symmetry, unitarity, crossing) to constrain or determine correlation functions without solving a microscopic model. The bootstrap mindset is closely related to "constrain the model by empirical regularities the data must satisfy."
- **AdS/CFT correspondence and quantum gravity** — the holographic duality. Kaplan's stated current academic interest is using CFT to learn about quantum gravity via the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## How the physics shows up in his AI framings

- Scaling laws as physics-style empirical regularities — a power law over seven orders of magnitude is treated as the kind of result a physicist trusts in a way an ML researcher trained on benchmarks might not.
- Architecture as a second-order variable within a wide band — analogous to universality classes: many microscopic differences flow to the same macroscopic behaviour.
- "The dumbest questions can lead to the biggest breakthroughs" — the YC 2025 framing parallels the physicist's reflex to check whether a trend obviously holds at larger scale before reasoning about why it should.
- Safety scaling with capability — the RSP's structure mirrors a renormalisation-group sensibility: as you flow to larger capabilities, you need new effective rules; the same safety category does not apply across scales.
