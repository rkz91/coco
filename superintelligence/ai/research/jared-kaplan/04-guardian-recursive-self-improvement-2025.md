## Verbatim quotes attributed to Jared Kaplan

On the decision whether to allow AIs to train the next generation of AIs:

> "That's the thing that we view as maybe the biggest decision or scariest thing to do… once no one's involved in the process, you don't really know."

On the risk profile of recursive self-improvement:

> "One is do you lose control over it? Do you even know what the AIs are doing?"

On the centring question for AI development:

> "The main question there is: are the AIs good for humanity? Are they helpful? Are they going to be harmless?"

## Reported stances (paraphrased in coverage)

- Humanity will need to decide, sometime between 2027 and 2030, whether to allow AI systems to recursively self-improve.
- Kaplan calls this "the biggest decision yet" and "extremely high-stakes."
- He predicts AI will be capable of "most white-collar work" within two to three years from the interview date.
- He continues to argue that scaling laws extrapolated through post-training and reinforcement learning predict capability gains comparable to or faster than hardware cycles. The framing is consistent with his earlier YC AI Startup School talk.
