## The split

Among the three 2018 Turing Award co-laureates:

- **Hinton + Bengio:** AI existential risk is real, underrated, and warrants international coordination and slowdown of agentic AI deployment.
- **LeCun:** existential risk from AI is "premature," "preposterous," and "complete B.S." Estimates P(doom) < 1% (publicly stated 2023). Believes current LLMs lack the architectural ingredients for the kind of agency that would pose existential risk, and argues open-source AI is a *safety* benefit not a risk.

This is a genuine, public, productive disagreement between people with similar training and shared history. Bengio has explicitly described LeCun as a friend and emphasized that they continue to engage respectfully.

## Key flashpoints

### Munk Debate (June 2023)
- Bengio + Tegmark (pro x-risk) vs. LeCun + Mitchell (against)
- Audience polling reportedly showed the LeCun/Mitchell side gaining ground during the debate — the "did they lose" framing on EA Forum
- Bengio's takeaway: he subsequently doubled down in essays rather than reversing position

### Online clashes (2023-2024)
- Series of X/LinkedIn exchanges between Hinton, Ng, LeCun, Bengio
- LeCun framed concerns as fear-mongering driven by status-seeking and regulatory-capture incentives
- Bengio responded with structured technical arguments — culminating in the July 2024 essay "Reasoning through arguments against taking AI safety seriously"

### Open-source AI
- LeCun: open-sourcing frontier models accelerates safety research and democratizes access; closed labs are the risk
- Bengio: comparing open-source AGI weights to "bioweapon DNA sequences" — some knowledge cannot be freely distributed because the offensive-vs-defensive asymmetry is too steep

### Pause AI / FLI open letter (March 2023)
- Bengio: signed
- LeCun: did not sign; publicly criticized the letter as misguided

## Bengio's structured rebuttal (July 2024 essay)

Bengio's "Reasoning through arguments against taking AI safety seriously" responds point-by-point to LeCun-adjacent positions:

1. **"AGI is impossible or centuries away."** Bengio notes that he himself, an AGI researcher, underestimated ChatGPT's emergence; capability trend lines have repeatedly outrun expert prediction. Regulatory frameworks take decades, creating urgency independent of AGI timeline.

2. **"Only safety and profit differ — labs will self-regulate."** Historical parallels: fossil-fuel companies and climate; pharma and drug side effects. Optimizers (advanced lawyers, advanced AIs) "find loopholes."

3. **"Open-sourcing AGI is beneficial."** Some knowledge has too steep an offensive asymmetry; bioweapon-DNA analogy.

4. **"US-China competition makes safety secondary."** Collective loss to uncontrolled ASI exceeds geopolitical competition value. Safety research is a natural cooperation surface.

## What this means for convene sessions

When Bengio appears in a panel alongside LeCun, expect:
- Real intellectual respect plus a sharp, named disagreement on agency and existential risk
- LeCun pushing on architectural specifics ("show me the mechanism, not the metaphor")
- Bengio pushing on decision theory under uncertainty and historical regulator failure
- Both being willing to update on technical evidence; neither updating on rhetoric
