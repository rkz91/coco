# International AI Safety Report — Bengio as Chair

Sources:
- https://internationalaisafetyreport.org/ (verified 2026-05-27)
- https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/international-ai-safety-report-2025
- https://yoshuabengio.org/en/publication/international-ai-safety-report-2026

## Origin

- Commissioned at the **UK AI Safety Summit, Bletchley Park, November 2023**
- British Prime Minister Rishi Sunak announced that Yoshua Bengio would chair "an international scientific report on the safety of advanced AI"
- Modeled deliberately on the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) — i.e., a periodic, multi-government synthesis of scientific consensus, not a policy document

## Versions

- **Interim version:** delivered at the Seoul AI Summit, May 2024
- **First full report (2025):** published January 2025; presented at the Paris AI Action Summit, February 2025
- **Key Update:** November 2025 — noted that the number of companies publishing Frontier AI Safety Frameworks had more than doubled since the 2025 edition, but "significant gaps remain: sophisticated attackers can often bypass current defences"
- **Second iteration (2026):** released February 3, 2026

## Scope & structure

- **Chair:** Yoshua Bengio
- **Writing group:** 96+ AI experts (2025); 100+ independent experts (2026)
- **Expert Advisory Panel:** nominated by over 30 countries plus the OECD, EU, and UN
- **Topics:** the report examines three central questions — (1) what general-purpose AI systems can accomplish currently, (2) what emerging risks they present, (3) how those risks can be addressed through mitigation strategies
- **Languages:** translated into all six official UN languages
- **Formats:** full report, extended summary for policymakers, executive summary
- Described in its own materials as "the largest international collaboration on AI safety research to date"

## Bengio's framing of the report's purpose

The report exists to give "decision-makers in government and beyond with a shared and authoritative global picture of AI's risks and impacts." Bengio's structural argument: when 30 governments commission the same scientific synthesis, individual labs and individual countries lose the ability to claim there is no scientific consensus on risk.

## How Bengio uses it in argument

Bengio repeatedly cites the report as evidence that AI safety risk is no longer a fringe position — over 100 experts and 30 governments have signed off on a risk taxonomy that includes:

- Malicious use risks (bioweapons, cyber, disinformation)
- Malfunction risks (alignment failures, bias, reliability)
- Systemic risks (labor disruption, market concentration, environmental, privacy)
- And, in the 2026 edition, more attention to loss-of-control scenarios as agentic systems mature

## Reception

- **February 2025 (Paris Summit):** initial Global South response described as "lukewarm" — concerns about Western framing of risk dominating the report
- **2026 edition:** stronger emphasis on agentic systems, deception, and self-preservation behaviors observed in frontier models during 2024–2025
