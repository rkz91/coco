# Yoshua Bengio — Wikipedia Biographical Notes

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoshua_Bengio (verified 2026-05-27)

## Identity

- Born March 5, 1964, in Paris, France
- Jewish Moroccan-origin family; emigrated to Canada in his childhood
- Father Carlo Bengio: pharmacist and Sephardic theater playwright in Montreal
- Mother Célia Moreno: Moroccan theater actress, co-founded a multimedia theater troupe in Montreal in 1980
- Brother: Samy Bengio, senior director of AI/ML research at Apple
- Canadian citizen

## Education

- Bachelor of Engineering (Computer Engineering), MSc (CS), and PhD (CS) — all from McGill University
- Doctoral advisor: Renato De Mori
- Dissertation: "Artificial Neural Networks and their Application to Sequence Recognition" (1991)
- Postdoctoral fellowship at MIT under Michael I. Jordan
- Postdoctoral fellowship at AT&T Bell Labs

## Academic positions

- Faculty member at Université de Montréal since 1993 (Full Professor, Computer Science and Operations Research)
- Founder and Scientific Director of Mila (Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms / Quebec AI Institute) — served as scientific director until 2025, transitioning to Founder and Scientific Advisor role
- Co-directs the Learning in Machines & Brains program at the Canadian Institute for Advanced Research (CIFAR)
- Canada CIFAR AI Chair
- Special Advisor and founding scientific director of IVADO

## Industry

- Co-founded Element AI in October 2016 (Montreal-based AI incubator)
- Element AI acquired by ServiceNow in November 2020; Bengio remained as advisor
- Scientific and technical advisor for Recursion Pharmaceuticals
- Scientific advisor for Valence Discovery

## Research contributions

Considered one of three people most responsible for deep-learning advancement in the 1990s–2000s (alongside Geoffrey Hinton and Yann LeCun — collectively called the "Godfathers of AI"). Major technical contributions:

- Neural probabilistic language models and word embeddings
- Neural machine translation
- Attention models (Bahdanau, Cho, Bengio, 2015 — predecessor work for the Transformer)
- Generative adversarial networks — Ian Goodfellow was his student; Bengio is widely credited as supervisor/advisor on the original GAN paper
- Denoising autoencoders
- Generative flow networks (GFlowNets)
- Formal analysis of vanishing gradients ("Learning Long-Term Dependencies with Gradient Descent is Difficult", Bengio, Simard, Frasconi, 1994)

As of November 2025, the first AI researcher with more than one million Google Scholar citations, and described on his own site as "the most-cited computer scientist worldwide, and the most-cited living scientist across all fields (by total citations)."

## Awards & honors

- 2017: Officer of the Order of Canada; Marie-Victorin Prize; Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada
- 2018: **ACM A.M. Turing Award** (shared with Geoffrey Hinton and Yann LeCun) — "for conceptual and engineering breakthroughs that have made deep neural networks a critical component of computing"
- 2019: Killam Prize
- 2020: Fellow of the Royal Society of London
- 2022: Princess of Asturias Award in Scientific Research (with LeCun, Hinton, Hassabis)
- 2023: Knight of the Legion of Honour (France); appointed to UN Scientific Advisory Board
- 2024: Time 100 Most Influential People; VinFuture Prize (grand prize, shared with Hinton, LeCun, Jensen Huang, Fei-Fei Li)
- 2025: Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (with Dally, Hinton, Hopfield, LeCun, Huang, Li); McGill Honorary Doctorate; Officer of the National Order of Quebec

## AI safety pivot (2023 onward)

- May 2023: Told BBC he felt "lost" over his life's work; called for stronger regulation; warned of bad actors with advanced AI
- March 2023: Signed Future of Life Institute open letter calling for a six-month pause on training systems more powerful than GPT-4
- November 2023: At UK AI Safety Summit, British PM Rishi Sunak announced Bengio would chair an "international scientific report on the safety of advanced AI"
- May 2024: Interim version of the report delivered at the Seoul Summit
- January 2025: First full International AI Safety Report published; February 3, 2026: second iteration of the report released
- June 3, 2025: Founded LawZero — nonprofit dedicated to safe-by-design AI; Bengio is co-president and scientific director; funded by Future of Life Institute, Open Philanthropy, Schmidt Sciences, Silicon Valley Community Foundation, Jaan Tallinn; ~$30M initial philanthropic capital
- December 2025: Publicly argued granting rights to AI systems would be a "huge mistake"; "being able to shut them down is essential for safety"
- January 2026: LawZero appointed 7 global leaders to its board and advisory bodies including former New Zealand Prime Minister Jacinda Ardern
