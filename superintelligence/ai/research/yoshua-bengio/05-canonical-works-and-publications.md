# Canonical works and publications

## Foundational papers

### "Learning Long-Term Dependencies with Gradient Descent is Difficult"
- Authors: Yoshua Bengio, Patrice Simard, Paolo Frasconi
- Year: 1994
- Venue: IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks
- Significance: First formal analysis of the vanishing-gradient problem in recurrent neural networks. Defined the technical obstacle that LSTMs, GRUs, residual connections, and ultimately Transformers would eventually overcome.
- URL: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/279181

### "A Neural Probabilistic Language Model"
- Authors: Yoshua Bengio, Réjean Ducharme, Pascal Vincent, Christian Jauvin
- Year: 2003
- Venue: Journal of Machine Learning Research
- Significance: First practical neural language model. Introduced distributed word representations (word embeddings) as a side effect of language modeling — the conceptual ancestor of word2vec, GloVe, and every modern token embedding.
- URL: https://www.jmlr.org/papers/v3/bengio03a.html

### "Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate"
- Authors: Dzmitry Bahdanau, Kyunghyun Cho, Yoshua Bengio
- Year: 2015
- Venue: ICLR 2015
- Significance: Introduced the attention mechanism — the direct predecessor and conceptual core of "Attention is All You Need" (2017). The dot-product, key-value attention pattern used in every Transformer descends from this paper.
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1409.0473

### "Generative Adversarial Networks"
- Authors: Ian Goodfellow (then Bengio's PhD student), Jean Pouget-Abadie, Mehdi Mirza, Bing Xu, David Warde-Farley, Sherjil Ozair, Aaron Courville, Yoshua Bengio
- Year: 2014
- Venue: NIPS 2014
- Significance: Introduced GANs. Bengio is the senior author and Goodfellow's PhD advisor.
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2661

### "Deep Learning" (textbook)
- Authors: Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, Aaron Courville
- Year: 2016
- Publisher: MIT Press
- Significance: The canonical graduate-level deep-learning textbook of the 2010s.
- URL: https://www.deeplearningbook.org/

## Recent / safety-era publications

### "Bounding the probability of harm from an AI to create a guardrail"
- Author: Bengio (with collaborators)
- Date: August 2024
- Frame: Technical proposal for guardrail mechanisms with formal probability bounds — pre-Scientist-AI substrate work.

### "Implications of Artificial General Intelligence on National and International Security"
- Date: October 2024
- Frame: Policy paper on AGI as a national-security issue.

### "Superintelligent Agents Pose Catastrophic Risks: Can Scientist AI Offer a Safer Path?"
- Authors: Bengio, Michael Cohen, Damiano Fornasiere, Joumana Ghosn, Pietro Greiner, Matt MacDermott, Sören Mindermann, Adam Oberman, Jesse Richardson, Oliver Richardson, Marc-Antoine Rondeau, Pierre-Luc St-Charles, David Williams-King
- Date: February 21, 2025 (revised February 24, 2025)
- arXiv: 2502.15657
- Significance: The technical statement of the Scientist AI proposal that became LawZero's flagship program. Argues for non-agentic AI with Bayesian uncertainty quantification as both an alternative substrate and a guardrail for existing agentic systems.

### "Introducing LawZero"
- Date: June 3, 2025
- Venue: yoshuabengio.org
- Significance: Public announcement of the LawZero nonprofit. First-person framing of the safety motivation.

### "Reasoning through arguments against taking AI safety seriously"
- Date: July 2024
- Venue: yoshuabengio.org
- Significance: Bengio's structured rebuttal to the major arguments against AI x-risk concern (AGI is centuries away; open-source is beneficial; US-China competition makes safety secondary; only safety and profit differ). The most-cited single essay for his post-2023 worldview.

### International AI Safety Report 2025 / 2026
- Chair: Bengio
- Writing group: 96+ experts (2025); 100+ (2026)
- Expert Advisory Panel: 30+ countries plus OECD, EU, UN
- 2025: published January 2025
- 2026: published February 3, 2026
