# Wikipedia — John Schulman (retrieved 2026-05-27)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Schulman

## Core biographical facts

- Born 1987 or 1988.
- Caltech, BS Physics, 2010.
- UC Berkeley, PhD in Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences, advised by Pieter Abbeel. Berkeley framing of his PhD years credits him with three distinct contributions: robot motion planning, robot imitation learning, and the foundation of deep reinforcement learning (TRPO, GAE, PPO).
- December 2015: co-founded OpenAI alongside Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, and others. Led the reinforcement learning team. Described as the "architect" of ChatGPT.
- August 2024: left OpenAI for Anthropic. His public reason was to "deepen his focus on AI alignment and return to more hands-on technical work." At Anthropic he was reunited with Jan Leike, the former OpenAI superalignment co-lead.
- February 2025: left Anthropic after roughly five to six months. Joined Thinking Machines Lab as Chief Scientist alongside Mira Murati (founder/CEO, former OpenAI CTO) and Barret Zoph (CTO, former OpenAI research leader).
- 2025: received the Mark Bingham Award for Excellence in Achievement by Young Alumni from UC Berkeley (presented May 22, 2025).

## Significance

- Schulman is the canonical reinforcement-learning algorithmist of the deep-learning era. TRPO (2015), GAE (2015), and PPO (2017) are his lineage. PPO is the most widely deployed RL algorithm of the last decade.
- His applied work — leading post-training and RLHF for ChatGPT — is his most consequential industry contribution. He is consistently cited as the person who turned the GPT base model into a usable assistant.
- After Jan Leike's departure from OpenAI, Schulman became head of OpenAI's alignment science effort (post-training team). He held that role only briefly before leaving for Anthropic.

## What Wikipedia does NOT capture

- The specific Anthropic exit reasoning is not on Wikipedia. His Feb 7, 2025 X post is the primary source.
- Wikipedia does not discuss his views on hallucination as a reward-modeling problem; that comes from his April 2023 Berkeley talk and his May 2024 Dwarkesh appearance.
