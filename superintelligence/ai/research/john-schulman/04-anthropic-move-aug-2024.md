# John Schulman → Anthropic (August 5, 2024)

Sources:
- https://techcrunch.com/2024/08/05/openai-co-founder-leaves-for-anthropic/
- https://www.cnbc.com/2024/08/06/openai-co-founder-john-schulman-says-he-will-join-rival-anthropic.html
- https://the-decoder.com/john-schulman-openai-co-founder-and-key-chatgpt-developer-leaves-for-anthropic/

## What happened

After nearly nine years at OpenAI, John Schulman announced on August 5-6, 2024 that he was leaving the company to join Anthropic. At the time of his departure, he was head of OpenAI's alignment science team — the role created after Jan Leike resigned in May 2024. Schulman had only held the alignment-science lead role for a few months.

## His stated reasoning (X post)

"I've decided to pursue this goal at Anthropic, where I believe I can gain new perspectives and do research alongside people deeply engaged with the topics I'm most interested in."

He framed the move as a chance to "deepen his focus on AI alignment" and "return to more hands-on technical work." The implication, picked up by all the news coverage: the alignment science lead role had pulled him too far from the bench, and he wanted to write code again. The secondary implication: Anthropic was where the alignment-aligned cohort had already coalesced (Leike, Amodei siblings, Schiefer, etc.).

## OpenAI's response

Sam Altman, on X: "John is a brilliant researcher, a deep thinker about product and society, and mostly, you are a great friend to all of us."

OpenAI corporate statement: "We're grateful for John's contributions as a founding team member at OpenAI and his dedicated efforts in advancing alignment research."

After Schulman's exit, only three of OpenAI's eleven original founders remained at the company.

## Reunion with Jan Leike

At Anthropic, Schulman re-joined Jan Leike, who had left OpenAI three months earlier publicly criticizing OpenAI's safety culture. The coverage framed this as the consolidation of an "OpenAI alignment diaspora" at Anthropic — by August 2024, Anthropic had absorbed Leike, Schulman, William Saunders, and several others who had publicly worried about OpenAI's direction.

## Why this matters for the persona

- His stated motivation — alignment plus hands-on technical work — is the most reliable signal of what he optimizes for as a researcher: he picks the role that puts him closest to the algorithm, not the role that puts him closest to executive influence.
- The brevity of his Anthropic tenure (six months) makes the August 2024 reasoning more important than the February 2025 departure for understanding his stable preferences. The Thinking Machines move is best read as: he found an environment that satisfied both the "hands-on" and "do alignment via post-training" axes even more directly than Anthropic.
