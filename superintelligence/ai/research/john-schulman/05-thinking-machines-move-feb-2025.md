## What happened

On February 6-7, 2025, John Schulman announced his departure from Anthropic after roughly five to six months. He joined Mira Murati's stealth-mode startup, now publicly named Thinking Machines Lab, as Chief Scientist. Barret Zoph, who had left OpenAI the same day Murati did (in September 2024), serves as CTO. The lab also pulled in Lilian Weng, Jonathan Lachman, Christian Gibson, and Mario Saltarelli — together, roughly 30 senior researchers, weighted heavily toward former OpenAI post-training and infrastructure staff.

## Schulman's stated reasoning (X post, February 7, 2025)

The status URL: https://x.com/johnschulman2/status/1891924467711926531

The tweet text: "Excited to build a new AI research lab with some of my favorite former colleagues and some great new ones. Looking forward to sharing more in the coming weeks."

Earlier in the same thread, regarding his Anthropic exit: "Leaving wasn't easy because I enjoyed the stimulating research environment and the kind and talented people I was working with, but I decided to go with another opportunity that I found extremely compelling."

He did not publicly elaborate on what specifically about TML was more compelling than Anthropic. The most plausible read, supported by Murati's own public framing of TML, is that TML offered a "build the whole stack the way we want from day one" greenfield, with a research-first, product-second mandate that mirrors OpenAI circa 2017-2019 — the era Schulman has described in interviews as his most productive.

## Mira Murati's framing of TML

Murati's public framing of Thinking Machines Lab, at the February 2025 unveiling and at the October 2025 Tinker launch, emphasizes three things:
1. AI systems should be "more widely understood, customizable, and generally capable" — i.e., open the post-training layer to people outside frontier labs.
2. The lab credits its founders with prior work on ChatGPT, Character.AI, Mistral, PyTorch, OpenAI Gym, Fairseq, and Segment Anything — a deliberate signal that the team is a post-training and tooling alumni reunion, not a scaling lab.
3. Research and engineering have to share a roof; the wall between them inside OpenAI is treated as a mistake to avoid.

## Significance for the persona

- Schulman's TML role is Chief Scientist. He is no longer "the alignment lead" in title; he is the chief technical voice on the research agenda. This matters for the cell assignment: his cell role is `lead-driver` because he is back in the position where his stances on RL, reward modeling, and post-training shape the entire lab's roadmap, not just one team's.
- The TML move closes the "research-first vs product-first" question the Anthropic move did not fully resolve. Schulman is now at the lab most explicitly defining itself as research-first with productization second (Tinker is infrastructure for other researchers, not a consumer product).
- Pairs_well_with relationships in the persona — with Murati, Zoph, Weng, and Abbeel — are now organizational facts, not inferred affinities.
