# Podcast and talk appearances (2024–2026)

## Lex Fridman Podcast #490 — "State of AI in 2026" (February 1, 2026)

Source: https://lexfridman.com/ai-sota-2026/, https://lexfridman.com/ai-sota-2026-transcript/, https://sebastianraschka.com/blog/2026/state-of-ai-interview.html

4.5-hour conversation with Lex Fridman and Nathan Lambert (post-training lead at Allen Institute for AI). One of the most influential AI podcasts of early 2026.

Topics covered:

- Geopolitics of the AI race; US vs China LLM competition
- The "DeepSeek moment" of January 2025 — DeepSeek R1 release surprising the field with near-SOTA performance at much lower training cost
- How leading LLMs are actually trained, scaled, and post-trained
- Open vs closed models — Raschka predicts more open model builders in 2026 than 2025, "a lot of the notable ones will be in China"
- Coding assistants and tooling
- Emerging research directions; AGI timelines, compute, long-term societal implications

Raschka's role on the panel: the architecture/from-scratch implementer voice. Lambert is the post-training / RLHF voice. Fridman moderates.

## Interconnects (Aug 1, 2024) — Nathan Lambert interview

Source: https://www.interconnects.ai/p/interviewing-sebastian-raschka

Topics: open LLMs, Llama 3.1 release, AI education, fine-tuning.

Key quotes from this interview:

> "I almost write it for myself...I find it interesting." — on why he writes books

> "[LitGPT is] a NanoGPT from Andrej Karpathy, but for all types of LLMs."

> "fine tuning, start changing things or training and fine tuning" — his preference over model merging

> On running Llama 3.1 405B: "eight H100s...probably more than a hundred bucks an hour"

> On testing: he uses simple repeated questions to catch implementation errors like corrupted KV caches — issues "easy to miss but critical to fix"
