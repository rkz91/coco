# Sebastian Raschka — biography and career

Source: https://sebastianraschka.com/ (about page), https://2026.pycon.de/keynote-sebastian-raschka/, https://www.manning.com/books/build-a-large-language-model-from-scratch, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sebastian_Raschka

## Real name

Sebastian Raschka, PhD. Twitter handle: @rasbt.

## Current role (2026)

Staff Research Engineer / LLM Research Engineer at Lightning AI. He has been at Lightning AI since 2022, where he works on LitGPT and on bridging educational content with the company's training framework (PyTorch Lightning / Fabric).

His self-description on sebastianraschka.com positions him as an "LLM Research Engineer" with "over a decade of artificial intelligence experience" focused on "LLM research and the development of high-performance AI systems, with a deep focus on practical, code-driven implementations."

## Past affiliations

- **Lightning AI** — joined 2022 as Senior / Staff Research Engineer. Maintains LitGPT (the company's open-source LLM library) and contributes to Lightning Fabric. Continues to write books and the Ahead of AI newsletter as part of his role.
- **University of Wisconsin–Madison** — Assistant Professor of Statistics (2018–2022). Taught statistical learning and deep learning courses. Many of his early educational artifacts (the Python Machine Learning book series, mlxtend) come out of this period.
- **Michigan State University** — PhD in Quantitative Biology and Molecular Biology (Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology). Dissertation work focused on computational biology / drug discovery, using ML to predict protein-ligand interactions. Defended 2017.
- He grew up in Germany and writes regularly about returning to Germany for conferences (PyCon DE 2026 is being held in Darmstadt).

## Books authored (in order)

1. **Python Machine Learning** — Packt, 2015, with subsequent editions (2nd ed. 2017, 3rd ed. 2019 with Vahid Mirjalili). One of the most widely sold ML textbooks of the 2010s; translated into 14+ languages.
2. **Machine Learning with PyTorch and Scikit-Learn** (Packt, 2022, with Yuxi Liu and Vahid Mirjalili). 770 pages. Essentially the spiritual successor to Python Machine Learning, rebuilt on PyTorch.
3. **Machine Learning Q and AI** — No Starch Press, 2024. 264 pages. 30 essential questions on modern ML/AI.
4. **Build a Large Language Model (From Scratch)** — Manning, September 2024. Solo author. ISBN 978-1633437166. 368 pages. Bestseller; the canonical "build it in PyTorch to understand it" book of the LLM era.
5. **Build a Reasoning Model (From Scratch)** — Manning, 2024/2025 (in-progress / staggered release). ISBN 9781633434677. Sequel covering inference-time scaling, self-refinement, GRPO, distillation for reasoning.

## Newsletter

- **Ahead of AI** (magazine.sebastianraschka.com / Substack). 191,000+ subscribers as of mid-2026. Publishes roughly twice monthly with deep technical analyses of new LLM architectures, research paper roundups, and an annual end-of-year State of LLMs review.

## Public-facing 2026 keynote

- **PyCon DE & PyData 2026, Darmstadt, April 14–16 2026** — keynote "LLMs in 2026 — From Architecture to Production." Covers GPT-style models in pure PyTorch, attention mechanisms, training loops, scaling across hardware, Python vs Rust/C++/CUDA boundary, reasoning models, inference-time scaling, RL techniques.
