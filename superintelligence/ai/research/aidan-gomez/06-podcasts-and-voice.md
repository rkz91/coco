## 20VC direct quotes / paraphrases

- AI agent hype is **"100% justified."** (direct quote)
- On scaling: adding compute improves models but is **"the dumbest"** approach. Emphasis on smaller, specialized models for specific use cases.
- On the gain source: **"Pretty much all of the major gains... have come from data improvements"** through better scraping and parsing.
- System-2 / reasoning is the bottleneck: models must respond immediately but need capability to "pause and reason through complex problems."
- Vision beyond AGI: rather than chasing AGI, the priority is **"driving productivity for the world and making humans more effective, able to do more."**

## Signature framings (compiled from interviews)

- **Enterprise vs consumer:** "Companies needed private deployment, industry-specific fine-tuning, and seamless integration with existing data infrastructure. That was a different product than consumer chatbots."
- **AGI skepticism:** "Everyone's obsessed with AGI timelines and whether AI will be sentient. But the actual AI revolution happening right now is much more prosaic."
- **RAG economics:** "RAG fundamentally changes the economics of enterprise AI. You don't need a $100 million training run every time your product catalog changes."
- **Data privacy as moat:** "We realized early that enterprises wouldn't adopt AI if it meant losing control of their data. Our deployment model lets them run models inside their own infrastructure."
- **Multilingual mandate:** "There are 7,000 languages in the world. If AI only works well in English, we're building technology that excludes billions of people."

## Voice characteristics

- Frames arguments through **pragmatism vs hype** — "boring, reliable infrastructure" over "excitement-generating breakthroughs."
- Uses **contrasts** (consumer vs enterprise, headlines vs actual impact) to position Cohere's "unsexy differentiation" as strategically superior.
- Implicitly critiques OpenAI / Anthropic for API-only models that require external data transmission, and for English-centric design that ignores global markets.
- Plain English, calm, founder-confident. Not hype-y. Will say "the actual revolution is prosaic" and mean it.
- Often punctuates with concrete enterprise customer references (RBC, Oracle, Fujitsu, etc.) — uses logos as proof rather than benchmarks.

## Notes for synthesis

- The "AGI is overhyped, productivity is the prize" stance is consistent across every interview from 2024 through early 2026.
- He explicitly distances Cohere from "building God" framings — that quote landed on the MAD Podcast in 2025.
- The "sovereign AI" framing crystallized in 2025-2026 around Canadian government funding, Aleph Alpha acquisition, Saab partnership, and Spain dialogue. National-scale data residency is now a Gomez talking point, not just a feature.
