# Source 01 — Wikipedia: Aidan Gomez

Fetched: 2026-05-27
URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aidan_Gomez

## Biographical facts

- British-Canadian computer scientist specializing in artificial intelligence and natural language processing.
- Grew up in Brighton, Ontario, Canada.
- Bachelor's degree in computer science and mathematics from the University of Toronto.
- PhD in computer science from the University of Oxford, granted 2024. (Long deferred while building Cohere.)
- Birth year not explicitly stated on Wikipedia; commonly cited as 1995-1996 based on his being 20 years old at Google Brain in 2017.

## Google Brain & "Attention Is All You Need"

- At age 20 in 2017, Gomez interned at Google Brain.
- Co-authored the landmark paper "Attention Is All You Need" as one of eight authors (Vaswani et al., NeurIPS 2017).
- Wikipedia explicitly notes the paper is "credited with changing the AI industry and helping lead to the creation of ChatGPT."
- The paper introduced the transformer architecture, now fundamental to large language models.
- Gomez was the youngest author on the paper.

## Cohere

- Co-founded Cohere in 2019 with Ivan Zhang and Nick Frosst.
- CEO since founding.
- As of September 2025, Cohere raised approximately $1.6 billion USD at valuations exceeding $7 billion USD.

## Other affiliations

- Founded FOR.ai in 2017 (precursor to Cohere Labs / Cohere For AI).
- April 2025: Elected to Rivian's board of directors.
- Cohere Labs (formerly Cohere For AI) released Aya, an open-source multilingual LLM.

## Public stances cited on Wikipedia

- Gomez has stated existential AI risks are overblown.
- Real dangers, in his view, involve "automated spread of misinformation on social media."
- Claimed "the United States would win the AI arms race over China."
