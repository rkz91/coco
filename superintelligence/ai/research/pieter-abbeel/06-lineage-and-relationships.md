## Peers / collaborators (lateral)

- **Sergey Levine** (Berkeley) — closest peer; co-authored SAC, many manipulation papers. Both on the BAIR leadership team.
- **Ken Goldberg** (Berkeley) — robotics elder, frequent joint events. They did the 2023 TechCrunch "generative AI applications" piece together.
- **Jitendra Malik** (Berkeley) — co-faculty; Robot Brains guest. The vision-first counterweight to Abbeel's RL-first frame.
- **Andrew Ng** (Stanford / DeepLearning.AI) — former advisor; still in occasional public dialog.
- **Demis Hassabis** (DeepMind) — peer at the AGI-via-embodiment level; both run physical-AI programs. Mutual respect, no public conflict.
- **Yann LeCun** (Meta) — productive disagreement. LeCun's world-models-first thesis (JEPA, V-JEPA) is a contrast to Abbeel's data-collection-first thesis. They publicly part ways on whether massive sim or massive real deployment is the bridge.

## Productive conflict / contrast personas

- **Yann LeCun**: world-models priors, "we don't need this much data" — directly contrasts with Abbeel's "deploy and collect" doctrine.
- **Noam Shazeer**: text-only scaling and Transformer-uber-alles. Abbeel's embodiment frame argues text-only is not enough; Shazeer's career bet is that it is.

## Notable absences

- Abbeel does NOT have a strong public relationship with Sam Altman (despite Covariant founders being ex-OpenAI). The Covariant founding was a clean exit from OpenAI ~2017.
- He does NOT publicly engage with the Eliezer Yudkowsky / Connor Leahy doomer axis. His AI-risk posture is implicit and operationally-grounded ("robots can break things, so reward modeling must be careful") rather than existential.
