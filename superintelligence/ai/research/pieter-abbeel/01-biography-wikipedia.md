## Birth and background

Born 1977 in Antwerp, Belgium. Belgian national. Grew up in Brasschaat. Played basketball at Sint-Michielscollege and KU Leuven.

## Education

- BS and MS in Electrical Engineering, KU Leuven (2000).
- PhD in Computer Science, Stanford University (2008). **First PhD student of Andrew Ng.** Dissertation: "Apprenticeship Learning and Reinforcement Learning with Application to Robotic Control" (August 2008).

## Academic career

- Joined UC Berkeley EECS faculty after Stanford PhD.
- Achieved tenure in 2017.
- **Current titles (2026):**
  - Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences, UC Berkeley
  - Director, Berkeley Robot Learning Lab
  - Co-Director, Berkeley AI Research (BAIR) Lab

## Industry ventures

### Gradescope (2014)
Co-founded with Arjun Singh, Sergey Karayev, Ibrahim Awwal. Online grading platform. **Acquired by Turnitin in 2018.** Reached over 500 universities.

### Covariant (2017)
Co-founded with Peter Chen, Rocky Duan, Tianhao Zhang (all ex-OpenAI). Originally named "Embodied Intelligence." Launched publicly January 2020. Builds AI for warehouse robotics — bin picking, sortation, depalletization. Underlying platform is the "Covariant Brain."

### Amazon (August 2024 - present)
Amazon licensed Covariant's robotic foundation models and hired the founders + ~25% of staff in what was characterized as a "reverse acquihire" on August 30, 2024. Reported deal value: $380M + $20M deferred licensing payment (per 2025 whistleblower complaint). Pre-deal Covariant 2023 valuation: $625M.

Abbeel joined Amazon's Fulfillment Technologies & Robotics Team as a Distinguished Scientist / VP / Scholar.

**December 2025 reorg:** When CEO Andy Jassy reshuffled Amazon's AI leadership (Rohit Prasad out, Peter DeSantis in to lead AGI), Abbeel was named **head of the frontier model research team** within the AGI organization — the base-model builders for Amazon Nova. He continues to work with the robotics team in parallel.

## Investment activity

Joined AIX Ventures as Investment Partner in 2021.

## Podcast

Hosts **The Robot Brains Podcast** (since 2020). Conversations with leading AI and robotics researchers. Continues actively in 2025–2026 with monthly+ episodes.
