# Dan Hendrycks — Biography, Education, Affiliations

Source: Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dan_Hendrycks), Berkeley EECS page (https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~hendrycks/), CAIS media page (https://safe.ai/about/media), Fortune profile (https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/scale-ai-dan-hendrycks-elon-musk-xai-safety-trump-ties/). Retrieved 2026-05-27.

## Identity

- Full name: Dan Hendrycks
- Born: Marshfield, Missouri; raised in an evangelical Christian household.
- American machine-learning researcher specializing in AI safety, robustness, and machine ethics.

## Education

- BS, University of Chicago, 2018.
- PhD, Computer Science, UC Berkeley, 2018-2022. Advisors: Jacob Steinhardt and Dawn Song.
- Cites the EA-affiliated 80,000 Hours career advisory program as the influence that shifted him from general ML toward AI safety as a career focus.

## Current affiliations (as of 2026-05-27)

- Executive Director, Center for AI Safety (CAIS). Founded the nonprofit in 2022 with Oliver Zhang. Continues to lead CAIS through 2026; CAIS has roughly 30 staff and is the primary nonprofit safety-research and policy-advocacy organization in the United States.
- Safety Advisor, xAI (Elon Musk's AI startup, founded 2023). Receives a symbolic $1 annual salary and holds no equity to avoid conflict of interest. Role announced July 2023.
- Advisor, Scale AI. Joined November 2024, also at a $1 symbolic salary. Public framing: improving safety practices at major AI data labeling and evaluation providers.

## Past affiliations

- UC Berkeley graduate researcher (Steinhardt and Song labs), 2018-2022.
- Various research internships during PhD that produced GELU (2016) and OOD-detection work.

## Public reputation

- TIME100 Most Influential People in AI list, September 2023.
- Profile in The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, Bloomberg, The Economist, Politico, Fortune over 2023-2026 framing him as the central figure linking academic ML safety research, US AI policy, and frontier-lab safety advisory.
- Frequent op-ed author in The Economist by-invitation column (2025) and the AI Frontiers blog (2025).
- Critics describe his profile as "the high priest of AI safety" (Wolfshead Online, 2024) reflecting both his prominence and the political polarization around his work.

## Notes for synthesis

- The xAI advisor role is a known point of friction. Hendrycks himself addresses the conflict-of-interest perception by taking the symbolic salary; commentators in the safety community periodically raise it.
- He is consciously building the safety field beyond Effective Altruism, stating publicly "EA != AI safety. AI safety has outgrown the EA community." (X post, late 2023, archived at https://twitter.com/DanHendrycks/status/1728564429728674127).
