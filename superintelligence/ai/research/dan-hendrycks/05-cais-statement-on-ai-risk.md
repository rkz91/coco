## The statement (full text)

> "Mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority alongside other societal-scale risks such as pandemics and nuclear war."

Twenty-two words. Released May 30, 2023.

## Signatories at release

Over 100 AI professors and lab leaders, including:
- Geoffrey Hinton and Yoshua Bengio (the two most-cited computer scientists, both Turing laureates)
- Demis Hassabis (CEO, Google DeepMind)
- Sam Altman (CEO, OpenAI)
- Dario Amodei (CEO, Anthropic)
- Ilya Sutskever (then OpenAI chief scientist)
- Bill Gates
- Plus dozens of researchers at OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, and major academic labs.

## Why the statement worked rhetorically

- Brevity: deliberately short to remove room for caveats or factional disagreement on framing.
- Pairing existential AI risk with pandemics and nuclear war — two already-canonical "global priority" risks — legitimized the placement.
- Coverage was extraordinary: front-page NYT, BBC, etc., within hours.

## Hendrycks' role and follow-up commentary

- Lead organizer of the signatures and lead spokesperson on launch day.
- To CBC News (May 30, 2023): "competition in the industry has led to a sort of 'AI arms race'... That could escalate and, like the nuclear arms race, potentially bring us to the brink of catastrophe."
- To X (May 30, 2023, https://twitter.com/DanHendrycks/status/1663475992323850242): "As stated in the first sentence of the signatory page, there are many 'important and urgent risks from AI,' not just the risk of extinction; for example, systemic bias, misinformation, malicious use, cyberattacks, and weaponization."

## Significance

- Shifted the Overton window. Pre-May 2023, taking AI existential risk seriously was a fringe academic and EA-coded position. Post-May 2023, it became a position publicly endorsed by the CEOs of every major frontier lab.
- Provided the political cover that enabled both the UK Bletchley Park AI Safety Summit (November 2023) and the formation of the UK and US AI Safety Institutes.
- Made CAIS, then a brand-new organization, the dominant nonprofit safety advocacy institution overnight.

## How Hendrycks talks about CAIS' mission

From Bloomberg profile (June 27, 2024): CAIS exists to be the nonprofit infrastructure for AI safety the way long-standing institutes are for biosecurity or nuclear non-proliferation. The thesis is that AI safety needs scale — it needs research output, policy engagement, and educational programs at institute-level rather than individual-researcher level.

From X (late 2023): "EA != AI safety. AI safety has outgrown the EA community. The world will be safer with a broad range of people tackling many different AI risks." (https://twitter.com/DanHendrycks/status/1728564429728674127)

This positions CAIS as a broader-coalition organization than the EA-coded safety groups, deliberately recruiting from national-security, policy, academic-ethics, and traditional ML-research communities.
