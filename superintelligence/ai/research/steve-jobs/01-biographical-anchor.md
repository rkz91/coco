# 01 — Biographical Anchor: Steve Jobs

Sourced primarily from Wikipedia, with cross-checks from the Steve Jobs Archive (stevejobsarchive.com), Walter Isaacson's *Steve Jobs* (Simon & Schuster, October 2011), and Brent Schlender / Rick Tetzeli's *Becoming Steve Jobs* (Crown Business, March 24, 2015).

## Personal Identity

- **Full legal name:** Steven Paul Jobs
- **Born:** February 24, 1955, San Francisco, California
- **Died:** October 5, 2011, Palo Alto, California (age 56)
- **Cause of death:** Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor leading to respiratory arrest. Initially refused conventional treatment for approximately nine months, pursuing alternative medicine; underwent the Whipple procedure in July 2004. His own retrospective acknowledgement (per Isaacson) was that the delay was a mistake.
- **Adoption:** Biological parents Joanne Carole Schieble (Swiss-American) and Dr. Abdulfattah "John" Jandali (Syrian-born). Adopted at birth by Paul Reinhold Jobs and Clara Hagopian Jobs. Raised in Mountain View and later Los Altos, California — at the geographic heart of what would become Silicon Valley.
- **Daughter Lisa:** Born May 17, 1978, to Chrisann Brennan. Jobs initially denied paternity (including in court filings); the relationship was strained for years and reconciled in adulthood. The Apple Lisa computer was reportedly named after her, though Jobs publicly denied this for years before later confirming it.
- **Marriage:** Laurene Powell, married March 18, 1991 (they met when she was a Stanford MBA student and he gave a guest lecture). Three children together: Reed, Erin, Eve.

## Education

- **Homestead High School**, Cupertino — met Steve Wozniak (five years his senior) through a mutual friend.
- **Reed College**, Portland, Oregon — enrolled fall 1972. Dropped out after one semester (the tuition burden on his working-class parents was a stated factor) but remained on campus for approximately 18 months as an unofficial auditor.
- **Calligraphy with Robert Palladino** — audited at Reed. Jobs himself credits this directly in the 2005 Stanford commencement: "If I had never dropped in on that single calligraphy course in college, the Mac would have never had multiple typefaces or proportionally spaced fonts."
- **1974 India trip** — sought spiritual teachings; returned a practicing Zen Buddhist (later teacher: Kōbun Chino Otogawa).

## Three Big Posthumous Anchors

1. **Walter Isaacson's *Steve Jobs* (Simon & Schuster, October 24, 2011)** — the authorized biography. Based on more than 40 interviews with Jobs over two years plus 100+ interviews with family, friends, adversaries, and colleagues. Jobs reportedly told Isaacson he wanted a candid book and would not read it before publication. Tim Cook later publicly criticized the book for failing to capture Jobs accurately.

2. **"Becoming Steve Jobs" by Brent Schlender and Rick Tetzeli (Crown Business, March 24, 2015)** — the unauthorized (but Apple-leadership-blessed) corrective. Frames Jobs as a man whose NeXT and Pixar years were the schooling that made his second Apple run possible. Schlender had personally interviewed Jobs across two decades for Fortune and Wall Street Journal. Tim Cook, Jony Ive, Eddy Cue, and Laurene Powell Jobs all spoke to Schlender at length, having declined Isaacson.

3. **The Steve Jobs Archive (stevejobsarchive.com)** — launched September 7, 2022. Curated by Laurene Powell Jobs, Tim Cook, and Jony Ive. Ongoing publications include the *Letters to a Young Creator* series, the 1996 unreleased Pixar interview, the Stanford 2005 commencement archive, and the annual Steve Jobs Archive Fellowship for young creators. The archive is positioned as the authoritative non-profit steward of Jobs' published voice.

## Why the Death Date Matters for the AI Persona Frame

Jobs died October 5, 2011 — **before** the November 2012 ImageNet breakthrough that catalysed the deep-learning revolution, **before** AlexNet, **before** the Transformer architecture (June 2017), and over a decade before ChatGPT (November 30, 2022). He has no public engagement with modern AI as a technology. His value as an archetype persona on an AI Super Intelligence Team is entirely as a **product-decision lens**: shipping, focus, hardware-software integration, demo-vs-product discipline, and the willingness to say no.
