# 07 — The Quote Canon

Primary aggregation source: Wikiquote (en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Steve_Jobs), cross-checked against Steve Jobs Archive *Make Something Wonderful* (2023), the Stanford 2005 transcript, the iPhone 2007 transcript, the 1996 Wired interview by Gary Wolf, the 1995 Cringely Lost Interview, and the Isaacson biography.

Each quote below is annotated with: source, year, and a one-line synthesis of what stance the quote evidences.

## On Design

> "Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works."
> — *The New York Times Magazine*, "The Guts of a New Machine," November 30, 2003 (interview by Rob Walker). Stance: integrated design.

> "Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." (Apple slogan; attributed to da Vinci but adopted by Jobs as personal motto)
> — Stance: reductive design.

> "We don't have a chance of beating them on cost. But we have a chance of beating them on engineering and design." — On the iPad market entry strategy, 2010 keynote. Stance: design as moat.

## On Focus

> "People think focus means saying yes to the thing you've got to focus on. But that's not what it means at all. It means saying no to the hundred other good ideas that there are. You have to pick carefully. I'm actually as proud of the things we haven't done as the things I have done. Innovation is saying no to a thousand things."
> — WWDC 1997. Stance: focus as elimination.

> "It's not the customer's job to know what they want."
> — Variant attributed to multiple Jobs talks; the canonical formulation is in *BusinessWeek*, May 25, 1998: "A lot of times, people don't know what they want until you show it to them." Stance: founder-led product taste over market research.

## On Shipping

> "Real artists ship."
> — La Playa Hotel Mac team retreat, January 1983. Documented in Frank Rose's *West of Eden* (1989) and folklore.org. Stance: completion as the test of craft.

> "It's better to be a pirate than to join the Navy."
> — Pajaro Dunes / Carmel Mac team retreats, 1982-1983. Stance: small autonomous teams beat large consensus organizations.

## On Death and Direction (Stanford 2005)

> "You can't connect the dots looking forward; you can only connect them looking backwards. So you have to trust that the dots will somehow connect in your future."
> — Stanford commencement, June 12, 2005. Stance: follow curiosity, validate retrospectively.

> "If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?"
> — Stanford 2005. Stance: daily discipline through mortality.

> "Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life. Don't be trapped by dogma — which is living with the results of other people's thinking. Don't let the noise of others' opinions drown out your own inner voice."
> — Stanford 2005. Stance: contrarian autonomy.

> "Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish."
> — Stanford 2005 closing (originally from *The Whole Earth Catalog*, 1974). Stance: signature voice marker.

## On People

> "The only way to do great work is to love what you do. If you haven't found it yet, keep looking. Don't settle."
> — Stanford 2005. Stance: passion as prerequisite.

> "A players hire A players. B players hire C players and C players hire D players. Once you start down that path, you very quickly end up with Z players."
> — 1995 Cringely Lost Interview (paraphrased; consistent across multiple Jobs talks). Stance: ruthless hiring quality.

> "It doesn't make sense to hire smart people and then tell them what to do. We hire smart people so they can tell us what to do."
> — Multiple Jobs talks. Stance: delegated trust within strict vision.

## On Technology and the Liberal Arts

> "It's in Apple's DNA that technology alone is not enough — it's technology married with the liberal arts, married with the humanities, that yield the results that make our hearts sing."
> — iPad introduction, January 27, 2010. Stance: integration of craft and engineering.

> "What a computer is to me is the most remarkable tool that we have ever come up with. It's the equivalent of a bicycle for our minds."
> — Multiple variants; canonical formulation 1980 / 1990. Originally cited from a Scientific American article on locomotive efficiency. Stance: tool-augmentation framing of personal computing.

## On the Industry (1995 Lost Interview)

> "The only problem with Microsoft is they just have no taste. They have absolutely no taste. And I don't mean that in a small way, I mean that in a big way. In the sense that they don't think of original ideas, and they don't bring much culture into their product."
> — Cringely interview, 1995. Stance: taste as the missing variable in scale-driven competitors.

> "When the sales guys run the company, the product guys don't matter so much, and a lot of them just turn off. It happened at Apple when [John] Sculley came in. It happened at Xerox when the Xerox PARC group lost out to the copier salesmen."
> — Cringely 1995. Stance: product leadership over sales leadership.

## On the iPhone

> "Every once in a while a revolutionary product comes along that changes everything."
> — iPhone keynote, January 9, 2007. Stance: category-creator framing of product launches.

> "An iPod, a phone, and an internet communicator. Are you getting it? These are not three separate devices. This is one device."
> — iPhone keynote, January 9, 2007. Stance: synthesis as the product.

> "We are all born with the ultimate pointing device — our fingers — and iPhone uses them to create the most revolutionary user interface since the mouse."
> — Apple Newsroom press release, January 9, 2007. Stance: interaction model as the strategic axis.

## On Steve Wozniak and the Founding

> "Woz and I started Apple in my parents' garage when I was 20. We worked hard, and in 10 years Apple had grown from just the two of us in a garage into a $2 billion company with over 4,000 employees."
> — Stanford 2005. Stance: founder narrative as legitimacy.

## On the Whole Earth Catalog (Stewart Brand)

> "It was sort of like Google in paperback form, 35 years before Google came along. It was idealistic, overflowing with neat tools and great notions."
> — Stanford 2005. Stance: counterculture origin of the Apple aesthetic.

## On NeXT and the Wilderness

> "I didn't see it then, but it turned out that getting fired from Apple was the best thing that could have ever happened to me."
> — Stanford 2005. Stance: setback as opportunity.

## On Innovation Process

> "Creativity is just connecting things. When you ask creative people how they did something, they feel a little guilty because they didn't really do it, they just saw something. It seemed obvious to them after a while. That's because they were able to connect experiences they've had and synthesize new things."
> — 1996 Wired interview by Gary Wolf. Stance: synthesis as the core creative operation.

## On the Mortality of Companies

> "We're here to put a dent in the universe. Otherwise, why else even be here?"
> — Attributed to Jobs at multiple Mac-era team meetings, 1983-1984. Stance: stakes-first product motivation.
