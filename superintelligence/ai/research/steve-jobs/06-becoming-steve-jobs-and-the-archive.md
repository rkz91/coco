# 06 — Becoming Steve Jobs (2015) and the Steve Jobs Archive (2022–)

Sources: Penguin Random House page for *Becoming Steve Jobs* (penguinrandomhouse.com/books/223401/), Wikipedia entry, Fortune Q&A with Schlender and Tetzeli (April 7, 2015), Steve Jobs Archive home page (stevejobsarchive.com), CNBC coverage of Archive launch (September 2022).

## *Becoming Steve Jobs: The Evolution of a Reckless Upstart into a Visionary Leader*

Authors: Brent Schlender (Fortune and Wall Street Journal technology reporter, ~25 years of direct interviews with Jobs) and Rick Tetzeli (Fast Company editor). Published by Crown Business / Penguin Random House, March 24, 2015. Foreword by Marc Andreessen.

**Central thesis:** Jobs' second Apple run (1997–2011) was not a return to form — it was the result of a personal evolution that took place at NeXT and Pixar during his "wilderness years." The reckless, abrasive young founder who was forced out of Apple in 1985 became, over the next decade, a more patient, more strategic, more delegating leader. The Schlender/Tetzeli framing: he learned. The Isaacson framing: he was always the same person, only the circumstances changed.

**What the book adds that Isaacson missed:**

1. **NeXT as a graduate school.** Jobs' tyrannical fiats at NeXT (he insisted on a magnesium cube case at extraordinary cost; he refused to license NeXTSTEP until far too late) almost sank the company. The lesson: tyranny without compounding success kills you. He carried this lesson back to Apple, where his demands were channeled by a leadership team (Tim Cook for operations, Jony Ive for design, Phil Schiller for marketing) who could absorb and translate them.

2. **Pixar as the corrective laboratory.** At Pixar, Jobs was the funder and chairman, not the creative head. John Lasseter and Ed Catmull made the creative calls. Jobs' role was to protect them from external pressure and finance the work. He learned how to *not* interfere with talent. This is the single most underweighted shift in his career — and the framing that explains how he could trust Jony Ive's design team and the iPhone engineering team at Apple in a way the 1984 Mac-team Jobs could not have.

3. **Andy Grove and the corporate-discipline mentorship.** Schlender documents the relationship between Jobs and Intel's Andy Grove, who counseled Jobs through the 1997 return decision and the subsequent restructuring. The Grove influence (paranoia, focus, financial discipline) shows up in every Apple decision from 1998 forward.

4. **The Tim Cook hire.** Cook joined Apple in March 1998. By 2004 he was running day-to-day operations. The book reframes the Cook hire as one of the two most consequential decisions of Jobs' career (the other being the Pixar acquisition). Cook's operational rigor made it possible for Jobs to concentrate on product. Without Cook, the iPhone supply chain and the China manufacturing build-out would not have been possible.

5. **The Laurene Powell Jobs portrait.** Far more present in *Becoming* than in Isaacson. The Schlender/Tetzeli account treats the marriage as a stabilizing force and the family as a domain where Jobs' learning was most visible.

**Why this matters for the AI persona:** the Schlender/Tetzeli portrait is the version of Jobs that an AI Super Intelligence Team is most useful summoning. The 2007–2011 Jobs — the iPhone, App Store, iPad, MacBook Air, Apple Stores, iCloud era — was a leader who had learned to trust delegated craft within a strict product-vision frame. He is the version to model when the convene call needs both product-decisiveness *and* the discipline to let specialists do their work. The 1984 Jobs alone is too brittle.

## The Steve Jobs Archive (stevejobsarchive.com)

**Founded:** September 7, 2022.
**Curators (per Archive press materials):** Laurene Powell Jobs (chair), Tim Cook, Jony Ive.
**Mission:** "We aim to inspire by sharing Steve's life and to advance his ideas about creativity, technology, and human possibility."

The Archive is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. It is consciously *not* a museum. Per the launch communications, it functions more like an editorial publication and a fellowship program than a static repository.

**Ongoing publications:**

- **"Make Something Wonderful" (April 2023)** — a free curated digital book of Jobs' speeches, interviews, and correspondence, presented chronologically. Released for free on the Apple Books store.
- **"Letters to a Young Creator"** — a series of essays by accomplished creators on making meaningful work, framed as letters to a younger generation. Contributors have included Jony Ive, Ed Catmull, Tim Cook, and others.
- **The previously unreleased 1996 Pixar interview** with Jobs (released 2022).
- **Annual Stanford Commencement Address republications**, including the 20th-anniversary curated package in June 2025.

**The Archive Fellowship:**

Launched 2024. A program supporting young creators in the early stages of meaningful work. The 2025 fellowship class was announced publicly. Includes financial support, mentorship from a curated network, and access to Archive resources.

**Significance for the persona profile:**

The Archive is the ongoing institutional voice that keeps Jobs' published canon current. For a persona file with `last_verified: 2026-05-28`, the Archive is the single most important source of *forward-dated* materials. The "persistent_signals" field of the YAML draws heavily on Archive releases because they are how Jobs continues to "publish" in the years after his death.
