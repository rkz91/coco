# Liang Wenfeng — Wikipedia (English)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liang_Wenfeng
Retrieved: 2026-05-28

## Basic biographical facts

- **Born:** 1985 in Mililing village, Wuchuan, Guangdong province, China. Age 40–41 in 2026.
- **Parents:** Both primary school teachers.
- **Pre-college:** Top scorer in Zhanjiang region for the college entrance exam (gaokao). Self-taught high-school and university-level mathematics.
- **Notable declined opportunity:** DJI founder Wang Tao invited Liang to co-found his company during college; Liang declined to pursue AI in financial markets instead.

## Education

- **Zhejiang University** (enrolled 2002, age 17)
  - **Bachelor of Engineering in Electronic Information Engineering** (2007)
  - **Master of Engineering in Information & Communication Engineering** (2010)
  - **Thesis:** "Study on Object Tracking Algorithm Based on Low-Cost PTZ Camera"
  - **Academic advisor:** Xiang Zhiyu

## Career milestones

### 2008–2015 — early quant ventures
- 2008: Formed a team during the global financial crisis to explore quantitative trading using machine learning.
- Post-graduation: Relocated to Chengdu; ran multiple failed AI startups before settling on finance.
- **2013:** Co-founded **Hangzhou Yakebi Investment Management** with Zhejiang University alumnus Xu Jin.
- **2015:** Co-founded **Hangzhou Huanfang Technology** (now Zhejiang Jiuzhang Asset Management).

### 2016–2022 — High-Flyer Capital
- **February 2016:** Co-founded **Ningbo High-Flyer Quantitative Investment Management Partnership** with two engineering classmates.
- **2019:** Established **High-Flyer AI** for AI algorithm research.
- **By 2019:** High-Flyer managed over 10 billion yuan (~$1.4B) in assets.
- **2021:** Began purchasing thousands of Nvidia GPUs for an AI side project while running High-Flyer. Partners reportedly skeptical, believing only large players like ByteDance and Alibaba could succeed in such ventures.
- **February 2021:** Wrote preface for the Chinese edition of Gregory Zuckerman's *The Man Who Solved the Market*. Noted he had internalized Jim Simons' principle: "There must be a way to model prices."

### 2023–2026 — DeepSeek
- **May 2023:** Announced that High-Flyer would pursue AGI; launched **DeepSeek**. Disclosed that High-Flyer had acquired ~10,000 Nvidia A100 GPUs before US export restrictions tightened. DeepSeek is funded by High-Flyer; Liang personally holds 1% (some sources say 84% via shell corps); High-Flyer holds 99%. The funding model allows DeepSeek to avoid commercial pressure and rigid KPIs.
- **July 2024:** Interview revealed that DeepSeek-V2's aggressive pricing forced Alibaba and Baidu to cut LLM API rates by over 95%.
- **December 2024:** Released **DeepSeek-V3** (671B MoE). Attributed cost reductions in part to multi-head latent attention (MLA) developed via a young researcher's personal-interest initiative.
- **January 20, 2025:**
  - Released **DeepSeek-R1**, a 671B-parameter open-weights reasoning model, under MIT license.
  - Trained with 2,048 Nvidia H800 GPUs at a reported $5.6M cost.
  - Attended Premier Li Qiang's closed-door symposium with experts and entrepreneurs (one of nine invitees) and provided input on the draft government work report.
  - By January 27, DeepSeek surpassed ChatGPT as #1 free app on the US iOS App Store.
- **January 27, 2025:** Nvidia fell ~17%, losing $588.8 billion in market cap in a single day — the largest single-day market-cap loss in US stock-market history — driven primarily by DeepSeek-R1's release.
- **February 17, 2025:** Invited to a symposium with **General Secretary Xi Jinping** and other private-sector leaders at the Great Hall of the People (alongside Jack Ma, Pony Ma, Zeng Yuqun of CATL).
- **May 28, 2025:** Released **DeepSeek-R1-0528** — an update rather than the originally promised R2. Liang was reportedly not satisfied with R2's performance.
- **August 21, 2025:** Released **DeepSeek-V3.1** with a "DeepThink" toggle that switches a single model between thinking and non-thinking modes.
- **December 1, 2025:** Released **DeepSeek-V3.2** with sparse attention.
- **April 24, 2026:** Released **DeepSeek-V4 preview** — 1.6T parameters (Pro, 49B active) and 284B (Flash), 1M-token context, MIT licensed, weights on Hugging Face.

## Recognition

- **Fortune 100 Most Powerful People in Business 2025**.

## Character notes

- Colleagues describe him as having a "unique capacity to learn" and a "geek" personality. Notably secretive — Gregory Zuckerman compared him to Jim Simons in a January 2025 WSJ piece.
- DeepSeek operates with a low-hierarchy, project-based structure; recruits employees with "ability and passion" over experience; encourages bottom-up initiative.
- Liang has not appeared publicly since February 17, 2025 (Xi symposium). The April 28, 2026 SCMP "mystery" piece noted senior researcher Chen Deli is now DeepSeek's public face.
