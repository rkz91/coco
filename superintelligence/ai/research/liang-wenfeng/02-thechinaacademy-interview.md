# "We're Done Following. It's Time to Lead." — TheChinaAcademy interview

Source: https://thechinaacademy.org/interview-with-deepseek-founder-were-done-following-its-time-to-lead/
Originally a July 2024 interview conducted by Chinese outlet An Yong; translated and republished in English January–February 2025.
Mirrored at: https://mronline.org/2025/02/07/interview-with-deepseek-founder-were-done-following/

This is the single most-cited 2025 Liang Wenfeng English-language artifact. It is the source for most of his quotable framings on open-source, China's role, hiring, and AGI.

## Direct quotes — open source as a strategic asset

> "We won't go closed-source. We believe that establishing a robust technology ecosystem matters more."

> "Open-sourcing and publishing papers don't result in significant losses. For technologists, being followed is rewarding."

> "Open source is more of a cultural behavior than a commercial one, and contributing to it earns us respect."

> "In disruptive tech, closed-source moats are fleeting. Our real moat lies in our team's growth — accumulating know-how, fostering an innovative culture."

> "Secrets don't exist, but replication takes time and cost. That's the real moat."

## Direct quotes — China's role in AI

> "We believe that China's AI cannot remain a follower forever. We often say there's a gap of a year or two between Chinese and US AI, but the real gap is between originality and imitation."

> "We believe that with economic development, China must gradually transition from being a beneficiary to a contributor."

> "Over the past 30 years of the IT revolution, China barely participated in core tech innovation."

> "Someone must step onto the frontier." (Regarding China needing supportive tech ecosystems like the West.)

> "OpenAI is not a god (OpenAI 不是神), they won't necessarily always be at the forefront."

## Direct quotes — hiring and team

> "Our hiring standard has always been passion and curiosity. Many of our team members have unusual experiences."

> "The team behind the V2 model doesn't include anyone returning to China from overseas — they are all local."

> "The most attractive thing for top-tier talent is the opportunity to solve the world's toughest challenges."

> "Their hunger for research far outweighs monetary concerns."

## Direct quotes — organizational structure

> "DeepSeek remains entirely bottom-up. Natural division of labor emerges."

> "There are no limits on accessing compute resources or team members. If someone has an idea, they can tap into our training clusters anytime."

## Direct quotes — funding and commercialization

> "We have no short-term fundraising plans. Our main problem has never been money — it's the export restrictions on high-end chips."

> "Money has never been the problem for us; bans on shipments of advanced chips are the problem."

> "Our ultimate goal is still to achieve AGI. Providing cloud services isn't our main goal."

> "Our focus will remain on foundational models and cutting-edge innovation."

## Direct quotes — innovation theory

> "More investment doesn't necessarily result in more innovation. If that were the case, big tech companies would have monopolized all innovation."

> "Innovation is, first and foremost, a matter of belief. Why is Silicon Valley so innovative? Because they dare to try."

## Direct quotes — AGI timeline

> "It could take two years, five years, or ten years — but it will happen within our lifetime."

## Why this interview matters

- Conducted *before* DeepSeek-R1 became a global story (July 2024). Re-published in English just as R1 was sending Nvidia into freefall (Jan/Feb 2025). It became the de facto worldview document.
- Western media coverage of Liang almost universally cites this interview because there is so little else.
- The "we're done following" framing now functions as a Liang signature in Chinese AI policy discourse — the way "we're summoning ghosts" functions for Karpathy.
