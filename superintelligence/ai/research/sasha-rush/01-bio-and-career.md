## Career timeline (per rush-nlp.com and Cornell Tech profile)

- **2016–2021:** Assistant Professor of Computer Science, Harvard SEAS (Harvard NLP group).
- **2019–2024:** Concurrent role as Researcher at Hugging Face — co-author on the Transformers library paper (EMNLP 2020) and many open-source release programs (Zephyr, etc.).
- **2021–2025/2026:** Associate Professor at Cornell Tech (Cornell University's NYC campus). Recipient of NSF CAREER Award and Sloan Fellowship during this period.
- **March 2025 → present:** ML Researcher at **Cursor**, focused on post-training of coding-agent models. Based in NYC. Continued some Cornell affiliation through 2026 academic year, but Cursor is now the primary working address (publicly confirmed via LinkedIn, his personal site, Composer 2 technical report authorship, and the April 2026 "Information Bottleneck" podcast).
- **2024:** Co-founded **COLM (Conference on Language Modeling)** — a new venue dedicated specifically to LM theory, practice, and applications. Currently serves as President of the conference.
- **2024–2025:** Visiting Scientist and Program Organizer at the **Simons Institute for the Theory of Computing**, UC Berkeley — Special Year on Large Language Models and Transformers (Parts 1 and 2). https://simons.berkeley.edu/people/sasha-rush

## Why his career arc matters for the persona

The Harvard → Cornell Tech → Hugging Face (concurrent) → Cursor trajectory tracks the structural shift of NLP research from university labs to open-source community institutions to application-layer companies. He carries the credibility of all three. He is one of the few people who has stayed publicly bullish on **literate pedagogy** the entire time, treating an annotated Jupyter notebook as a first-class research output rather than a side project.
