# Beth Barnes — METR Team Page and Career History

Sources cross-referenced: `https://metr.org/team/beth-barnes/`, `https://metr.org/about`, `https://barnes.page/`, `https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/METR`, `https://aiwiki.ai/wiki/metr` (the last hit a 429 on retry but the substance was captured before the rate limit).

## Role

Founder and CEO of METR (Model Evaluation and Threat Research), a 501(c)(3) nonprofit research institute based in Berkeley, California. She oversees the technical team that designs and conducts evaluations of generative AI models, with a specific focus on dangerous autonomous capabilities.

## Career path

- **OpenAI alignment team** (approximately 2021 through 2022). Worked on safety targets and on evaluating scalable oversight techniques for alignment and code models. She has stated publicly that the desire to be an independent voice and to be able to talk directly with policymakers and other labs was a factor in her decision to leave OpenAI.
- **Google DeepMind** (prior to OpenAI). Collaborated with the Chief Scientist's organization on scaling laws and on forecasting deep learning progress. Her exposure to scaling-law forecasting at DeepMind anchors METR's later long-task-horizon work.
- **ARC Evals** (2022 through 2023). Founded the evaluations division of Paul Christiano's Alignment Research Center after leaving OpenAI. ARC Evals ran the first widely cited pre-deployment dangerous-capability evaluations on GPT-4 and on early Claude models in 2023.
- **METR** (December 2023 onward). ARC Evals was spun off as an independent 501(c)(3) nonprofit and renamed METR. Beth Barnes is founder and CEO.

## Why the spinout matters

The ARC Evals → METR transition is structurally important and consistently emphasized on her public profiles. METR is positioned as independent from any single lab, from any single funder, and from the Alignment Research Center's broader research agenda. METR has stated publicly that it has not accepted payment from frontier AI labs for running evaluations — an explicit guardrail intended to insulate the organization's evaluation reports from the perception of being for hire. Funding sources for METR are philanthropic.
