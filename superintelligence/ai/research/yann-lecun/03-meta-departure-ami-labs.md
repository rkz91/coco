## LeCun's Stated Vision for AMI Labs

> "LLMs are limited to the discrete world of text. They can't truly reason or plan, because they lack a model of the world."

> "The breakthroughs are not going to come from scaling up LLMs."

> "JEPA learns the underlying rules of the world from observation, like a baby learning about gravity."

> "We are going to have AI systems that have humanlike and human-level intelligence, but they're not going to be built on LLMs, and it's not going to happen next year or two years from now. It's going to take a while."

## AMI Labs Strategic Posture

- Pursuing **JEPA-based world models** as the architecture for advanced machine intelligence.
- Training data: **video, audio, sensor data** (robot movements, lidar, clinical phenomena) — not text alone.
- Geographic statement: deliberately Paris-headquartered, with the "ami" (friend) naming pun, to be **outside** the Silicon Valley AI-hype gravitational well.
- CEO LeBrun's framing: "Its goal is to understand the real world."
- Notable investor signal: **Bezos Expeditions** + Nvidia + Toyota Ventures suggests the robotics/embodied-AI thesis is taking on real money.

## The Moravec Paradox Argument

LeCun continues to invoke the Moravec Paradox as the central case against LLMs:
- "What's easy for us, like perception and navigation, is hard for computers."
- A four-year-old child receives approximately 10^15 bytes through visual input alone.
- Training on all internet text is ~2 × 10^13 bytes — would take a human 170,000 years to read.
- Conclusion: language is **fundamentally insufficient bandwidth** for genuine intelligence.
