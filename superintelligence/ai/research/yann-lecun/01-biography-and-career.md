# Yann LeCun — Biography and Career Timeline

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun (fetched 2026-05-27)
Cross-referenced: https://amturing.acm.org/award_winners/lecun_6017366.cfm, https://www.nyu.edu/about/news-publications/news/2019/march/courant-s-lecun-wins-turing-award-for-breakthroughs-in-artificia.html

## Identity

- Full name: Yann André LeCun
- Born: July 8, 1960, in Soisy-sous-Montmorency, France
- Nationality: French-American

## Education

- **ESIEE Paris** — Diplôme d'Ingénieur (1983)
- **Université Pierre et Marie Curie** — PhD in Computer Science (1987). Thesis proposed an early form of backpropagation (HLM — Hierarchical Learning Machine).
- Postdoc with Geoffrey Hinton at University of Toronto (1987–1988)

## Key Affiliations Detail

### Meta / FAIR (2013–2025)
- Founded Facebook AI Research (FAIR) in December 2013 at Mark Zuckerberg's invitation.
- Title: Director of AI Research initially, then Chief AI Scientist.
- Reported into Meta GenAI leadership; final period under Alexandr Wang (Meta Chief AI Officer).
- Departed November 19, 2025, after twelve years.

### Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (AMI Labs) — 2025–present
- Announced departure from Meta on November 19, 2025.
- Company name "AMI" pronounced "ami" (French: friend).
- Headquartered in **Paris**, with offices in New York, Montreal, and Singapore.
- LeCun is **Executive Chairman**, not CEO.
- Co-founders / leadership team:
  - Alexandre LeBrun — CEO
  - Laurent Solly — COO (former Meta VP for Europe)
  - Saining Xie — Chief Science Officer (NYU + Google DeepMind alumnus)
  - Pascale Fung — Chief Research and Innovation Officer
  - Michael Rabbat — VP of World Models (co-author on I-JEPA)
- **Funding (announced March 10, 2026): $1.03 billion at $3.5B pre-money valuation**
- Lead investors: Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, HV Capital, Bezos Expeditions
- Other notable investors: Nvidia, Samsung, Sea, Temasek, Toyota Ventures
- Angels: Tim Berners-Lee, Jim Breyer, Mark Cuban, Eric Schmidt

## Awards

- **2018 ACM Turing Award** (shared with Yoshua Bengio + Geoffrey Hinton) — "for conceptual and engineering breakthroughs that have made deep neural networks a critical component of computing"
- 2019 AAAI Fellow
- 2021 Member, National Academy of Sciences (US)
- 2023 Knight of the French Legion of Honour
- 2024 VinFuture Prize (grand prize, shared)
- 2025 Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (shared with seven others)
