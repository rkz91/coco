# Demis Hassabis — Biographical Foundation

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis (retrieved 2026-05-27)
Cross-references: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Demis-Hassabis, https://achievement.org/achiever/demis-hassabis-ph-d/, https://eboona.com/ai-startup-founder/demis-hassabis/

## Identity

- **Full name:** Sir Demis Hassabis (born Dimitrios Hassapis)
- **Born:** 27 July 1976, London, England
- **Heritage:** Greek Cypriot father, Singaporean mother
- **Title:** Knight Bachelor (2024)

## Education

- **Cambridge (Queens' College):** Computer Science Tripos, graduated 1997 with double-first. Cambridge had asked him to defer entry by a year because of his young age; he used the gap year for his early games career.
- **University College London:** PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience, 2009. Thesis title "Neural Processes Underpinning Episodic Memory" supervised by Eleanor Maguire. The work on the role of the hippocampus in imagination and future thinking yielded a high-impact 2007 Science paper (Hassabis, Kumaran, Vann, Maguire — "Patients with hippocampal amnesia cannot imagine new experiences").
- **Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit, UCL:** Henry Wellcome postdoctoral fellowship, 2009. Worked with Peter Dayan on the computational substrate of reward, memory, and planning.

## Early career — chess and games

- Chess prodigy from age 4. Reached master strength by 13 (Elo ≈ 2300). Captain of England junior chess teams.
- First computer: ZX Spectrum 48K, paid for with chess winnings, 1984.
- **Bullfrog Productions (1992–1994):** Co-designed and lead-programmed *Theme Park* alongside Peter Molyneux at age 17. The game became a multi-million-seller and was an early example of emergent-AI simulation in a commercial title.
- **Lionhead Studios (1997–1998):** Lead AI programmer on *Black & White*.
- **Elixir Studios (1998–2005):** Founded his own independent games studio. Shipped *Republic: The Revolution* (2003) and *Evil Genius* (2004). Closed Elixir in 2005 to return to academia.

## DeepMind era

- **2010:** Co-founded DeepMind with Shane Legg and Mustafa Suleyman in London.
- **2014:** Google acquired DeepMind for ~£400M (~$650M). Hassabis remained as CEO.
- **April 2023:** DeepMind merged with Google Brain to form **Google DeepMind**. Hassabis became CEO of the merged organization.
- **2021:** Co-founded **Isomorphic Labs** as an Alphabet spinout focused on AI-first drug discovery; serves as CEO.

## Honours

- CBE 2017 (services to science and technology).
- Fellow of the Royal Society, 2018.
- Knighthood, 2024 (services to artificial intelligence).
- **Nobel Prize in Chemistry, 2024** — shared with John Jumper (Google DeepMind, for AlphaFold) and David Baker (University of Washington, separately for computational protein design).
- Time 100 lists, 2017 and 2025. Time Person of the Year shortlist 2024.
- Prior major prizes: Lasker Award (2023), Breakthrough Prize in Life Sciences (2023), Canada Gairdner Award (2023), Keio Medical Science Prize (2024).
