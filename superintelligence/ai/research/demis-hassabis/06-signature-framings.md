# Signature framings — the load-bearing Hassabis sentences

Sources collated from the four research files above. URLs cited inline.

## "Solve intelligence, then use it to solve everything else"

The single most-quoted Hassabis sentence. Origin: DeepMind founding mission statement, ~2010. Has appeared in essentially every major interview since. Re-cited in the Fortune Feb 2026 piece (https://fortune.com/2026/02/11/demis-hassabis-nobel-google-deepmind-predicts-ai-renaissance-radical-abundance/).

Why it matters: it is the **ordering** that is load-bearing — intelligence is the prior, application is downstream. Frames DeepMind as a research lab that ships products, not a product company that does research.

## "We're summoning ghosts, not building animals"

This is actually Karpathy's coinage (Dwarkesh Oct 2025). Hassabis's structurally similar move is "we're building artefacts, not biological systems" but with the opposite valence: he believes biological inspiration is *useful* (hippocampus → episodic memory → AlphaGo's tree search). Cross-reference his UCL PhD on memory.

## "Race to the bottom for safety"

Used on 60 Minutes April 2025 and in multiple 2024–2026 settings. The framing: competitive pressure between labs and nation-states pushes everyone to under-invest in safety. The implicit policy ask is **coordination**, not unilateral slowdown.

## "AGI is a system that can exhibit ALL the cognitive capabilities humans can — and I mean all"

Davos Jan 2026. This is the **definitional move** that distinguishes Hassabis from peers. Anthropic's "powerful AI" definition and OpenAI's operational ladders are narrower. Hassabis explicitly raises the bar so that benchmark wins do not equal AGI.

## "It's harder to come up with a good conjecture than to solve it"

Lex Fridman #475, July 2025. The most novel framing in recent Hassabis interviews. Implies that current LLM benchmarks measure the easy half of science. Hypothesis generation, not problem-solving, is the unsolved capability.

## "World models are the future"

Recurrent across the August 2025 Genie 3 launch, the Lex podcast, and Decoder interviews. Hassabis's bet is that **next-generation systems will be built on internalised models of physical reality**, not just on text + tools. This shapes DeepMind's investment in Veo, Genie, SIMA, and AlphaEarth.

## "Radical abundance"

Fortune Feb 2026. The end-state of AI applied to science: cheap energy, cured diseases, new materials. A specific counter to "AI takes everyone's jobs" pessimism — abundance is framed as the positive-sum case.

## "One or two more breakthroughs before AGI"

Davos Jan 2026. The most important counter to the pure-scaling-hypothesis camp. Hassabis is not anti-scaling, but he does not believe scaling alone gets you to his AGI bar. This is the **specific lever** that pairs him productively with Bengio (deep learning legitimacy) and puts him in productive conflict with Altman (scaling will suffice).

## "AI should be taught like a child"

60 Minutes April 2025. The pedagogy frame for alignment. Maps to Constitutional AI / instruction tuning / value learning — guardrails as inductive bias rather than as filters.

## How these collapse into a persona

Hassabis's reasoning has two layers:

1. **Neuroscience-informed architecture.** Intelligence is a structured cognitive substrate — memory, planning, world model, hypothesis generation. AGI is not a single scaling regime; it is a sequence of architectural unlocks.
2. **Civilization-scale stakes.** The application is scientific discovery at planetary scale; the risk is multi-decade geopolitical race conditions; the answer is coordinated, careful deployment.

He resists framings that collapse either layer: pure scalers collapse layer 1; pure doomers collapse layer 2. He sits in the productive middle and pulls debate up the abstraction stack.
