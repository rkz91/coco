## Davos 2026 (WEF, 23 January 2026) — joint panel with Dario Amodei

Hassabis quotes:

> "Nowhere near" human-level AGI yet.

> AGI in "five to 10 years."

> "Maybe we need one or two more breakthroughs before we'll get to AGI."

> "My definition of [AGI] is a system that can exhibit all the cognitive capabilities humans can — and I mean all" — including the highest-level creative achievements of scientists and artists.

Identified gating capabilities:
- Learning from very few examples.
- Continuous (online) learning.
- Long-term memory.
- Reasoning and planning.

Tension with Amodei: Amodei's timeline is shorter; Hassabis said a slower path "would be better for the world" but acknowledged neither lab can unilaterally slow down because of the geopolitical race.

## 60 Minutes (Scott Pelley, CBS, April 2025)

Hassabis quotes (reported in VentureBeat write-up and CBS clips):

> "One of my big worries is that the race for AI dominance could become a race to the bottom for safety."

> AI should be created with guardrails "much like you would teach a child."

> AGI as soon as 2030 with systems that understand environments "in very nuanced and deep ways."

Substance: Pelley toured the DeepMind London HQ. Hassabis distinguished two risks — misuse by bad actors and emergent autonomy beyond human control — and called for coordination between leading labs and nation-states.

## Axios (26 May 2026)

Hassabis told Axios he still expects AGI around 2030, but with **2029 now a possibility**. This is a slight pull-forward from his earlier "5–10 years" framing and is consistent with Gemini 3 having beaten reasoning expectations.

## Fortune (11 February 2026) — "Titans and Disruptors" podcast with Alyson Shontell

Hassabis quotes:

> "In 10, 15 years' time, we'll be in a kind of new golden era of discovery that [is] a kind of new renaissance."

> "If we don't disrupt ourselves, someone else will. You're better off doing it on your terms."

> "We set out with the mission of … solving intelligence and then using it to solve everything else."

> Medicine in 10–15 years "won't look like it does today" — personalised treatments, major-disease cures within reach.

> Energy and new materials unlock the ability for humanity to "travel the stars and explore the galaxy."

Recurring frame: **radical abundance**. AI runs the hypothesis-experiment-publication cycle autonomously; net result is a multi-decade scientific renaissance.

## Persona threads emerging

1. **Multi-decade scientific framing.** Where Altman / Amodei reach for 2027 AGI dates, Hassabis consistently says ~2030 and frames the question as "what breakthroughs are missing," not "how many GPUs."
2. **Race-to-the-bottom on safety.** This is his consistent geopolitical anchor.
3. **AGI as a moving definition.** He sets the bar at *all* human cognitive capabilities, which is materially higher than the operational bars Anthropic and OpenAI use.
4. **AI for science is the destination, not the byproduct.** Recurring everywhere — Nobel, Fortune, Lex, Davos. This separates him from commercial-frontier-lab CEOs.
