## Stated research focus

Lewis describes his work as the intersection of information retrieval (IR) and large language models (LLMs), with particular emphasis on "Retrieval-Augmented Language Models." He frames the core problem as how to represent, store, and access knowledge so that LLMs can perform well on knowledge-intensive NLP tasks such as Question Answering and Fact Checking.

## Key works highlighted on the site

- "Atlas: Few-shot Learning with Retrieval-Augmented Language Models" (2022) — joint first author with Gautier Izacard.
- "PAQ: 65 Million Probably-Asked Questions and What You Can Do With Them" (TACL 2021).
- "Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks" (NeurIPS 2020) — the canonical RAG paper. First author.
- "Question and Answer Test-Train Overlap in Open-Domain Question Answering Datasets" (EACL 2021, Best Paper).
- "KILT: a Benchmark for Knowledge Intensive Language Tasks" (NAACL 2021) — co-author with Petroni et al.
- "Dense Passage Retrieval for Open-Domain Question Answering" (EMNLP 2020) — DPR, second-tier author, foundational to dense retrieval.
