# Patrick Lewis — TIME 100 AI 2024 profile

Source: <https://time.com/7012883/patrick-lewis/> (TIME profile, September 2024)
Companion source: Cohere LinkedIn celebration post <https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cohere-ai_time100-ai-2024-patrick-lewis-activity-7237848950785880064-PodD>
Master list: <https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/>

## Why he was selected

TIME named Lewis one of the 100 Most Influential People in AI 2024 specifically for **introducing the technique of Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)** in his 2020 paper. The framing TIME uses:

- "RAG allows AI models to answer queries by drawing on external texts, be it company documents or a news website."
- "[RAG] can also reduce hallucinations and give models access to up-to-the-minute information."
- "Over the last year, the technique has been adopted by Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Nvidia."

## What Cohere highlighted in its celebration post

The Cohere LinkedIn post (September 2024) credited Lewis with developing "technology to verify AI-generated answers efficiently, helping address AI hallucinations — a major industry challenge." The post explicitly positions Lewis as the source of Cohere's "global leader[ship] in accurate, secure, and multilingual AI systems." This is the institutional confirmation that, internally, Cohere uses Lewis's RAG work as part of its competitive identity, not just a technical capability.

## What TIME said about his Cohere work

Since joining Cohere in 2022 (TIME's framing), Lewis has continued to expand RAG by **creating AI systems that cite any external sources used, making it easier for humans to verify their output.** This is a notable framing: TIME does not credit him primarily with Cohere's commercial wins — it credits him with the *citation-grounding* line of work that connects his original RAG architecture to the auditable enterprise deployment story that Cohere's Command R / Command A models advertise.

## The self-deprecating "apologized for the acronym" anecdote

A commonly-cited Lewis quote that appears in multiple write-ups: he has publicly apologised for the "unflattering" acronym RAG, joking that he and his co-authors didn't realize the term would become industry-canonical. This is a small-but-load-bearing voice-style data point — he is self-deprecating, low-key, and not interested in branding his own framework.

## What the TIME entry tells us about persona shape

1. He is recognized industry-wide for **one foundational paper, not for a string of public-facing manifestos**. This is the inverse of someone like Karpathy, whose recognition is distributed across essays, videos, and tweets. Lewis's persona must be drawn from the architecture of his idea, not from a corpus of his prose.
2. The recognition explicitly frames RAG as a **hallucination-reduction** and **freshness** technology, not as an AGI-adjacent step. This aligns him cleanly with Gomez's Cohere thesis (productivity, enterprise, ground truth) rather than with the OpenAI / Anthropic AGI camp.
3. The "citation grounding" line of work is highlighted as his post-Meta contribution. That positions him as the author of the architectural pattern that makes Cohere's "auditable AI for regulated enterprises" pitch possible.
