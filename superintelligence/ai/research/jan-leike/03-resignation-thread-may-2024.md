# Jan Leike — May 17, 2024 Resignation Thread

The defining public artifact for Leike's persona. Posted as a long X thread on 2024-05-17, one day after Ilya Sutskever announced his departure from OpenAI. Reconstructed here from CNN, Fortune, CNBC, CCN, the Center for AI Policy retrospective, and the Wikipedia summary (the original tweets remain at `x.com/janleike` but are paywalled via WebFetch).

## Core message

Leike framed his departure as a values-grounded resignation, not a career move. Key claims in the thread:

1. **He resigned because of disagreement with OpenAI leadership over "core priorities."** Specifically: how much of the company's bandwidth should go to safety, security, monitoring, preparedness, adversarial robustness, alignment, and societal impact versus shipping products.
2. **Most-quoted line:** *"Over the past years, safety culture and processes have taken a backseat to shiny products."*
3. **Resource starvation:** The Superalignment team had been promised 20% of OpenAI's compute (announced July 2023). In practice, Leike said the team had been **"under-resourced"** and **"sailing against the wind"** in the months before his departure.
4. **Loss of trust:** He said he had **"gradually lost trust"** in OpenAI's leadership about whether the company would actually prioritize the safety work needed to handle superintelligent systems.
5. **The inherent stakes:** *"Building smarter-than-human machines is an inherently dangerous endeavor"* and *"we need to figure out how to steer and control AI systems much smarter than us."*
6. **Implicit call to action:** OpenAI must become a "safety-first AGI company" and the rest of the industry must internalize the gravity of the problem.

## Context

- Sutskever announced his departure May 14, 2024. Leike's thread followed three days later, on May 17.
- Within hours, OpenAI confirmed it was **dissolving the Superalignment team** and folding the remaining members into other research groups.
- Daniel Kokotajlo had resigned earlier in April 2024 over similar concerns. Together the three departures became known as the "Superalignment exodus."
- Eleven days later, on May 28, 2024, Leike posted: *"I'm excited to join @AnthropicAI to continue the superalignment mission! My new team will work on scalable oversight, weak-to-strong generalization, and automated alignment research."* (Status URL `x.com/janleike/status/1795497960509448617`.)

## Why this artifact matters for the persona

- It establishes Leike as someone willing to **trade a senior role at the most-watched AI lab for public criticism of safety culture**. That move is a constant in how the broader community reads his subsequent stances — every statement at Anthropic is implicitly compared to "is this the lab culture he said he was looking for?"
- It made **"safety culture and processes have taken a backseat to shiny products"** the canonical phrase quoted whenever critics characterize OpenAI's safety posture under Sam Altman.
- It is the **most-cited single tweet thread** in the post-2023 alignment discourse — the artifact that turned alignment-team resource allocation from an internal HR question into a public investor-relations question.
