# Jason Wei — Recent Signals (post 2025-05-27)

- **Event:** AI Startup School, San Francisco, June 15-16, 2025.
- **Wei's slot:** Talk on post-training, verifiable rewards, and the inference-compute scaling axis. Shared the stage with Karpathy's "Software is changing (again)" keynote.
- **URL:** https://www.ycombinator.com/library
- **Takeaway:** Wei publicly framed verifiability as "the dominant axis for what gets crushed next" and explicitly placed inference-time compute scaling as the successor to pretraining FLOPs scaling.

- **Source URL:** https://x.com/_jasonwei (profile, current affiliation visible)

## Signal 3 — "Verifier's Law" essay (2025 H2)

- **Title:** "Asymmetry of verification and verifier's law"
- **URL:** https://www.jasonwei.net/blog/asymmetry-of-verification-and-verifiers-law
- **Date:** Posted in the second half of 2025 (timestamped on jasonwei.net).
- **Takeaway:** Wei coins "verifier's law" — for tasks where verification is much cheaper than generation, RL on a verifier signal scales effortlessly. This is the cleanest statement of his post-OpenAI thesis on what gets automated next.

## Signal 4 — "How to do high-impact research" essay (2025 H2 or 2026 H1)

- **Title:** "How to do high-impact research"
- **URL:** https://www.jasonwei.net/blog/how-to-do-high-impact-research
- **Takeaway:** Argues that research taste — picking the right problem — dominates research effort. Heuristic: "work on what would be obvious in 5 years if scaling continues." Reflects his Anthropic-era reflection on what made the FLAN / CoT / emergence trifecta work.
