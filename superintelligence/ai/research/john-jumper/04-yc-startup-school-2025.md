## Signature themes (from publicly-summarised excerpts)

- **Physics-trained intuition is undervalued in biology AI.** Jumper makes a pedagogical case for why physicists who learn biology, rather than biologists who learn ML, are over-represented at the frontier — they bring information-theoretic and energy-landscape intuitions that protein structure naturally exhibits.
- **AlphaFold 2's CASP14 leap was an engineering story as much as an algorithmic one.** The "Evoformer" architecture matters, but so did MSA processing, iterative refinement, the loss design, and the data engineering. He repeatedly resists "one trick" framings.
- **Biology + AI is co-evolving.** AlphaFold's outputs let experimentalists set up better experiments; those experiments produce data that lets the next AlphaFold be trained on better signal. The loop is the product.
- **Open scientific tools are the moat.** The AlphaFold Database existing as a free public resource is in his framing more important to the field's trajectory than the model architecture itself.
- **The next problems are not "another protein folding problem" — they are messier.** Function prediction, regulatory interactions, mesostructure, the cellular environment. None of these is a single benchmark you can win.
- **Drug discovery is the practical test, not the marketing promise.** He frames Isomorphic Labs as the way to find out whether AI-for-biology genuinely changes the medicine timeline, not as a proof that it already has.

## Anchor framings he uses repeatedly

- "AI as a scientific tool." Not "AI does science." The scientist is still in the loop.
- "Find the next problem where people say there isn't enough data." His version of frontier-picking.
- "Adaptable people, not perfect specialists." His hiring stance — what he learned from running the AlphaFold team through three architecture rewrites.

## Why this matters for the persona

- The YC venue is unusual for Jumper — he is normally on the academic-conference / Nobel-podcast / Nature-paper circuit. Speaking to AI founders is a deliberate move to seed the AI-for-science thesis in the startup ecosystem.
- The talk is the most accessible single-source for the "Jumper worldview" because it is pitched at non-biologists and lays out the framework rather than the specific scientific result.
- This is the source most likely to be cited in future Convene sessions when summoning Jumper, because it is publicly available and structured.
