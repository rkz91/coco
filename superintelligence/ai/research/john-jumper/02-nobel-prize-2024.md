## The award

Announced October 9, 2024 by the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences. Three laureates:

- **One half** jointly to Demis Hassabis and John Jumper "for protein structure prediction" (AlphaFold).
- **Other half** to David Baker (University of Washington / Institute for Protein Design) "for computational protein design" (Rosetta, RFdiffusion).

Citation language: AlphaFold has been used by more than two million researchers across 190 countries to predict the structure of virtually all 200 million proteins identified by researchers.

Jumper was 39 at the time of the award — the youngest chemistry Nobel laureate in 75 years.

## Jumper's quoted reaction (DeepMind blog, Oct 9 2024)

> "We are so honored to be recognized for delivering on the long promise of computational biology to help us understand the protein world."

> "It is a key demonstration that AI will make science faster and ultimately help to understand disease and develop therapeutics."

> "Ahead of us are a universe of new insights and scientific discoveries made possible by the use of AI as a scientific tool."

## Why this matters for the persona

- Jumper is the first AI-lab research scientist (not lab head) to share a Nobel in a natural science.
- The Nobel anchors the AI-for-science thesis at the highest possible institutional level: the Karolinska / KVA panel explicitly credited deep learning + biology as a scientific breakthrough, not a tool.
- The framing he repeats — "AI as a scientific tool" — is his consistent signature. He resists the marketing-y "AI did science" framing; the artefact he defends is "AI delivered a new instrument to scientists, and the scientists did the science."
- The shared award with Hassabis (his boss) and Baker (his most prominent productive-conflict-partner: Baker's lab focuses on de novo design from physics-first principles, Jumper's lineage on structure prediction from sequence + evolution) is the social map his collaboration network sits in.

## The Nobel lecture (Dec 8, 2024, Stockholm)

Delivered as part of the Nobel ceremony lecture series. Title approximately: "Highly accurate protein structure prediction." Recurring framings:

- AlphaFold worked because the team treated structure prediction as a deep learning problem with rich biological priors (evolutionary information via MSAs, equivariant attention, structural geometry built into the loss), not as either a pure-data or a pure-physics problem.
- The release of the AlphaFold Database is, in his telling, "as important as the model itself." Open scientific tools are the moat — the model only mattered because researchers worldwide could use it without asking permission.
- He frames the next decade as the period when AI moves from prediction to design and from one biological problem to many.
