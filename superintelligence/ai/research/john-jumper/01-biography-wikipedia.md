## Identity

- Full name: John Michael Jumper
- Born: January 1, 1985, in Little Rock, Arkansas
- High school: Pulaski Academy, graduated 2003
- Nationality: American

## Education

- Vanderbilt University, B.S. in Physics and Mathematics (2007)
- University of Cambridge, MPhil in Theoretical Condensed Matter Physics (2010), St Edmund's College, Marshall Scholarship
- University of Chicago, M.S. in Theoretical Chemistry (2012)
- University of Chicago, Ph.D. in Theoretical Chemistry (2017)
  - Doctoral advisors: Tobin R. Sosnick and Karl Freed (joint experimental–theoretical lab)
  - Dissertation: "New Methods Using Rigorous Machine Learning for Coarse-Grained Protein Folding and Dynamics"

## Career

- D.E. Shaw Research (between Cambridge MPhil and UChicago PhD, ~2011–2012): molecular dynamics research engineer working on long-timescale protein simulations. This is where his hands-on intuition for the dynamics side of protein behaviour was built.
- University of Chicago (PhD 2012–2017, brief postdoc with Sosnick afterwards)
- Google DeepMind (joined 2017): hired into the AlphaFold project. Promoted by Demis Hassabis in July 2018 to lead AlphaFold 2 after the team's CASP13 entry placed first but missed atomic-level accuracy. Now Senior Staff Research Scientist / Director / Distinguished Scientist at Google DeepMind (titles vary across DeepMind, Royal Society, and press references — Royal Society profile uses "Distinguished Scientist").
- Isomorphic Labs (founded by Hassabis in 2021, Alphabet drug-discovery spinout): Jumper has been involved in the AlphaFold-to-Isomorphic translation since the spinout; AlphaFold 3 was co-developed between DeepMind and Isomorphic.

## AlphaFold lineage

- AlphaFold 1 (2018, CASP13): placed first at CASP13 but not at experimental accuracy.
- AlphaFold 2 (CASP14, November 2020): median backbone error of ~1 Å, "solved" the 50-year protein folding problem in CASP organisers' view. Methods paper "Highly accurate protein structure prediction with AlphaFold" (Jumper et al., Nature 2021) — Jumper is first author. Open-sourced July 2021.
- AlphaFold Protein Structure Database (with EMBL-EBI): 200M+ predicted structures by 2022, 214M+ by Jan 2024. Free for academic use. Used by 2M+ researchers in 190 countries (Nobel committee count, Oct 2024).
- AlphaFold-Multimer (2021–2022): extension to protein complexes.
- AlphaFold 3 (May 2024, Nature): proteins + DNA + RNA + small-molecule ligands + ions. Diffusion-based. Co-authored by Abramson, Adler, Dunger, Jumper et al. Co-developed by DeepMind and Isomorphic Labs. Initially server-only; weights released for non-commercial use Nov 2024; publicly available Feb 2025.
- AlphaMissense (Sep 2023): predicts pathogenicity of missense variants.
- AlphaProteo (Sep 2024): generative protein-binder design.
- AlphaGenome (announced 2025): full genomic interpretation.
