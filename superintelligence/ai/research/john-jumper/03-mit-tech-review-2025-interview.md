# John Jumper — MIT Technology Review interview, November 24/25, 2025

Source: https://www.technologyreview.com/2025/11/24/1128322/whats-next-for-alphafold-a-conversation-with-a-google-deepmind-nobel-laureate/

Conducted post-Nobel, post-AlphaFold-3, this is the canonical late-2025 long-form interview with Jumper. It is the most-quoted "where does AlphaFold go next" piece in the calendar.

## Direct quotes

On AlphaFold as a database of predictions, not a database of truth:

> "That doesn't mean that we're certain of everything in there. It's a database of predictions, and it comes with all the caveats of predictions."

On unexpected uses:

> Researchers used AlphaFold for things he never imagined — honeybee disease resistance, sperm-egg binding protein identification, plastic-degrading enzyme design. He praised the community for using the tool with "neither too much nor too little" credulity.

On screening as a workflow change:

> "You would never do 2,000 structures looking for one answer" — but researchers using AlphaFold did exactly that to identify candidate proteins, fundamentally changing the search shape in biology.

On synthetic protein design speed:

> AlphaFold Multimer "can make the design process 10 times faster."

On LLMs entering science (this is his most-quoted late-2025 line):

> "I'll be shocked if we don't see more and more LLM impact on science."

On AlphaFold's limits — the consistent humility move:

> "AlphaFold will only tell us some [things] about biology and there is so much more — about the cell, about interactions, about the mesostructure, about regulation — that isn't directly even askable with the AlphaFold framework."

> "This was not the only problem in biology."

On his next chapter, post-Nobel:

> At 39 (youngest chemistry laureate in 75 years), he says he wants to pursue "smaller things, little ideas" rather than chasing another Nobel-Prize-level breakthrough. The thesis: many medium-sized wins for AI-in-biology beat one more giant one.

> "I really do hope that in time we will see AlphaFold as one of the first AI systems that deeply mattered [for] biology but just one of many, many that did."

On fusing AlphaFold's deep narrow power with LLMs' breadth:

> He wants to "fuse the deep but narrow power of AlphaFold with the broad sweep of LLMs." Translation: structured biology models + reasoning models, used together.

## Why this matters for the persona

- The "I'll be shocked if we don't see more and more LLM impact on science" line is the single strongest 2025 signal that Jumper's worldview is NOT text-vs-structured-bio sectarian. He sees LLMs and structured scientific models as complementary, not competitive.
- The "smaller things, little ideas" frame is a deliberate counter-signal to the prize-chasing AI-lab culture. It is the Jumper analogue of Hassabis's "multi-decade program" framing — instead of one more giant milestone, many medium wins compounding.
- The "database of predictions, with caveats" line is his canonical epistemic humility move. He will treat any AI-for-science output as a hypothesis, not a fact, and he will push back on systems that present predictions as truths.
