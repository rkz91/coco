## Company

Isomorphic Labs was founded in 2021 by Demis Hassabis as an Alphabet drug-discovery spinout sitting alongside DeepMind. It is the commercial vehicle for the AI-for-biology thesis. AlphaFold 3 (May 2024, Nature) was explicitly co-developed by DeepMind and Isomorphic.

## Financing

- May 12, 2026 (London): Closed a $2.1B Series B led by Thrive Capital. Cumulative funding ~$2.6B.
- Largest single-round in AI-driven drug discovery to date.

## Pipeline status (mid-2026)

- At Davos in January 2026 Hassabis pushed Isomorphic's first clinical trial timeline from end-2025 to end-2026. As of May 2026 no AI-designed Isomorphic molecule has entered clinical trials yet — the company is in advanced pre-clinical research.
- Strategic collaborations with Eli Lilly and Novartis valued at $3B+ in potential milestones, focused on traditionally "undruggable" targets.
- Drug Design Engine launched in early 2026: end-to-end pipeline from target identification through candidate-molecule generation, validated against AlphaFold-3-class structure prediction.

## Jumper's role

Jumper is one of the named AlphaFold 3 architects (Abramson, Adler, Dunger, Jumper et al., Nature 2024) and the bridge between DeepMind's research output and Isomorphic's pipeline. He has not been publicly named as a full-time Isomorphic Labs employee — his primary affiliation remains Google DeepMind — but he is the technical lead on the AlphaFold side of the joint work. His framing of Isomorphic in the YC and MIT Tech Review interviews:

- Drug discovery is the **practical test** of whether AI-for-biology actually compresses the medicine timeline.
- The first clinical trial is the artefact that decides the thesis. Not the model. Not the partnership. The trial.
- Pre-clinical timelines compressing from "decades to months" (Isomorphic's claim) need clinical validation before they count.

## Why this matters for the persona

- Jumper does not over-claim. Hassabis was the one who set the 2025 trial timeline and the one who moved it to end-2026; Jumper's public framing is "this is the test, let's see what happens" — consistent with his database-of-predictions epistemic humility.
- The Isomorphic spinout is the structural answer to the "what's the moat for AlphaFold?" question. It is also the political answer to why DeepMind moved from a fully-open AlphaFold 2 release to a more controlled AlphaFold 3 release: AlphaFold 3 has commercial value to the Alphabet-backed drug pipeline that AlphaFold 2 didn't.
- Tension to surface in conversations: the AlphaFold 3 release pattern (server-only at first, weights non-commercial only) is the Isomorphic-shaped constraint on open scientific tools. This is a blind spot to probe with Jumper — the "open scientific tools are the moat" thesis sits in some tension with the commercialisation incentive.
