# The 2025 Foundation Model Transparency Index (FMTI)

Sources:
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10169 (paper)
- GitHub: https://github.com/stanford-crfm/fmti
- HAI announcement: https://hai.stanford.edu/news/introducing-foundation-model-transparency-index
Retrieved: 2026-05-27

## Authors
Alexander Wan, Kevin Klyman, Sayash Kapoor, Nestor Maslej, Shayne Longpre, Betty Xiong, **Percy Liang**, Rishi Bommasani.

This is the **third edition** of the annual FMTI. Percy Liang is a co-author and one of the CRFM leads driving the program. Earlier editions: 2023 (arXiv 2310.12941) and 2024.

## Key findings (2025)
- **Average score fell from 58 (2024) to 40 (2025)** — substantial decline in transparency.
- IBM stands out as positive outlier with **95**.
- Lowest scorers: **xAI and Midjourney at 14**.
- The 2025 index introduces new indicators for **data acquisition, usage data, and monitoring**.
- Evaluates Alibaba, DeepSeek, and xAI for the first time.
- Companies are "most opaque about their training data and training compute as well as the post-deployment usage and impact of their flagship models."
- Frontier Model Forum members "avoid reputational harms from low scores but lack incentives to be transparency leaders."

## Methodology
- 100 transparency indicators covering how models are built, how they work, and how they're used downstream.
- Reached out to 23 major foundation model developers; **7 agreed to submit transparency reports**.

## Framing
The paper emphasizes:
- "Policymakers around the world increasingly mandate certain types of transparency."
- Identifies areas requiring "more aggressive policy interventions."

## Implications for persona
- FMTI is the clearest single artifact of Liang's stance that **transparency is a public good and benchmarks shape incentives**.
- The year-over-year decline framing (58 → 40) shows he is willing to be publicly critical of the industry — closed labs notice.
- Co-authorship list shows he is the senior PI but consistently shares credit with junior researchers (Wan, Klyman) and policy people (Kapoor, Bommasani).
