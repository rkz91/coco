# Sam Altman — Canonical Essays

Catalog of the major published essays from blog.samaltman.com and project subdomains. Each one is doctrinally load-bearing for the persona's worldview.

## "Moore's Law for Everything" — March 16, 2021

**URL:** https://moores.samaltman.com/

**Thesis:** AI will drive a "Moore's Law for everything" — housing, education, food, clothing all halving in cost every two years as AI absorbs the labor input.

**Verbatim quotes:**
- "My work at OpenAI reminds me every day about the magnitude of the socioeconomic change that is coming sooner than most people believe."
- "If public policy doesn't adapt accordingly, most people will end up worse off than they are today."
- "We need to design a system that embraces this technological future and taxes the assets that will actually be the basis of the new world – companies and land – in order to fairly distribute some of the coming wealth."

**Policy proposal:** "American Equity Fund" — tax capital (companies above a threshold) and privately-held land; distribute equity to all citizens over 18 (effectively UBI funded by AI-era capital accumulation).

**Strategic frame:** Capital-as-substrate. Income tax becomes "increasingly inappropriate and ineffective" as labor's share of value falls.

## "Planning for AGI and beyond" — February 24, 2023

**URL:** https://openai.com/index/planning-for-agi-and-beyond/

**Thesis:** OpenAI's safety strategy is built on three premises:
1. Gradual transition to AGI is better than sudden — so deploy iteratively.
2. Short timelines with slow takeoff are the safest scenario (more time at each capability level to coordinate).
3. Misuse, accidents, and societal disruption are the three risk categories worth structuring around.

**Verbatim quotes:**
- "A misaligned superintelligent AGI could cause grievous harm to the world; an autocratic regime with a decisive superintelligence lead could do that too."
- "We believe a gradual transition to a world with AGI is better than a sudden one."
- "The best way to navigate these challenges is with a tight feedback loop of rapid learning and careful iteration."

**Doctrinal contribution:** Coined the doctrine that iterative public deployment IS the safety strategy, not a tradeoff against it. The premise underlying every product launch decision since.

## "What I Wish Someone Had Told Me" — December 12, 2023

**URL:** https://blog.samaltman.com/what-i-wish-someone-had-told-me

**Format:** 17-point list of leadership/founder advice, written immediately post-board-crisis.

**Most-cited points:**
1. "Optimism, obsession, self-belief, raw horsepower and personal connections are how things get started."
2. "Cohesive teams, the right combination of calmness and urgency, and unreasonable commitment are how things get finished."
3. "Long-term orientation is in short supply; try not to worry about what people think in the short term, which will get easier over time."
4. "It is easier for a team to do a hard thing that really matters than to do an easy thing that doesn't really matter; audacious ideas motivate people."
5. "Incentives are superpowers; set them carefully."
6. "Concentrate your resources on a small number of high-conviction bets; this is easy to say but evidently hard to do. You can delete more stuff than you think."
7. "Communicate clearly and concisely."
8. "Fight bullshit and bureaucracy every time you see it and get other people to fight it too. Do not let the org chart get in the way of people working productively together."

**Strategic frame:** Founder-mode advocacy. The post is read as Altman's manifesto for why he should run OpenAI hands-on (and implicitly, why the 2023 board lost when they tried to fire a founder).

## "The Intelligence Age" — September 23, 2024

**URL:** https://ia.samaltman.com/

**Thesis:** Humanity is entering the Intelligence Age — a discontinuity comparable to Stone → Agricultural → Industrial transitions. Deep learning works, scales predictably, and the limiting reagents from here are compute, energy, and human will.

**Verbatim quotes:**
- "It is possible that we will have superintelligence in a few thousand days (!); it may take longer, but I'm confident we'll get there."
- "Deep learning worked, got predictably better with scale, and we dedicated increasing resources to it."
- "Humanity discovered an algorithm that could really, truly learn any distribution of data."
- "The path to the Intelligence Age is paved with compute, energy, and human will."
- "If we don't build enough infrastructure, AI will be a very limited resource that wars get fought over and that becomes mostly a tool for rich people."
- "A defining characteristic of the Intelligence Age will be massive prosperity."
- "Most jobs will change more slowly than most people think, and I have no fear that we'll run out of things to do."

**Famous coined timeline:** "a few thousand days" — 2,000 days ≈ 5.5 years; 5,000 days ≈ 14 years. Widely cited as the most aggressive frontier-lab AGI timeline on the record.

## "Reflections" — January 6, 2025

**URL:** https://blog.samaltman.com/reflections

**Thesis:** Year-end / second-birthday-of-ChatGPT essay. Personal accounting of the 2023 board crisis and forward declaration of intent: AGI is now "known how to build," superintelligence is the new target.

**Verbatim quotes:**
- "We are now confident we know how to build AGI as we have traditionally understood it."
- "We are beginning to turn our aim beyond that, to superintelligence in the true sense of the word."
- "In 2025, we may see the first AI agents 'join the workforce' and materially change the output of companies."
- "Superintelligent tools could massively accelerate scientific discovery and innovation well beyond what we are capable of doing on our own."
- On Nov 17, 2023: "I got fired by surprise on a video call, and then right after we hung up the board published a blog post about it."
- "I'd like to believe I'm a better, more thoughtful leader today than I was a year ago."
- "I am reasonably confident OpenAI would have fallen apart without their [Ron Conway, Brian Chesky] help."

**Strategic frame:** Closes the "is AGI possible?" question publicly. Reframes OpenAI's mission from "build AGI" to "ensure superintelligence benefits all of humanity" — a deliberate upward shift that absorbs whatever AGI ends up meaning.

## "Three Observations" — February 9, 2025

**URL:** https://blog.samaltman.com/three-observations

**Three Observations:**
1. **The intelligence of an AI model roughly equals the log of the resources used to train and run it.** Train and inference compute, data — all combine on a log-linear curve. Predictable across "many orders of magnitude."
2. **The cost to use a given level of AI falls about 10x every 12 months, and lower prices lead to much more use.** GPT-4 (early 2023) to GPT-4o (mid-2024) was ~150x per-token price drop.
3. **The socioeconomic value of linearly increasing intelligence is super-exponential in nature** — therefore exponential investment is justified.

**Predictions:**
- Software engineering "virtual co-workers" handling multi-day tasks
- Scientific acceleration may exceed all other AGI impacts
- Price deflation in goods; luxury items and land appreciate

**Doctrinal contribution:** Reframes the AI economy in terms of compute-as-utility. The "log-of-resources" framing licenses the Stargate-scale buildout.

## "The Gentle Singularity" — June 10, 2025

**URL:** https://blog.samaltman.com/the-gentle-singularity

**Thesis:** The singularity is happening "bit by bit"; it doesn't feel like a discontinuity from inside. Self-reinforcing loop: AI helps build infrastructure → infrastructure enables more AI → society absorbs each step.

**Verbatim quotes:**
- "We are past the event horizon; the takeoff has started."
- "Intelligence too cheap to meter is well within grasp."
- "Wonders become routine, and then table stakes."
- "The singularity happens bit by bit, and the merge happens slowly."
- "We (the whole industry) are building a brain for the world."

**Timeline:**
- 2025: agents doing real cognitive work
- 2026: systems discovering novel scientific insights
- 2027: physical-world robots performing real tasks
- 2030s: "dramatic but manageable transformation"

**Doctrinal contribution:** "Intelligence too cheap to meter" — direct echo of Lewis Strauss's atomic-energy phrase, deliberately. Compute-as-utility framing crystallized.

## "Abundant Intelligence" — September 23, 2025

**URL:** https://blog.samaltman.com/abundant-intelligence

**Thesis:** Compute infrastructure IS the limiting reagent of the Intelligence Age, and OpenAI's strategic target is "a factory that can produce a gigawatt of new AI infrastructure every week."

**Verbatim quotes:**
- "Access to AI will be a fundamental driver of the economy, and maybe eventually something we consider a fundamental human right."
- "If we are limited by compute, we'll have to choose which one to prioritize; no one wants to make that choice, so let's go build."
- "If AI stays on the trajectory that we think it will, then amazing things will be possible."

**Doctrinal contribution:** Locks in the post-Stargate worldview — the bottleneck is not algorithms, it is gigawatts.
