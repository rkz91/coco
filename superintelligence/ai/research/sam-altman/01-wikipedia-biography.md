# Sam Altman — Wikipedia Biographical Synthesis

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Altman (retrieved 2026-05-27)
**Related:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Removal_of_Sam_Altman_from_OpenAI, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musk_v._Altman, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stargate_LLC

## Identity

- **Full name:** Samuel Harris Altman
- **Born:** April 22, 1985, Chicago, Illinois (raised in Clayton / St. Louis, Missouri)
- **Mother:** Connie Gibstine, dermatologist
- **Father:** Jerry Altman, real estate broker (deceased)
- **Siblings:** Jack Altman (brother, Lattice CEO, co-investor in Hydrazine Capital), Max Altman (brother), Annie Altman (sister, estranged)
- **Spouse:** Oliver Mulherin (married January 2024)
- **Child:** Son born 2025
- **Heritage:** Jewish; great-grandfather from Płock, Poland
- **Sexuality:** Came out at age 17 in high school; previously partnered with Loopt co-founder Nick Sivo (nine years)

## Education

- John Burroughs School (Ladue, Missouri) — private K-12
- Stanford University, computer science — enrolled 2003, dropped out 2005 without degree. Later said he "learned more playing poker with classmates than from professors."
- Honorary doctorate from University of Waterloo (2017)

## Controversies & Critiques

- **November 2023 ouster:** Helen Toner (May 2024 TED Interview) alleged Altman provided "inaccurate information about formal safety processes," learned about ChatGPT launch via Twitter, did not disclose ownership of OpenAI Startup Fund. Toner and Tasha McCauley wrote The Economist op-ed: "self-governance cannot reliably withstand the pressure of profit incentives."
- **Annie Altman lawsuit (Jan 2025):** Sister filed sexual abuse suit in E.D. Mo. covering alleged events 1997–2006. Sam denied; family (mother, Jack, Max) issued joint denial. Counter-suit for defamation. Annie's lawyers withdrew April 2026 citing breakdown of attorney-client relationship.
- **Worldcoin biometric concerns:** Regulators in France, UK, Bavaria, South Korea, Spain, Portugal, Hong Kong, Kenya investigated or suspended operations.
- **Safety vs. velocity tension:** Departure of Jan Leike, Ilya Sutskever, and other Superalignment team members (2024) frequently framed as Altman prioritizing capability launches over safety pacing.
- **Musk v. Altman:** Filed Feb 2024; tried Apr 2026; OpenAI prevailed on statute-of-limitations technicality. Underlying dispute about for-profit conversion remains ideologically live.
