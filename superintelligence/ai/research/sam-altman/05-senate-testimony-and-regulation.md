# Senate Testimony & Regulatory Posture Evolution

## May 16, 2023 — First Senate Testimony

**Venue:** US Senate Judiciary Subcommittee on Privacy, Technology, and the Law
**Hearing title:** "Oversight of AI: Rules for Artificial Intelligence"
**Co-witnesses:** Christina Montgomery (IBM), Gary Marcus (NYU)

**Altman's stated positions:**
- Endorsed a new federal agency to license large AI models above a capability threshold
- Endorsed safety standards including evaluations for autonomy and self-replication
- Endorsed third-party auditing of frontier models
- Conceded "if this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong"
- Senator Richard Blumenthal (D-CT) opened the hearing by playing an AI-generated clone of his own voice reading remarks written by ChatGPT

**Key Altman quote:**
> "I think if this technology goes wrong, it can go quite wrong. We want to be vocal about that. We want to work with the government to prevent that from happening."

**Reception at the time:** Lauded as a rare case of a tech CEO actively requesting regulation. Compared favorably to social-media-CEO testimony of prior years.

## May 8, 2025 — Second Senate Testimony

**Venue:** US Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation
**Hearing title:** "Winning the AI Race: Strengthening US Capabilities in Computing and Innovation"
**Co-witnesses:** Brad Smith (Microsoft President), Lisa Su (AMD CEO), Michael Intrator (CoreWeave CEO)

**Sources:**
- https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2025/05/08/altman-congress-openai-regulation/
- https://www.techpolicy.press/transcript-sam-altman-testifies-at-us-senate-hearing-on-ai-competitiveness/
- https://www.commerce.senate.gov/meetings/winning-the-ai-race-strengthening-u-s-capabilities-in-computing-and-innovation/

**Altman's stated positions — substantially reversed from 2023:**
- Requiring government approval to release powerful AI software would be "disastrous" for US leadership
- A patchwork of state regulation would be unworkable: "It is very difficult to imagine us figuring out how to comply with 50 different sets of regulations"
- Advocated for "one federal framework that is light touch, that we can understand, and it lets us move with the speed that this moment calls for"
- Reframed the policy question from "how to constrain AI" to "how to win the race against China"

**Key Altman quote:**
> "We need to make sure that the technology of the world is built on American AI, with American values."

**Reception:** Marked the inflection point at which the AI industry consolidated around an anti-regulatory posture. The Washington Post lede: "Altman's testimony marks a notable shift in posture for some of the industry leaders, such as Altman, who just two years prior openly embraced calls for AI regulation."

## What Changed Between 2023 and 2025

Three structural drivers, each consistent with Altman's stated worldview:

1. **DeepSeek R1 shock (January 2025).** Chinese frontier model demonstrated that US capability lead is contingent, not durable. Reframed the marginal regulation as a self-imposed handicap in a race.
2. **Trump administration installed.** Biden's EO 14110 was rescinded; the political window for licensing regimes closed. Altman's posture tracks where the policy levers actually are.
3. **Stargate announcement (Jan 21, 2025).** OpenAI is now operationally a national AI champion. Industrial policy and regulation are mirror images — one accelerates, the other constrains the same buildout.

## Altman's Coherent (If Self-Serving) Narrative

He has been consistent on one underlying claim: **the right level of oversight depends on the capability of the model being released**. In 2023, "frontier models" meant GPT-4 — capable but contained. In 2025, "frontier models" meant systems on the path to AGI and a Chinese race condition. Altman argues the calculation flipped because the world flipped, not because he flipped.

The opposition view (Toner, Leike, Anthropic-side commentators) is that the calculation flipped because the commercial incentives flipped — OpenAI now needs Stargate-scale capital deployment, and any meaningful regulation slows that down.
