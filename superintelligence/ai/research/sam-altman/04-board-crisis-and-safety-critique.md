## The Firing — November 17, 2023

**Board at the time:**
- Helen Toner (Center for Security and Emerging Technology, Georgetown)
- Tasha McCauley (RAND-adjacent, EA-aligned)
- Adam D'Angelo (Quora CEO)
- Ilya Sutskever (OpenAI co-founder, Chief Scientist)
- Greg Brockman (Chair, OpenAI President)
- Sam Altman

**Vote:** 4-2 to remove Altman (Toner, McCauley, D'Angelo, Sutskever in favor)

**Method:** Altman informed via Google Meet, ~5-10 minutes before the public blog post went live. He was watching the Las Vegas Grand Prix at the time.

**Stated reason (board's public statement):** Altman was "not consistently candid in his communications with the board, hindering its ability to exercise its responsibilities."

## Helen Toner's Stated Reasons (TED Interview, May 2024)

Toner gave the most comprehensive board-side account on the TED AI Show podcast with Bilawal Sidhu, May 28, 2024. Key claims:

1. **ChatGPT launch was not pre-disclosed to the board.** "When ChatGPT came out in November 2022, the board was not informed in advance about that. We learned about ChatGPT on Twitter."
2. **Altman did not disclose ownership of the OpenAI Startup Fund** despite repeatedly telling the board he had no financial interest in the company.
3. **Altman provided "inaccurate information about the small number of formal safety processes the company had in place"** on multiple occasions, making board oversight of safety effectively impossible.
4. **Two senior executives came to the board in October 2023** with "screenshots and documentation" describing "psychological abuse" and saying they could not trust Altman.
5. **Triggering event:** Toner co-authored a paper (October 2023) that compared Anthropic's safety approach favorably to OpenAI's. Altman reportedly retaliated by trying to push her off the board, lying to other board members in the process — which crystallized the loss-of-confidence vote.

## The Reinstatement — November 21, 2023

- ~700 of OpenAI's ~770 employees signed an open letter demanding Altman's reinstatement and the board's resignation, threatening to follow Altman to Microsoft (Satya Nadella had publicly offered to hire the entire OpenAI team)
- Sutskever publicly flipped, said he regretted his vote
- Toner and McCauley exited the board
- New initial board: Bret Taylor (chair), Larry Summers, Adam D'Angelo
- Microsoft observer seat added
- Altman reinstated as CEO; later restored to the board

## The Toner / McCauley Op-Ed — May 2024

Toner and McCauley published in The Economist (May 26, 2024):
> "We believe that self-governance cannot reliably withstand the pressure of profit incentives."

They argued that the post-crisis configuration of OpenAI — Altman back on the board, the Superalignment team disbanded, safety-focused talent leaving — vindicated the board's original concerns rather than refuting them.

## Departures of Safety-Focused Talent (2024-2025)

This is the recurring critique that follows Altman:
- **Ilya Sutskever** — left May 2024, founded Safe Superintelligence Inc.
- **Jan Leike** — co-lead of Superalignment, left May 2024. Tweeted: "safety culture and processes have taken a backseat to shiny products." Joined Anthropic.
- **Daniel Kokotajlo** — quit over "loss of confidence" that OpenAI would behave responsibly around AGI
- **Mira Murati** — CTO, left September 2024
- **Bob McGrew, Barret Zoph** — left around same time as Murati
- **Miles Brakes** (policy/preparedness) — multiple departures over 2024-2025
- Superalignment team disbanded mid-2024

## Altman's Public Response (in "Reflections," Jan 2025)

> "I am not proud of being conflict-averse, which has caused pain for myself and OpenAI, and of handling myself badly in a conflict with the previous board that led to problems for the company."

This is the closest he has come to a public concession of fault. He does not concede the substance of Toner's specific allegations.

## What This Reveals About Altman's Worldview

1. **Founder-mode supremacy.** The successful post-crisis outcome — board reconstituted around him, employee bloc backing him, commercial trajectory intact — vindicates (in his framing) the founder-centric model over independent-board oversight.
2. **Iterative deployment > board oversight as the safety mechanism.** The safety strategy is to ship and observe in public, not to gate releases through internal committees.
3. **Profit-incentive pressure is a feature, not a bug.** Altman's view: the capital is what builds the gigawatts; without the for-profit structure there is no Stargate.
4. **Helen Toner / Tasha McCauley / Jan Leike / Daniel Kokotajlo are the *opposition voice* in the AI safety discourse.** A persona profile of Altman must acknowledge that opposition exists and is substantive — even if Altman disagrees with it on the merits.

## Blind-Spot Material for the Profile

- **Track record on safety pacing.** The departures and the Leike framing are durable evidence that velocity has pulled harder than caution at OpenAI in 2024-2025.
- **Conflict aversion as self-diagnosis.** Altman's own admission of this trait is also a tell for why governance challenges to him have not stuck.
- **Family allegations.** The Annie Altman lawsuit is unresolved and ongoing; it sits in the background of any public persona discussion.
