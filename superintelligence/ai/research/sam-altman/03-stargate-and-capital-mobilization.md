## Announcement

**Date:** January 21, 2025
**Venue:** White House press conference, presented alongside President Donald Trump on his second day in office
**Principals on stage:** Sam Altman (OpenAI), Larry Ellison (Oracle), Masayoshi Son (SoftBank)

## Structure

- Stargate LLC, Delaware
- Joint venture: OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, MGX (Abu Dhabi sovereign-wealth tech fund)
- Initial capital: SoftBank and OpenAI each $19B (40% ownership each); Oracle and MGX each $7B; remainder from LPs and debt financing
- Microsoft is a technology partner but not an equity holder in Stargate (Microsoft holds 27% of OpenAI Group PBC separately)

## Headline Numbers

- $500 billion total commitment over four years to AI infrastructure in the United States
- 10 gigawatts of AI-dedicated compute capacity
- Initial site: Abilene, Texas (Crusoe-built data center)
- September 23, 2025 expansion: five new sites — Shackelford County TX, Doña Ana County NM, Lordstown OH, Milam County TX, plus one midwestern site
- Stated trajectory: full $500B / 10GW commitment secured by end of 2025, ahead of schedule

## Strategic Significance

Altman's framing places Stargate as the **operational embodiment of the "compute is the new commodity" thesis**. The investment thesis is downstream of "Three Observations":
- If intelligence scales with log(compute), then 10x more compute is a known step on the capability curve
- If cost per token drops 10x per year, demand absorbs supply faster than supply can be built
- Therefore the rate-limiter is gigawatts on the ground, not algorithms in the lab

## Quotes from the Announcement

Altman in the press release:
> "We want to thank President Trump for his leadership on AI and infrastructure. We wouldn't be able to do this without you, Mr. President. We want to do this in the United States."

Altman in the months following:
> "Compute is the currency of the future."
> "We need to be building gigawatts on the ground in the US, not just optimizing models in a lab."

## Political Context

- The announcement was made the day after Trump rescinded Biden's AI Executive Order 14110 (which had constrained large-model training and required reporting)
- Stargate is widely read as the OpenAI / Trump-admin alignment moment — Altman shifted from being the leading industry voice for federal AI licensing (May 2023 Senate testimony) to being the leading voice against patchwork regulation (May 2025 Senate testimony)
- Elon Musk attacked the announcement on X, claiming SoftBank did not have the capital
- Anthropic, DeepMind, and xAI did not participate — making this an OpenAI-coded national-champion play

## What This Reveals About Altman's Worldview

1. **Compute is the strategic moat.** Algorithms travel; gigawatts don't.
2. **Industrial policy beats individual lab strategy.** The relevant unit is "the US national AI program," not "OpenAI's product."
3. **The administration that owns the buildout owns the leverage.** Hence the Trump-admin pivot.
4. **The 14H model:** OpenAI's role is to be the orchestrator of capital and compute that makes the Intelligence Age physically possible, not just to ship the smartest model.
