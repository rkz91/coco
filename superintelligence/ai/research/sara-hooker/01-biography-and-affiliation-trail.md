## Origins

Sara Hooker was born in Dublin, Ireland. Her family relocated to Lesotho when she was four, and she grew up across Lesotho, South Africa, Mozambique, Eswatini, and Kenya until age 19. This origin story is referenced repeatedly in her own framing of her work: she describes a "necessary humility about the world" formed by growing up across Sub-Saharan Africa, and frames the multilingual AI program (Aya) as deliberately serving the wealth of talent that exists outside rich countries.

## Education

- Bachelor of Arts, Carleton College (class of 2013). Carleton lists her major as economics in their TIME 100 announcement; her own bio elides the major but Carleton's identification is the primary source.
- Ph.D., Computer Science, supervised by Hugo Larochelle and Aaron Courville. Wikipedia lists Mila / Université de Montréal as the granting institution. The Ph.D. was earned during/around her Google Brain tenure.

The economics-to-ML transition matters narratively: she did not enter the field through a conventional CS pipeline, and she leans on that fact when she talks about who else does not have a conventional pipeline into AI.

## Founder of Delta Analytics (2014)

In 2014, while still early in her career, she founded Delta Analytics, a non-profit focused on "developing technical capacity for non-profits." This is the first signal of her institution-building instinct that later produced Cohere For AI and Adaption Labs.

## Google Brain / Google DeepMind (2017–2022)

She joined Google Brain in 2017 as a research scientist. Two artifacts from this period define her public identity:

1. The Hardware Lottery essay (2020) — see file 02.
2. "What Do Compressed Deep Neural Networks Forget?" (Hooker et al, 2019) — see file 03.

She was also the founder of Google's first AI research office in Accra, Ghana, which prefigures Cohere For AI's global-recruiting strategy. Wikipedia confirms she "established Google's inaugural AI research office in Accra, Ghana."

## Cohere / Cohere For AI / Cohere Labs (April 2022 – August/September 2025)

- Joined Cohere in April 2022 to direct what was then called Cohere For AI (later rebranded to Cohere Labs).
- Title progression: head of Cohere For AI → Vice President of Research.
- Launched the Aya program (Aya Dataset, Aya 23, Aya Expanse 8B and 32B, Aya Vision).
- Launched the Cohere For AI Scholars Program — a paid research residency targeted at researchers from underrepresented regions.
- Departed Cohere in August 2025; remained through September 2025 to oversee the next Cohere For AI Scholars cohort. TechCrunch (October 22, 2025) and BetaKit confirm both the departure timing and the wind-down.

## Adaption Labs (September/October 2025 – present)

- Co-founded with Sudip Roy, former director of inference computing at Cohere. Both are also Google alumni.
- Adaption Labs was announced quietly on October 7–9, 2025 (BetaKit places the announcement October 9; TechCrunch covered the public framing October 22).
- $50M seed announced February 4, 2026, led by Emergence Capital Partners with Mozilla Ventures, Fifty Years, Threshold Ventures, Alpha Intelligence Capital, E14 Fund, and Neo participating.
- Hooker is CEO. Sudip Roy is CTO.
- Headquartered in San Francisco. Hybrid hiring in Canada and the UK.
- Stated focus: adaptive data, adaptive intelligence, adaptive interfaces. Gradient-free learning at inference, on-the-fly adapter merging, dynamic decoding.

## Advisory and Recognition

- TIME 100 AI (2024). Cohere For AI head at the time of recognition.
- Observer AI Power Index 2025 — she tweeted on September 18, 2025 that she was "honored to be recognized in @observer 2025 AI Power Index. It is very meaningful to receive as I close one chapter and begin another." This is the public confirmation of the Cohere → Adaption Labs transition.
- Fortune 13 top AI innovators (2023).
- Kaggle ML Advisory Research Board member.
- World Economic Forum council on the Future of Artificial Intelligence and Data Frontiers.
