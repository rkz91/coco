# Mira Murati — Biography & Career Arc

Source synthesis: Wikipedia, Fortune (Oct 3 2025), Dartmouth Thayer, CNBC Changemaker 2026, Built In.

## Personal background

- Born **December 16, 1988** in **Vlorë, Albania**.
- Albanian-American; fluent in Italian.
- Education path: Pearson College UWC (IB, 2007) → Colby College (BA, 2011, dual-degree) → **Dartmouth Thayer School of Engineering (BEng mechanical engineering, 2012)**.
- Built a hybrid race car as an undergraduate engineering project at Dartmouth.

## Pre-OpenAI career (2011–2018)

| Year | Role | Org |
|------|------|-----|
| 2011 | Summer analyst (Tokyo) | Goldman Sachs |
| 2012–2013 | Advanced Concepts Engineer | Zodiac Aerospace |
| 2013–2016 | Senior Product Manager, Model X program | Tesla |
| 2016–2018 | VP Product & Engineering | Leap Motion (now Ultraleap) |

The Tesla Model X role is the formative product-engineering experience — first exposure to integrating perception/AI systems with consumer-facing product. Leap Motion gave her hands-on multimodal-input experience (hand-tracking) before joining OpenAI.

## OpenAI (June 2018 – September 2024)

| Year | Role |
|------|------|
| 2018 | VP, Applied AI & Partnerships |
| 2020 | SVP, Research, Product & Partnerships |
| May 2022 | **Chief Technology Officer** |
| Nov 17–22, 2023 | **Interim CEO** (board ouster of Sam Altman, ~3-day tenure) |
| Sept 25, 2024 | **Departure announcement** |

Products shipped under her CTO tenure:
- **ChatGPT** (Nov 2022) — the product launch that made OpenAI a consumer company.
- **DALL-E** series (image generation).
- **Codex** (code generation precursor to GitHub Copilot).
- **GPT-4** (March 2023).
- **GPT-4o** (May 2024) — native voice mode.
- **Sora** (Dec 2024, conceived and developed under her watch though publicly released after her departure).

Stepping-away memo (Sept 25, 2024): "I want to create the time and space to do my own exploration."

## Interim-CEO interlude (Nov 2023)

- Collaborated with Ilya Sutskever; Sutskever's 52-page memo to the board concerning Altman drew heavily on screenshots and information Murati provided.
- Took the interim CEO role for roughly 48 hours before the board moved to Emmett Shear; Altman returned within five days; Murati reverted to CTO.
- Under deposition (subsequently surfaced in legal proceedings during 2025–2026), she testified that Altman had cultivated patterns of inconsistency in his communications with leadership — material that the press has flagged as a delayed but consequential corroboration of the board's original "not consistently candid" framing.

## Thinking Machines Lab (Feb 2025 – present)

- **Founded Feb 18, 2025**, San Francisco. Public benefit corporation.
- **CEO** (with deciding-vote board structure; her vote is weighted to give her majority decision power).
- Co-founders / leadership: **John Schulman** (Chief Scientist, OpenAI co-founder), **Barret Zoph** (CTO, ex-OpenAI VP Research), **Lilian Weng** (ex-OpenAI VP, safety/applied), **Andrew Tulloch** (later poached back to Meta), **Luke Metz** (returned to OpenAI Jan 2026).
- **Advisers:** Bob McGrew (former OpenAI Chief Research Officer), Alec Radford (ex-OpenAI researcher).
- **~30 employees** at launch from OpenAI, Meta, Mistral, Character AI, Google DeepMind; **~100 employees** by 2026.
- **Funding:**
  - May 2025: **$10M** from the Albanian government (required Albanian budget amendment — Murati's heritage tie made material).
  - June/July 2025: closed **$2B seed round** at **$12B post-money** valuation, led by **a16z** with Nvidia, AMD, Cisco, Accel, ServiceNow, Jane Street, Conviction Partners. Largest seed round in Silicon Valley history.
  - March 2026: undisclosed Nvidia follow-on + multi-year compute agreement.
  - April 2026: **multi-billion-dollar Google Cloud deal** (single-digit billions, non-exclusive) for GB300 access.
- **Meta poaching attempt (mid-2025):** Zuckerberg's "full-scale raid" with offers of $200M–$1.5B per researcher. Reportedly zero defections — Fortune attributed to "both Murati's leadership and the team's belief in Thinking Machines' mission."

## Public posture

- "AI should serve as an extension of individual agency and, in the spirit of freedom, be distributed as widely and equitably as possible."
- "It's important for OpenAI and companies like ours to bring this into the public consciousness in a way that's controlled and responsible."
- "For capabilities and safety, they're actually not separate domains. They go hand-in-hand. It's much easier to direct a smarter system by telling it, okay, just don't do these things."
- TIME 100 AI 2024 listed her with the framing that her next-gen text model could "possess the intelligence of a PhD student for some specific tasks."

## Controversy thread

- 2024 stumbles: (a) evasive answer on whether Sora's training data drew from YouTube; (b) the "creative jobs that maybe shouldn't have been there in the first place" comment that drew artist/writer backlash.
- These remain the highest-profile public-perception risks attached to her, and shape how she now leads with safety framing first when discussing capability roll-out.
