# Horace He's Move from Meta/PyTorch to Thinking Machines Lab

Primary source: https://www.thonking.ai/p/why-pytorch-is-an-amazing-place-to ("Why PyTorch is an amazing place to work… and Why I'm Joining Thinking Machines"), dated **March 4, 2025**.
Secondary press: https://news.aibase.com/news/15968 ("Horace He, a leading figure in PyTorch, departs Meta to join a startup founded by OpenAI's former CTO").
Related industry departure: https://soumith.ch/blog/2025-11-06-leaving-meta-and-pytorch.md.html (Soumith Chintala's own departure post, separate but contextually relevant — PyTorch lead leaves Meta later in 2025).

## What he said (his words from the Substack post)

He praised PyTorch as a workplace, citing four reasons:

1. **Industry impact.** He estimates "at least 75% of these GPUs are running some kind of PyTorch code."
2. **Mission alignment among colleagues.** "Many of the people on the project _are_ mission aligned — they do genuinely care about the overall success of PyTorch."
3. **Open-source culture.** PyTorch enables career growth through community impact, not corporate roadmap politics.
4. **"Ungameable" impact.** "OSS users only care that 1. you're solving a problem they have, and 2. they like using your software." You cannot fake adoption.

He then laid out three reasons to leave anyway:

1. **Team quality.** Direct access to former collaborators he respects, now consolidated in one organization.
2. **Founding-engineer leverage.** Joining early gives him asymmetric influence on culture and direction.
3. **Mission resonance.** He prefers Thinking Machines' framing — **"product-focus + open science"** — over a pure safety-research orientation. He wants to address societal impacts of AI by making capabilities broadly diffused, not by gating them.

## Where his focus shifts

At Thinking Machines, his stated areas:

- **Collaborative AI products over purely autonomous agents.** This is the line that telegraphs the May 2026 "Interaction Models" direction the lab later announces.
- **Open science and systems work to democratize AI knowledge.** This is why his first Connectionism post is a freely-released open-source library, not a closed result.
- **Broader diffusion of AI capability across society.** Less safety-pessimist, more capability-distribution-optimist.

## Why this matters for the persona

- Affiliation as of 2026-05-27: **Thinking Machines Lab** (founding-team / ML-systems researcher). Past: Meta PyTorch core.
- His public motivation frame is **"product + open science"** — that shapes how he reads architectural proposals. He will favor: (a) shipped artifacts, (b) public benchmarks, (c) human-in-the-loop products. He will disfavor: pure safety theatre with no shipped capability, and closed-source claims of progress.
- The Thinking Machines team has been deliberately quiet in 2025–2026 beyond a handful of blog posts and the May 2026 Interaction Models preview, so his publicly observable output during this window is constrained.
