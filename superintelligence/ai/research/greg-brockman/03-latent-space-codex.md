# Latent Space — "How GPT5 + Codex took over Agentic Coding" with Greg Brockman (2025-09-16)

Source: https://www.latent.space/p/gpt5-codex

## On agentic engineering company goal
- "At the beginning of the year, we set a company goal of an agentic software engineer by the end of the year."

## On code review bottleneck
- "The big bottleneck for us was with increased amounts of code needing to be reviewed is like the amount of review simply that people had to do on the teams."
- "We decided to really focus on a very high signal Codex mode where it's able to review a PR and really think deeply about the contract and the intention that you were meaning to implement."

## On model reliability / doubling-down problem
- "I remember for GPT-3 and for GPT-4 really focusing on the doubling down problem. Do you remember if the AI would say something wrong and you'd point out the mistake? It would try to convince you that it was right."
- "It's really amazing to see that we're at a level where even when it's not quite zeroed in on the right thing, it's highlighting stuff that matters."

## On AI engineering as a skill
- "There is a real skill in extracting the most from these models. And it requires this tenacity, right, of really trying to understand the shape of the model skills and weaknesses."
- "You want to have multiple agents, right? You want to be a manager of agents, not just one instance of the model operating."

## On codebases designed for AI
- "You really build code bases around the strengths and weaknesses of these models. More self-contained units have very good unit tests that run super quickly and that have good documentation."

## Signature framing
Engineering productivity is now a function of how well your codebase, your review pipeline, and your tooling are shaped for the agent — not just the model.
