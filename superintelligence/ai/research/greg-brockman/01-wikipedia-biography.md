# Greg Brockman — Wikipedia biography (extracted 2026-05-27)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greg_Brockman

## Early life and education
- Born November 29, 1987, Thompson, North Dakota
- Silver medal, 2006 International Chemistry Olympiad
- First North Dakota finalist in the Intel Science Talent Search since 1973
- Canada/USA Mathcamp 2003, 2005, 2007
- Harvard 2008 (one year), transferred to MIT, then dropped out

## Career
- Stripe 2010–2015: joined as early employee under MIT classmate Patrick Collison; became first CTO in 2013; scaled the company from 5 to 205 employees; left May 2015
- OpenAI co-founder December 2015 (with Sam Altman, Ilya Sutskever, et al.); led recruiting; company initially operated "from Brockman's living room"

### Key technical contributions
- OpenAI Gym (RL benchmarking standard)
- OpenAI Five (Dota 2)
- GPT-2 announcement, February 14, 2019
- GPT-4 live demo, March 14, 2023

### Boardroom crisis November 2023
- November 17, 2023: removed as CTO and board chair when board fired Sam Altman
- November 20, 2023: briefly joined Microsoft with Altman
- November 21, 2023: returned to OpenAI when Altman was reinstated

### Sabbatical and return
- August 2024 to November 2024 sabbatical
- Returned November 12, 2024, posting "Back to building OpenAI"; focus on "key technical challenges"
- Created new "Scaling" team merging deep-learning engineering of research and applied teams

## Personal
- Married Anna in November 2019 at OpenAI offices; Ilya Sutskever officiated

## 2025–2026 political activity
- $12.5M each (he and his wife) to Trump's MAGA Inc Super PAC
- $50M to "Leading the Future" Super PAC for AI deregulation (co-founded with Marc Andreessen, Ben Horowitz)
- OpenAI's stated policy opposes such political contributions

## Legal
- Jury selection April 27, 2026 in Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI and executives including Brockman; trial testimony April 28, 2026
