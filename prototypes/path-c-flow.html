1

Design Philosophy

A PM brain that feels like a luxury instrument.
Principle 01

Command-First

Type what you need, don't hunt for it. No sidebar, no menu hierarchy. Press / and the entire platform is at your fingertips.

Principle 02

Cards with Depth

Every item shows context, not just a title. Status, owner, age, urgency — all visible at glance. Expand for the full story.

Principle 03

Keyboard Native

3 keystrokes max for any action. j/k to navigate, Enter to expand, x to act. Mouse is optional, never required.

Pros & Cons

Strengths

  • Fastest possible interaction — keyboard-first, zero navigation
  • Minimal learning curve: it's a list you scroll and act on
  • Works like email/todo apps people already know (Superhuman, Linear)
  • Ultra-lightweight frontend — could be under 500 LOC
  • One mental model: everything is a card in the stream
  • No state management complexity — the stream IS the state

Trade-offs

  • No visual portfolio overview (tree/hierarchy not visible by default)
  • Deep content (knowledge base, long docs) can feel cramped inline
  • Org chart and agent hierarchy require explicit command to view
  • Self-improve cycle workflow doesn't map naturally to flat stream
  • Requires keyboard comfort — not mouse-friendly by design
  • Unfamiliar pattern for users expecting traditional dashboards
8

Why This Approach

CoCo Platform started as a dashboard for everything. 17 pages, sidebar navigation, modals, slide-overs, tabs — the works. But Rijul is one person. He doesn't need an enterprise dashboard. He doesn't need 17 pages to manage 13 teams.

He needs one stream that shows what matters and lets him act instantly. Open the app, see the most urgent thing, press a key, done. That's it. That's the entire product.

This is that stream, wrapped in craft. Every pixel, every interaction, every keystroke has been considered. Not because complexity was added — because everything unnecessary was removed.
